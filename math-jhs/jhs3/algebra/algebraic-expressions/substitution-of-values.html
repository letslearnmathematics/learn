Introduction

A formula is a mathematical relationship expressed using symbols and variables. Formulas allow us to describe relationships between quantities in a concise way.

In this lesson, we will learn how to substitute given values into formulae to evaluate them and use them to solve real-world problems. This skill is fundamental in algebra and has applications across many areas of mathematics and science.

Example of a simple formula:

\[ A = l \times w \]

Where:

  • \( A \) = Area
  • \( l \) = length
  • \( w \) = width

Key Concepts:

  • Identify the variables in the formula
  • Replace variables with given values
  • Follow the correct order of operations (BODMAS)
  • Include appropriate units in your answer

Basic Substitution

Step-by-Step Process

When substituting values into a formula:

  1. Write down the formula clearly
  2. Identify the variables and their given values
  3. Replace the variables with the given values
  4. Perform the calculations following BODMAS rules
  5. State your final answer with appropriate units

Example 1: Evaluate \( y = 3x + 5 \) when \( x = 4 \)

Solution:

\[ \begin{align*} y &= 3x + 5 \\ &= 3(4) + 5 \quad \text{(Substitute } x = 4) \\ &= 12 + 5 \\ &= 17 \end{align*} \]

Example 2: Find the area of a rectangle with length 8cm and width 5cm using \( A = l \times w \)

Solution:

\[ \begin{align*} A &= l \times w \\ &= 8 \times 5 \quad \text{(Substitute } l = 8, w = 5) \\ &= 40 \text{ cm}^2 \end{align*} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Evaluate \( P = 2(l + w) \) when \( l = 12 \) and \( w = 7 \)

\[ \begin{align*} P &= 2(l + w) \\ &= 2(12 + 7) \\ &= 2(19) \\ &= 38 \end{align*} \]

Question 2

Find the value of \( y = 4x^2 - 3x + 7 \) when \( x = 2 \)

\[ \begin{align*} y &= 4x^2 - 3x + 7 \\ &= 4(2)^2 - 3(2) + 7 \\ &= 4(4) - 6 + 7 \\ &= 16 - 6 + 7 \\ &= 17 \end{align*} \]

Question 3

Calculate \( V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \) when \( r = 7 \) (use \( \pi = \frac{22}{7} \))

\[ \begin{align*} V &= \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \\ &= \frac{4}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 7^3 \\ &= \frac{4}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 343 \\ &= \frac{4}{3} \times 22 \times 49 \\ &= \frac{4 \times 22 \times 49}{3} \\ &= \frac{4312}{3} \\ &\approx 1437.33 \text{ cubic units} \end{align*} \]

Real-World Applications

Practical Examples

Formulae are used extensively in real-world situations. Here are some common examples:

Example 1: The formula \( C = \frac{5}{9}(F - 32) \) converts Fahrenheit to Celsius. Convert 77°F to Celsius.

Solution:

\[ \begin{align*} C &= \frac{5}{9}(F - 32) \\ &= \frac{5}{9}(77 - 32) \\ &= \frac{5}{9}(45) \\ &= 25\text{°C} \end{align*} \]

Example 2: The simple interest formula is \( I = PRT \). Calculate the interest on Gh₵5000 at 8% for 3 years.

Solution:

\[ \begin{align*} I &= PRT \\ &= 5000 \times 0.08 \times 3 \\ &= 5000 \times 0.24 \\ &= \text{Gh₵}1200 \end{align*} \]

Important Notes:

  • Ensure units are consistent (e.g., time in years for interest calculations)
  • Convert percentages to decimals before substitution (8% = 0.08)
  • Round final answers appropriately based on context

Complex Formulae

Handling Complex Formulae

Some formulae have multiple operations and require careful substitution:

Example 1: Evaluate \( z = \frac{x^2 - y}{x + 2y} \) when \( x = 5 \) and \( y = 3 \)

Solution:

\[ \begin{align*} z &= \frac{x^2 - y}{x + 2y} \\ &= \frac{5^2 - 3}{5 + 2(3)} \\ &= \frac{25 - 3}{5 + 6} \\ &= \frac{22}{11} \\ &= 2 \end{align*} \]

Example 2: The quadratic formula is \( x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \). Find \( x \) when \( a = 1 \), \( b = -5 \), and \( c = 6 \).

Solution:

\[ \begin{align*} x &= \frac{-(-5) \pm \sqrt{(-5)^2 - 4(1)(6)}}{2(1)} \\ &= \frac{5 \pm \sqrt{25 - 24}}{2} \\ &= \frac{5 \pm \sqrt{1}}{2} \\ &= \frac{5 \pm 1}{2} \\ \end{align*} \]

Therefore, the two solutions are:

\[ x = \frac{5 + 1}{2} = 3 \quad \text{and} \quad x = \frac{5 - 1}{2} = 2 \]

Word Problems

Solving Word Problems

Many real-world problems require you to:

  1. Identify the correct formula to use
  2. Extract the relevant values from the problem
  3. Substitute them into the formula
  4. Solve and interpret the result

Example 1: A rectangular garden is 12m long and 8m wide. Calculate the cost to fence it if fencing costs Gh₵25 per meter.

Solution:

First, find the perimeter: \( P = 2(l + w) \)

\[ \begin{align*} P &= 2(12 + 8) \\ &= 2(20) \\ &= 40\text{m} \end{align*} \]

Now calculate the cost: \( \text{Cost} = 40 \times 25 = \text{Gh₵}1000 \)

Example 2: The volume of a cone is given by \( V = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h \). Find the volume when \( r = 7 \)cm and \( h = 12 \)cm (use \( \pi = \frac{22}{7} \)).

Solution:

\[ \begin{align*} V &= \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h \\ &= \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 7^2 \times 12 \\ &= \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 49 \times 12 \\ &= \frac{1}{3} \times 22 \times 7 \times 12 \\ &= \frac{1}{3} \times 1848 \\ &= 616\text{cm}^3 \end{align*} \]

Final Practice Exercise

Question 1

The area of a trapezium is \( A = \frac{1}{2}(a + b)h \). Find the area when \( a = 8 \)cm, \( b = 12 \)cm, and \( h = 5 \)cm.

\[ \begin{align*} A &= \frac{1}{2}(a + b)h \\ &= \frac{1}{2}(8 + 12) \times 5 \\ &= \frac{1}{2} \times 20 \times 5 \\ &= 10 \times 5 \\ &= 50\text{cm}^2 \end{align*} \]

Question 2

The kinetic energy formula is \( KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \). Calculate the kinetic energy when \( m = 10 \)kg and \( v = 5 \)m/s.

\[ \begin{align*} KE &= \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \\ &= \frac{1}{2} \times 10 \times 5^2 \\ &= 5 \times 25 \\ &= 125\text{J} \end{align*} \]

Question 3

A car travels at constant speed. The distance formula is \( d = st \). How far will it travel in 3.5 hours at 80km/h?

\[ \begin{align*} d &= st \\ &= 80 \times 3.5 \\ &= 280\text{km} \end{align*} \]

Question 4

The surface area of a cylinder is \( A = 2\pi r(r + h) \). Find the surface area when \( r = 7 \)cm and \( h = 10 \)cm (use \( \pi = \frac{22}{7} \)).

\[ \begin{align*} A &= 2\pi r(r + h) \\ &= 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times (7 + 10) \\ &= 2 \times 22 \times 17 \\ &= 44 \times 17 \\ &= 748\text{cm}^2 \end{align*} \]
