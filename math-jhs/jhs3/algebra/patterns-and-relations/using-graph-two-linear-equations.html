Introduction

Linear equations are equations of the first degree, meaning they involve only variables raised to the power of one. When we have two linear equations with the same variables, we can solve them simultaneously to find the values of the variables that satisfy both equations.

One powerful method for solving such systems is the graphical method, where we plot both equations on the same coordinate plane and find their point of intersection. This point represents the solution that satisfies both equations simultaneously.

Consider the system of equations:

\[ \begin{cases} y = 2x + 1 \\ y = -x + 4 \end{cases} \]

The solution is the point where these two lines intersect on the graph.

Key Concepts

  • A linear equation in two variables forms a straight line when graphed
  • The solution to a system of two linear equations is the point where their graphs intersect
  • Graphical solutions provide a visual representation of the relationship between equations
  • This method works best when solutions are integer values that can be precisely plotted

Plotting Linear Equations

Steps for Plotting

To graph a linear equation, follow these steps:

1. Rewrite in Slope-Intercept Form

Ensure the equation is in the form \( y = mx + b \), where:

  • \( m \) is the slope (steepness)
  • \( b \) is the y-intercept (where the line crosses the y-axis)

2. Plot the Y-Intercept

Start by marking point \( (0, b) \) on the y-axis.

For \( y = 2x + 1 \):

Plot point \( (0, 1) \)

3. Use the Slope to Find Another Point

From the y-intercept, use the slope \( m = \frac{rise}{run} \) to find a second point.

For \( y = 2x + 1 \) (slope = 2 = \(\frac{2}{1}\)):

From \( (0,1) \), move up 2 units and right 1 unit to \( (1,3) \)

4. Draw the Line

Connect the points with a straight line extending in both directions.

Important Notes

  • For horizontal lines (e.g., \( y = 3 \)): slope \( m = 0 \)
  • For vertical lines (e.g., \( x = 2 \)): slope is undefined
  • When the equation isn't in slope-intercept form, solve for y first
  • Always plot at least two points to ensure accuracy

Solving Simultaneous Equations

Finding the Solution

To solve a system of two linear equations graphically:

1. Graph Both Equations

Plot each equation on the same coordinate plane using the methods described earlier.

2. Identify the Intersection Point

The point where the two lines cross is the solution to the system.

For:

\[ \begin{cases} y = 2x + 1 \\ y = -x + 4 \end{cases} \]

The lines intersect at \( (1, 3) \), so \( x = 1 \), \( y = 3 \) is the solution.

3. Verify the Solution

Substitute the solution back into both original equations to ensure it satisfies both.

For \( (1, 3) \):

First equation: \( 3 = 2(1) + 1 \) → \( 3 = 3 \) ✓

Second equation: \( 3 = -1 + 4 \) → \( 3 = 3 \) ✓

Worked Example

Solve the following system graphically:

\[ \begin{cases} 2x + y = 5 \\ x - y = 1 \end{cases} \]

Step 1: Rewrite in Slope-Intercept Form

First equation: \( y = -2x + 5 \)

Second equation: \( y = x - 1 \)

Step 2: Plot Both Equations

For \( y = -2x + 5 \):

  • Y-intercept: \( (0, 5) \)
  • Slope: -2 (down 2, right 1) → next point \( (1, 3) \)

For \( y = x - 1 \):

  • Y-intercept: \( (0, -1) \)
  • Slope: 1 (up 1, right 1) → next point \( (1, 0) \)

Step 3: Find Intersection

The lines intersect at \( (2, 1) \), so the solution is \( x = 2 \), \( y = 1 \).

Step 4: Verification

First equation: \( 2(2) + 1 = 5 \) → \( 5 = 5 \) ✓

Second equation: \( 2 - 1 = 1 \) → \( 1 = 1 \) ✓

Types of Solutions

Possible Outcomes

When solving systems of linear equations graphically, there are three possible scenarios:

1. One Solution (Independent System)

The lines intersect at exactly one point, meaning there is one unique solution.

Example:

\[ \begin{cases} y = 2x + 1 \\ y = -x + 4 \end{cases} \]

Solution: \( (1, 3) \)

2. No Solution (Inconsistent System)

The lines are parallel and never intersect, meaning there is no solution that satisfies both equations.

Example:

\[ \begin{cases} y = 2x + 1 \\ y = 2x - 3 \end{cases} \]

Both have slope 2 but different y-intercepts → parallel lines

3. Infinite Solutions (Dependent System)

The lines are identical (coincide), meaning all points on the line are solutions.

Example:

\[ \begin{cases} y = 2x + 1 \\ 2y = 4x + 2 \end{cases} \]

Second equation is just the first multiplied by 2 → same line

Quick Identification

You can often determine the type of solution by examining the equations:

  • One solution: Different slopes
  • No solution: Same slope, different y-intercepts
  • Infinite solutions: Same slope and same y-intercept (identical equations)

Real-World Applications

Practical Uses

Solving systems of linear equations has many real-world applications:

1. Business and Economics

Finding the break-even point where cost equals revenue:

Cost equation: \( C = 5x + 1000 \)

Revenue equation: \( R = 10x \)

Solution shows how many units \( x \) need to be sold to break even.

2. Physics

Determining when two moving objects will meet:

Car A: \( d = 60t \) (60 km/h)

Car B: \( d = 40t + 50 \) (40 km/h with 50 km head start)

Solution shows time \( t \) when they meet.

3. Chemistry

Finding the optimal mixture of two solutions to achieve desired concentration.

4. Everyday Decisions

Comparing two phone plans to determine which is better based on usage:

Plan A: \( C = 20 + 0.10m \)

Plan B: \( C = 30 + 0.05m \)

Solution shows at what minutes \( m \) the costs are equal.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Solve the following system of equations graphically:

\[ \begin{cases} y = x + 2 \\ y = -2x + 5 \end{cases} \]

Step 1: Graph both equations on the same coordinate plane.

Step 2: The lines intersect at \( (1, 3) \).

Solution: \( x = 1 \), \( y = 3 \).

Question 2

Solve graphically:

\[ \begin{cases} 2x + y = 4 \\ x - y = 2 \end{cases} \]

First rewrite in slope-intercept form:

\( y = -2x + 4 \) and \( y = x - 2 \)

The lines intersect at \( (2, 0) \).

Solution: \( x = 2 \), \( y = 0 \).

Question 3

Determine whether the following system has one, none, or infinite solutions:

\[ \begin{cases} y = 3x - 2 \\ 6x - 2y = 4 \end{cases} \]

Rewrite second equation: \( 2y = 6x - 4 \) → \( y = 3x - 2 \)

Both equations are identical → infinite solutions.

Question 4

Two phone plans are available:

  • Plan A: Gh₵20 monthly fee + Gh₵0.50 per minute
  • Plan B: Gh₵30 monthly fee + Gh₵0.20 per minute

At what usage level do both plans cost the same?

Equations:

Plan A: \( C = 20 + 0.5m \)

Plan B: \( C = 30 + 0.2m \)

Solution: \( m = 33.\overline{3} \) minutes

At about 33 minutes 20 seconds, both plans cost Gh₵36.67.

Question 5

Graphically solve:

\[ \begin{cases} x + y = 5 \\ 2x - y = 1 \end{cases} \]

Rewrite as:

\( y = -x + 5 \) and \( y = 2x - 1 \)

Lines intersect at \( (2, 3) \).

Solution: \( x = 2 \), \( y = 3 \).
