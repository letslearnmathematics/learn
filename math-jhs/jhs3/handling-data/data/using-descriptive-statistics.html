Introduction to Descriptive Statistics

Descriptive statistics are used to summarize and describe the main features of a dataset. They help us organize, analyze, and interpret data to answer questions and make decisions.

In this lesson, we will learn about key descriptive statistics including:

  • Minimum and maximum values
  • Measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode)
  • Range

These tools help us understand patterns and trends in data, making complex information easier to comprehend.

Key Terms

Dataset: A collection of related data points (e.g., test scores, heights, temperatures).

Minimum: The smallest value in a dataset.

Maximum: The largest value in a dataset.

Measures of Central Tendency

Mean (Average)

The mean is the sum of all values divided by the number of values.

Formula:

\[ \text{Mean} = \frac{\text{Sum of all values}}{\text{Number of values}} \]

Example: Find the mean of 5, 7, 9, 11, 13

\[ \text{Mean} = \frac{5 + 7 + 9 + 11 + 13}{5} = \frac{45}{5} = 9 \]

Median

The median is the middle value when data is arranged in order.

Steps to find the median:

  1. Arrange the data in ascending order
  2. For odd number of values: Median is the middle number
  3. For even number of values: Median is the average of the two middle numbers

Example 1 (odd): Find the median of 5, 3, 9, 1, 7

Arranged: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9 → Median = 5

Example 2 (even): Find the median of 4, 8, 2, 6

Arranged: 2, 4, 6, 8 → Median = (4 + 6)/2 = 5

Mode

The mode is the value that appears most frequently in a dataset.

Example 1: Find the mode of 2, 3, 4, 4, 5, 5, 5, 6

Mode = 5 (appears most frequently)

Example 2: Find the mode of 1, 2, 3, 4

No mode (all values appear equally)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Find the mean, median, and mode of the following test scores: 85, 90, 78, 92, 85, 88, 90

Mean: (85 + 90 + 78 + 92 + 85 + 88 + 90)/7 = 608/7 ≈ 86.86

Median: Arranged: 78, 85, 85, 88, 90, 90, 92 → 88

Mode: 85 and 90 (both appear twice)

Question 2

The ages of students in a class are: 12, 13, 12, 14, 13, 12, 13, 14, 12. Find all measures of central tendency.

Mean: (12×4 + 13×3 + 14×2)/9 = (48 + 39 + 28)/9 ≈ 12.78

Median: Arranged: 12,12,12,12,13,13,13,14,14 → 13

Mode: 12 (appears most frequently)

Question 3

Calculate the mean, median, and mode for the data: 5, 8, 6, 5, 4, 7, 6, 6, 3, 5

Mean: (5+8+6+5+4+7+6+6+3+5)/10 = 55/10 = 5.5

Median: Arranged: 3,4,5,5,5,6,6,6,7,8 → (5+6)/2 = 5.5

Mode: 5 and 6 (both appear three times)

Range

Understanding Range

The range is the difference between the maximum and minimum values in a dataset. It shows how spread out the data is.

Formula:

\[ \text{Range} = \text{Maximum value} - \text{Minimum value} \]

Example: Find the range of 12, 15, 18, 22, 10, 25

Maximum = 25, Minimum = 10

Range = 25 - 10 = 15

Key Points

Large range: Indicates data is spread out widely

Small range: Indicates data points are close together

Range is sensitive to outliers (extreme values)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Find the range of temperatures recorded over a week: 28°C, 30°C, 25°C, 32°C, 27°C, 31°C, 26°C

Maximum = 32°C, Minimum = 25°C

Range = 32 - 25 = 7°C

Question 2

The heights (in cm) of students are: 145, 152, 138, 160, 148, 155, 142. Calculate the range.

Maximum = 160cm, Minimum = 138cm

Range = 160 - 138 = 22cm

Question 3

A runner recorded these times (in seconds) for 100m: 12.5, 11.8, 13.2, 12.1, 12.9, 11.5. What is the range?

Maximum = 13.2s, Minimum = 11.5s

Range = 13.2 - 11.5 = 1.7s

Data Interpretation

Analyzing Data

Descriptive statistics help us interpret data and draw meaningful conclusions. Here's how to approach data interpretation:

  1. Organize the data (arrange in order, create frequency tables)
  2. Calculate measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode)
  3. Determine the range
  4. Compare different datasets
  5. Draw conclusions based on the analysis

Example: Test Scores Analysis

Consider these test scores from two classes:

Class A: 65, 70, 75, 80, 85, 90, 95

Class B: 50, 60, 70, 80, 90, 100, 100

Analysis:

Measure Class A Class B
Mean 80 ≈78.57
Median 80 80
Mode None 100
Range 30 50

Interpretation: While both classes have similar central tendencies, Class B has more variability (larger range) with some very high and low scores.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Analyze the following data on daily sales (in GH₵): 120, 150, 180, 200, 150, 170, 190. Calculate all descriptive statistics and interpret the results.

Mean: (120+150+180+200+150+170+190)/7 ≈ 165.71

Median: Arranged: 120,150,150,170,180,190,200 → 170

Mode: 150

Range: 200 - 120 = 80

Interpretation: The average daily sales are about GH₵165.71, with most frequent sales at GH₵150. Sales vary by GH₵80 from lowest to highest.

Question 2

The weights (in kg) of two groups of students are shown below. Compare their descriptive statistics.

Group X: 45, 48, 50, 52, 55, 58, 60

Group Y: 40, 42, 50, 55, 58, 60, 65

Group X: Mean ≈ 52.57, Median = 52, Mode = None, Range = 15

Group Y: Mean ≈ 52.86, Median = 55, Mode = None, Range = 25

Comparison: Both groups have similar means, but Group Y has a higher median and larger range, indicating more variability in weights.

Real-World Applications

Practical Uses

Descriptive statistics are used in various real-world situations:

  • Education: Analyzing test scores to evaluate class performance
  • Business: Tracking sales trends and customer behavior
  • Healthcare: Monitoring patient vital signs and treatment effectiveness
  • Sports: Evaluating player performance statistics
  • Government: Analyzing census data and economic indicators

Case Study: Market Analysis

A shopkeeper recorded daily customers for a week: 45, 50, 55, 60, 65, 70, 40

Analysis:

Mean ≈ 55 customers/day

Median = 55 customers

Mode = None

Range = 30 customers

Action: The shopkeeper can plan staffing and inventory based on these averages, while preparing for fluctuations of up to 30 customers from the lowest to highest days.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A farmer recorded weekly egg production (in dozens): 12, 15, 18, 20, 15, 17, 19. Calculate all descriptive statistics and suggest how this information could help the farmer.

Mean: ≈16.57 dozen/week

Median: 17 dozen

Mode: 15 dozen

Range: 8 dozen

Application: The farmer can plan sales and storage based on average production, while preparing for weekly variations of up to 8 dozen.

Question 2

A teacher recorded the number of questions students answered correctly on a quiz: 5, 8, 6, 5, 4, 7, 6, 6, 3, 5. Analyze the data and suggest how the teacher might use this information.

Mean: 5.5

Median: 5.5

Mode: 5 and 6

Range: 5

Application: The teacher might review concepts that most students scored 5-6 on, and provide additional support for students scoring below average.
