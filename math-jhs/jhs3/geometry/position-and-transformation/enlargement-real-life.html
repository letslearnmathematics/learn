Introduction

Enlargement is a transformation that changes the size of a shape while maintaining its proportions. It involves scaling a shape by a factor called the scale factor from a fixed point called the center of enlargement.

In real life, enlargement is used in many situations:

  • Photocopying documents (enlarging or reducing)
  • Projecting images onto screens
  • Creating scale models of buildings
  • Zooming in or out on digital images

Drawing Enlargement of Shapes

Steps to Draw an Enlargement

  1. Identify the center of enlargement (if not given, assume origin)
  2. Measure distances from center to each vertex of original shape
  3. Multiply these distances by the scale factor
  4. Mark new points at these scaled distances from the center
  5. Connect the new points to form the enlarged shape

Example

Draw an enlargement of triangle ABC with vertices at (1,1), (3,1), and (2,3) using scale factor 2 about the origin.

Solution:

Original points: A(1,1), B(3,1), C(2,3)

Enlarged points (multiply each coordinate by 2):

A'(2,2), B'(6,2), C'(4,6)

Connect A'B'C' to form the enlarged triangle.

Scale Factor Characteristics

Investigating Scale Factors

Negative Scale Factor (K < 0)

The image is inverted (rotated 180°) and enlarged/reduced

Example: K = -2 means the image is twice as big and upside down

Scale Factor > 1 or < -1

The image is larger than the original object

Example: K = 3 means the image is 3 times larger

Example: K = -1.5 means the image is 1.5 times larger and inverted

Fractional Scale Factor (-1 < K < 1)

The image is smaller than the original object (reduction)

Example: K = 0.5 means the image is half the size

Example: K = -0.25 means the image is quarter size and inverted

Determining Scale Factor

Calculating Scale Factor

The scale factor (K) can be determined by comparing corresponding lengths of the object and its image:

\[ K = \frac{\text{Length of image}}{\text{Length of object}} \]

Example: If an object is 4cm long and its image is 10cm long, the scale factor is:

K = 10cm / 4cm = 2.5

Question 2

Given triangle A with vertices (2,2), (4,2), (3,4) and triangle B with vertices (4,4), (8,4), (6,8):

i. What transformation maps A to B?

ii. Determine the scale factor

i. Enlargement with scale factor 2 about origin

ii. K = 2 (all sides doubled in length)

Question 3

Investigate what happens when you enlarge a shape with scale factor:

a) K = -3

b) K = 0.5

c) K = -0.75

a) Image is 3 times larger and inverted

b) Image is half the size (reduction)

c) Image is 3/4 the size and inverted

Question 4

A photo is 10cm × 15cm. When enlarged, it becomes 25cm × 37.5cm. What is the scale factor?

K = 25/10 = 2.5 or 37.5/15 = 2.5

Question 5
