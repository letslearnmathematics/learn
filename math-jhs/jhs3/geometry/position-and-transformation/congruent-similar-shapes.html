Introduction

In geometry, shapes can be related in special ways called congruence and similarity. These relationships help us understand how shapes compare to each other in terms of their size and shape.

We will investigate these concepts by examining their properties and verifying them using multiple examples, particularly focusing on shapes on a coordinate plane and their enlargements.

Congruent Shapes

Definition

Two shapes are congruent if they have exactly the same size and shape. This means one can be transformed into the other by a combination of translations, rotations, and reflections.

Properties of Congruent Shapes

Corresponding Sides

All corresponding sides are equal in length:

\[ AB = A'B', BC = B'C', \text{ etc.} \]

Corresponding Angles

All corresponding angles are equal in measure:

\[ \angle A = \angle A', \angle B = \angle B', \text{ etc.} \]

Area

Congruent shapes have equal areas:

\[ \text{Area}_1 = \text{Area}_2 \]

Congruence Tests

We can prove triangles are congruent using these tests:

SSS (Side-Side-Side)

All three sides are equal

SAS (Side-Angle-Side)

Two sides and included angle are equal

ASA (Angle-Side-Angle)

Two angles and included side are equal

RHS (Right-Hypotenuse-Side)

Right triangles with equal hypotenuse and one side

Similar Shapes

Definition

Two shapes are similar if they have the same shape but not necessarily the same size. One is an enlargement or reduction of the other.

Properties of Similar Shapes

Corresponding Angles

All corresponding angles are equal:

\[ \angle A = \angle A', \angle B = \angle B', \text{ etc.} \]

Corresponding Sides

All corresponding sides are proportional:

\[ \frac{AB}{A'B'} = \frac{BC}{B'C'} = \text{scale factor} \]

Area Ratio

The ratio of areas is the square of the scale factor:

\[ \frac{\text{Area}_1}{\text{Area}_2} = \left(\frac{\text{scale factor}}{1}\right)^2 \]

Similarity Tests

We can prove triangles are similar using these tests:

AA (Angle-Angle)

Two corresponding angles are equal

SSS (Side-Side-Side)

All corresponding sides are proportional

SAS (Side-Angle-Side)

Two sides proportional and included angles equal

On Coordinate Plane

Enlargement on Coordinate Plane

We can verify congruence and similarity by examining shapes on a coordinate plane. Enlargement (scaling) is a key transformation that helps us understand similarity.

Calculating Scale Factor

The scale factor (k) determines how much a shape is enlarged or reduced:

For two similar triangles ABC and A'B'C':

\[ k = \frac{A'B'}{AB} = \frac{B'C'}{BC} = \frac{A'C'}{AC} \]

If k > 1, the shape is enlarged. If 0 < k < 1, the shape is reduced.

Example on Coordinate Plane

Consider triangle ABC with vertices at A(1,1), B(3,1), C(1,4).

An enlargement with scale factor 2 centered at the origin produces triangle A'B'C' with vertices at:

\[ A'(2,2), B'(6,2), C'(2,8) \]

We can verify the similarity by checking:

  1. Corresponding angles are equal
  2. Corresponding sides are in ratio 1:2

Verification

Verifying Congruence and Similarity

To verify if two shapes are congruent or similar, we systematically check their properties using the definitions and tests we've learned.

Verification Steps for Congruence

  1. Measure all corresponding sides - they should be equal
  2. Measure all corresponding angles - they should be equal
  3. Check if one can be transformed into the other without changing size
  4. Apply one of the congruence tests (SSS, SAS, ASA, RHS)

Verification Steps for Similarity

  1. Measure all corresponding angles - they should be equal
  2. Calculate ratios of corresponding sides - they should be equal
  3. Determine the scale factor of enlargement/reduction
  4. Apply one of the similarity tests (AA, SSS, SAS)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Triangle ABC has vertices at A(0,0), B(2,0), C(0,3). Triangle DEF has vertices at D(0,0), E(4,0), F(0,6). Are these triangles similar? Verify your answer.

Yes, they are similar. Verification steps:

  1. Both have right angles at A and D
  2. Both have the same angles at B and E, and C and F
  3. Side ratios: AB/DE = 2/4 = 0.5, AC/DF = 3/6 = 0.5, BC/EF = √13/√52 = 0.5
  4. All corresponding angles equal and sides proportional ⇒ similar

Question 2

Rectangle PQRS has vertices at P(1,1), Q(4,1), R(4,3), S(1,3). Rectangle WXYZ has vertices at W(2,2), X(8,2), Y(8,6), Z(2,6). Are these rectangles congruent or similar? Verify.

They are similar but not congruent. Verification steps:

  1. Both are rectangles (all angles 90° and equal)
  2. Side ratios: PQ/WX = 3/6 = 0.5, QR/XY = 2/4 = 0.5
  3. Scale factor is 2 (enlargement)
  4. Corresponding sides not equal ⇒ not congruent
  5. Corresponding angles equal and sides proportional ⇒ similar

Question 3

Triangle LMN has vertices at L(1,1), M(3,1), N(1,4). Triangle STU has vertices at S(2,2), M(6,2), U(2,8). Verify if these triangles are congruent or similar.

They are similar but not congruent. Verification steps:

  1. Corresponding angles are equal (both right-angled at L and S)
  2. Side ratios: LM/ST = 2/4 = 0.5, LN/SU = 3/6 = 0.5, MN/TU = √13/√52 = 0.5
  3. Scale factor is 2
  4. Sides not equal ⇒ not congruent
  5. Angles equal and sides proportional ⇒ similar

Question 4

Quadrilateral ABCD has vertices at A(0,0), B(2,0), C(2,2), D(0,2). Quadrilateral EFGH has vertices at E(1,1), F(3,1), G(3,3), H(1,3). Are these shapes congruent? Verify.

Yes, they are congruent. Verification steps:

  1. Both are squares (all sides equal, all angles 90°)
  2. Corresponding sides: AB=EF=2, BC=FG=2, CD=GH=2, DA=HE=2
  3. Corresponding angles all equal (90°)
  4. One can be obtained by translating the other by (1,1)
  5. All sides and angles equal ⇒ congruent

Question 5

Triangle JKL has vertices at J(0,0), K(3,0), L(0,4). Triangle MNP has vertices at M(0,0), N(6,0), P(0,8). Verify the relationship between these triangles.

They are similar but not congruent. Verification steps:

  1. Both are right-angled at J and M
  2. Corresponding angles equal
  3. Side ratios: JK/MN = 3/6 = 0.5, JL/MP = 4/8 = 0.5, KL/NP = 5/10 = 0.5
  4. Scale factor is 2
  5. Sides not equal ⇒ not congruent
  6. Angles equal and sides proportional ⇒ similar
