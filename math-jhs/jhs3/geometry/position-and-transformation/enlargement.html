Introduction to Enlargement

Enlargement refers to the process of making something larger or more extensive. In mathematics, it specifically means increasing the dimensions of an object while maintaining its shape through scaling.

Enlargement situations are common in our daily lives. Understanding these helps us appreciate how mathematics applies to real-world scenarios. The nature of enlargement movements can be vertical, horizontal, or a combination of both.

Vertical Enlargement

What is Vertical Enlargement?

Vertical enlargement occurs when an object or image is stretched upward or downward, increasing its height while maintaining or changing its width.

Real-Life Examples

  1. Building Construction: When additional floors are added to a building, it undergoes vertical enlargement.
  2. Stretching Exercises: When a person stretches their arms upward, they create a vertical enlargement of their body's silhouette.
  3. Balloon Inflation: When a balloon is inflated vertically, it becomes taller while maintaining its width.
  4. Tree Growth: As trees grow taller over time, they demonstrate natural vertical enlargement.
  5. Digital Zoom: When you zoom in on a digital image vertically only, it becomes taller while the width remains the same.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Identify three objects in your environment that demonstrate vertical enlargement.

Possible answers:

  1. Lamp posts along the street
  2. Stacked chairs in a classroom
  3. Water towers in the community

Question 2

Explain how a flagpole demonstrates vertical enlargement when a flag is raised.

As the flag is raised:

  1. The flag moves upward along the pole
  2. The visible portion of the flag's height increases
  3. The width of the flag remains constant
  4. This creates a vertical enlargement effect

Horizontal Enlargement

What is Horizontal Enlargement?

Horizontal enlargement occurs when an object or image is stretched sideways, increasing its width while maintaining or changing its height.

Real-Life Examples

  1. Building Extensions: When a house is extended sideways to add more rooms, it undergoes horizontal enlargement.
  2. Road Widening: When roads are expanded to add more lanes, they demonstrate horizontal enlargement.
  3. Table Extensions: Expandable tables that can be widened by adding leaves show horizontal enlargement.
  4. Rubber Band Stretching: When a rubber band is stretched horizontally, it becomes wider while its thickness decreases.
  5. Digital Photo Editing: When an image is stretched horizontally in editing software, it becomes wider while the height remains the same.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Name three objects in your school that can demonstrate horizontal enlargement.

Possible answers:

  1. Classroom windows that slide open
  2. Blackboards that can be extended
  3. Bookshelves that can be expanded

Question 2

Explain how a concertina gate demonstrates horizontal enlargement when it's opened.

When a concertina gate opens:

  1. The folded sections extend outward
  2. The width of the gate increases
  3. The height remains constant
  4. This creates a horizontal enlargement effect

Combined Enlargement

What is Combined Enlargement?

Combined enlargement occurs when an object or image is stretched both vertically and horizontally, increasing both its height and width proportionally or disproportionately.

Real-Life Examples

  1. Balloon Inflation: When a balloon is inflated in all directions, it demonstrates combined enlargement.
  2. Building Construction: When a building is expanded both upward and outward, it shows combined enlargement.
  3. Digital Zoom: When an image is zoomed in equally in all directions, it undergoes proportional combined enlargement.
  4. Baking: When bread dough rises, it expands both vertically and horizontally, showing natural combined enlargement.
  5. Rubber Sheet Stretching: When a rubber sheet is pulled at all edges, it enlarges in all directions simultaneously.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Compare and contrast vertical, horizontal, and combined enlargement using real-world examples.

Type Example Description
Vertical Growing tree Height increases while width remains relatively constant
Horizontal Road widening Width increases while height remains constant
Combined Inflating balloon Both height and width increase proportionally

Question 2

Describe a situation where combined enlargement occurs disproportionately (height and width increase by different amounts).

Example: Stretching a rubber band:

  1. When pulled at both ends, it lengthens significantly (vertical enlargement)
  2. At the same time, it becomes thinner (negative horizontal enlargement)
  3. This creates disproportionate combined enlargement
