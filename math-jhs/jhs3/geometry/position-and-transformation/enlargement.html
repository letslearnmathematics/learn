Introduction

Vector addition is the process of combining two or more vectors to produce a resultant vector. Unlike scalar quantities that can be added arithmetically, vectors require consideration of both magnitude and direction.

There are two fundamental laws for adding vectors geometrically: the Triangle Law and the Parallelogram Law. Both methods produce the same resultant vector but use different geometric constructions.

Vector Properties:

  • Have both magnitude (size) and direction
  • Represented by arrows (length = magnitude, direction = arrow direction)
  • Examples: displacement, velocity, force

Resultant Vector:

The single vector that has the same effect as the original vectors combined.

Triangle Law of Vector Addition

Deduction of the Triangle Law

The Triangle Law states that if two vectors are represented as two sides of a triangle taken in order, then the resultant vector is represented by the third side of the triangle taken in the opposite order.

Given vectors a and b:

  1. Draw vector a
  2. From the head of a, draw vector b
  3. The resultant R = a + b is the vector from the tail of a to the head of b
\[ \vec{R} = \vec{a} + \vec{b} \]

Key Features:

  • Vectors must be placed head-to-tail
  • The order of addition doesn't matter (commutative)
  • Works for any number of vectors (polygon law)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Using the Triangle Law, find the resultant of two vectors: 5 units east and 3 units north.

The resultant vector would be the hypotenuse of a right-angled triangle with sides 5 and 3.

Magnitude: \( R = \sqrt{5^2 + 3^2} = \sqrt{25 + 9} = \sqrt{34} \approx 5.83 \) units

Direction: \( \theta = \tan^{-1}(3/5) \approx 30.96^\circ \) north of east

Question 2

Vector A is 8 units at 30° north of east. Vector B is 6 units west. Find the resultant using the Triangle Law.

First resolve Vector A into components:

\( A_x = 8\cos(30°) \approx 6.93 \) units east

\( A_y = 8\sin(30°) = 4 \) units north

Vector B is purely west: \( B_x = -6 \) units, \( B_y = 0 \)

Resultant components: \( R_x = 6.93 - 6 = 0.93 \) units east

\( R_y = 4 + 0 = 4 \) units north

Magnitude: \( R = \sqrt{0.93^2 + 4^2} \approx 4.11 \) units

Direction: \( \theta = \tan^{-1}(4/0.93) \approx 77^\circ \) north of east

Parallelogram Law of Vector Addition

Investigation of the Parallelogram Law

The Parallelogram Law states that if two vectors are represented as two adjacent sides of a parallelogram, then the resultant vector is represented by the diagonal of the parallelogram starting from the same point.

Given vectors a and b:

  1. Draw both vectors from the same origin point
  2. Complete the parallelogram by drawing equal and parallel sides
  3. The resultant R is the diagonal from the origin
\[ \vec{R} = \vec{a} + \vec{b} \]

Key Features:

  • Vectors must originate from the same point
  • Works best for two vectors at a time
  • Particularly useful when vectors have different angles

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Using the Parallelogram Law, find the resultant of two forces: 40 N at 30° and 60 N at 120° from the x-axis.

First resolve both vectors into components:

Vector A (40 N at 30°):

\( A_x = 40\cos(30°) \approx 34.64 \) N

\( A_y = 40\sin(30°) = 20 \) N

Vector B (60 N at 120°):

\( B_x = 60\cos(120°) = -30 \) N

\( B_y = 60\sin(120°) \approx 51.96 \) N

Resultant components:

\( R_x = 34.64 - 30 = 4.64 \) N

\( R_y = 20 + 51.96 = 71.96 \) N

Magnitude: \( R = \sqrt{4.64^2 + 71.96^2} \approx 72.11 \) N

Direction: \( \theta = \tan^{-1}(71.96/4.64) \approx 86.31^\circ \) from x-axis

Question 2

Two velocity vectors are 20 m/s east and 15 m/s north. Find the resultant using the Parallelogram Law.

Using the Parallelogram Law:

The resultant is the diagonal of the parallelogram formed by the two vectors.

Magnitude: \( R = \sqrt{20^2 + 15^2} = \sqrt{400 + 225} = \sqrt{625} = 25 \) m/s

Direction: \( \theta = \tan^{-1}(15/20) = \tan^{-1}(0.75) \approx 36.87^\circ \) north of east

Comparison of Both Laws

Triangle Law

  • Vectors added head-to-tail
  • More intuitive for sequential addition
  • Easily extends to multiple vectors (polygon law)
  • Better for path-dependent problems

Parallelogram Law

  • Vectors originate from same point
  • Better visualization of concurrent forces
  • Naturally shows symmetry in vector addition
  • More suitable for equilibrium problems

Important Note:

Both methods will give exactly the same resultant vector - they are just different geometric approaches to the same mathematical operation.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Solve the same problem using both laws to verify they give the same result: vectors 6 units east and 8 units northeast.

Triangle Law Solution:

1. Draw 6 units east

2. From head, draw 8 units at 45° (northeast)

Components of second vector: \( 8\cos(45°) \approx 5.66 \) east, \( 8\sin(45°) \approx 5.66 \) north

Total east: \( 6 + 5.66 = 11.66 \)

Total north: \( 5.66 \)

Resultant: \( \sqrt{11.66^2 + 5.66^2} \approx 12.96 \) units

Direction: \( \tan^{-1}(5.66/11.66) \approx 25.9^\circ \) north of east

Parallelogram Law Solution:

1. Draw both vectors from origin

2. Complete parallelogram

3. Diagonal will have same components and resultant as above

Both methods give identical results
