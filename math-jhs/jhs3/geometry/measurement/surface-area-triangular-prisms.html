Introduction

A triangular prism is a three-dimensional shape with two identical triangular bases and three rectangular faces connecting the corresponding sides of the triangles.

The surface area of a triangular prism is the total area of all its faces. To find the surface area, we can use the net of the prism, which is a two-dimensional representation of all its faces laid out flat.

Key components of a triangular prism:

  • 2 triangular bases
  • 3 rectangular lateral faces

Understanding Nets

What is a Net?

A net is a two-dimensional pattern that can be folded to form a three-dimensional shape. For a triangular prism, the net consists of:

  • The two triangular bases
  • The three rectangular faces (lateral faces) that connect the corresponding sides of the triangles

Visualizing the Net

When unfolded, a triangular prism's net typically shows the two triangles separated by three rectangles. The rectangles represent the sides of the prism, and their lengths correspond to the sides of the triangular bases.

For a triangular prism with:

  • Base sides: a, b, c
  • Height (length) of prism: h

The net would show:

  • Two triangles with sides a, b, c
  • Three rectangles with dimensions: a×h, b×h, c×h

Calculating Surface Area

Surface Area Formula

The total surface area (SA) of a triangular prism is the sum of:

  • The areas of the two triangular bases
  • The areas of the three rectangular faces

Formula:

\[ SA = 2 \times \text{(Area of triangular base)} + \text{(Perimeter of base)} \times \text{(Height of prism)} \]

Or:

\[ SA = 2 \times \left(\frac{1}{2} \times b \times h_{\text{triangle}}\right) + (a + b + c) \times h_{\text{prism}} \]

Step-by-Step Method

  1. Identify the dimensions of the triangular base (a, b, c) and its height
  2. Calculate the area of one triangular base: \( \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height} \)
  3. Multiply by 2 for both triangular bases
  4. Calculate the perimeter of the triangular base: \( a + b + c \)
  5. Multiply the perimeter by the height (length) of the prism to get the lateral area
  6. Add the areas from steps 3 and 5 to get the total surface area

Example Calculation

Given a triangular prism with:

  • Base triangle sides: 3cm, 4cm, 5cm (right triangle)
  • Height of triangle: 4cm (corresponding to 3cm base)
  • Prism height: 10cm

Calculations:

  1. Area of one triangle: \( \frac{1}{2} \times 3 \times 4 = 6 \text{cm}^2 \)
  2. Two triangles: \( 2 \times 6 = 12 \text{cm}^2 \)
  3. Perimeter: \( 3 + 4 + 5 = 12 \text{cm} \)
  4. Lateral area: \( 12 \times 10 = 120 \text{cm}^2 \)
  5. Total SA: \( 12 + 120 = 132 \text{cm}^2 \)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A triangular prism has a base that is an equilateral triangle with sides of 6cm. The height of the prism is 8cm. Calculate the total surface area.

Steps:

  1. Area of equilateral triangle: \( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} \times 6^2 = 15.59 \text{cm}^2 \)
  2. Two bases: \( 2 \times 15.59 = 31.18 \text{cm}^2 \)
  3. Perimeter: \( 3 \times 6 = 18 \text{cm} \)
  4. Lateral area: \( 18 \times 8 = 144 \text{cm}^2 \)
  5. Total SA: \( 31.18 + 144 = 175.18 \text{cm}^2 \)

Question 2

A triangular prism has a right triangular base with legs 5cm and 12cm. The prism is 15cm long. Find its surface area.

Steps:

  1. Hypotenuse: \( \sqrt{5^2 + 12^2} = 13 \text{cm} \)
  2. Area of triangle: \( \frac{1}{2} \times 5 \times 12 = 30 \text{cm}^2 \)
  3. Two bases: \( 2 \times 30 = 60 \text{cm}^2 \)
  4. Perimeter: \( 5 + 12 + 13 = 30 \text{cm} \)
  5. Lateral area: \( 30 \times 15 = 450 \text{cm}^2 \)
  6. Total SA: \( 60 + 450 = 510 \text{cm}^2 \)

Question 3

The net of a triangular prism shows two triangles with area 24cm² each and three rectangles measuring 7cm×10cm, 8cm×10cm, and 9cm×10cm. What is the surface area?

Steps:

  1. Two triangular bases: \( 2 \times 24 = 48 \text{cm}^2 \)
  2. Rectangular areas: \( (7 \times 10) + (8 \times 10) + (9 \times 10) = 70 + 80 + 90 = 240 \text{cm}^2 \)
  3. Total SA: \( 48 + 240 = 288 \text{cm}^2 \)

Question 4

A triangular prism has a base perimeter of 21cm and lateral area of 189cm². If the triangular base has an area of 18cm², find the total surface area.

Steps:

  1. Two triangular bases: \( 2 \times 18 = 36 \text{cm}^2 \)
  2. Lateral area given: 189cm²
  3. Total SA: \( 36 + 189 = 225 \text{cm}^2 \)

Question 5

Draw the net of a triangular prism with base sides 4cm, 5cm, 6cm and height 7cm, then calculate its surface area. (Assume the height of the triangular base is 3.5cm)

Steps:

  1. Area of one triangle: \( \frac{1}{2} \times 5 \times 3.5 = 8.75 \text{cm}^2 \)
  2. Two bases: \( 2 \times 8.75 = 17.5 \text{cm}^2 \)
  3. Perimeter: \( 4 + 5 + 6 = 15 \text{cm} \)
  4. Lateral area: \( 15 \times 7 = 105 \text{cm}^2 \)
  5. Total SA: \( 17.5 + 105 = 122.5 \text{cm}^2 \)
