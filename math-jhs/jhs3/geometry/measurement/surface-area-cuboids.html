Introduction

A cuboid is a three-dimensional shape with six rectangular faces. To find its surface area, we can use its net, which is a two-dimensional representation of the cuboid when unfolded.

The surface area of a cuboid is the total area of all its faces. By using the net, we can visualize all the faces at once and calculate their combined area.

Understanding Nets

What is a Net?

A net is a two-dimensional shape that can be folded to form a three-dimensional figure. For a cuboid, the net consists of six rectangles arranged in a specific pattern that can be folded to form the cuboid.

Properties of Cuboid Nets

A cuboid net has the following properties:

  • It consists of six rectangles (three pairs of identical rectangles)
  • The rectangles represent the six faces of the cuboid
  • The arrangement shows how the faces are connected in 3D space

Calculating Surface Area

Surface Area Formula

The surface area (SA) of a cuboid can be calculated using the formula:

\[ SA = 2(lw + lh + wh) \]

Where:

  • \( l \) = length
  • \( w \) = width
  • \( h \) = height

Using Nets to Find Surface Area

Follow these steps to find surface area using nets:

  1. Identify all six faces in the net
  2. Measure the dimensions of each face
  3. Calculate the area of each face
  4. Add all the areas together

Example: For a cuboid with dimensions 5cm × 3cm × 2cm:

The net will show:

  • Two faces of 5cm × 3cm (front and back)
  • Two faces of 5cm × 2cm (top and bottom)
  • Two faces of 3cm × 2cm (left and right sides)

Total surface area = 2(5×3) + 2(5×2) + 2(3×2) = 30 + 20 + 12 = 62cm²

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A cuboid has dimensions 8cm × 5cm × 3cm. Find its surface area using the net method.

The cuboid has:

  • Two faces of 8cm × 5cm (area = 40cm² each)
  • Two faces of 8cm × 3cm (area = 24cm² each)
  • Two faces of 5cm × 3cm (area = 15cm² each)

Total surface area = 2(40) + 2(24) + 2(15) = 80 + 48 + 30 = 158cm²

Question 2

The net of a cuboid shows three pairs of rectangles with areas 24cm², 30cm², and 20cm². What is the total surface area?

Total surface area = 2(24) + 2(30) + 2(20) = 48 + 60 + 40 = 148cm²

Question 3

A cuboid has a surface area of 94cm². Two of its dimensions are 4cm and 3cm. Find the third dimension.

Let the third dimension be \( x \).

Using the formula: 2(4×3 + 4×x + 3×x) = 94

2(12 + 4x + 3x) = 94 → 2(12 + 7x) = 94

12 + 7x = 47 → 7x = 35 → x = 5cm

Question 4

A cuboidal box has length 10cm, width 6cm and height 4cm. Draw its net and calculate the surface area.

The net will show:

  • Two 10cm × 6cm rectangles
  • Two 10cm × 4cm rectangles
  • Two 6cm × 4cm rectangles

Total surface area = 2(60) + 2(40) + 2(24) = 120 + 80 + 48 = 248cm²
