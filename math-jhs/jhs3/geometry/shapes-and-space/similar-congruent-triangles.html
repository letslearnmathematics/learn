Introduction

Triangles are fundamental shapes in geometry with three sides and three angles. Understanding the relationships between triangles helps us solve complex geometric problems.

In this lesson, we'll explore two important relationships between triangles:

  1. Similar Triangles - Triangles that have the same shape but may differ in size
  2. Congruent Triangles - Triangles that are identical in both shape and size

Similar Triangles

Properties of Similar Triangles

Two triangles are similar if:

  1. Their corresponding angles are equal (AAA)
  2. Their corresponding sides are proportional (SSS)
  3. Two sides are proportional and the included angles are equal (SAS)

The symbol for similarity is ∼ (e.g., △ABC ∼ △DEF)

Solving Problems with Similar Triangles

When triangles are similar, we can set up proportions between corresponding sides to find unknown values.

Example: Given △ABC ∼ △DEF, with AB = 4cm, BC = 6cm, and DE = 8cm, find EF.

Solution:

Since the triangles are similar, corresponding sides are proportional:

\[ \frac{AB}{DE} = \frac{BC}{EF} \] \[ \frac{4}{8} = \frac{6}{EF} \] \[ \frac{1}{2} = \frac{6}{EF} \] \[ EF = 12 \text{ cm} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

△PQR ∼ △XYZ. If PQ = 5cm, QR = 7cm, and XY = 15cm, find the length of YZ.

Solution:

The triangles are similar, so corresponding sides are proportional:

\[ \frac{PQ}{XY} = \frac{QR}{YZ} \] \[ \frac{5}{15} = \frac{7}{YZ} \] \[ \frac{1}{3} = \frac{7}{YZ} \] \[ YZ = 21 \text{ cm} \]

Question 2

In the diagram below, DE ∥ BC. Find the length of AD if AB = 12cm, AE = 4cm, and EC = 6cm.

Solution:

Since DE ∥ BC, △ADE ∼ △ABC by AA similarity.

\[ \frac{AD}{AB} = \frac{AE}{AC} \] \[ \frac{AD}{12} = \frac{4}{10} \] \[ AD = \frac{4 \times 12}{10} = 4.8 \text{ cm} \]

Congruent Triangles

Properties of Congruent Triangles

Two triangles are congruent if:

  1. All three sides are equal (SSS)
  2. Two sides and the included angle are equal (SAS)
  3. Two angles and the included side are equal (ASA)
  4. Two angles and a non-included side are equal (AAS)
  5. Right angle, hypotenuse and one side are equal (RHS)

The symbol for congruence is ≅ (e.g., △ABC ≅ △DEF)

Solving Problems with Congruent Triangles

When triangles are congruent, all corresponding sides and angles are equal. This allows us to find unknown values.

Example: △ABC ≅ △DEF. If AB = 5cm, ∠B = 50°, and ∠C = 70°, find DE and ∠F.

Solution:

Since the triangles are congruent:

DE = AB = 5cm (corresponding sides)

∠F = ∠C = 70° (corresponding angles)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

△PQR ≅ △XYZ. If PQ = 8cm, QR = 10cm, and PR = 12cm, find the perimeter of △XYZ.

Solution:

Since the triangles are congruent, all corresponding sides are equal:

XY = PQ = 8cm

YZ = QR = 10cm

XZ = PR = 12cm

Perimeter = 8 + 10 + 12 = 30cm

Question 2

In the diagram, AB = CD and AD = BC. Prove that △ABD ≅ △CDB.

Solution:

Given:

AB = CD (given)

AD = BC (given)

BD is common to both triangles

Therefore, △ABD ≅ △CDB by SSS congruence.

Solving for Variables

Strategy for Solving

To find unknown variables (like x) in triangle problems:

  1. Determine if the triangles are similar or congruent
  2. Identify corresponding sides and angles
  3. Set up appropriate equations based on the relationship
  4. Solve for the unknown variable

Example Problems

Example 1 (Similar Triangles): Find x in the similar triangles below where AB = 6, DE = 9, BC = x, EF = 12.

Solution:

\[ \frac{AB}{DE} = \frac{BC}{EF} \] \[ \frac{6}{9} = \frac{x}{12} \] \[ \frac{2}{3} = \frac{x}{12} \] \[ x = \frac{2 \times 12}{3} = 8 \]

Example 2 (Congruent Triangles): Given △ABC ≅ △DEF, with AB = 2x + 3 and DE = 11, find x.

Solution:

Since the triangles are congruent, AB = DE:

\[ 2x + 3 = 11 \] \[ 2x = 8 \] \[ x = 4 \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

△ABC ∼ △DEF. If AB = 4, BC = x, DE = 6, and EF = 9, find the value of x.

Solution:

\[ \frac{AB}{DE} = \frac{BC}{EF} \] \[ \frac{4}{6} = \frac{x}{9} \] \[ \frac{2}{3} = \frac{x}{9} \] \[ x = \frac{2 \times 9}{3} = 6 \]

Question 2

△PQR ≅ △XYZ. If PQ = 3x - 2 and XY = 10, find the value of x.

Solution:

Since the triangles are congruent, PQ = XY:

\[ 3x - 2 = 10 \] \[ 3x = 12 \] \[ x = 4 \]
