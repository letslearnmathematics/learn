Introduction

A proportional relationship between two quantities is one in which the ratio of one quantity to the other is constant. This means that as one quantity changes, the other changes at a constant rate.

In this lesson, we will learn how to recognize proportional relationships, graph them, interpret the unit rate as the slope of the graph, and use these concepts to solve real-world problems.

Example of a proportional relationship:

If a car travels at a constant speed of 60 km/h, the distance it covers is proportional to the time traveled:

\[ \text{Distance} = 60 \times \text{Time} \]

This can be written as \( y = kx \), where \( k \) is the constant of proportionality.

Key Characteristics

Proportional relationships have these key features:

  1. The graph passes through the origin (0,0)
  2. The ratio \( \frac{y}{x} \) is constant for all points
  3. The relationship can be represented by the equation \( y = kx \)
  4. The graph is a straight line

Recognizing Proportional Relationships

How to Recognize

There are several ways to determine if a relationship is proportional:

Table Method

Check if all ratios \( \frac{y}{x} \) are equal:

Time (hours) Distance (km) Ratio (km/h)
1 60 60
2 120 60
3 180 60

Since all ratios are equal (60 km/h), this is a proportional relationship.

Graph Method

A proportional relationship will:

  • Be a straight line
  • Pass through the origin (0,0)

Equation Method

The equation will be of the form:

\[ y = kx \]

Where \( k \) is the constant of proportionality (unit rate).

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Determine if the following table shows a proportional relationship:

Hours Worked Pay (GHC)
2 30
4 60
6 90

Calculate the ratios:

30/2 = 15

60/4 = 15

90/6 = 15

All ratios are equal (15 GHC/hour), so this is a proportional relationship.

Question 2

Which of these equations represents a proportional relationship?

a) y = 3x + 2

b) y = 5x

c) y = x/4

d) y = 2x - 1

Proportional relationships must be of the form y = kx (no added constant).

Correct answers are b) y = 5x and c) y = x/4.

Graphing Proportional Relationships

Steps to Graph

To graph a proportional relationship:

  1. Create a table of values (including (0,0))
  2. Plot the points on a coordinate plane
  3. Draw a straight line through the points

Example: Graph y = 2x

x y
0 0
1 2
2 4
3 6

The graph will be a straight line passing through (0,0), (1,2), (2,4), etc.

Important Notes

  • The graph must pass through the origin (0,0)
  • The graph must be a straight line
  • The steeper the line, the greater the constant of proportionality

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Graph the proportional relationship y = 3x

Create a table:

x y
0 0
1 3
2 6

Plot these points and draw a straight line through them.

Question 2

Which of these graphs represents a proportional relationship?

a) A straight line passing through (0,0) and (1,4)

b) A straight line passing through (0,2) and (1,5)

c) A curve passing through (0,0) and (1,1)

Only a) represents a proportional relationship because:

  • It's a straight line
  • It passes through the origin

Unit Rate as Slope

Understanding the Connection

In a proportional relationship:

  • The unit rate is the constant of proportionality (k in y = kx)
  • The slope of the graph is the same as the unit rate

Example: If a car travels 300 km in 5 hours:

Unit rate = 300 km / 5 h = 60 km/h

The slope of the distance-time graph will be 60

The equation would be: distance = 60 × time

Calculating Slope

To calculate the slope (unit rate) from a graph:

\[ \text{Slope} = \frac{\text{change in y}}{\text{change in x}} = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} \]

Choose any two points on the line and calculate the ratio.

For points (1,3) and (2,6) on the line y = 3x:

\[ \text{Slope} = \frac{6-3}{2-1} = \frac{3}{1} = 3 \]

This matches the unit rate (coefficient of x in the equation).

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Find the unit rate and slope for the relationship represented by y = 4x

The unit rate is the coefficient of x, which is 4.

The slope of the graph will also be 4.

Question 2

A graph passes through (0,0) and (3,9). What is the unit rate?

Calculate the slope:

\[ \frac{9-0}{3-0} = \frac{9}{3} = 3 \]

The unit rate is 3.

Solving Problems

Problem-Solving Strategies

To solve problems involving proportional relationships:

  1. Identify the two quantities that are in proportion
  2. Determine the constant of proportionality (unit rate)
  3. Write the equation y = kx
  4. Use the equation to solve for unknown values

Example problem:

If 5 notebooks cost GHC 15, how much would 8 notebooks cost?

Solution:

1. Find unit price: 15 ÷ 5 = GHC 3 per notebook

2. Write equation: cost = 3 × number of notebooks

3. Calculate for 8 notebooks: cost = 3 × 8 = GHC 24

Real-World Applications

Proportional relationships appear in many real-world situations:

  • Speed, distance, and time
  • Cost and quantity of items
  • Exchange rates between currencies
  • Scale drawings and maps
  • Simple interest calculations

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A car travels 240 km in 3 hours. Assuming constant speed, how far will it travel in 7 hours?

1. Find speed (unit rate): 240 km ÷ 3 h = 80 km/h

2. Write equation: distance = 80 × time

3. Calculate for 7 hours: distance = 80 × 7 = 560 km

Question 2

The exchange rate is $1 = GHC 6. How many cedis would you get for $25?

1. Unit rate is GHC 6 per $1

2. Equation: cedis = 6 × dollars

3. Calculate for $25: cedis = 6 × 25 = GHC 150
