Introduction

Proportional relationships are mathematical relationships where two quantities maintain a constant ratio. These relationships are fundamental in solving various real-world problems involving ratios, percentages, and proportional changes.

In this lesson, we will learn how to apply proportional relationships to solve multistep problems involving:

  • Simple interest calculations
  • Value Added Tax (VAT)
  • Discounts on purchases
  • Commissions on sales
  • Depreciation of assets
  • National Health Insurance Levy (NHIL)
  • Insurance premiums

Simple Interest

Simple Interest Formula

Simple interest is calculated using the formula:

\[ I = \frac{P \times R \times T}{100} \]

Where:

  • \( I \) = Simple Interest
  • \( P \) = Principal amount (initial amount)
  • \( R \) = Rate of interest (in percentage)
  • \( T \) = Time period (in years)

Example:

Calculate the simple interest on GH₵5,000 at 8% per annum for 3 years.

Solution:

\[ I = \frac{5000 \times 8 \times 3}{100} = \frac{120,000}{100} = GH₵1,200 \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Kwame invested GH₵12,000 at a simple interest rate of 6.5% per annum. Calculate the interest earned after 4 years.

Solution:

\[ I = \frac{12000 \times 6.5 \times 4}{100} = \frac{312,000}{100} = GH₵3,120 \]

Question 2

How long will it take for GH₵8,000 to earn GH₵1,440 in simple interest at 6% per annum?

Solution:

\[ T = \frac{I \times 100}{P \times R} = \frac{1440 \times 100}{8000 \times 6} = \frac{144,000}{48,000} = 3 \text{ years} \]

Tax (VAT)

VAT Calculations

Value Added Tax (VAT) in Ghana is currently 12.5%. When an item's price is VAT inclusive, it means the price already includes the VAT.

Example:

A man bought an item at GH₵4,500.00, VAT inclusive. Calculate:

  1. The basic cost of the item
  2. The VAT paid by the man

Solution:

Let the basic cost be \( x \). Then:

\[ x + 0.125x = 4500 \] \[ 1.125x = 4500 \] \[ x = \frac{4500}{1.125} = GH₵4,000 \]

VAT paid = Total price - Basic cost = GH₵4,500 - GH₵4,000 = GH₵500

Practice Exercise

Question 1

The VAT inclusive price of a refrigerator is GH₵6,750. Calculate:

  1. The basic price
  2. The VAT amount

Solution:

Let basic price be \( x \):

\[ x + 0.125x = 6750 \] \[ 1.125x = 6750 \] \[ x = \frac{6750}{1.125} = GH₵6,000 \]

VAT amount = GH₵6,750 - GH₵6,000 = GH₵750

Discount

Discount Calculations

Discount is a reduction in the original price of an item. It is usually expressed as a percentage of the original price.

Discount Amount = Original Price × Discount Rate

New Price = Original Price - Discount Amount

Example:

If a car costs GH₵80,500.00, what is its new value if there is a discount of 10%?

Solution:

Discount Amount = GH₵80,500 × 10% = GH₵8,050

New Price = GH₵80,500 - GH₵8,050 = GH₵72,450

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A laptop is priced at GH₵3,200 with a 15% discount. Calculate:

  1. The discount amount
  2. The new price after discount

Solution:

Discount Amount = GH₵3,200 × 15% = GH₵480

New Price = GH₵3,200 - GH₵480 = GH₵2,720

Commission

Commission Calculations

Commission is a payment made to an agent or employee for transacting a piece of business or performing a service, often calculated as a percentage of the value involved.

Commission = Sales Amount × Commission Rate

Example:

A salesperson earns 7% commission on all sales. If she sells goods worth GH₵25,000, calculate her commission.

Solution:

Commission = GH₵25,000 × 7% = GH₵1,750

Practice Exercise

Question 1

An insurance agent receives 12% commission on all premiums collected. If he collects GH₵48,000 in premiums, how much commission does he earn?

Solution:

Commission = GH₵48,000 × 12% = GH₵5,760

Depreciation

Depreciation Calculations

Depreciation is the reduction in the value of an asset over time due to wear and tear, obsolescence, or other factors. It is often calculated as a percentage of the asset's value.

Annual Depreciation = Original Value × Depreciation Rate

Value After Depreciation = Original Value - Depreciation Amount

Example:

A company purchased machinery for GH₵120,000. If it depreciates at 10% per annum, calculate its value after 3 years.

Solution:

Year 1 Depreciation = GH₵120,000 × 10% = GH₵12,000

Value after Year 1 = GH₵120,000 - GH₵12,000 = GH₵108,000

Year 2 Depreciation = GH₵108,000 × 10% = GH₵10,800

Value after Year 2 = GH₵108,000 - GH₵10,800 = GH₵97,200

Year 3 Depreciation = GH₵97,200 × 10% = GH₵9,720

Value after Year 3 = GH₵97,200 - GH₵9,720 = GH₵87,480

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A vehicle purchased for GH₵65,000 depreciates at 15% per annum. Calculate its value after 2 years.

Solution:

Year 1 Depreciation = GH₵65,000 × 15% = GH₵9,750

Value after Year 1 = GH₵65,000 - GH₵9,750 = GH₵55,250

Year 2 Depreciation = GH₵55,250 × 15% = GH₵8,287.50

Value after Year 2 = GH₵55,250 - GH₵8,287.50 = GH₵46,962.50

NHIL

NHIL Calculations

The National Health Insurance Levy (NHIL) is a 2.5% levy on goods and services in Ghana to fund the National Health Insurance Scheme.

NHIL Amount = Price × NHIL Rate (2.5%)

NHIL Inclusive Price = Original Price + NHIL Amount

Example:

The NHIL inclusive price of a television set is GH₵1,200.00. If the NHIL is charged at a rate of 2.5%, find:

  1. The cost of the television set (NHIL exclusive)
  2. The NHIL charged

Solution:

Let the NHIL exclusive price be \( x \):

\[ x + 0.025x = 1200 \] \[ 1.025x = 1200 \] \[ x = \frac{1200}{1.025} \approx GH₵1,170.73 \]

NHIL charged = GH₵1,200 - GH₵1,170.73 = GH₵29.27

Practice Exercise

Question 1

The NHIL inclusive price of a refrigerator is GH₵3,280. Calculate:

  1. The NHIL exclusive price
  2. The NHIL amount

Solution:

Let NHIL exclusive price be \( x \):

\[ x + 0.025x = 3280 \] \[ 1.025x = 3280 \] \[ x = \frac{3280}{1.025} = GH₵3,200 \]

NHIL amount = GH₵3,280 - GH₵3,200 = GH₵80

Insurance

Insurance Calculations

Insurance premium is the amount paid periodically to the insurer for coverage. It is often calculated as a percentage of the insured value.

Premium = Insured Value × Premium Rate

Example:

Kofi Mereku insured his house and paid a premium of GH₵30,000.00. If the insurance company fixed the rate at 5% of the value of the house, calculate the insured value of the house.

Solution:

Let the insured value be \( x \):

\[ 0.05x = 30,000 \] \[ x = \frac{30,000}{0.05} = GH₵600,000 \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A business owner paid GH₵18,750 as insurance premium at a rate of 3% of the insured value. What was the insured value?

Solution:

Let insured value be \( x \):

\[ 0.03x = 18,750 \] \[ x = \frac{18,750}{0.03} = GH₵625,000 \]
