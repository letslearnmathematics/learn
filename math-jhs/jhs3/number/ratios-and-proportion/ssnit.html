Introduction

SSNIT (Social Security and National Insurance Trust) is Ghana's national pension scheme that provides retirement, invalidity, and survivorship benefits to workers and their dependents.

There are two main laws governing SSNIT contributions and benefits:

  1. Act 766 (National Pensions Act, 2008) - Current law
  2. PNDCL 247 (Social Security Law, 1991) - Previous law

In this lesson, we'll learn how to calculate contributions and benefits under both laws using proportional reasoning.

Employer/Employee Obligations

Contribution Rates

Under Act 766

Total contribution rate: 18.5% of basic salary

  • Employee contribution: 5.5%
  • Employer contribution: 13%

Additional 2.5% goes to National Health Insurance (NHIL)

Under PNDCL 247

Total contribution rate: 17.5% of basic salary

  • Employee contribution: 5%
  • Employer contribution: 12.5%

No separate NHIL contribution under this law

Important Notes

  • Contributions are based on basic salary (excluding allowances)
  • There's a minimum and maximum contribution amount
  • Act 766 introduced a three-tier pension system (we'll focus on Tier 1 - mandatory basic SSNIT scheme)

Act 766 Contributions

Calculating Contributions

Under Act 766, contributions are calculated as follows:

Example: If an employee's basic salary is Gh₵2,000:

  1. Employee contribution: \[ 5.5\% \times 2,000 = 0.055 \times 2,000 = \text{Gh₵}110 \]
  2. Employer contribution: \[ 13\% \times 2,000 = 0.13 \times 2,000 = \text{Gh₵}260 \]
  3. Total SSNIT contribution: \[ 110 + 260 = \text{Gh₵}370 \]
  4. NHIL contribution: \[ 2.5\% \times 2,000 = 0.025 \times 2,000 = \text{Gh₵}50 \]
  5. Total deduction from employer: \[ 260 + 50 = \text{Gh₵}310 \]

Salary Ceiling

There's a maximum salary (ceiling) for contributions:

  • Maximum basic salary for contributions: Gh₵-- (as of --)
  • For salaries above this amount, contributions are calculated on the ceiling amount

Example: If ceiling is Gh₵3,500 and salary is Gh₵4,000:

  1. Employee contribution: \[ 5.5\% \times 3,500 = \text{Gh₵}192.50 \]
  2. Employer contribution: \[ 13\% \times 3,500 = \text{Gh₵}455 \]

PNDCL 247 Contributions

Calculating Contributions

Under PNDCL 247, contributions were calculated as follows:

Example: If an employee's basic salary was Gh₵2,000:

  1. Employee contribution: \[ 5\% \times 2,000 = 0.05 \times 2,000 = \text{Gh₵}100 \]
  2. Employer contribution: \[ 12.5\% \times 2,000 = 0.125 \times 2,000 = \text{Gh₵}250 \]
  3. Total SSNIT contribution: \[ 100 + 250 = \text{Gh₵}350 \]

Historical Context

  • PNDCL 247 was in effect from 1991 to 2008
  • No separate NHIL contribution
  • Different benefit calculation method (see next sections)
  • Still relevant for calculating benefits for those who contributed under this law

Act 766 Benefits

Calculating Retirement Benefits

Under Act 766, monthly pension is calculated using:

\[ \text{Monthly Pension} = \frac{0.5 \times \text{Average 3-Year Salary} \times \text{Total Contribution Years}}{20} \]

Example: Employee retires after 25 years with average 3-year salary of Gh₵5,000:

\[ \text{Pension} = \frac{0.5 \times 5,000 \times 25}{20} = \frac{62,500}{20} = \text{Gh₵}3,125 \text{ per month} \]

Lump Sum:

Also receives a lump sum equal to total contributions plus interest

Minimum Pension:

Guaranteed minimum pension (currently Gh₵300/month)

Other Benefits

  • Invalidity Pension: For those permanently disabled
  • Survivors' Lump Sum: Paid to dependents if contributor dies
  • Emigration Benefit: For non-Ghanaians leaving Ghana permanently

PNDCL 247 Benefits

Calculating Retirement Benefits

Under PNDCL 247, monthly pension was calculated differently:

\[ \text{Monthly Pension} = 0.5 \times \text{Average 5-Year Salary} \times \frac{\text{Total Contribution Years}}{40} \]

Example: Employee retires after 25 years with average 5-year salary of Gh₵5,000:

\[ \text{Pension} = 0.5 \times 5,000 \times \frac{25}{40} = 2,500 \times 0.625 = \text{Gh₵}1,562.50 \text{ per month} \]

Key Differences:

  • Based on 5-year average salary (vs 3-year under Act 766)
  • Different divisor (40 vs 20)
  • Generally results in lower monthly pension than Act 766

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Calculate the employee, employer, and total SSNIT contributions under Act 766 for a worker with a basic salary of Gh₵2,500.

  1. Employee contribution: \[ 5.5\% \times 2,500 = \text{Gh₵}137.50 \]
  2. Employer contribution: \[ 13\% \times 2,500 = \text{Gh₵}325 \]
  3. Total SSNIT contribution: \[ 137.50 + 325 = \text{Gh₵}462.50 \]
  4. NHIL contribution: \[ 2.5\% \times 2,500 = \text{Gh₵}62.50 \]

Question 2

A worker earning Gh₵3,200 basic salary contributed under PNDCL 247 for 30 years. If their average 5-year salary is Gh₵4,800, calculate their monthly pension.

\[ \text{Pension} = 0.5 \times 4,800 \times \frac{30}{40} = 2,400 \times 0.75 = \text{Gh₵}1,800 \text{ per month} \]

Question 3

Compare the total monthly deductions (SSNIT + NHIL) for an employee earning Gh₵3,000 under both Act 766 and PNDCL 247.

Under Act 766:

  1. Employee SSNIT: \[ 5.5\% \times 3,000 = \text{Gh₵}165 \]
  2. NHIL: \[ 2.5\% \times 3,000 = \text{Gh₵}75 \]
  3. Total deduction: \[ 165 + 75 = \text{Gh₵}240 \]

Under PNDCL 247:

  1. Employee SSNIT: \[ 5\% \times 3,000 = \text{Gh₵}150 \]
  2. No NHIL
  3. Total deduction: \[ \text{Gh₵}150 \]

Question 4

An employee retiring under Act 766 has an average 3-year salary of Gh₵6,000 after contributing for 35 years. Calculate their monthly pension.

\[ \text{Pension} = \frac{0.5 \times 6,000 \times 35}{20} = \frac{105,000}{20} = \text{Gh₵}5,250 \text{ per month} \]

Question 5

If the SSNIT contribution ceiling is Gh₵3,500, calculate the employer's total monthly payment (SSNIT + NHIL) for an employee with a basic salary of Gh₵4,000 under Act 766.

  1. Employer SSNIT: \[ 13\% \times 3,500 = \text{Gh₵}455 \]
  2. NHIL: \[ 2.5\% \times 3,500 = \text{Gh₵}87.50 \]
  3. Total payment: \[ 455 + 87.50 = \text{Gh₵}542.50 \]
