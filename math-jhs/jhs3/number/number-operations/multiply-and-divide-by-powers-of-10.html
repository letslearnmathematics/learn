Introduction

Powers of 10 are numbers like 10, 100, 1000 that can be expressed as 10 raised to a positive integer (101, 102, 103). Similarly, fractions like \(\frac{1}{10}\), \(\frac{1}{100}\), \(\frac{1}{1000}\) are powers of 10 with negative exponents (10-1, 10-2, 10-3).

Understanding how to multiply and divide by powers of 10 is essential for working with decimals, large numbers, and scientific notation. This lesson will help you master these operations with whole numbers and decimals.

Multiplication Facts

Multiplication Facts up to 144

Before working with powers of 10, it's important to recall basic multiplication facts up to 12×12=144 and their related division facts.

These facts form the foundation for understanding larger multiplication and division problems.

Key Patterns

Notice these patterns in multiplication tables that relate to powers of 10:

Multiples of 10

\[ 10 \times n = n0 \]

Any number multiplied by 10 adds a zero at the end.

Multiples of 100

\[ 100 \times n = n00 \]

Any number multiplied by 100 adds two zeros at the end.

Division Facts

\[ n \div 10 = \text{move decimal one place left} \]

Division by powers of 10 moves the decimal point to the left.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Here's a partial list (complete up to 5×5):

  • 1×1=1, 1×2=2, ..., 1×12=12
  • 2×1=2, 2×2=4, ..., 2×12=24
  • 3×1=3, 3×2=6, ..., 3×12=36
  • 4×1=4, 4×2=8, ..., 4×12=48
  • 5×1=5, 5×2=10, ..., 5×12=60

Continue this pattern up to 12×12=144.

Question 2

Write the related division facts for 7×8=56.

The related division facts are:

  • 56 ÷ 7 = 8
  • 56 ÷ 8 = 7

Question 3

Complete: 12 × ___ = 144 and 144 ÷ 12 = ___

The answers are:

  • 12 × 12 = 144
  • 144 ÷ 12 = 12

Benchmark Fractions

Common Benchmark Fractions

Benchmark fractions are common fractions that have simple decimal equivalents. These are important to memorize for quick calculations.

Here are some key benchmark fractions and their decimal/percentage equivalents:

Fraction-Decimal-Percentage Equivalents

Halves

  • \(\frac{1}{2} = 0.5 = 50\%\)
  • \(\frac{1}{4} = 0.25 = 25\%\)
  • \(\frac{3}{4} = 0.75 = 75\%\)

Thirds

  • \(\frac{1}{3} \approx 0.333... \approx 33.\overline{3}\%\)
  • \(\frac{2}{3} \approx 0.666... \approx 66.\overline{6}\%\)

Tenths

  • \(\frac{1}{10} = 0.1 = 10\%\)
  • \(\frac{3}{10} = 0.3 = 30\%\)
  • \(\frac{9}{10} = 0.9 = 90\%\)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Convert these benchmark fractions to decimals and percentages: \(\frac{1}{5}\), \(\frac{2}{5}\), \(\frac{4}{5}\)

  • \(\frac{1}{5} = 0.2 = 20\%\)
  • \(\frac{2}{5} = 0.4 = 40\%\)
  • \(\frac{4}{5} = 0.8 = 80\%\)

Question 2

Convert these decimals to fractions and percentages: 0.125, 0.375, 0.875

  • \(0.125 = \frac{1}{8} = 12.5\%\)
  • \(0.375 = \frac{3}{8} = 37.5\%\)
  • \(0.875 = \frac{7}{8} = 87.5\%\)

Question 3

Convert these percentages to fractions and decimals: 15%, 62.5%, 87.5%

  • \(15\% = \frac{3}{20} = 0.15\)
  • \(62.5\% = \frac{5}{8} = 0.625\)
  • \(87.5\% = \frac{7}{8} = 0.875\)

Multiplying Decimals by Powers of 10

The Rule

When multiplying a decimal by 10, 100, 1000, etc., the decimal point moves to the right by the same number of places as there are zeros in the power of 10.

For example:

  • Multiply by 10: move decimal 1 place right
  • Multiply by 100: move decimal 2 places right
  • Multiply by 1000: move decimal 3 places right

Examples

Example 1

\( 3.45 \times 10 = 34.5 \)

The decimal moved 1 place to the right.

Example 2

\( 0.067 \times 100 = 6.7 \)

The decimal moved 2 places to the right.

Example 3

\( 2.3 \times 1000 = 2300 \)

The decimal moved 3 places to the right (adding zeros as needed).

Question 3

The decimal moves 3 places to the right.

Question 4

Calculate: \( 0.005 \times 10,000 \)

\( 0.005 \times 10,000 = 50 \)

The decimal moves 4 places to the right.

Dividing Decimals by Powers of 10

The Rule

When dividing a decimal by 10, 100, 1000, etc., the decimal point moves to the left by the same number of places as there are zeros in the power of 10.

For example:

  • Divide by 10: move decimal 1 place left
  • Divide by 100: move decimal 2 places left
  • Divide by 1000: move decimal 3 places left

Examples

Example 1

\( 45.6 \div 10 = 4.56 \)

The decimal moved 1 place to the left.

Example 2

\( 7.8 \div 100 = 0.078 \)

The decimal moved 2 places to the left.

Example 3

\( 1234.5 \div 1000 = 1.2345 \)

The decimal moved 3 places to the left.

Practice Exercise

The decimal moves 3 places to the left.

Question 4

The decimal moves 4 places to the left.

Combined Operations

Multiplying and Dividing by Fractions

Multiplying by fractions like \(\frac{1}{10}\), \(\frac{1}{100}\), \(\frac{1}{1000}\) is equivalent to dividing by 10, 100, 1000.

Similarly, dividing by these fractions is equivalent to multiplying by 10, 100, 1000.

Examples

Example 1

\( 45 \times \frac{1}{10} = 45 \div 10 = 4.5 \)

Example 2

\( 7.8 \div \frac{1}{100} = 7.8 \times 100 = 780 \)

Example 3

\( 0.123 \times \frac{1}{1000} = 0.123 \div 1000 = 0.000123 \)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Calculate: \( 67 \times \frac{1}{10} \)

\( 67 \times \frac{1}{10} = 67 \div 10 = 6.7 \)

Question 2

Calculate: \( 0.89 \div \frac{1}{100} \)

\( 0.89 \div \frac{1}{100} = 0.89 \times 100 = 89 \)

Question 3

Calculate: \( 1234 \times \frac{1}{1000} \)

\( 1234 \times \frac{1}{1000} = 1234 \div 1000 = 1.234 \)

Question 4

Calculate: \( 0.0567 \div \frac{1}{10,000} \)

\( 0.0567 \div \frac{1}{10,000} = 0.0567 \times 10,000 = 567 \)
