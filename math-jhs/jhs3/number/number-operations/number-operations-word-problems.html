Introduction

Word problems are mathematical problems presented in the form of a story or real-life situation. To solve them, we need to identify the relevant information, determine which operations to use, and perform calculations to find the solution.

In this lesson, we'll focus on solving word problems that involve a combination of two or more basic operations: addition (+), subtraction (-), multiplication (×), and division (÷).

Example Problem:

A trader sells oranges from two baskets, A and B. Basket A contained 85 oranges and she sold 48. She sold 59 oranges from basket B and was left with the same number of oranges as in Basket A. How many oranges were originally in Basket B?

Problem Solving Steps

The 4-Step Approach

Follow these steps to solve any word problem:

  1. Understand the problem (read carefully, identify what's given and what's asked)
  2. Plan the solution (decide which operations to use and in what order)
  3. Solve (perform the calculations step by step)
  4. Check (verify if your answer makes sense in the context of the problem)

Key Questions to Ask

When solving word problems, ask yourself:

  • What information is given?
  • What am I being asked to find?
  • Which operations will help me solve this?
  • In what order should I perform these operations?
  • Does my final answer make sense?

Addition & Subtraction Problems

Example 1

Problem: A school has 785 students. 128 new students enrolled this term, while 95 students transferred to other schools. How many students are in the school now?

Solution:

Initial students: 785

Add new students: 785 + 128 = 913

Subtract transferred students: 913 - 95 = 818

Final answer: The school now has 818 students.

Example 2

Problem: In a bookstore, there were 450 books on Monday. During the week, 175 books were sold and 230 new books arrived. How many books are in the store at the end of the week?

Solution:

Initial books: 450

Subtract books sold: 450 - 175 = 275

Add new books: 275 + 230 = 505

Final answer: There are 505 books in the store.

Multiplication & Division Problems

Example 1

Problem: A factory produces 245 toys each day. The toys are packed into boxes of 15. How many complete boxes can be made each day, and how many toys will be left over?

Solution:

Total toys: 245

Toys per box: 15

Number of boxes: 245 ÷ 15 = 16 with remainder 5

Final answer: 16 complete boxes with 5 toys left over.

Example 2

Problem: A school has 24 classrooms. Each classroom has 35 students. If the school wants to divide all students equally among 8 buses for a field trip, how many students will be on each bus?

Solution:

Total students: 24 × 35 = 840

Number of buses: 8

Students per bus: 840 ÷ 8 = 105

Final answer: Each bus will have 105 students.

Mixed Operations Problems

Example 1 (From Indicator)

Problem: A trader sells oranges from two baskets, A and B. Basket A contained 85 oranges and she sold 48. She sold 59 oranges from basket B and was left with the same number of oranges as in Basket A. How many oranges were originally in Basket B?

Solution:

Step 1: Find oranges left in Basket A

85 (initial) - 48 (sold) = 37 oranges remaining in A

Step 2: Basket B has same remaining amount after selling 59

Let x = original oranges in B

x - 59 = 37

x = 37 + 59

x = 96

Final answer: Basket B originally had 96 oranges.

Example 2

Problem: A farmer has 150 mangoes. He sells 45 mangoes on Monday and twice as many on Tuesday. He then divides the remaining mangoes equally among his 3 children. How many mangoes does each child get?

Solution:

Initial mangoes: 150

Sold on Monday: 45

Sold on Tuesday: 2 × 45 = 90

Total sold: 45 + 90 = 135

Remaining mangoes: 150 - 135 = 15

Mangoes per child: 15 ÷ 3 = 5

Final answer: Each child gets 5 mangoes.

Example 3

Problem: A school bought 15 packs of notebooks. Each pack contains 12 notebooks. They distributed 5 notebooks to each of 25 students. How many notebooks are left?

Solution:

Total notebooks: 15 × 12 = 180

Notebooks distributed: 25 × 5 = 125

Notebooks remaining: 180 - 125 = 55

Final answer: There are 55 notebooks left.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A shop had 350 bags of rice. They sold 125 bags on Monday and received a delivery of 80 more bags on Tuesday. How many bags are in the shop now?

Solution:

Initial bags: 350

After Monday sale: 350 - 125 = 225

After Tuesday delivery: 225 + 80 = 305

Answer: 305 bags

Question 2

A factory produces 48 toys every hour. How many toys will it produce in a 7-hour day, and if packed in boxes of 12, how many full boxes can be made?

Solution:

Total toys: 48 × 7 = 336

Boxes: 336 ÷ 12 = 28

Answer: 336 toys, 28 boxes

Question 3

In a school, there are 18 classrooms with 35 students each. If the school wants to divide all students equally among 9 buses for a trip, how many students will be on each bus?

Solution:

Total students: 18 × 35 = 630

Students per bus: 630 ÷ 9 = 70

Answer: 70 students per bus

Question 4

A farmer harvested 560 oranges. He sold 120 oranges at the market and packed the rest into bags of 20 oranges each. How many full bags did he make?

Solution:

After sale: 560 - 120 = 440

Number of bags: 440 ÷ 20 = 22

Answer: 22 full bags

Question 5

A bookstore had 250 books. They sold 45 books on Monday, 38 on Tuesday, and received a shipment of 120 new books on Wednesday. How many books are in the store now?

Solution:

Initial books: 250

After Monday: 250 - 45 = 205

After Tuesday: 205 - 38 = 167

After Wednesday: 167 + 120 = 287

Answer: 287 books
