Introduction

Word problems are mathematical problems presented in everyday language rather than mathematical notation. Solving word problems involves identifying the relevant information, determining which operations to use, performing calculations, and interpreting the results.

In this lesson, we will focus on solving word problems involving the four basic operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division) and rounding answers to specified decimal places or significant figures.

Four Basic Operations

Key Operations

The four basic operations in mathematics are:

  1. Addition (+) - Combining numbers to find their total
  2. Subtraction (-) - Finding the difference between numbers
  3. Multiplication (√ó) - Repeated addition of the same number
  4. Division (√∑) - Splitting a number into equal parts

Operation Relationships

These operations are related to each other. For example:

Addition and Subtraction

Are inverse operations:

\[ \text{If } a + b = c \text{, then } c - b = a \]

Multiplication and Division

Are inverse operations:

\[ \text{If } a \times b = c \text{, then } c \div b = a \]

Rounding Numbers

Rounding to Decimal Places

To round a number to a certain number of decimal places:

  1. Identify the digit at the required decimal place
  2. Look at the digit immediately to its right
  3. If that digit is 5 or greater, round up the identified digit
  4. If it's less than 5, leave the identified digit unchanged
  5. Drop all digits to the right of the required decimal place

Example: Round 3.14159 to:

  • 2 decimal places: 3.14
  • 3 decimal places: 3.142
  • 4 decimal places: 3.1416

Significant Figures

Significant figures are the digits in a number that carry meaning contributing to its precision. Rules for identifying significant figures:

  1. All non-zero digits are significant
  2. Zeros between non-zero digits are significant
  3. Leading zeros are not significant
  4. Trailing zeros in a decimal number are significant

Examples:

  • 123.45 has 5 significant figures
  • 0.0045 has 2 significant figures
  • 100.00 has 5 significant figures
  • 1200 has 2 significant figures (unless written as 1200.0, which has 5)

Problem Solving Steps

Step-by-Step Approach

Follow these steps to solve word problems:

  1. Read carefully: Understand what the problem is asking
  2. Identify given information: Extract numbers and relationships
  3. Determine what's needed: What quantity are you solving for?
  4. Choose operations: Decide which operations to use
  5. Set up calculations: Write the mathematical expressions
  6. Perform calculations: Solve step by step
  7. Check reasonableness: Does the answer make sense?
  8. Round if required: Apply rounding rules as specified

Example 3: Paint Calculation

Problem: A painter needs to cover a wall that is 4.25 meters high and 12.6 meters long. One liter of paint covers approximately 8.5 square meters. How many liters of paint are needed to paint the wall? Give your answer to 3 significant figures.

Solution:

1. Calculate wall area: height √ó length

\[ 4.25 \times 12.6 = 53.55 \text{ m}^2 \]

2. Calculate paint needed: area √∑ coverage per liter

\[ 53.55 \div 8.5 \approx 6.3 \text{ liters} \]

3. To 3 significant figures: 6.30 liters
