Introduction

The distributive property is a fundamental property of numbers that relates multiplication to addition and subtraction. It allows us to simplify expressions and solve problems more efficiently.

For any three numbers a, b, and c, the distributive property states that:

Distributive Over Addition:

\[ a \times (b + c) = (a \times b) + (a \times c) \]

Distributive Over Subtraction:

\[ a \times (b - c) = (a \times b) - (a \times c) \]

Distributive Over Addition

Understanding the Property

The distributive property over addition states that multiplying a number by a sum is the same as multiplying the number by each addend and then adding the products.

Example: Verify that \( 4 \times (3 + 5) = (4 \times 3) + (4 \times 5) \)

Solution:

Left side: \( 4 \times (3 + 5) = 4 \times 8 = 32 \)

Right side: \( (4 \times 3) + (4 \times 5) = 12 + 20 = 32 \)

Both sides equal 32, so the property holds.

Visual Representation

Imagine a rectangle divided into two smaller rectangles. The area of the large rectangle equals the sum of the areas of the two smaller rectangles.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Use the distributive property to expand: \( 5 \times (7 + 2) \)

\( 5 \times (7 + 2) = (5 \times 7) + (5 \times 2) = 35 + 10 = 45 \)

Question 2

Expand using the distributive property: \( 3 \times (x + 4) \)

\( 3 \times (x + 4) = (3 \times x) + (3 \times 4) = 3x + 12 \)

Question 3

Simplify using the distributive property: \( 12 \times (10 + 3) \)

\( 12 \times (10 + 3) = (12 \times 10) + (12 \times 3) = 120 + 36 = 156 \)

Distributive Over Subtraction

Understanding the Property

The distributive property over subtraction states that multiplying a number by a difference is the same as multiplying the number by each term and then subtracting the products.

Example: Verify that \( 6 \times (9 - 4) = (6 \times 9) - (6 \times 4) \)

Solution:

Left side: \( 6 \times (9 - 4) = 6 \times 5 = 30 \)

Right side: \( (6 \times 9) - (6 \times 4) = 54 - 24 = 30 \)

Both sides equal 30, so the property holds.

Visual Representation

Imagine a large rectangle with a smaller rectangle removed from it. The remaining area equals the difference between the areas of the two rectangles.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Use the distributive property to expand: \( 8 \times (10 - 3) \)

\( 8 \times (10 - 3) = (8 \times 10) - (8 \times 3) = 80 - 24 = 56 \)

Question 2

Expand using the distributive property: \( 5 \times (y - 7) \)

\( 5 \times (y - 7) = (5 \times y) - (5 \times 7) = 5y - 35 \)

Question 3

Simplify using the distributive property: \( 15 \times (20 - 5) \)

\( 15 \times (20 - 5) = (15 \times 20) - (15 \times 5) = 300 - 75 = 225 \)

Practical Applications

Real-World Problems

The distributive property is useful in solving various real-world problems, especially those involving calculations with multiple items or quantities.

Example 1: A store sells pens at ¢5 each and pencils at ¢3 each. If you buy 4 pens and 4 pencils, how much do you pay?

Solution using distributive property:

Total cost = \( 4 \times (5 + 3) = (4 \times 5) + (4 \times 3) = 20 + 12 = ¢32 \)

Example 2: A rectangle has length (x + 5) cm and width 8 cm. Find its area.

Solution using distributive property:

Area = \( 8 \times (x + 5) = (8 \times x) + (8 \times 5) = 8x + 40 \) cm²

Mental Math Tricks

The distributive property can help simplify mental calculations:

  • \( 7 \times 98 = 7 \times (100 - 2) = 700 - 14 = 686 \)
  • \( 6 \times 105 = 6 \times (100 + 5) = 600 + 30 = 630 \)
  • \( 12 \times 25 = (10 + 2) \times 25 = 250 + 50 = 300 \)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A book costs ¢12 and a notebook costs ¢8. What is the total cost for 5 books and 5 notebooks?

Total cost = \( 5 \times (12 + 8) = (5 \times 12) + (5 \times 8) = 60 + 40 = ¢100 \)

Question 2

Calculate mentally using the distributive property: \( 9 \times 103 \)

\( 9 \times 103 = 9 \times (100 + 3) = (9 \times 100) + (9 \times 3) = 900 + 27 = 927 \)

Question 3

A garden has length (y + 10) meters and width 6 meters. Express its area using the distributive property.

Area = \( 6 \times (y + 10) = (6 \times y) + (6 \times 10) = 6y + 60 \) m²
