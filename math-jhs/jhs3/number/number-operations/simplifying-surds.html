Introduction to Surds

Surds are irrational numbers that cannot be simplified to remove a square root (or cube root etc). They are roots of numbers that produce non-terminating, non-repeating decimals.

Examples of surds include √2, √3, √5, etc. These cannot be simplified to exact fractions or decimals.

Examples of surds:

\[ \sqrt{2} \approx 1.414213... \] \[ \sqrt{3} \approx 1.732050... \] \[ \sqrt{5} \approx 2.236067... \]

Not Surds:

These are not surds because they can be simplified to exact values:

\[ \sqrt{4} = 2 \] \[ \sqrt{9} = 3 \] \[ \sqrt{16} = 4 \]

Exact Form:

Surds are often left in exact form rather than as decimal approximations to maintain precision in calculations.

Basic Simplification of Surds

Simplification Rule

To simplify a surd, we look for perfect square factors of the number under the root:

\[ \sqrt{a \times b} = \sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{b} \]

Where a is the largest perfect square factor of the number.

Step-by-Step Simplification

  1. Find the largest perfect square that divides the number under the root
  2. Rewrite the surd as product of two roots
  3. Simplify the perfect square root

Example 1: Simplify √12

\[ \begin{align*} \sqrt{12} &= \sqrt{4 \times 3} \\ &= \sqrt{4} \times \sqrt{3} \\ &= 2\sqrt{3} \end{align*} \]

Example 2: Simplify √50

\[ \begin{align*} \sqrt{50} &= \sqrt{25 \times 2} \\ &= \sqrt{25} \times \sqrt{2} \\ &= 5\sqrt{2} \end{align*} \]

Addition & Subtraction of Surds

Key Rule

Surds can only be added or subtracted when they have the same radical part (like terms):

\[ a\sqrt{b} + c\sqrt{b} = (a + c)\sqrt{b} \] \[ a\sqrt{b} - c\sqrt{b} = (a - c)\sqrt{b} \]

Steps for Addition/Subtraction

  1. Simplify each surd completely
  2. Identify like terms (same radical part)
  3. Add/subtract the coefficients

Example 1: Simplify √12 + √27

\[ \begin{align*} \sqrt{12} + \sqrt{27} &= 2\sqrt{3} + 3\sqrt{3} \\ &= (2 + 3)\sqrt{3} \\ &= 5\sqrt{3} \end{align*} \]

Example 2: Simplify 3√8 - √50

\[ \begin{align*} 3\sqrt{8} - \sqrt{50} &= 3 \times 2\sqrt{2} - 5\sqrt{2} \\ &= 6\sqrt{2} - 5\sqrt{2} \\ &= (6 - 5)\sqrt{2} \\ &= \sqrt{2} \end{align*} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Simplify √18 + √32

\[ \begin{align*} \sqrt{18} + \sqrt{32} &= 3\sqrt{2} + 4\sqrt{2} \\ &= (3 + 4)\sqrt{2} \\ &= 7\sqrt{2} \end{align*} \]

Question 2

Simplify 2√27 - √75

\[ \begin{align*} 2\sqrt{27} - \sqrt{75} &= 2 \times 3\sqrt{3} - 5\sqrt{3} \\ &= 6\sqrt{3} - 5\sqrt{3} \\ &= (6 - 5)\sqrt{3} \\ &= \sqrt{3} \end{align*} \]

Question 3

Simplify 3√50 + 2√8 - √98

\[ \begin{align*} 3\sqrt{50} + 2\sqrt{8} - \sqrt{98} &= 3 \times 5\sqrt{2} + 2 \times 2\sqrt{2} - 7\sqrt{2} \\ &= 15\sqrt{2} + 4\sqrt{2} - 7\sqrt{2} \\ &= (15 + 4 - 7)\sqrt{2} \\ &= 12\sqrt{2} \end{align*} \]

Multiplication & Division of Surds

Key Rules

Multiplication: √a × √b = √(a×b)

Division: √a ÷ √b = √(a÷b) (where b ≠ 0)

Steps for Multiplication

  1. Multiply the coefficients
  2. Multiply the radicands (numbers under the roots)
  3. Simplify the resulting surd

Example 1: Simplify √6 × √10

\[ \begin{align*} \sqrt{6} \times \sqrt{10} &= \sqrt{6 \times 10} \\ &= \sqrt{60} \\ &= \sqrt{4 \times 15} \\ &= 2\sqrt{15} \end{align*} \]

Example 2: Simplify 2√3 × 5√6

\[ \begin{align*} 2\sqrt{3} \times 5\sqrt{6} &= (2 \times 5) \times (\sqrt{3} \times \sqrt{6}) \\ &= 10 \times \sqrt{18} \\ &= 10 \times 3\sqrt{2} \\ &= 30\sqrt{2} \end{align*} \]

Steps for Division

  1. Divide the coefficients
  2. Divide the radicands
  3. Simplify the resulting surd

Example: Simplify √50 ÷ √2

\[ \begin{align*} \sqrt{50} \div \sqrt{2} &= \sqrt{50 \div 2} \\ &= \sqrt{25} \\ &= 5 \end{align*} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Simplify √8 × √6

\[ \begin{align*} \sqrt{8} \times \sqrt{6} &= \sqrt{48} \\ &= \sqrt{16 \times 3} \\ &= 4\sqrt{3} \end{align*} \]

Question 2

Simplify 3√5 × 2√10

\[ \begin{align*} 3\sqrt{5} \times 2\sqrt{10} &= 6\sqrt{50} \\ &= 6 \times 5\sqrt{2} \\ &= 30\sqrt{2} \end{align*} \]

Question 3

Simplify √72 ÷ √2

\[ \begin{align*} \sqrt{72} \div \sqrt{2} &= \sqrt{36} \\ &= 6 \end{align*} \]

Rationalizing Denominators

What is Rationalizing?

Rationalizing means eliminating surds from the denominator of a fraction by multiplying both numerator and denominator by an appropriate surd.

Simple Denominators (√a)

Multiply numerator and denominator by the surd in the denominator:

\[ \frac{b}{\sqrt{a}} = \frac{b \times \sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{a}} = \frac{b\sqrt{a}}{a} \]

Example: Rationalize \(\frac{3}{\sqrt{5}}\)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{3}{\sqrt{5}} &= \frac{3 \times \sqrt{5}}{\sqrt{5} \times \sqrt{5}} \\ &= \frac{3\sqrt{5}}{5} \end{align*} \]

Binomial Denominators (a±√b)

Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate of the denominator:

The conjugate of (a + √b) is (a - √b), and vice versa.

Example: Rationalize \(\frac{2}{3 + \sqrt{5}}\)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{2}{3 + \sqrt{5}} &= \frac{2 \times (3 - \sqrt{5})}{(3 + \sqrt{5})(3 - \sqrt{5})} \\ &= \frac{6 - 2\sqrt{5}}{9 - 5} \\ &= \frac{6 - 2\sqrt{5}}{4} \\ &= \frac{3 - \sqrt{5}}{2} \end{align*} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Rationalize \(\frac{4}{\sqrt{7}}\)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{4}{\sqrt{7}} &= \frac{4 \times \sqrt{7}}{\sqrt{7} \times \sqrt{7}} \\ &= \frac{4\sqrt{7}}{7} \end{align*} \]

Question 2

Rationalize \(\frac{5}{2 - \sqrt{3}}\)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{5}{2 - \sqrt{3}} &= \frac{5 \times (2 + \sqrt{3})}{(2 - \sqrt{3})(2 + \sqrt{3})} \\ &= \frac{10 + 5\sqrt{3}}{4 - 3} \\ &= 10 + 5\sqrt{3} \end{align*} \]

Question 3

Rationalize \(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{5} - \sqrt{3}}\)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{5} - \sqrt{3}} &= \frac{\sqrt{2} \times (\sqrt{5} + \sqrt{3})}{(\sqrt{5} - \sqrt{3})(\sqrt{5} + \sqrt{3})} \\ &= \frac{\sqrt{10} + \sqrt{6}}{5 - 3} \\ &= \frac{\sqrt{10} + \sqrt{6}}{2} \end{align*} \]
