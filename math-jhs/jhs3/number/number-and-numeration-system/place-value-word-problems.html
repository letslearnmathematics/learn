Introduction

Place value is fundamental in mathematics and everyday life. It refers to the value of each digit in a number based on its position. Understanding place value helps us:

  • Read and write large numbers accurately
  • Perform arithmetic operations correctly
  • Solve real-world problems involving quantities
  • Make sense of numerical information in daily life

In this lesson, we'll apply our knowledge of place value to create and solve practical problems we encounter in real life situations.

Solving Real Life Problems

Understanding the Approach

To solve real-life problems using place value:

  1. Identify the numerical information in the problem
  2. Determine the place value of each digit
  3. Perform the necessary calculations

Example Problem

Problem: A company sold 3,450 units of a product at Gh₵120 each. What was their total revenue?

Solution:
1. Identify the numbers: 3,450 units and Gh₵120 per unit
2. Multiply using place value understanding:
Gh₵120 $\times$ 3,450 = Gh₵120 $\times$ (3,000 + 400 + 50)
= (120 $\times$ 3,000) + (120 $\times$ 400) + (120 $\times$ 50)
= 360,000 + 48,000 + 6,000
= Gh₵414,000
3. Final answer in standard form: Gh₵414,000

Creating Story Problems

How to Create Story Problems

Creating real-life story problems involves:

  1. Choosing a realistic scenario (shopping, construction, population, etc.)
  2. Incorporating numerical data with clear place values
  3. Formulating a question that requires place value understanding
  4. Ensuring the problem can be solved using the given information

Example Created Problem

Scenario: A school is organizing a field trip. There are 1,250 students going on the trip, and each student needs to pay Gh₵35 for transportation and Gh₵15 for lunch.

Problem: Calculate the total amount of money collected if all students participate.

Solution:
1. Total cost per student = Gh₵35 + Gh₵15 = Gh₵50
2. Total amount = 1,250 $\times$ Gh₵50
= (1,000 + 200 + 50) $\times$ Gh₵50
= (1,000 $\times$ Gh₵50) + (200 $\times$ Gh₵50) + (50 $\times$ Gh₵50)
= Gh₵50,000 + Gh₵10,000 + Gh₵2,500
3. Final answer: Gh₵62,500

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A housing project consists of 45 blocks. Each block has 12 floors, and each floor has 8 apartments. How many apartments are there in total?

Solution:
1. Apartments per block = 12 floors × 8 apartments = 96
2. Total apartments = 45 × 96
= 45 × (100 - 4)
= (45 × 100) - (45 × 4)
= 4,500 - 180
= 4,320
3. Final answer: 4,320 apartments

Question 2

Create a story problem involving population growth where you need to calculate the total population after a certain number of years, given annual growth.

Example Problem:
A town has a population of 25,000. The population grows by 1,200 people each year. What will be the population after 15 years?

Solution:
1. Annual growth = 1,200
2. Growth over 15 years = 1,200 × 15 = 18,000
3. Total population = 25,000 + 18,000 = 43,000
4. Final answer: 43,000 people

Question 3

A factory produces 2,450 items per day. Each item is sold for Gh₵25. How much revenue does the factory generate in a 30-day month? Write your answer in standard form.

Solution:
1. Daily revenue = 2,450 × Gh₵25
= (2,000 + 400 + 50) × Gh₵25
= (2,000 × Gh₵25) + (400 × Gh₵25) + (50 × Gh₵25)
= Gh₵50,000 + Gh₵10,000 + Gh₵1,250 = Gh₵61,250
2. Monthly revenue = Gh₵61,250 × 30
= (Gh₵60,000 × 30) + (Gh₵1,250 × 30)
= Gh₵1,800,000 + Gh₵37,500
3. Final answer: Gh₵1,837,500
