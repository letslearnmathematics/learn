Introduction

Significant figures and decimal places are important concepts in mathematics that help us express numbers with appropriate precision. They are used in various fields including science, engineering, and finance.

In this lesson, we will learn how to:

  1. Express integers to a specified number of significant figures
  2. Express decimal numbers to a given number of decimal places

Significant Figures

What are Significant Figures?

Significant figures are the digits in a number that carry meaning contributing to its precision. This includes all digits except:

  • Leading zeros (zeros before the first non-zero digit)
  • Trailing zeros in a number without a decimal point (they may or may not be significant)

Rules for Identifying Significant Figures

  1. All non-zero digits are significant (1-9)
  2. Zeros between non-zero digits are significant
  3. Leading zeros are not significant
  4. Trailing zeros in a number with a decimal point are significant
Number Significant Figures Explanation
1234 4 All non-zero digits
1002 4 Zeros between non-zero digits are significant
0.0045 2 Leading zeros are not significant
150.00 5 Trailing zeros after decimal are significant

Expressing Numbers to Significant Figures

To express a number to a given number of significant figures:

  1. Count the number of significant figures needed
  2. Identify the digit at the position where you'll round
  3. Look at the next digit (the first non-significant digit)
  4. If it's 5 or more, round up the last significant digit
  5. If it's less than 5, keep the last significant digit unchanged
  6. Replace any remaining digits with zeros if necessary (for numbers without decimal points)

Examples:

Express 34,567 to 3 significant figures:

34,567 → 34,600 (The '5' in the hundreds place means we round up the '4' to '5')

Express 0.0045678 to 2 significant figures:

0.0045678 → 0.0046 (The '6' means we round up the '5' to '6')

Decimal Places

What are Decimal Places?

Decimal places refer to the number of digits to the right of the decimal point in a number. When we express a number to a certain number of decimal places, we're specifying its precision.

Rules for Decimal Places

  1. Count the digits after the decimal point
  2. Identify the digit at the specified decimal place
  3. Look at the next digit (the first non-included digit)
  4. If it's 5 or more, round up the last decimal digit
  5. If it's less than 5, keep the last decimal digit unchanged
  6. Drop any remaining digits after the specified decimal places

Examples:

Express 3.14159 to 2 decimal places:

3.14159 → 3.14 (The next digit '1' is less than 5)

Express 2.71828 to 3 decimal places:

2.71828 → 2.718 (The next digit '2' is less than 5)

Express 9.80665 to 1 decimal place:

9.80665 → 9.8 (The next digit '0' is less than 5)

Relationship Between Significant Figures and Decimal Places

While both concepts deal with precision, they serve different purposes:

  • Significant figures count all meaningful digits in a number, regardless of decimal point position
  • Decimal places count only digits after the decimal point

0.0045678 0.00457 0.005

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Express 45,678 to 2 significant figures.

Solution:

45,678 → 46,000 (The '5' means we round up the '4' to '5')

Question 2

Express 0.007654 to 1 significant figure.

Solution:

0.007654 → 0.008 (The '6' means we round up the '7' to '8')

Question 3

Express 12.3456 to 3 decimal places.

Solution:

12.3456 → 12.346 (The '6' means we round up the '5' to '6')

Question 4

Express 987.654 to 1 decimal place.

Solution:

987.654 → 987.7 (The '5' means we round up the '6' to '7')

Question 5

Express 0.00045678 to 3 significant figures.

Solution:

0.00045678 → 0.000457 (The '7' means we round up the '6' to '7')
