Introduction

Fractions represent parts of a whole or a collection. A fraction has two parts:

\[ \frac{\text{Numerator}}{\text{Denominator}} \]

Numerator:

The number above the fraction bar that represents the parts being considered.

Denominator:

The number below the fraction bar that represents the total number of equal parts.

Types of Fractions

Fractions can be classified into different types:

  • Proper fractions: Numerator is less than denominator (e.g., ½)
  • Improper fractions: Numerator is greater than or equal to denominator (e.g., 5/2)
  • Mixed numbers: Combination of a whole number and a proper fraction (e.g., 1½)
  • Equivalent fractions: Different fractions that represent the same value (e.g., ½ = 2/4)

Converting Between Fraction Types

Improper to Mixed

To convert an improper fraction to a mixed number:

Convert \( \frac{7}{3} \) to a mixed number:

1. Divide numerator by denominator: \( 7 ÷ 3 = 2 \) with remainder 1

2. Write as: \( 2\frac{1}{3} \)

Mixed to Improper

To convert a mixed number to an improper fraction:

Convert \( 2\frac{1}{3} \) to an improper fraction:

1. Multiply whole number by denominator: \( 2 × 3 = 6 \)

2. Add numerator: \( 6 + 1 = 7 \)

3. Write as: \( \frac{7}{3} \)

Basic Operations on Fractions

Addition and Subtraction

To add or subtract fractions:

  1. Find a common denominator (preferably the Least Common Denominator - LCD)
  2. Convert each fraction to an equivalent fraction with the common denominator
  3. Add or subtract the numerators
  4. Keep the denominator the same
  5. Simplify the result if possible

Example: \( \frac{1}{4} + \frac{2}{5} \)

1. LCD of 4 and 5 is 20

2. Convert: \( \frac{1}{4} = \frac{5}{20} \) and \( \frac{2}{5} = \frac{8}{20} \)

3. Add: \( \frac{5}{20} + \frac{8}{20} = \frac{13}{20} \)

Multiplication

To multiply fractions:

  1. Multiply the numerators together
  2. Multiply the denominators together
  3. Simplify the result if possible

Example: \( \frac{2}{3} × \frac{3}{5} \)

1. Multiply numerators: \( 2 × 3 = 6 \)

2. Multiply denominators: \( 3 × 5 = 15 \)

3. Result: \( \frac{6}{15} \) which simplifies to \( \frac{2}{5} \)

Division

To divide fractions:

  1. Multiply by the reciprocal of the second fraction (flip the numerator and denominator)
  2. Follow the rules for multiplication
  3. Simplify the result if possible

Example: \( \frac{3}{4} ÷ \frac{2}{5} \)

1. Reciprocal of \( \frac{2}{5} \) is \( \frac{5}{2} \)

2. Multiply: \( \frac{3}{4} × \frac{5}{2} = \frac{15}{8} \)

3. Convert to mixed number: \( 1\frac{7}{8} \)

Word Problems with Fractions

Problem-Solving Strategy

When solving fraction word problems:

  1. Read the problem carefully and identify what is being asked
  2. Determine which operation(s) to use (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division)
  3. Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions if needed
  4. Perform the calculations
  5. Simplify your answer and convert back to mixed numbers if appropriate
  6. Check that your answer makes sense in the context of the problem

Example Problems

Addition Problem

John ate \( \frac{1}{4} \) of a pizza and Mary ate \( \frac{1}{3} \) of the same pizza. What fraction of the pizza did they eat together?

Solution:

1. LCD of 4 and 3 is 12

2. Convert: \( \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{12} \) and \( \frac{1}{3} = \frac{4}{12} \)

3. Add: \( \frac{3}{12} + \frac{4}{12} = \frac{7}{12} \)

Multiplication Problem

A recipe calls for \( \frac{3}{4} \) cup of flour. If you want to make \( 2\frac{1}{2} \) times the recipe, how much flour will you need?

Solution:

1. Convert \( 2\frac{1}{2} \) to improper fraction: \( \frac{5}{2} \)

2. Multiply: \( \frac{3}{4} × \frac{5}{2} = \frac{15}{8} \)

3. Convert to mixed number: \( 1\frac{7}{8} \) cups

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A tank contains \( 12\frac{1}{2} \) liters of water. If \( 7\frac{3}{4} \) liters are used, how much water remains in the tank?

1. Convert to improper fractions:

\( 12\frac{1}{2} = \frac{25}{2} \) and \( 7\frac{3}{4} = \frac{31}{4} \)

2. LCD of 2 and 4 is 4

3. Convert \( \frac{25}{2} = \frac{50}{4} \)

4. Subtract: \( \frac{50}{4} - \frac{31}{4} = \frac{19}{4} \)

5. Convert to mixed number: \( 4\frac{3}{4} \) liters

Question 2

If \( \frac{2}{5} \) of a number is 24, what is \( \frac{3}{4} \) of the same number?

1. Let the number be \( x \): \( \frac{2}{5}x = 24 \)

2. Solve for \( x \): \( x = 24 × \frac{5}{2} = 60 \)

3. Find \( \frac{3}{4} \) of 60: \( \frac{3}{4} × 60 = 45 \)

Question 3

A piece of wood is \( 8\frac{1}{2} \) meters long. How many \( \frac{3}{4} \) meter pieces can be cut from it?

1. Convert \( 8\frac{1}{2} \) to improper fraction: \( \frac{17}{2} \)

2. Divide: \( \frac{17}{2} ÷ \frac{3}{4} = \frac{17}{2} × \frac{4}{3} = \frac{68}{6} = \frac{34}{3} \)

3. Convert to mixed number: \( 11\frac{1}{3} \)

4. Interpretation: 11 full pieces can be cut with \( \frac{1}{3} \) meter remaining
