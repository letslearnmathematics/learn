Introduction

Probability is a measure of how likely an event is to occur. It helps us predict the chance of something happening, from simple events like flipping a coin to complex situations like weather forecasting.

\[ \text{Probability ranges from } 0 \text{ (impossible) to } 1 \text{ (certain)} \]

Key Probability Terms

Understanding these terms is essential for working with probability:

  • Experiment: A process that produces outcomes (e.g., rolling a die)
  • Outcome: A possible result of an experiment (e.g., getting a 3)
  • Event: A collection of outcomes (e.g., getting an even number)
  • Sample Space: All possible outcomes (e.g., {1,2,3,4,5,6} for a die)

Example: When flipping a coin, the sample space is {Heads, Tails}

Calculating Probability

Basic Probability Formula

The probability of an event A occurring is:

\[ P(A) = \frac{\text{Number of favorable outcomes}}{\text{Total number of possible outcomes}} \]

Example 1: Flipping a coin

Probability of getting Heads:

\[ P(\text{Heads}) = \frac{1}{2} \]

Example 2: Rolling a die

Probability of rolling a 5:

\[ P(5) = \frac{1}{6} \]

Probability of Multiple Events

For compound events (events with multiple outcomes):

\[ P(\text{Event}) = \frac{\text{Number of outcomes in the event}}{\text{Total number of outcomes}} \]

Example: Probability of rolling an even number on a die:

\[ P(\text{Even}) = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2} \]

(Even numbers: 2, 4, 6 → 3 outcomes)

Probability Scale

Probability Description
0 Impossible (will never happen)
0.1 Very unlikely
0.5 Even chance
0.9 Very likely
1 Certain (will always happen)

Expressing Probability

Different Forms

Probability can be expressed in several equivalent forms:

As a Fraction

\[ P(\text{Rolling a 3}) = \frac{1}{6} \]

As a Decimal

\[ \frac{1}{6} \approx 0.1667 \]

As a Percentage

\[ 0.1667 \times 100 \approx 16.67\% \]

As a Ratio

\[ 1:5 \text{ (1 chance for, 5 against)} \]

Conversion Methods

To convert between probability forms:

  1. Fraction to Decimal: Divide numerator by denominator
  2. Decimal to Percentage: Multiply by 100 and add %
  3. Fraction to Ratio:
    • Numerator becomes first number
    • Denominator minus numerator becomes second number

Example: Convert \(\frac{2}{5}\) to all forms:

Decimal: \(2 ÷ 5 = 0.4\)

Percentage: \(0.4 × 100 = 40\%\)

Ratio: \(2:3\) (2 chances for, 3 against)

Simplifying Probabilities

Always express probabilities in simplest form:

Example: A bag has 4 red and 8 blue marbles.

Probability of red:

\[ \frac{4}{12} = \frac{1}{3} \]

Applications

Practical Examples

Probability is used in many real-world situations:

Weather Forecast

A 30% chance of rain means:

\[ P(\text{Rain}) = 0.3 = \frac{3}{10} = 3:7 \]

Games of Chance

Probability of drawing an Ace from a deck:

\[ \frac{4}{52} = \frac{1}{13} \approx 7.69\% \]

Quality Control

If 5 out of 100 items are defective:

\[ P(\text{Defective}) = \frac{5}{100} = 0.05 = 5\% \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A spinner has 8 equal sections (3 red, 2 blue, 3 green). What is P(blue) as a fraction, decimal, and percentage?

\[ \text{Fraction: } \frac{2}{8} = \frac{1}{4} \] \[ \text{Decimal: } 0.25 \] \[ \text{Percentage: } 25\% \]

Question 2

A jar has 5 white, 7 black, and 3 yellow marbles. Find P(not white) as a ratio.

Total marbles: 5 + 7 + 3 = 15

Non-white marbles: 7 + 3 = 10

\[ P(\text{not white}) = \frac{10}{15} = \frac{2}{3} \]

Ratio: 2:1 (2 chances for, 1 against)

Question 3

Convert 0.125 to a fraction and percentage probability.

\[ \text{Fraction: } \frac{125}{1000} = \frac{1}{8} \] \[ \text{Percentage: } 12.5\% \]

Question 4

A die is rolled. Find P(prime number) in all forms.

Prime numbers on die: 2, 3, 5 (3 outcomes)

\[ \text{Fraction: } \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2} \] \[ \text{Decimal: } 0.5 \] \[ \text{Percentage: } 50\% \] \[ \text{Ratio: } 1:1 \]

Question 5

A weather report says there's a 0.4 chance of snow. Express this as a fraction and ratio.

\[ \text{Fraction: } \frac{4}{10} = \frac{2}{5} \] \[ \text{Ratio: } 2:3 \]

Question 6

In a class of 30 students, 18 are girls. What is P(boy) as a percentage?

Number of boys: 30 - 18 = 12

\[ P(\text{boy}) = \frac{12}{30} = 0.4 = 40\% \]
