Introduction

Probability helps us understand how likely something is to happen. We can describe events using words like:

  • Impossible: Will never happen (0% chance)
  • Possible: Might happen (more than 0% but less than 100% chance)
  • Certain: Will definitely happen (100% chance)

Understanding Likelihood

Let's look at some examples from everyday life:

  1. Impossible: "The dog will fly tomorrow" - Dogs cannot fly, so this is impossible.
  2. Possible: "Someone in the class would be a teacher in the future" - This could happen.
  3. Certain: "Ghana will still be an African country tomorrow" - This is definitely true.

Event Examples

Classifying Events

Here are more examples to help understand these probability terms:

Impossible Events

  • The sun will rise in the west tomorrow
  • A human will grow wings and fly
  • Water will flow uphill without any help

Possible Events

  • It will rain tomorrow
  • You will get an A on your next math test
  • Your favorite team will win their next match

Certain Events

  • The sun will rise tomorrow
  • You will grow older each year
  • Water will freeze at 0Â°C under normal conditions

Thinking About Probability

Sometimes whether an event is impossible, possible, or certain depends on the situation:

Example: "A new-born baby will be a girl"

  • For any single birth: Possible (could be boy or girl)
  • For a specific baby already known to be a girl: Certain
  • For a family that only has boys: Impossible (if we know all children are boys)

Discussion: Is this impossible, possible, or certain? Why?

Event D: It will rain in Winneba in the first week of January

Discussion: Is this impossible, possible, or certain? Why?

Class Discussion

After your group discussion, let's share answers as a class:

  1. Coin lands heads up: Possible (it could be heads or tails)
  2. Day after Monday is Tuesday: Certain (this is how the week always works)
  3. New-born baby is a girl: Possible (could be boy or girl)
  4. Rain in Winneba in first week of January: Possible (it might rain, but not guaranteed)

Real-world Applications

Everyday Probability

Understanding probability helps us in daily life:

Weather Forecasts

When the weather report says "60% chance of rain," they're using probability to help you decide whether to carry an umbrella.

Games and Sports

Probability helps us understand our chances in games like dice, cards, or predicting sports outcomes.

Making Decisions

We use probability when deciding things like: "Should I study for the test?" (higher chance of good grade if you do).

Critical Thinking

Can you think of other situations where we use probability in daily life?

  • Choosing which line to join at the market
  • Deciding when to cross the street
  • Guessing how much food to prepare for a party
