Introduction

The mean, often called the average, is one of the most important measures of central tendency in statistics. It represents the central value of a data set.

\[ \text{Mean} = \frac{\text{Sum of all values}}{\text{Number of values}} \]

Understanding the Mean

The mean gives us a single value that summarizes an entire data set. It's useful for:

  • Comparing different data sets
  • Finding a typical value in a data set
  • Making predictions based on data

Example: If 5 students scored 70, 80, 85, 90, and 95 on a test, their mean score is:

\[ \frac{70 + 80 + 85 + 90 + 95}{5} = \frac{420}{5} = 84 \]

Basic Mean Calculation

Calculating Mean for Simple Data Sets

To find the mean of a simple data set:

  1. Add up all the values in the data set
  2. Count how many values there are
  3. Divide the sum by the count

Example 1: Test Scores

Find the mean of these test scores: 85, 90, 78, 92, 88

\[ \text{Sum} = 85 + 90 + 78 + 92 + 88 = 433 \] \[ \text{Count} = 5 \] \[ \text{Mean} = \frac{433}{5} = 86.6 \]

Example 2: Daily Temperatures

Find the mean daily temperature for a week: 28°C, 30°C, 29°C, 31°C, 27°C, 30°C, 29°C

\[ \text{Sum} = 28 + 30 + 29 + 31 + 27 + 30 + 29 = 204 \] \[ \text{Count} = 7 \] \[ \text{Mean} = \frac{204}{7} \approx 29.14°C \]

Mean from Frequency Table

Calculating Mean with Frequency Tables

When data is presented in a frequency table:

  1. Multiply each value by its frequency
  2. Add all these products to get the total sum
  3. Add all frequencies to get the total count
  4. Divide the total sum by the total count

Example: Calculate the mean from this frequency table:

Value (x) Frequency (f)
5 3
6 5
7 4
8 2

Solution:

\[ \text{Total sum} = (5×3) + (6×5) + (7×4) + (8×2) = 15 + 30 + 28 + 16 = 89 \] \[ \text{Total count} = 3 + 5 + 4 + 2 = 14 \] \[ \text{Mean} = \frac{89}{14} \approx 6.36 \]

Frequency Table Practice

Calculate the mean from this frequency table showing student absences:

Days Absent (x) Number of Students (f)
0 12
1 8
2 5
3 3
4 2
\[ \text{Total sum} = (0×12) + (1×8) + (2×5) + (3×3) + (4×2) = 0 + 8 + 10 + 9 + 8 = 35 \] \[ \text{Total count} = 12 + 8 + 5 + 3 + 2 = 30 \] \[ \text{Mean} = \frac{35}{30} \approx 1.17 \text{ days} \]

Problem Solving

Solving Problems with Mean

The mean can be used to solve various real-world problems. Here are some common types:

Finding Missing Values

If the mean of 5 numbers is 20, and four numbers are 18, 22, 19, and 21, find the fifth number.

Let the fifth number be x:

\[ \frac{18 + 22 + 19 + 21 + x}{5} = 20 \] \[ \frac{80 + x}{5} = 20 \] \[ 80 + x = 100 \] \[ x = 20 \]

Combined Means

Class A has 30 students with mean score of 75. Class B has 20 students with mean score of 80. What is the combined mean?

Total for Class A = 30 × 75 = 2250

Total for Class B = 20 × 80 = 1600

Combined total = 2250 + 1600 = 3850

Total students = 30 + 20 = 50

\[ \text{Combined mean} = \frac{3850}{50} = 77 \]

Practical Application

A shopkeeper wants the mean daily sales for 6 days to be at least ₵500. In the first 5 days, sales were ₵480, ₵520, ₵490, ₵510, and ₵530. What must sales be on the 6th day?

Let x be the 6th day sales:

\[ \frac{480 + 520 + 490 + 510 + 530 + x}{6} \geq 500 \] \[ \frac{2530 + x}{6} \geq 500 \] \[ 2530 + x \geq 3000 \] \[ x \geq 470 \]

The shopkeeper must make at least ₵470 on the 6th day.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Calculate the mean of these numbers: 12, 15, 18, 21, 24

\[ \text{Sum} = 12 + 15 + 18 + 21 + 24 = 90 \] \[ \text{Count} = 5 \] \[ \text{Mean} = \frac{90}{5} = 18 \]

Question 2

Find the mean from this frequency table:

Value (x) Frequency (f)
10 4
15 6
20 5
\[ \text{Total sum} = (10×4) + (15×6) + (20×5) = 40 + 90 + 100 = 230 \] \[ \text{Total count} = 4 + 6 + 5 = 15 \] \[ \text{Mean} = \frac{230}{15} \approx 15.33 \]

Question 3

The mean of four numbers is 25. Three numbers are 20, 30, and 25. Find the fourth number.

Let x be the fourth number:

\[ \frac{20 + 30 + 25 + x}{4} = 25 \] \[ \frac{75 + x}{4} = 25 \] \[ 75 + x = 100 \] \[ x = 25 \]

Question 4

A class has 20 students with mean height of 150cm. Another class has 30 students with mean height of 155cm. Find the combined mean height.

Total height first class = 20 × 150 = 3000cm

Total height second class = 30 × 155 = 4650cm

Combined total = 3000 + 4650 = 7650cm

Total students = 20 + 30 = 50

\[ \text{Combined mean} = \frac{7650}{50} = 153\text{cm} \]

Question 5

The mean of five numbers is 12. If one number is removed, the mean becomes 11. What number was removed?

Total of five numbers = 5 × 12 = 60

Total of four numbers = 4 × 11 = 44

Number removed = 60 - 44 = 16

Question 6

Calculate the mean of these temperatures: 22°C, 24°C, 21°C, 23°C, 25°C, 22°C, 20°C

\[ \text{Sum} = 22 + 24 + 21 + 23 + 25 + 22 + 20 = 157 \] \[ \text{Count} = 7 \] \[ \text{Mean} = \frac{157}{7} \approx 22.43°C \]
