Introduction

A questionnaire is a research tool consisting of a series of questions used to collect information from respondents. In mathematics, we use questionnaires to gather data that we can analyze to answer questions and solve problems.

Key purposes of questionnaires:

  • Collect data from a specific group of people
  • Gather information about opinions, behaviors, or characteristics
  • Provide data for mathematical analysis

Types of Questions

Questionnaires typically contain different types of questions:

Question Type Example
Closed-ended (Multiple choice) Do you prefer math or science? (a) Math (b) Science
Rating scale Rate your math skills from 1 (poor) to 5 (excellent)
Open-ended What do you find most challenging about math?

Designing Questionnaires

Creating Effective Questions

Follow these guidelines when designing questionnaire questions:

  1. Keep questions clear and simple
  2. Avoid leading or biased questions
  3. Use appropriate question types for the information needed
  4. Keep the questionnaire short and focused
  5. Include instructions where needed

Good Question Example

"How many hours per week do you spend on math homework?"

Options: (a) Less than 1 hour (b) 1-2 hours (c) 3-4 hours (d) More than 4 hours

Poor Question Example

"Don't you think math is the most important subject?" (This is leading)

Sample Questionnaire

Here's a sample questionnaire about study habits:

Student Study Habits Survey

1. What is your favorite subject? _________________

2. How many hours per day do you study math?

( ) Less than 30 minutes
( ) 30-60 minutes
( ) 1-2 hours
( ) More than 2 hours

3. Where do you usually do your homework?

( ) At home
( ) At school
( ) At a library
( ) Other: _________

4. Rate your math skills: (circle one)

1 (Poor) 2 3 4 5 (Excellent)

Administering Surveys

Conducting the Survey

When administering your questionnaire:

Select Your Sample

Choose a small group of classmates (5-10 students) to survey

Give Clear Instructions

Explain the purpose and how to complete the form

Ensure Anonymity

Let respondents know their answers are confidential

Collect Completed Forms

Gather all responses for analysis

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Design a questionnaire with 3 questions about students' favorite foods.

Sample solution:

1. What is your favorite type of food? (a) Local (b) Continental (c) Chinese (d) Other ______

2. How often do you eat your favorite food? (a) Daily (b) Weekly (c) Monthly (d) Rarely

3. Where do you usually eat your favorite food? (a) Home (b) School (c) Restaurant (d) Other ______

Question 2

You surveyed 8 classmates about their preferred school clubs. 3 chose Drama, 2 chose Science, 1 chose Debate, and 2 chose Sports. Create a frequency table for this data.

Club Number of Students
Drama 3
Science 2
Debate 1
Sports 2

Question 3

What's wrong with this survey question: "Don't you agree that math is the most wonderful subject?"

This is a leading question that suggests a preferred answer ("yes"). It should be neutral like: "How do you feel about math?" with rating options.

Question 4

You asked 6 classmates how many siblings they have. The answers were: 2, 1, 3, 2, 4, 2. Organize this data in a frequency table.

Number of Siblings Number of Students
1 1
2 3
3 1
4 1

Question 5

Why is it important to keep questionnaires anonymous when asking about personal opinions?

Anonymity helps respondents answer honestly without fear of judgment, which leads to more accurate data collection.
