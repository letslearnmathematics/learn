Introduction

Word problems with fractions help us solve real-life situations involving parts of wholes. To solve these problems, we need to understand what the question is asking and then translate it into mathematical operations with fractions.

Key Steps:

  1. Read the problem carefully
  2. Identify what is being asked
  3. Determine which fractions are involved
  4. Decide whether to add or subtract
  5. Find a common denominator if needed
  6. Perform the calculation
  7. Simplify your answer

Understanding Fraction Word Problems

When solving fraction word problems, pay attention to these key words:

  • Addition words: total, sum, combined, altogether, in all
  • Subtraction words: difference, remaining, left, more than, less than

Example: "Mary ate \(\frac{1}{4}\) of the pizza and John ate \(\frac{1}{3}\). What fraction of the pizza was eaten altogether?"

This is an addition problem: \(\frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{3}\)

Addition Problems

Solving Addition Problems

To add fractions in word problems:

  1. Identify the fractions to be added
  2. Find a common denominator
  3. Convert each fraction to have this denominator
  4. Add the numerators
  5. Simplify if possible

Example 1: Same Denominator

Sarah walked \(\frac{2}{5}\) km to school and then \(\frac{1}{5}\) km to the store. How far did she walk in total?

\[ \frac{2}{5} + \frac{1}{5} = \frac{2+1}{5} = \frac{3}{5} \text{ km} \]

Example 2: Different Denominators

A recipe calls for \(\frac{1}{2}\) cup of flour and \(\frac{1}{3}\) cup of sugar. What is the total amount of dry ingredients?

Find common denominator (6):

\[ \frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{6}, \frac{1}{3} = \frac{2}{6} \] \[ \frac{3}{6} + \frac{2}{6} = \frac{5}{6} \text{ cup} \]

Practice Addition Problems

Problem 1: Tom drank \(\frac{1}{4}\) liter of milk in the morning and \(\frac{3}{8}\) liter in the evening. How much milk did he drink in total?

Common denominator is 8:

\[ \frac{1}{4} = \frac{2}{8} \] \[ \frac{2}{8} + \frac{3}{8} = \frac{5}{8} \text{ liter} \]

Problem 2: A farmer planted \(\frac{2}{7}\) of his field with corn and \(\frac{3}{7}\) with wheat. What fraction of the field is planted?

\[ \frac{2}{7} + \frac{3}{7} = \frac{5}{7} \text{ of the field} \]

Subtraction Problems

Solving Subtraction Problems

To subtract fractions in word problems:

  1. Identify the fractions involved
  2. Find a common denominator
  3. Convert each fraction to have this denominator
  4. Subtract the numerators
  5. Simplify if possible

Example 1: Same Denominator

A tank had \(\frac{7}{8}\) gallons of water. \(\frac{3}{8}\) gallons were used. How much water remains?

\[ \frac{7}{8} - \frac{3}{8} = \frac{4}{8} = \frac{1}{2} \text{ gallon} \]

Example 2: Different Denominators

Mary had \(\frac{3}{4}\) meter of ribbon. She used \(\frac{1}{3}\) meter for a project. How much ribbon is left?

Common denominator (12):

\[ \frac{3}{4} = \frac{9}{12}, \frac{1}{3} = \frac{4}{12} \] \[ \frac{9}{12} - \frac{4}{12} = \frac{5}{12} \text{ meter} \]

Practice Subtraction Problems

Problem 1: A cake was cut into 6 equal slices. After dinner, \(\frac{5}{6}\) of the cake remained. The next day, \(\frac{1}{2}\) was eaten. How much cake is left now?

Common denominator is 6:

\[ \frac{5}{6} - \frac{1}{2} = \frac{5}{6} - \frac{3}{6} = \frac{2}{6} = \frac{1}{3} \text{ of the cake} \]

Problem 2: A bottle contained \(\frac{7}{8}\) liter of juice. Sam drank \(\frac{1}{4}\) liter. How much juice remains?

Common denominator is 8:

\[ \frac{7}{8} - \frac{1}{4} = \frac{7}{8} - \frac{2}{8} = \frac{5}{8} \text{ liter} \]

Mixed Problems

Combined Addition and Subtraction

Some word problems require both addition and subtraction operations. Read carefully to determine the correct sequence of operations.

Example 1

Lisa had \(\frac{5}{6}\) cup of flour. She used \(\frac{1}{3}\) cup for cookies and \(\frac{1}{4}\) cup for pancakes. How much flour is left?

First add what was used, then subtract from total:

Common denominator is 12:

\[ \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{4}{12} + \frac{3}{12} = \frac{7}{12} \] \[ \frac{5}{6} - \frac{7}{12} = \frac{10}{12} - \frac{7}{12} = \frac{3}{12} = \frac{1}{4} \text{ cup} \]

Example 2

A tank was \(\frac{2}{3}\) full. After adding \(\frac{1}{6}\) more, \(\frac{1}{2}\) was used. What fraction of the tank remains?

First add, then subtract:

Common denominator is 6:

\[ \frac{2}{3} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{4}{6} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{5}{6} \] \[ \frac{5}{6} - \frac{1}{2} = \frac{5}{6} - \frac{3}{6} = \frac{2}{6} = \frac{1}{3} \text{ of the tank} \]

Practice Mixed Problems

Problem 1: A farmer harvested \(\frac{3}{5}\) of his crops on Monday and \(\frac{1}{4}\) on Tuesday. What fraction of crops remains to be harvested?

First add harvested portions:

Common denominator is 20:

\[ \frac{3}{5} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{12}{20} + \frac{5}{20} = \frac{17}{20} \]

Then subtract from whole:

\[ 1 - \frac{17}{20} = \frac{20}{20} - \frac{17}{20} = \frac{3}{20} \text{ remaining} \]

Problem 2: A rope was \(\frac{7}{8}\) meters long. \(\frac{1}{2}\) meter was cut off, then \(\frac{1}{4}\) meter was added. What is the new length?

First subtract, then add:

Common denominator is 8:

\[ \frac{7}{8} - \frac{1}{2} = \frac{7}{8} - \frac{4}{8} = \frac{3}{8} \] \[ \frac{3}{8} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{8} + \frac{2}{8} = \frac{5}{8} \text{ meter} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Emma read \(\frac{1}{3}\) of her book on Monday and \(\frac{1}{4}\) on Tuesday. What fraction of the book has she read?

Common denominator is 12:

\[ \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{4}{12} + \frac{3}{12} = \frac{7}{12} \]

Question 2

A water tank was \(\frac{5}{6}\) full. After some was used, \(\frac{1}{3}\) remained. What fraction was used?

Common denominator is 6:

\[ \frac{5}{6} - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{5}{6} - \frac{2}{6} = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2} \]

Question 3

John walked \(\frac{3}{4}\) km to school and then \(\frac{2}{5}\) km to the library. What was his total distance walked?

Common denominator is 20:

\[ \frac{3}{4} + \frac{2}{5} = \frac{15}{20} + \frac{8}{20} = \frac{23}{20} = 1\frac{3}{20} \text{ km} \]

Question 4

A pizza was divided into 8 slices. Mary ate 3 slices and John ate 2 slices. What fraction of the pizza was left?

Total eaten: \(\frac{3}{8} + \frac{2}{8} = \frac{5}{8}\)

Left: \(1 - \frac{5}{8} = \frac{3}{8}\)

Question 5

A recipe requires \(\frac{1}{2}\) cup sugar and \(\frac{1}{3}\) cup flour. How much more sugar than flour is needed?

Common denominator is 6:

\[ \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{3}{6} - \frac{2}{6} = \frac{1}{6} \text{ cup} \]

Question 6

A tank had \(\frac{3}{4}\) liter of oil. \(\frac{1}{2}\) liter was added, then \(\frac{1}{3}\) liter was used. How much oil is now in the tank?

First add: \(\frac{3}{4} + \frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{4} + \frac{2}{4} = \frac{5}{4}\)

Then subtract: \(\frac{5}{4} - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{15}{12} - \frac{4}{12} = \frac{11}{12}\) liter
