Introduction

Multiplying fractions involves different approaches depending on what you're multiplying. We'll learn how to:

  • Multiply a whole number by a fraction (read as "times")
  • Multiply a fraction by a whole number (read as "of")
  • Multiply a fraction by another fraction (read as "of")
  • Calculate percentages of numbers

Key Concepts

A fraction represents a part of a whole. When multiplying fractions:

\[ \frac{\text{Numerator}}{\text{Denominator}} \]
  • Numerator: The number above the fraction bar
  • Denominator: The number below the fraction bar
  • Product: The result of multiplication

Whole Number × Fraction

Multiplication as "Times"

When multiplying a whole number by a fraction, we read it as "times":

  1. Multiply the whole number by the numerator
  2. Keep the same denominator
  3. Simplify if possible

Example 1: \(3 \times \frac{2}{5}\)

\[ 3 \times \frac{2}{5} = \frac{3 \times 2}{5} = \frac{6}{5} = 1\frac{1}{5} \]

Example 2: \(4 \times \frac{3}{8}\)

\[ 4 \times \frac{3}{8} = \frac{4 \times 3}{8} = \frac{12}{8} = \frac{3}{2} = 1\frac{1}{2} \]

Understanding the Concept

Multiplying a whole number by a fraction means taking that fraction multiple times:

\[ 3 \times \frac{2}{5} = \frac{2}{5} + \frac{2}{5} + \frac{2}{5} = \frac{6}{5} \]

This is why we multiply the numerator by the whole number.

Fraction × Whole Number

Multiplication as "Of"

When multiplying a fraction by a whole number, we read it as "of":

  1. Convert the whole number to a fraction (put it over 1)
  2. Multiply numerators together
  3. Multiply denominators together
  4. Simplify the result

Example 1: \(\frac{2}{3} \times 6\)

\[ \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{6}{1} = \frac{2 \times 6}{3 \times 1} = \frac{12}{3} = 4 \]

This means "two-thirds of six is four"

Example 2: \(\frac{3}{4} \times 8\)

\[ \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{8}{1} = \frac{3 \times 8}{4 \times 1} = \frac{24}{4} = 6 \]

This means "three-fourths of eight is six"

Simplifying Before Multiplying

You can simplify before multiplying to make calculations easier:

Example: \(\frac{3}{4} \times 8\)

Divide 4 (denominator) and 8 (whole number) by 4:

\[ \frac{3}{4} \times 8 = \frac{3}{1} \times 2 = 6 \]

Fraction × Fraction

Multiplying Two Fractions

To multiply two fractions (read as "of"):

  1. Multiply the numerators together
  2. Multiply the denominators together
  3. Simplify the resulting fraction

Example 1: \(\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{3}{4}\)

\[ \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{1 \times 3}{2 \times 4} = \frac{3}{8} \]

This means "half of three-fourths is three-eighths"

Example 2: \(\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{5}{7}\)

\[ \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{5}{7} = \frac{2 \times 5}{3 \times 7} = \frac{10}{21} \]

Cross-Simplification

Simplify before multiplying by canceling common factors:

Example: \(\frac{3}{4} \times \frac{8}{9}\)

Simplify 4 and 8 (divide by 4), and 3 and 9 (divide by 3):

\[ \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{8}{9} = \frac{1}{1} \times \frac{2}{3} = \frac{2}{3} \]

Calculating Percentages

Percentages as Fractions

Percent means "per hundred", so percentages are fractions with denominator 100:

\[ 25\% = \frac{25}{100} = \frac{1}{4} \]

To calculate a percentage of a number:

  1. Convert the percentage to a fraction or decimal
  2. Multiply by the number

Example 1: 20% of 50

\[ 20\% = \frac{20}{100} = \frac{1}{5} \] \[ \frac{1}{5} \times 50 = 10 \]

Example 2: 75% of 120

\[ 75\% = \frac{75}{100} = \frac{3}{4} \] \[ \frac{3}{4} \times 120 = 90 \]

Percentage Shortcuts

Some percentages have easy fraction equivalents:

Percentage Fraction
50% \(\frac{1}{2}\)
25% \(\frac{1}{4}\)
75% \(\frac{3}{4}\)
20% \(\frac{1}{5}\)
10% \(\frac{1}{10}\)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Calculate \(5 \times \frac{3}{4}\)

\[ 5 \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{15}{4} = 3\frac{3}{4} \]

Question 2

Find \(\frac{2}{3}\) of 15

\[ \frac{2}{3} \times 15 = \frac{30}{3} = 10 \]

Question 3

Multiply \(\frac{3}{5} \times \frac{2}{7}\)

\[ \frac{3}{5} \times \frac{2}{7} = \frac{6}{35} \]

Question 4

Calculate 30% of 80

\[ 30\% = \frac{30}{100} = \frac{3}{10} \] \[ \frac{3}{10} \times 80 = 24 \]

Question 5

Simplify before multiplying: \(\frac{4}{9} \times \frac{3}{8}\)

Simplify 4 and 8 (divide by 4), and 3 and 9 (divide by 3):

\[ \frac{4}{9} \times \frac{3}{8} = \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{6} \]

Question 6

A recipe calls for \(\frac{3}{4}\) cup of flour. How much is needed for 5 batches?

\[ 5 \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{15}{4} = 3\frac{3}{4} \text{ cups} \]
