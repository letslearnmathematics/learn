Introduction

Finding fractions of quantities is an essential skill in mathematics that helps us solve real-world problems. It involves calculating a portion of a whole number or quantity.

\[ \text{To find } \frac{a}{b} \text{ of a quantity } Q: \frac{a}{b} \times Q \]

Understanding the Concept

A fraction represents a part of a whole. When we find a fraction of a quantity, we're calculating what that part would be if the whole quantity were divided according to the fraction.

\[ \text{Example: } \frac{3}{4} \text{ of 20 means } \frac{3}{4} \times 20 = 15 \]
  • Numerator: The number of parts we want (top number)
  • Denominator: How many equal parts the whole is divided into (bottom number)

Multiplying by Fractions

Multiplying Fractions by Quantities

Multiplying a quantity by a fraction is similar to multiplying by a whole number, but with an additional division step:

  1. Multiply the quantity by the numerator
  2. Divide the result by the denominator

Example 1: \(\frac{2}{3}\) of 120

\[ \frac{2}{3} \times 120 = \frac{2 \times 120}{3} = \frac{240}{3} = 80 \]

Example 2: \(\frac{5}{8}\) of 64

\[ \frac{5}{8} \times 64 = \frac{5 \times 64}{8} = \frac{320}{8} = 40 \]

Shortcut Method

When possible, simplify before multiplying:

Example: \(\frac{3}{4}\) of 28

\[ \frac{3}{4} \times 28 = 3 \times \left(\frac{28}{4}\right) = 3 \times 7 = 21 \]

This method is often faster and reduces the chance of calculation errors.

Word Problems

Solving Real-World Problems

Word problems involving fractions of quantities require careful reading and step-by-step solving:

Problem 1: Class Composition

There are 132 learners in a class. If \(\frac{2}{3}\) of the learners are girls, how many boys are in the class?

Solution:

1. Find number of girls: \(\frac{2}{3} \times 132 = 88\)

2. Subtract from total to find boys: \(132 - 88 = 44\)

Answer: There are 44 boys in the class.

Problem 2: Shopping Discount

A shirt costs \$45. If you get \(\frac{1}{5}\) off the price, what is the discount amount?

Solution:

\[ \frac{1}{5} \times 45 = 9 \]

Answer: The discount is \$9.

Problem 3: Recipe Adjustment

A recipe requires \(\frac{3}{4}\) cup of flour. If you're making half the recipe, how much flour do you need?

Solution:

\[ \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{3}{8} \text{ cup} \]

Answer: You need \(\frac{3}{8}\) cup of flour.

Problem-Solving Strategy

Follow these steps to solve fraction word problems:

  1. Identify the total quantity
  2. Determine what fraction of the quantity is being asked for
  3. Multiply the quantity by the fraction
  4. If needed, perform additional operations (like subtraction in the class problem)
  5. State your final answer with proper units

Pie Chart Representation

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Find \(\frac{3}{4}\) of 80

\[ \frac{3}{4} \times 80 = 60 \]

Question 2

A school has 720 students. If \(\frac{5}{8}\) are boys, how many girls are there?

Number of boys: \(\frac{5}{8} \times 720 = 450\)

Number of girls: \(720 - 450 = 270\)

Question 3

Calculate \(\frac{7}{10}\) of 150 meters

\[ \frac{7}{10} \times 150 = 105 \text{ meters} \]

Question 4

In a basket of 96 fruits, \(\frac{3}{8}\) are mangoes. How many fruits are not mangoes?

Mangoes: \(\frac{3}{8} \times 96 = 36\)

Other fruits: \(96 - 36 = 60\)

Question 5

A tank holds 240 liters of water. If \(\frac{5}{12}\) of the water is used, how much remains?

Water used: \(\frac{5}{12} \times 240 = 100\) liters

Water remaining: \(240 - 100 = 140\) liters

Question 6

In a class election, Sarah received \(\frac{3}{5}\) of 120 votes cast. How many votes did she get?

\[ \frac{3}{5} \times 120 = 72 \text{ votes} \]
