Introduction

Dividing fractions is an important mathematical operation that helps us solve problems where we need to split quantities into equal parts. Understanding fraction division is essential for many real-world applications.

\[ \text{Examples: } \frac{1}{2} \div \frac{1}{4}, \frac{3}{4} \div \frac{2}{5}, 2 \div \frac{1}{3} \]

Key Concepts

Before dividing fractions, it's important to understand these concepts:

  • Reciprocal: The reciprocal of a fraction is obtained by flipping its numerator and denominator
  • Whole numbers as fractions: Any whole number can be written as a fraction with denominator 1
  • Simplifying fractions: Reducing fractions to their simplest form

Examples:

Reciprocal of \(\frac{3}{4}\) is \(\frac{4}{3}\)

5 can be written as \(\frac{5}{1}\)

Dividing Fractions

The Division Rule

To divide by a fraction:

  1. Find the reciprocal of the divisor (the fraction you're dividing by)
  2. Multiply the dividend (the first fraction) by this reciprocal
  3. Simplify the resulting fraction if possible

Example 1: \(\frac{1}{2} \div \frac{1}{4}\)

\[ \frac{1}{2} \div \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{4}{1} = \frac{4}{2} = 2 \]

Example 2: \(\frac{3}{4} \div \frac{2}{5}\)

\[ \frac{3}{4} \div \frac{2}{5} = \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{5}{2} = \frac{15}{8} = 1\frac{7}{8} \]

Dividing Whole Numbers by Fractions

When dividing a whole number by a fraction:

  1. Write the whole number as a fraction (with denominator 1)
  2. Follow the same division rule (multiply by reciprocal)

Example: \(3 \div \frac{1}{2}\)

\[ 3 \div \frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{1} \times \frac{2}{1} = \frac{6}{1} = 6 \]

Word Problems

Solving Word Problems

To solve word problems involving division of fractions:

  1. Read the problem carefully and identify what is being asked
  2. Determine which numbers represent the dividend and divisor
  3. Set up the division problem
  4. Solve using the fraction division rule
  5. Interpret the answer in the context of the problem

Example 1: Sharing Pizza

If a pizza is cut into \(\frac{1}{4}\) slices, how many slices can you get from \(\frac{3}{4}\) of a pizza?

\[ \frac{3}{4} \div \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{4}{1} = \frac{12}{4} = 3 \text{ slices} \]

Example 2: Ribbon Length

How many \(\frac{3}{8}\) meter pieces can be cut from a \(\frac{9}{4}\) meter ribbon?

\[ \frac{9}{4} \div \frac{3}{8} = \frac{9}{4} \times \frac{8}{3} = \frac{72}{12} = 6 \text{ pieces} \]

Problem Solving Strategies

Helpful strategies for fraction division word problems:

  • Draw diagrams to visualize the problem
  • Look for key words like "how many", "per", or "each"
  • Check if your answer makes sense in the real-world context
  • Estimate before calculating to check reasonableness

Example: If 1 liter of paint covers \(\frac{1}{6}\) of a wall, how many liters are needed for 2 walls?

\[ 2 \div \frac{1}{6} = 2 \times \frac{6}{1} = 12 \text{ liters} \]

Applications

Practical Examples

Fraction division is used in many real-world situations:

Cooking Measurements

If a recipe calls for \(\frac{1}{2}\) cup of flour per batch, how many batches can you make with \(3\frac{1}{2}\) cups?

\[ 3\frac{1}{2} \div \frac{1}{2} = \frac{7}{2} \times \frac{2}{1} = 7 \text{ batches} \]

Construction

How many \(\frac{3}{4}\) foot boards can be cut from a 12 foot plank?

\[ 12 \div \frac{3}{4} = \frac{12}{1} \times \frac{4}{3} = \frac{48}{3} = 16 \text{ boards} \]

Medicine Dosage

If a medicine dosage is \(\frac{1}{8}\) teaspoon per day, how many days will a \(\frac{3}{4}\) teaspoon bottle last?

\[ \frac{3}{4} \div \frac{1}{8} = \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{8}{1} = \frac{24}{4} = 6 \text{ days} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Calculate: \(\frac{2}{3} \div \frac{4}{5}\)

\[ \frac{2}{3} \div \frac{4}{5} = \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{5}{4} = \frac{10}{12} = \frac{5}{6} \]

Question 2

Solve: \(5 \div \frac{2}{3}\)

\[ 5 \div \frac{2}{3} = \frac{5}{1} \times \frac{3}{2} = \frac{15}{2} = 7\frac{1}{2} \]

Question 3

How many \(\frac{1}{4}\) cup servings are in \(\frac{3}{2}\) cups of yogurt?

\[ \frac{3}{2} \div \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{2} \times \frac{4}{1} = \frac{12}{2} = 6 \text{ servings} \]

Question 4

A car travels \(\frac{1}{5}\) mile per minute. How long will it take to travel 12 miles?

\[ 12 \div \frac{1}{5} = \frac{12}{1} \times \frac{5}{1} = 60 \text{ minutes} \]

Question 5

Calculate: \(\frac{7}{8} \div \frac{3}{4}\)

\[ \frac{7}{8} \div \frac{3}{4} = \frac{7}{8} \times \frac{4}{3} = \frac{28}{24} = \frac{7}{6} = 1\frac{1}{6} \]

Question 6

A recipe requires \(\frac{3}{4}\) pound of flour. How many recipes can be made with \(4\frac{1}{2}\) pounds of flour?

\[ 4\frac{1}{2} \div \frac{3}{4} = \frac{9}{2} \times \frac{4}{3} = \frac{36}{6} = 6 \text{ recipes} \]
