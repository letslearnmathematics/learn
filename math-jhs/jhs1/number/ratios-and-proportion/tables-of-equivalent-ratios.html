Introduction

Equivalent ratios are ratios that express the same relationship between quantities. When two ratios are equivalent, they can be written as equal fractions when simplified.

\[ \text{Example: } \frac{2}{3} \text{ and } \frac{4}{6} \text{ are equivalent ratios} \]

Key Concepts

A ratio compares two quantities by division. When quantities are proportional, their ratios remain constant even as the quantities change.

  • Ratio: A comparison of two quantities (e.g., 2:3 or \(\frac{2}{3}\))
  • Equivalent Ratios: Ratios that have the same value when simplified
  • Proportional Quantities: Quantities that maintain the same ratio

Example: If 2 pencils cost \$1, then 4 pencils cost \$2 (same ratio of 2:1)

Understanding Ratios

Writing Ratios as Fractions

Ratios can be written in three forms:

  1. Using the word "to" (2 to 3)
  2. Using a colon (2:3)
  3. As a fraction (\(\frac{2}{3}\))

Example 1: Simple Ratio

\[ \text{The ratio of boys to girls is 3 to 4} \] \[ \text{Can be written as } 3:4 \text{ or } \frac{3}{4} \]

Example 2: Equivalent Ratios

\[ \frac{3}{4} = \frac{6}{8} = \frac{9}{12} \]

All these ratios are equivalent

Finding Equivalent Ratios

To find equivalent ratios, multiply or divide both terms of the ratio by the same non-zero number:

\begin{eqnarray*} \frac{2}{3} &=& \frac{2 \times 2}{3 \times 2} = \frac{4}{6} \\ \frac{5}{10} &=& \frac{5 \div 5}{10 \div 5} = \frac{1}{2} \\ \end{eqnarray*}

Simplifying Ratios

To simplify a ratio, divide both terms by their greatest common factor (GCF):

Example: Simplify \(\frac{8}{12}\):

GCF of 8 and 12 is 4:

\[ \frac{8 \div 4}{12 \div 4} = \frac{2}{3} \]

Creating Tables of Equivalent Ratios

Step-by-Step Process

To create a table of equivalent ratios:

  1. Start with a given ratio
  2. Multiply or divide both terms by the same number to create equivalent ratios
  3. Organize the results in a table

Example 1: Simple Ratio Table

Given the ratio \(\frac{1}{2}\):

First Quantity Second Quantity Ratio
1 2 \(\frac{1}{2}\)
2 4 \(\frac{2}{4}\)
3 6 \(\frac{3}{6}\)
4 8 \(\frac{4}{8}\)

All these ratios simplify to \(\frac{1}{2}\)

Example 2: Real-World Application

If 3 apples cost \$2, create a table showing equivalent ratios:

Apples Cost (\$) Ratio
3 2 \(\frac{3}{2}\)
6 4 \(\frac{6}{4}\)
9 6 \(\frac{9}{6}\)
12 8 \(\frac{12}{8}\)

All these ratios simplify to \(\frac{3}{2}\)

Checking for Proportionality

To verify if quantities are proportional:

  1. Create a table of equivalent ratios
  2. Check if all ratios simplify to the same value
  3. If they do, the quantities are proportional

Example: Verify if these quantities are proportional:

X Y Ratio Y/X
2 5 \(\frac{5}{2}\) = 2.5
4 10 \(\frac{10}{4}\) = 2.5
6 15 \(\frac{15}{6}\) = 2.5

All ratios equal 2.5, so Y is proportional to X

Applications

Practical Examples

Equivalent ratios are useful in many real-world situations:

Recipe Scaling

If a recipe calls for 2 cups of flour and 1 cup of sugar (ratio 2:1), how much flour for 3 cups of sugar?

\[ \frac{2}{1} = \frac{x}{3} \Rightarrow x = 6 \text{ cups of flour} \]

Map Scales

If 1cm on a map represents 5km (ratio 1:5), how many km does 4cm represent?

\[ \frac{1}{5} = \frac{4}{x} \Rightarrow x = 20 \text{ km} \]

Currency Exchange

If \$1 = ¢12 (ratio 1:12), how many cedis for \$5?

\[ \frac{1}{12} = \frac{5}{x} \Rightarrow x = 60 \text{ cedis} \]

Question 2

Are \(\frac{4}{7}\) and \(\frac{12}{21}\) equivalent ratios?

Yes, because \(\frac{12}{21}\) simplifies to \(\frac{4}{7}\) (divided numerator and denominator by 3)

Question 3

Complete the table of equivalent ratios:

X Y
2 5
4 ?
? 15

Ratio is \(\frac{5}{2}\) = 2.5

For X=4: Y = 4 × 2.5 = 10

For Y=15: X = 15 ÷ 2.5 = 6

4 10
6 15

Question 5

Which pair of ratios are equivalent: \(\frac{2}{3}\) and \(\frac{4}{6}\), or \(\frac{3}{5}\) and \(\frac{6}{8}\)?

\(\frac{2}{3}\) and \(\frac{4}{6}\) are equivalent (both simplify to \(\frac{2}{3}\))

\(\frac{3}{5}\) ≈ 0.6 while \(\frac{6}{8}\) = 0.75, so they are not equivalent
