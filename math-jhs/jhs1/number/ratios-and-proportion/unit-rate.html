Introduction

A unit rate compares two quantities where one of the quantities is 1 unit. It shows how much of one quantity corresponds to one unit of another quantity. Unit rates are everywhere in daily life - from shopping to travel to cooking.

\[ \text{Examples: } \frac{60\,\text{km}}{1\,\text{hour}}, \frac{\$3.50}{1\,\text{kg}}, \frac{5\,\text{students}}{1\,\text{desk}} \]

Understanding Rates

A rate compares two different quantities measured in different units:

\[ \frac{\text{Quantity 1}}{\text{Quantity 2}} \]
  • Rate: Compares two different quantities (e.g., km/hour)
  • Unit Rate: A rate where the second quantity is 1 unit

Example: A car travels 120 km in 2 hours. The rate is \(\frac{120\,\text{km}}{2\,\text{hours}}\). The unit rate is \(\frac{60\,\text{km}}{1\,\text{hour}}\).

Calculating Unit Rates

Finding Unit Rates

To find a unit rate:

  1. Write the given rate as a fraction
  2. Divide both numerator and denominator by the denominator
  3. Simplify to get the quantity per 1 unit

Example 1: \(\frac{120\,\text{km}}{2\,\text{hours}}\)

\[ \frac{120\,\text{km}}{2\,\text{hours}} = \frac{120 \div 2}{2 \div 2} = \frac{60\,\text{km}}{1\,\text{hour}} \]

Example 2: \(\frac{\$15}{3\,\text{kg}}\)

\[ \frac{\$15}{3\,\text{kg}} = \frac{15 \div 3}{3 \div 3} = \frac{\$5}{1\,\text{kg}} \]

Unit Rate Formula

The general formula for finding a unit rate:

\[ \text{Unit Rate} = \frac{\text{Total Quantity}}{\text{Total Units}} \]
Given Rate Unit Rate
\(\frac{180\,\text{km}}{3\,\text{hours}}\) \(\frac{60\,\text{km}}{1\,\text{hour}}\)
\(\frac{\$24}{8\,\text{kg}}\) \(\frac{\$3}{1\,\text{kg}}\)
\(\frac{350\,\text{words}}{5\,\text{minutes}}\) \(\frac{70\,\text{words}}{1\,\text{minute}}\)

Solving Problems

Shopping Applications

Unit rates help compare prices to find the best deals:

Example 1: Comparing Prices

Which is cheaper: 5kg for \$12 or 8kg for \$18?

\[ \text{Option 1: } \frac{\$12}{5\,\text{kg}} = \frac{\$2.40}{1\,\text{kg}} \] \[ \text{Option 2: } \frac{\$18}{8\,\text{kg}} = \frac{\$2.25}{1\,\text{kg}} \]

Option 2 is cheaper per kg.

Example 2: Calculating Total Cost

If apples cost \$3.50 per kg, how much for 7kg?

\[ \$3.50 \times 7 = \$24.50 \]

Speed Problems

Speed is a common unit rate (distance per time):

Example: A car travels 240 km in 4 hours. What is its speed?

\[ \frac{240\,\text{km}}{4\,\text{hours}} = \frac{60\,\text{km}}{1\,\text{hour}} \]

The car's speed is 60 km/h.

Work Rate Problems

Unit rates can measure work efficiency:

Example: A worker completes 15 tasks in 3 hours. What is their work rate?

\[ \frac{15\,\text{tasks}}{3\,\text{hours}} = \frac{5\,\text{tasks}}{1\,\text{hour}} \]

The worker completes 5 tasks per hour.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Find the unit rate: 150 km in 3 hours

\[ \frac{150\,\text{km}}{3\,\text{hours}} = \frac{50\,\text{km}}{1\,\text{hour}} \]

Question 2

Which is the better buy: 6 pencils for \$1.20 or 10 pencils for \$1.90?

Calculate unit prices:

\[ \frac{\$1.20}{6} = \$0.20\,\text{per pencil} \] \[ \frac{\$1.90}{10} = \$0.19\,\text{per pencil} \]

10 pencils for \$1.90 is the better buy.

Question 3

A printer prints 120 pages in 4 minutes. What is its printing rate?

\[ \frac{120\,\text{pages}}{4\,\text{minutes}} = \frac{30\,\text{pages}}{1\,\text{minute}} \]

Question 4

If a car travels at 80 km/h, how far will it go in 5 hours?

\[ 80\,\text{km/h} \times 5\,\text{hours} = 400\,\text{km} \]

Question 5

Create a table showing distance vs. time for a car traveling at 65 km/h for 1, 2, and 3 hours.

Time (hours) Distance (km)
1 65
2 130
3 195

Question 6

A factory produces 480 widgets in 8 hours. What is the production rate per hour?

\[ \frac{480\,\text{widgets}}{8\,\text{hours}} = \frac{60\,\text{widgets}}{1\,\text{hour}} \]
