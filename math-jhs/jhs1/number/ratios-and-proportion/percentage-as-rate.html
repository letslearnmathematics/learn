Introduction

A percentage is a way to express a number as a fraction of 100. The word "percent" means "per hundred" or "out of 100." We use the percent symbol (%) to show percentages.

\[ \text{For example: } 25\% \text{ means } 25 \text{ out of } 100 \text{ or } \frac{25}{100} \]

Why Percentages Matter

Percentages are used everywhere in daily life:

  • Discounts: "25% off sale" means you pay 25% less than the original price
  • Grades: "You scored 85% on the test" means you got 85 out of 100 points
  • Interest Rates: "5% interest" means you pay 5 extra for every 100 you borrow

Understanding Percent

The Meaning of Percent

Percent means "per hundred" or "out of 100." It's a way to compare quantities by scaling them to a common base of 100.

Visual Representation

Imagine a grid with 100 squares:

25% would mean 25 squares are colored

50% would mean 50 squares are colored

100% means all squares are colored

Equivalent Forms

Percentages can be written as fractions and decimals:

\[ 25\% = \frac{25}{100} = 0.25 \] \[ 50\% = \frac{50}{100} = 0.5 \] \[ 100\% = \frac{100}{100} = 1 \]

Converting Between Forms

You can convert between percentages, fractions, and decimals:

Percentage Fraction Decimal
25% \(\frac{25}{100} = \frac{1}{4}\) 0.25
50% \(\frac{50}{100} = \frac{1}{2}\) 0.5
75% \(\frac{75}{100} = \frac{3}{4}\) 0.75
100% \(\frac{100}{100} = 1\) 1.0

Calculating Percentages

Finding a Percent of a Quantity

To find a percent of a quantity, follow these steps:

  1. Convert the percentage to a decimal (divide by 100)
  2. Multiply the decimal by the quantity

Example 1: Find 20% of 150

\[ 20\% = 0.20 \] \[ 0.20 \times 150 = 30 \]

So, 20% of 150 is 30

Example 2: Find 15% of 80

\[ 15\% = 0.15 \] \[ 0.15 \times 80 = 12 \]

So, 15% of 80 is 12

Alternative Method: Using Fractions

You can also find percentages using fractions:

Example: Find 25% of 200

\[ 25\% = \frac{25}{100} = \frac{1}{4} \] \[ \frac{1}{4} \times 200 = 50 \]

So, 25% of 200 is 50

Percentage Increase/Decrease

To calculate percentage increase or decrease:

  1. Find the difference between the new and old values
  2. Divide the difference by the original value
  3. Multiply by 100 to get the percentage

Example: A price increases from \$50 to \$60

\[ \text{Difference} = 60 - 50 = 10 \] \[ \frac{10}{50} = 0.2 \] \[ 0.2 \times 100 = 20\% \text{ increase} \]

Applications

Practical Examples

Understanding percentages is useful in many real-world situations:

Shopping Discounts

A \$120 jacket is on sale for 30% off. What's the sale price?

\[ 30\% \text{ of } 120 = 0.30 \times 120 = 36 \] \[ \text{Sale price} = 120 - 36 = \$84 \]

Tips at Restaurants

You want to leave a 15% tip on a \$45 meal bill.

\[ 15\% \text{ of } 45 = 0.15 \times 45 = 6.75 \]

Tip amount: \$6.75

Test Scores

You answered 34 out of 40 questions correctly. What percentage is this?

\[ \frac{34}{40} = 0.85 \] \[ 0.85 \times 100 = 85\% \]
