Introduction

Numbers can be expressed in different forms to make calculations easier and reveal interesting patterns. Today we'll learn three important ways to express numbers:

  1. As a product of other numbers (multiplication)
  2. As a power (using exponents)
  3. As a product of powers (combining both concepts)

Real-world Application

Understanding these concepts helps with:

  • Simplifying calculations
  • Finding common denominators
  • Working with area and volume
  • Understanding scientific notation

Example: The area of a square with sides of 5cm can be expressed as:

  • Product: 5 × 5 cm²
  • Power: 5² cm²

Expressing Numbers as Products

What is a Product?

A product is the result of multiplying numbers together. We can express any number as a product in multiple ways.

\[ \text{Product} = \text{Factor} \times \text{Factor} \times \text{Factor} \]

Method 1: Simple Products

Break the number into two factors:

24 = 6 × 4

24 = 3 × 8

24 = 2 × 12

Method 2: Multiple Factors

Break the number into more than two factors:

24 = 2 × 3 × 4

24 = 2 × 2 × 6

24 = 1 × 2 × 3 × 4

Key Points

  • Every number can be expressed as a product of 1 and itself
  • There are often multiple ways to express a number as a product
  • Prime numbers can only be expressed as 1 × themselves

Expressing Numbers as Powers

Understanding Powers

A power is a shorthand way to write repeated multiplication of the same number.

\[ \text{Base}^{\text{Exponent}} = \underbrace{\text{Base} \times \text{Base} \times \cdots \times \text{Base}}_{\text{Exponent times}} \]

Example: 5³ = 5 × 5 × 5 = 125

Common Powers to Know

Power Value Read As
2² 4 "Two squared"
2³ 8 "Two cubed"
3² 9 "Three squared"
5³ 125 "Five cubed"
10² 100 "Ten squared"

Example: Expressing 81 as a Power

1

Find factors of 81: 9 × 9

2

Notice 9 × 9 can be written as 9²

3

Alternatively, 81 = 3 × 3 × 3 × 3 = 3⁴

∴ 81 can be expressed as 9² or 3⁴

Expressing as Product of Powers

Combining Both Concepts

We can break numbers down into products where each factor is expressed as a power.

\[ \text{Number} = \text{Power} \times \text{Power} \times \cdots \]

Example: Expressing 72 as Product of Powers

1

Break 72 into prime factors: 72 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3

2

Group identical factors: (2 × 2 × 2) and (3 × 3)

3

Write as powers: 2³ × 3²

∴ 72 = 2³ × 3²

Visual Representation

2 2 2 → 2³
3 3 → 3²
= 2³ × 3²

Practice Exercises

Basic Level

  1. Express 16 as:
    • A product of two numbers (2 ways)
    • A power (2 ways)
  2. Write 5 × 5 × 5 × 5 as a power
  3. Express 36 as a product of powers
    • 4 × 4 or 2 × 8
    • 4² or 2⁴
  2. 5⁴
  3. 6² or 2² × 3²

Intermediate Level

  1. Express 120 as:
    • A product of three numbers
    • A product of powers
  2. Which is greater: 2⁵ or 5²?
  3. Express 144 as a product of powers in two different ways
    • 5 × 4 × 6 or 10 × 3 × 4
    • 2³ × 3 × 5
  2. 2⁵ = 32 > 25 = 5²
  3. 12² or 2⁴ × 3²

Advanced Level

  1. Express 360 as a product of powers of prime numbers
  2. Find a number that can be expressed as both a square and a cube (hint: it's less than 100)
  3. Express 1 as a product of powers in three different ways
  1. 2³ × 3² × 5
  2. 64 (8² or 4³)
  3. 1² × 1³, 2⁰ × 3⁰, 5⁰
