Convert Benchmark Fractions

Benchmark Fractions

A benchmark fraction is a common fraction that is usually used as a reference point. Examples include \(\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{4}, \frac{1}{3}\) and \(\frac{3}{4}\). These fractions can be represented as decimals or percentages.

They are mostly found on tickets, reciepts, invoices etc. We will learn how to convert these fractions to decimals and percentages below.

Fractions to Decimals

In order to convert a fraction to a decimal, divide the numerator (top number) by the denominator (bottom number).


Note:

You can use the long division to convert fractions to decimals.

Decimals to Percentages

Decimals & Powers of 10

Powers of 10

Powers of 10 are the numbers that can be expressed as \(10^n\), where \(n\) is a whole number. For example, \(10^2 = 100\), \(10^3 = 1000\), and so on.

Multiplying or dividing by powers of \(10\) is about shifting the decimal point to the right (if it is multiplication) or to the left (when dividing) \(n\) times.

In general,

When multiplying or dividing by powers of \(10\), the decimal point shifts to the right or left, respectively.

Multiplying

The decimal point moves a number of places to the right, depending on the number of zeros (0) present in the power of 10.

Dividing

The decimal point moves a number of places to the left, depending on the number of zeros (0) present in the power of 10.
