Introduction

Numbers written in index form (also called exponential form) consist of a base and an exponent (or index). In this lesson, we will learn how to evaluate such expressions.

\[ \text{Index form} = \text{base}^{\text{exponent}} \]

Understanding the Components

In an expression like \(a^n\):

  • \(a\) is called the base (the number being multiplied)
  • \(n\) is called the exponent or index (how many times the base is multiplied by itself)
\[ a^n = \underbrace{a \times a \times \cdots \times a}_{n \text{ times}} \]

Evaluating Indices

Step-by-Step Evaluation

To evaluate a number in index form:

  1. Identify the base and exponent
  2. Multiply the base by itself the number of times indicated by the exponent

Example 1: \(2^3\)

\[ 2^3 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 8 \]

Example 2: \(5^2\)

\[ 5^2 = 5 \times 5 = 25 \]

Special Cases

There are some special exponent cases to remember:

\begin{eqnarray*} a^1 &=& a \\ a^0 &=& 1 \quad (\text{for } a \neq 0) \\ 1^n &=& 1 \\ 0^n &=& 0 \quad (\text{for } n > 0) \end{eqnarray*}

Negative Bases

When the base is negative, pay attention to the exponent:

\begin{eqnarray*} (-2)^3 &=& -8 \quad \text{(odd exponent keeps negative)} \\ (-3)^2 &=& 9 \quad \text{(even exponent makes positive)} \end{eqnarray*}

Applications

Practical Examples

Index form is commonly used in various mathematical and real-world applications:

Scientific Notation

Large numbers are often written using powers of 10:

\[ 300,000 = 3 \times 10^5 \]

Area Calculations

Area of a square with side length 4 cm:

\[ \text{Area} = 4^2 = 16 \text{ cm}^2 \]

Compound Interest

The growth formula uses exponents:

\[ A = P(1 + r)^n \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Evaluate \(3^4\)

\[ 3^4 = 3 \times 3 \times 3 \times 3 = 81 \]

Question 2

Evaluate \(7^2\)

\[ 7^2 = 7 \times 7 = 49 \]

Question 3

Evaluate \((-2)^5\)

$(-2)^5$

$\Rightarrow (-2) \times (-2) \times (-2) \times (-2) \times (-2) $

$\Rightarrow -32$

Question 4

Evaluate \(10^0\)

Any non-zero number to the power of 0 is 1:

\[ 10^0 = 1 \]

Question 5

Evaluate \(1^{100}\)

1 to any power is always 1:

\[ 1^{100} = 1 \]

Question 6

Evaluate \(2^3 \times 3^2\)

First evaluate each part:

\[ 2^3 = 8 \] \[ 3^2 = 9 \]

Then multiply:

\[ 8 \times 9 = 72 \]
