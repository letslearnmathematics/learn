Introduction

In this lesson, we will use basic number techniques to find the product of two given numbers. The techniques that we will use are:

  1. Halving and Doubling technique
  2. distributive Property

Halving & Doubling Technique

Main Idea

The halving and doubling technique involves adjusting two numbers being multiplied, that is:

\(*\) Halve one of the numbers and

\(*\) Double the other number.

This adjustment simplifies calculations without changing the final product because, under the set of real numbers \((\mathbb{R})\), multiplication is both commutative and associative.

In general,

The product of two numbers remains constant if one number is halved and the other is doubled. \begin{eqnarray*} \dfrac{1}{2} \times 2 = 1 \end{eqnarray*}

It implies that:

Given \(a \ \text{and} \ b\), we can write;

\( a \times b \)

\(\Rightarrow \frac{1}{2} \times a \times 2 \times b\)

\(\Rightarrow \frac{1}{2}a \times 2b \)

Distributive Property

Main Idea

In Mathematics, we say that multiplication is distributive over addition and subtraction.

Given three numbers, \(a, b \ \text{and} \ c\), the following concepts are true.

Distribution over Addition \(\left(+\right)\)

The equation below describes the distributive property of multiplication over addition.

\begin{equation*} a\left(b + c\right) = \left(a \times b\right) + \left(a \times c\right) \end{equation*}

Distribution over Subtraction \(\left(-\right)\)

The equation below describes the distributive property of multiplication over subtraction.

\begin{equation*} a\left(b - c\right) = \left(a \times b\right) - \left(a \times c\right) \end{equation*}
