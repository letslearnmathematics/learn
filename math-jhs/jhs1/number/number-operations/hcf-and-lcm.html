Prime Factorization

Prime factorization is the process of expressing a number as a product of its prime factors. This is fundamental for finding HCF and LCM.

\[ \text{Prime factorization of } 12 = 2 \times 2 \times 3 = 2^2 \times 3^1 \]

Step-by-Step Factorization

To find prime factors:

  1. Divide the number by the smallest prime (2) until you can't
  2. Move to the next prime (3, 5, 7,...)
  3. Continue until the quotient is 1

Example: Factorize 60

\begin{align*} 60 ÷ 2 &= 30 \\ 30 ÷ 2 &= 15 \\ 15 ÷ 3 &= 5 \\ 5 ÷ 5 &= 1 \\ \text{Prime factors} &= 2^2 × 3^1 × 5^1 \end{align*}

Highest Common Factor (HCF)

Finding HCF Using Prime Factors

The HCF is the product of the lowest power of common prime factors.

Example: Find HCF of 36 and 60

\begin{align*} 36 &= 2^2 × 3^2 \\ 60 &= 2^2 × 3^1 × 5^1 \\ \text{HCF} &= 2^2 × 3^1 = 12 \end{align*}

Real-Life HCF Application

Problem: A baker has 24 cupcakes and 36 cookies. She wants to make identical gift bags with the same combination of cupcakes and cookies. What's the maximum number of gift bags she can make?

\begin{align*} \text{HCF of 24 and 36} &= 12 \\ \text{Each bag gets } &2 \text{ cupcakes and } 3 \text{ cookies} \end{align*}

Least Common Multiple (LCM)

Finding LCM Using Prime Factors

The LCM is the product of the highest power of all prime factors present.

Example: Find LCM of 12 and 18

\begin{align*} 12 &= 2^2 × 3^1 \\ 18 &= 2^1 × 3^2 \\ \text{LCM} &= 2^2 × 3^2 = 36 \end{align*}

Real-Life LCM Application

Problem: Bus A arrives every 15 minutes, Bus B every 20 minutes. If they arrive together at 8:00 AM, when will they next arrive together?

\begin{align*} \text{LCM of 15 and 20} &= 60 \\ \text{Next simultaneous arrival} &= 9:00 \text{ AM} \end{align*}

Word Problems

Combined HCF & LCM Problem

Problem: Two ropes are 48m and 80m long. We need to cut them into equal lengths with no leftovers. What's the longest possible length for each piece, and how many pieces will we get in total?

\begin{align*} \text{HCF of 48 and 80} &= 16 \\ \text{Pieces from 48m rope} &= 3 \\ \text{Pieces from 80m rope} &= 5 \\ \text{Total pieces} &= 8 \\ \text{Each piece} &= 16m \end{align*}

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Find the HCF of 28 and 42 using prime factorization.

\begin{align*} 28 &= 2^2 × 7^1 \\ 42 &= 2^1 × 3^1 × 7^1 \\ \text{HCF} &= 2^1 × 7^1 = 14 \end{align*}

Question 2

Find the LCM of 15 and 25 using prime factors.

\begin{align*} 15 &= 3^1 × 5^1 \\ 25 &= 5^2 \\ \text{LCM} &= 3^1 × 5^2 = 75 \end{align*}

Question 3

Three bells ring at intervals of 6, 8, and 12 minutes. If they ring together at noon, when will they next ring together?

\begin{align*} \text{LCM of 6, 8, 12} &= 24 \\ \text{Next simultaneous ring} &= 12:24 \text{ PM} \end{align*}

Question 4

A rectangular floor is 300cm by 450cm. What's the largest square tile that can cover it completely?

\begin{align*} \text{HCF of 300 and 450} &= 150 \\ \text{Largest square tile} &= 150\text{cm} × 150\text{cm} \end{align*}

Question 5

Find the smallest number divisible by both 18 and 24.

\begin{align*} 18 &= 2^1 × 3^2 \\ 24 &= 2^3 × 3^1 \\ \text{LCM} &= 2^3 × 3^2 = 72 \end{align*}
