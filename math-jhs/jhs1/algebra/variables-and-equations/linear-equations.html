Introduction

Many real-world problems can be translated into mathematical equations. In this lesson, we'll focus on converting word problems to linear equations with one variable and vice versa.

\[ \text{Example: } \text{"Five more than a number is 12"} \rightarrow x + 5 = 12 \]

Key Concepts

A linear equation in one variable is an equation that can be written in the form:

\[ ax + b = c \]
  • Variable: A symbol (usually x) representing an unknown value
  • Coefficient: The number multiplying the variable (a)
  • Constants: The known numbers (b and c)

Example: In \(3x + 7 = 16\), 3 is the coefficient, 7 and 16 are constants.

Flag Diagram Method

Understanding Flag Diagrams

A flag diagram helps visualize the relationship between a word problem and its equation:

  1. Write the word problem at the top
  2. Break it down into mathematical components
  3. Show the inverse operations needed to solve it

Example Problem:

"A number multiplied by 4 then subtracted by 5 gives 15"

Word Problem
↓
4x - 5 = 15
↓
Inverse: Add 5, then divide by 4

Solving with Flag Diagrams

Using the flag diagram method to solve equations:

Problem: \(2x + 3 = 11\)

Equation
↓
Subtract 3 from both sides: \(2x = 8\)
↓
Divide both sides by 2: \(x = 4\)

Translating Word Problems to Equations

Key Words and Phrases

Certain words in problems indicate specific mathematical operations:

Addition (+)

sum, more than, increased by, added to

\[ \text{"5 more than x"} \rightarrow x + 5 \]

Subtraction (-)

difference, less than, decreased by, subtracted from

\[ \text{"7 less than x"} \rightarrow x - 7 \]

Multiplication (×)

product, times, multiplied by, of

\[ \text{"twice x"} \rightarrow 2x \]

Division (÷)

quotient, divided by, per, ratio of

\[ \text{"x divided by 3"} \rightarrow \frac{x}{3} \]

Equals (=)

is, gives, yields, results in

\[ \text{"x plus 2 is 10"} \rightarrow x + 2 = 10 \]

Step-by-Step Translation

To translate word problems to equations:

  1. Identify the unknown (usually "what number" or "how many")
  2. Assign a variable to represent the unknown
  3. Translate words to mathematical operations
  4. Write the complete equation

Example: "If 5 is subtracted from twice a number, the result is 13"

  1. Let x = the unknown number
  2. "Twice a number" → 2x
  3. "5 is subtracted from" → 2x - 5
  4. "the result is 13" → 2x - 5 = 13

Common Problem Types

Problem Type Example Equation
Consecutive Numbers "Two consecutive numbers sum to 25" x + (x + 1) = 25
Age Problems "John is 5 years older than Mary" J = M + 5
Number Relationships "A number plus its double is 36" x + 2x = 36
Distance Problems "A car travels 60km in x hours" 60 = x × speed

Writing Word Problems for Equations

Reverse Translation

To create word problems from equations:

  1. Identify the mathematical operations in the equation
  2. Choose a real-world context (money, age, measurement, etc.)
  3. Use appropriate vocabulary for each operation
  4. Write a complete, clear problem statement

Example Equation: 3x + 4 = 19

Possible word problem:

"A student buys 3 books at the same price each, plus a \$4 notebook. The total cost is \$19. What is the price of one book?"

Tips for Good Word Problems

Effective word problems should:

  • Be clear and unambiguous
  • Use simple, understandable language
  • Have a realistic scenario
  • Contain all necessary information
  • Ask a specific question

Poor: "Some number times 3 plus 4 is 19"

Better: "A gardener plants 3 rows of flowers with equal number in each row, plus 4 extra flowers. Total is 19 flowers. How many in each row?"

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Translate to an equation: "Seven less than three times a number is 14"

\[ 3x - 7 = 14 \]

Question 2

Write a word problem for: \(2x + 10 = 24\)

Possible answer: "Two friends have the same amount of money. Together they have \$24, but one friend also has an extra \$10. How much does each friend have?"

Question 3

Translate to an equation: "The sum of a number and its half is 27"

\[ x + \frac{x}{2} = 27 \]

Question 4

Solve using flag diagram: \(5x - 3 = 22\)

5x - 3 = 22
↓
Add 3: 5x = 25
↓
Divide by 5: x = 5

Question 5

Translate to an equation: "The product of 4 and a number decreased by 6 is equal to 18"

\[ 4x - 6 = 18 \]

Question 6

Write a word problem for: \(\frac{x}{3} - 2 = 5\)

Possible answer: "After dividing my money into 3 equal parts and giving away \$2, I have \$5 left. How much did I have originally?"
