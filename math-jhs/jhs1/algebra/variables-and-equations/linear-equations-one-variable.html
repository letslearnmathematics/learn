Introduction

A linear equation in one variable is an equation that can be written in the form:

\[ ax + b = c \]

where:

  • x is the variable (unknown we need to solve for)
  • a, b, c are constants (numbers)
  • a is not equal to zero

Understanding Equations

An equation is like a balance scale - both sides must remain equal. The solution to an equation is the value of the variable that makes the equation true.

Example: In the equation \( 2x + 3 = 7 \):

The solution is \( x = 2 \) because:

\[ 2(2) + 3 = 7 \] \[ 4 + 3 = 7 \] \[ 7 = 7 \] (This is true!)

Balance Method

The Balance Concept

The balance method treats the equation like a balanced scale. Whatever you do to one side, you must do to the other to keep it balanced.

Example 1: Simple Equation

Solve \( x + 3 = 5 \)

Subtract 3 from both sides:

\[ x + 3 - 3 = 5 - 3 \] \[ x = 2 \]

Example 2: With Multiplication

Solve \( 2x = 8 \)

Divide both sides by 2:

\[ \frac{2x}{2} = \frac{8}{2} \] \[ x = 4 \]

Inverse Operations

To solve equations, we use inverse operations (opposite operations) to isolate the variable:

Operation Inverse Operation
Addition (+) Subtraction (-)
Subtraction (-) Addition (+)
Multiplication (×) Division (÷)
Division (÷) Multiplication (×)

Solving Steps

Step-by-Step Method

Follow these steps to solve linear equations:

  1. Simplify both sides - Remove parentheses and combine like terms
  2. Move variable terms - Get all variable terms on one side
  3. Move constant terms - Get all constant terms on the other side
  4. Isolate the variable - Divide or multiply to get x alone
  5. Check your solution - Substitute back into the original equation

Example 1: Two-Step Equation

Solve \( 3x - 5 = 10 \)

Step 1: Add 5 to both sides

\[ 3x - 5 + 5 = 10 + 5 \] \[ 3x = 15 \]

Step 2: Divide both sides by 3

\[ \frac{3x}{3} = \frac{15}{3} \] \[ x = 5 \]

Check: \( 3(5) - 5 = 15 - 5 = 10 \) ✓

Example 2: With Variables on Both Sides

Solve \( 2x + 3 = 5x - 9 \)

Step 1: Subtract 2x from both sides

\[ 2x + 3 - 2x = 5x - 9 - 2x \] \[ 3 = 3x - 9 \]

Step 2: Add 9 to both sides

\[ 3 + 9 = 3x - 9 + 9 \] \[ 12 = 3x \]

Step 3: Divide both sides by 3

\[ \frac{12}{3} = \frac{3x}{3} \] \[ 4 = x \]

Check: \( 2(4) + 3 = 8 + 3 = 11 \) and \( 5(4) - 9 = 20 - 9 = 11 \) ✓

Common Mistakes

Watch out for these common errors when solving equations:

  • Forgetting to perform the same operation on both sides
  • Making sign errors when moving terms
  • Dividing incorrectly (especially with fractions)
  • Misapplying the distributive property
  • Forgetting to check the solution

Applications

Practical Examples

Linear equations are used to solve many real-world problems:

Age Problems

John is 5 years older than Mary. In 3 years, the sum of their ages will be 31. How old is each now?

Let Mary's age = x, then John's age = x + 5

In 3 years: (x + 3) + (x + 5 + 3) = 31

\[ 2x + 11 = 31 \] \[ 2x = 20 \] \[ x = 10 \]

Mary is 10, John is 15

Number Problems

The sum of three consecutive numbers is 54. Find the numbers.

Let first number = x, then next numbers = x + 1, x + 2

\[ x + (x + 1) + (x + 2) = 54 \] \[ 3x + 3 = 54 \] \[ 3x = 51 \] \[ x = 17 \]

Numbers are 17, 18, 19

Distance Problems

A car travels 240 km in 3 hours. What is its speed?

Distance = Speed × Time

\[ 240 = s \times 3 \] \[ s = \frac{240}{3} = 80 \text{ km/h} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Solve for x: \( x + 7 = 15 \)

\[ x + 7 - 7 = 15 - 7 \] \[ x = 8 \]

Question 2

Solve for y: \( 3y = 27 \)

\[ \frac{3y}{3} = \frac{27}{3} \] \[ y = 9 \]

Question 3

Solve for a: \( 2a - 5 = 13 \)

\[ 2a - 5 + 5 = 13 + 5 \] \[ 2a = 18 \] \[ a = 9 \]

Question 4

Solve for b: \( 4b + 3 = 2b + 11 \)

\[ 4b - 2b + 3 = 2b - 2b + 11 \] \[ 2b + 3 = 11 \] \[ 2b = 8 \] \[ b = 4 \]

Question 5

The sum of two numbers is 45. One number is 9 more than the other. Find the numbers.

Let first number = x, second number = x + 9

\[ x + (x + 9) = 45 \] \[ 2x + 9 = 45 \] \[ 2x = 36 \] \[ x = 18 \]

Numbers are 18 and 27

Question 6

A rectangle's length is 5 cm more than its width. The perimeter is 38 cm. Find the dimensions.

Let width = w, length = w + 5

Perimeter = 2(length + width)

\[ 2(w + w + 5) = 38 \] \[ 2(2w + 5) = 38 \] \[ 4w + 10 = 38 \] \[ 4w = 28 \] \[ w = 7 \]

Width = 7 cm, Length = 12 cm
