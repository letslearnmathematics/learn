Introduction

Algebraic expressions are mathematical phrases that can contain numbers, variables, and operations. In this lesson, we'll learn how to add and subtract algebraic expressions with rational coefficients (fractions and whole numbers).

\[ \text{Examples: } 3x + 2, \quad \frac{1}{2}y - \frac{3}{4}, \quad 2a + 5b - c \]

Key Concepts

Before adding or subtracting algebraic expressions, remember these important rules:

  • Like terms: Terms with the same variable(s) and exponent(s) can be combined
  • Coefficients: The numerical part of a term (including the sign)
  • Rational coefficients: Coefficients can be whole numbers or fractions

Example: In \( \frac{2}{3}x + 5y - \frac{1}{4}x \), \( \frac{2}{3}x \) and \( -\frac{1}{4}x \) are like terms

Adding Algebraic Expressions

Steps for Addition

To add algebraic expressions:

  1. Identify and group like terms
  2. Add the coefficients of like terms
  3. Keep the variable part unchanged
  4. Write the simplified expression

Example 1: Simple Addition

Add \( 3x + 2 \) and \( 5x - 7 \)

\[ (3x + 2) + (5x - 7) \] \[ = 3x + 5x + 2 - 7 \] \[ = 8x - 5 \]

Example 2: With Fractions

Add \( \frac{1}{2}a + \frac{3}{4} \) and \( \frac{1}{4}a - \frac{1}{2} \)

\[ \left(\frac{1}{2}a + \frac{3}{4}\right) + \left(\frac{1}{4}a - \frac{1}{2}\right) \] \[ = \frac{1}{2}a + \frac{1}{4}a + \frac{3}{4} - \frac{1}{2} \] \[ = \frac{3}{4}a + \frac{1}{4} \]

Important Notes

When adding expressions with fractional coefficients:

  • Find a common denominator when adding fractions
  • Simplify the final expression
  • Keep track of positive and negative signs

Example: Adding \( \frac{2}{3}b \) and \( \frac{1}{6}b \):

\[ \frac{2}{3}b + \frac{1}{6}b = \frac{4}{6}b + \frac{1}{6}b = \frac{5}{6}b \]

Subtracting Algebraic Expressions

Steps for Subtraction

To subtract algebraic expressions:

  1. Distribute the negative sign to all terms in the second expression
  2. Identify and group like terms
  3. Combine the coefficients of like terms
  4. Write the simplified expression

Example 1: Simple Subtraction

Subtract \( 4y - 3 \) from \( 7y + 2 \)

\[ (7y + 2) - (4y - 3) \] \[ = 7y + 2 - 4y + 3 \] \[ = 3y + 5 \]

Example 2: With Fractions

Subtract \( \frac{3}{5}m - \frac{1}{2} \) from \( \frac{2}{5}m + \frac{3}{4} \)

\[ \left(\frac{2}{5}m + \frac{3}{4}\right) - \left(\frac{3}{5}m - \frac{1}{2}\right) \] \[ = \frac{2}{5}m + \frac{3}{4} - \frac{3}{5}m + \frac{1}{2} \] \[ = -\frac{1}{5}m + \frac{5}{4} \]

Important Notes

When subtracting expressions:

  • The negative sign affects all terms in the second expression
  • Be careful with signs when combining terms
  • With fractions, find common denominators when needed

Example: \( -\left(\frac{1}{3}x - \frac{2}{5}\right) = -\frac{1}{3}x + \frac{2}{5} \)

Combined Addition and Subtraction

Solving Combined Problems

When expressions involve both addition and subtraction:

  1. Remove parentheses by distributing signs
  2. Group like terms together
  3. Combine coefficients for each group of like terms
  4. Simplify the final expression

Example 1: Combined Operations

Simplify \( (2x + 3) + (5x - 4) - (3x + 2) \)

\[ 2x + 3 + 5x - 4 - 3x - 2 \] \[ = (2x + 5x - 3x) + (3 - 4 - 2) \] \[ = 4x - 3 \]

Example 2: With Fractions

Simplify \( \left(\frac{1}{2}a + \frac{1}{3}\right) - \left(\frac{1}{4}a - \frac{1}{6}\right) + \left(\frac{3}{4}a + \frac{1}{2}\right) \)

\[ \frac{1}{2}a + \frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{4}a + \frac{1}{6} + \frac{3}{4}a + \frac{1}{2} \] \[ = \left(\frac{1}{2}a - \frac{1}{4}a + \frac{3}{4}a\right) + \left(\frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{2}\right) \] \[ = a + 1 \]

Practical Applications

These skills are useful in many real-world situations:

  • Calculating total costs with different priced items
  • Combining measurements in recipes or construction
  • Solving problems in physics and engineering

Example: If one side of a triangle is \( \frac{3}{4}x + 2 \), another is \( \frac{1}{2}x - 1 \), and the third is \( x + \frac{1}{3} \), the perimeter is:

\[ \left(\frac{3}{4}x + 2\right) + \left(\frac{1}{2}x - 1\right) + \left(x + \frac{1}{3}\right) = \frac{9}{4}x + \frac{4}{3} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Add \( \frac{2}{3}x + 4 \) and \( \frac{1}{6}x - 2 \)

\[ \frac{2}{3}x + \frac{1}{6}x + 4 - 2 = \frac{5}{6}x + 2 \]

Question 2

Subtract \( 3y - \frac{1}{2} \) from \( 5y + \frac{3}{4} \)

\[ 5y + \frac{3}{4} - 3y + \frac{1}{2} = 2y + \frac{5}{4} \]

Question 3

Simplify \( (2a + 3b) + (5a - 2b) - (a + b) \)

\[ 2a + 5a - a + 3b - 2b - b = 6a \]

Question 4

Add \( \frac{1}{4}m - \frac{2}{3} \) and \( \frac{3}{4}m + \frac{5}{6} \)

\[ \frac{1}{4}m + \frac{3}{4}m - \frac{2}{3} + \frac{5}{6} = m + \frac{1}{6} \]

Question 5

Simplify \( \left(\frac{2}{5}x + 1\right) - \left(\frac{1}{10}x - \frac{1}{2}\right) + \left(\frac{3}{10}x + 2\right) \)

\[ \frac{2}{5}x - \frac{1}{10}x + \frac{3}{10}x + 1 + \frac{1}{2} + 2 = \frac{3}{5}x + 3.5 \]

Question 6

The lengths of three ropes are \( \frac{3}{4}x + 2 \), \( \frac{1}{2}x - 1 \), and \( x + \frac{1}{3} \). Find the total length.

\[ \frac{3}{4}x + \frac{1}{2}x + x + 2 - 1 + \frac{1}{3} = \frac{9}{4}x + \frac{4}{3} \]
