Introduction

A triangle is a three-sided polygon. To find its area, we can use its relationship with rectangles and parallelograms. The area of a triangle is always half the area of a rectangle or parallelogram with the same base and height.

Animation showing how a triangle's area relates to a rectangle

Key Terms

  • Base: Any side of the triangle (usually the bottom side when drawn)
  • Height: The perpendicular distance from the base to the opposite vertex
  • Unit Square: A square with sides of 1 unit length, used to measure area

Counting Unit Squares

Determining Area by Counting

We can estimate a triangle's area by counting how many unit squares fit inside it:

  1. Draw the triangle on grid paper
  2. Count the number of complete unit squares inside the triangle
  3. For partial squares, combine them to make whole squares

Example 1: Right Triangle

This right triangle covers 4 complete squares and 4 half squares, totaling 6 unit squares.

Example 2: Acute Triangle

This triangle covers 5 complete squares and 6 partial squares that combine to make 3 more, totaling 8 unit squares.

Triangle-Rectangle Relationship

Enclosing Rectangle

Every triangle can be enclosed in a rectangle. The area of the triangle is exactly half the area of this rectangle when the triangle has its base on one side of the rectangle and its opposite vertex on the opposite side.

Right Triangle

A right triangle forms half of a rectangle. The area is \(\frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}\).

Acute Triangle

An acute triangle divides its enclosing rectangle into the triangle itself and three right triangles.

Obtuse Triangle

An obtuse triangle extends beyond its base rectangle but still maintains the area relationship.

Deriving the Formula

From the rectangle relationship:

  1. Area of rectangle = base × height
  2. Triangle covers exactly half the rectangle
  3. Therefore, Area of triangle = \(\frac{1}{2}\) × base × height
\[ \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times b \times h \]

Triangle-Parallelogram Relationship

Forming Parallelograms

Two identical triangles can be combined to form a parallelogram:

  1. Take two copies of the same triangle
  2. Rotate one 180 degrees
  3. Combine them along corresponding sides

Two congruent triangles form a parallelogram with the same base and height.

Area Relationship

Since two identical triangles make one parallelogram:

  • Area of parallelogram = base × height
  • Therefore, Area of one triangle = \(\frac{1}{2}\) × base × height
\[ \text{Area of triangle} = \frac{\text{Area of parallelogram}}{2} = \frac{1}{2} \times b \times h \]

Cutting a Parallelogram

A parallelogram can be divided into two congruent triangles by drawing a diagonal:

The diagonal divides the parallelogram into two identical triangles, each with half the area.

Area Formula

The Standard Formula

From both relationships, we derive the standard formula for a triangle's area:

\[ \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height} \] \[ A = \frac{1}{2}bh \]

Where:

  • b = length of any side (base)
  • h = perpendicular height from that base to the opposite vertex

Applying the Formula

Examples of using the formula with different triangles:

Right Triangle

Base = 6 units, Height = 4 units

\[ A = \frac{1}{2} \times 6 \times 4 = 12 \text{ units}^2 \]

Acute Triangle

Base = 8 units, Height = 5 units

\[ A = \frac{1}{2} \times 8 \times 5 = 20 \text{ units}^2 \]

Obtuse Triangle

Base = 10 units, Height = 3 units

\[ A = \frac{1}{2} \times 10 \times 3 = 15 \text{ units}^2 \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

How many unit squares are in a right triangle that fits exactly in a 4×3 rectangle?

The rectangle has 12 unit squares (4×3). The triangle covers half: 6 unit squares.

Question 2

Find the area of a triangle with base 10cm and height 8cm.

\[ A = \frac{1}{2} \times 10 \times 8 = 40 \text{ cm}^2 \]

Question 3

If a parallelogram has area 24m², what's the area of one of its triangular halves?

\[ \text{Area of triangle} = \frac{24}{2} = 12 \text{ m}^2 \]

Question 4

A triangle has the same base and height as a 7×5 rectangle. Find its area.

Rectangle area = 7×5 = 35. Triangle area = 35/2 = 17.5 units²

Question 5

Draw a right triangle with legs 3 and 4 units. How many unit squares does it contain?

The triangle forms half of a 3×4 rectangle. It contains 6 unit squares.

Question 6

What's the base of a triangle with area 18cm² and height 6cm?

\[ 18 = \frac{1}{2} \times b \times 6 \] \[ b = \frac{18 \times 2}{6} = 6 \text{ cm} \]
