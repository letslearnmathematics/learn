Introduction

Perimeter is the total distance around a shape. When calculating perimeter, all sides must be in the same unit. This lesson will teach you how to handle shapes with sides measured in different units.

\[ \text{Perimeter} = \text{Sum of all sides of the shape} \]

Key Concepts

There are two main approaches when dealing with mixed units:

  • Convert to smaller unit: Change all measurements to the smaller unit before adding
  • Convert to decimal: Change the smaller unit to a decimal fraction of the larger unit

Example: 2m 50cm can be:

  • 200cm + 50cm = 250cm (smaller unit)
  • 2m + 0.5m = 2.5m (decimal fraction)

km and m

Converting km to m

To convert kilometers to meters:

  1. Remember that 1 km = 1000 m
  2. Multiply the km value by 1000
  3. Add the remaining meters

Example 1: Rectangle 3km 200m by 2km 500m

Convert all to meters:

3km 200m = (3 × 1000) + 200 = 3200m

2km 500m = (2 × 1000) + 500 = 2500m

Perimeter = 2 × (length + width)

= 2 × (3200 + 2500) = 2 × 5700 = 11,400m

Example 2: Triangle 0.5km 300m, 0.8km 150m, 1km 50m

Convert all to meters:

0.5km 300m = (0.5 × 1000) + 300 = 800m

0.8km 150m = (0.8 × 1000) + 150 = 950m

1km 50m = (1 × 1000) + 50 = 1050m

Perimeter = 800 + 950 + 1050 = 2800m

m and cm

Converting cm to m (Decimal)

To convert centimeters to meters as a decimal:

  1. Remember that 1m = 100cm
  2. Divide cm by 100 to get decimal meters
  3. Add to the existing meters

Example 1: 1m 75cm = 1m + (75 ÷ 100)m = 1.75m

Example 2: 2m 5cm = 2m + (5 ÷ 100)m = 2.05m

Calculating Perimeter

Example: Rectangle 4m 25cm by 3m 80cm

Method 1: Convert all to meters

4m 25cm = 4.25m

3m 80cm = 3.80m

Perimeter = 2 × (4.25 + 3.80) = 2 × 8.05 = 16.10m

Same example using cm:

4m 25cm = 425cm

3m 80cm = 380cm

Perimeter = 2 × (425 + 380) = 2 × 805 = 1610cm

cm and mm

Converting mm to cm (Decimal)

To convert millimeters to centimeters as a decimal:

  1. Remember that 1cm = 10mm
  2. Divide mm by 10 to get decimal cm
  3. Add to the existing cm

Example 1: 7cm 5mm = 7cm + (5 ÷ 10)cm = 7.5cm

Example 2: 12cm 8mm = 12cm + (8 ÷ 10)cm = 12.8cm

Calculating Perimeter

Example: Triangle with sides 5cm 4mm, 8cm 7mm, 6cm 9mm

Convert all to cm:

5cm 4mm = 5.4cm

8cm 7mm = 8.7cm

6cm 9mm = 6.9cm

Perimeter = 5.4 + 8.7 + 6.9 = 21.0cm

Same example using mm:

5cm 4mm = 54mm

8cm 7mm = 87mm

6cm 9mm = 69mm

Perimeter = 54 + 87 + 69 = 210mm

Applications

Practical Examples

Calculating perimeter with mixed units is useful in many real-world situations:

Fencing a Field

A field measures 150m 75cm by 200m 50cm. How much fencing is needed?

Convert all to cm:

150m 75cm = 15,075cm

200m 50cm = 20,050cm

Perimeter = 2 × (15,075 + 20,050) = 70,250cm (or 702.5m)

Picture Frame

A picture frame has sides 45cm 5mm and 30cm 8mm. What's the total length of framing material?

Convert all to mm:

45cm 5mm = 455mm

30cm 8mm = 308mm

Perimeter = 2 × (455 + 308) = 1,526mm (or 152.6cm)

Running Track

A rectangular track is 0.5km 250m long and 0.3km 175m wide. What distance is one lap?

Convert all to meters:

0.5km 250m = 750m

0.3km 175m = 475m

Perimeter = 2 × (750 + 475) = 2,450m (or 2.45km)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Find the perimeter of a rectangle with sides 2km 300m and 1km 750m (give answer in meters)

Convert to meters:

2km 300m = 2,300m

1km 750m = 1,750m

Perimeter = 2 × (2,300 + 1,750) = 8,100m

Question 2

Calculate the perimeter of a triangle with sides 1m 25cm, 2m 50cm, and 1m 75cm (give answer in meters)

Convert to meters:

1m 25cm = 1.25m

2m 50cm = 2.50m

1m 75cm = 1.75m

Perimeter = 1.25 + 2.50 + 1.75 = 5.50m

Question 3

A square has sides of 15cm 8mm. What is its perimeter in centimeters?

Convert to cm:

15cm 8mm = 15.8cm

Perimeter = 4 × 15.8 = 63.2cm

Question 4

Find the perimeter of a rectangle 3.5km 250m by 2km 750m (answer in km)

Convert to km:

3.5km 250m = 3.5 + 0.250 = 3.750km

2km 750m = 2 + 0.750 = 2.750km

Perimeter = 2 × (3.750 + 2.750) = 13.000km

Question 5

A pentagon has sides measuring 25cm 4mm each. What is its perimeter in millimeters?

Convert to mm:

25cm 4mm = 254mm

Perimeter = 5 × 254 = 1,270mm

Question 6

A triangular garden has sides 4m 50cm, 3m 75cm, and 5m 25cm. How much fencing is needed (answer in meters)?

Convert to meters:

4m 50cm = 4.50m

3m 75cm = 3.75m

5m 25cm = 5.25m

Perimeter = 4.50 + 3.75 + 5.25 = 13.50m
