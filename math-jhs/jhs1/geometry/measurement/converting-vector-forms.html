Introduction

Vectors can be represented in two main forms: column (component) form and magnitude-bearing form. Understanding how to convert between these forms is essential for solving vector problems.

\[ \text{Column form: } \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} \] \[ \text{Magnitude-bearing form: } r \text{ at } \theta^\circ \]

Understanding Vectors

A vector has both magnitude (size) and direction:

  • Column form: Shows horizontal (x) and vertical (y) components
  • Magnitude-bearing form: Shows length (r) and angle from North (θ)

Example: \(\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}\) means 3 units east and 4 units north

Component to Magnitude-Bearing

Finding the Magnitude

To find the magnitude (length) of a vector from its components:

  1. Square both components
  2. Add them together
  3. Take the square root

Example 1: Find magnitude of \(\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}\)

\[ \text{Magnitude} = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{9 + 16} = \sqrt{25} = 5 \]

Example 2: Find magnitude of \(\begin{pmatrix} -6 \\ 8 \end{pmatrix}\)

\[ \text{Magnitude} = \sqrt{(-6)^2 + 8^2} = \sqrt{36 + 64} = \sqrt{100} = 10 \]

Finding the Bearing

To find the bearing (direction) of a vector from its components:

  1. Find the angle θ using tanθ = opposite/adjacent
  2. Adjust for the quadrant (direction) of the vector
  3. Express as an angle from North (0° to 360°)

Example: Find bearing of \(\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}\):

\[ \tanθ = \frac{3}{4} \Rightarrow θ \approx 36.87^\circ \]

This is 36.87° east of north, so bearing = 036.87°

Conversion Steps Summary

Step Action Example
1 Identify x and y components \(\begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ -12 \end{pmatrix}\)
2 Calculate magnitude: \(\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}\) \(\sqrt{25 + 144} = 13\)
3 Find reference angle: \(\tan^{-1}(|y/x|)\) \(\tan^{-1}(12/5) ≈ 67.38^\circ\)
4 Determine actual bearing based on quadrant South-east: 180° - 67.38° = 112.62°
5 Express as magnitude and bearing 13 at 112.62°

Magnitude-Bearing to Component

Converting to Components

To convert from magnitude-bearing form to component form:

Step 1: Understand the bearing

Bearings are measured clockwise from North:

North = 0°, East = 90°, South = 180°, West = 270°

Step 2: Find reference angle

Determine the angle from the nearest North-South line:

For 120° bearing: 120° - 90° = 30° from East towards South

Step 3: Calculate components

Use trigonometry to find x and y components:

For vector 10 at 120°:

\[ x = 10 \sin(120°) ≈ 8.66 \] \[ y = 10 \cos(120°) = -5 \] \[ \text{Component form: } \begin{pmatrix} 8.66 \\ -5 \end{pmatrix} \]

Quadrant Considerations

The signs of components depend on the bearing quadrant:

  1. 0°-90°: +x (east), +y (north)
  2. 90°-180°: +x (east), -y (south)
  3. 180°-270°: -x (west), -y (south)
  4. 270°-360°: -x (west), +y (north)

Example: 200° bearing is in 3rd quadrant (both negative)

Practical Conversion

Complete example of conversion from magnitude-bearing to components:

Convert 15 at 300° to component form:

300° is in 4th quadrant (west and north):

\[ x = 15 \sin(300°) = 15 \times (-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}) ≈ -12.99 \] \[ y = 15 \cos(300°) = 15 \times \frac{1}{2} = 7.5 \] \[ \text{Component form: } \begin{pmatrix} -12.99 \\ 7.5 \end{pmatrix} \]

Applications

Practical Examples

Vector conversion is useful in many real-world situations:

Navigation

A ship sails 20 km at bearing 050°. Find its east and north displacement:

\[ x = 20 \sin(50°) ≈ 15.32 \text{ km east} \] \[ y = 20 \cos(50°) ≈ 12.86 \text{ km north} \]

Physics Problems

A force of 100 N acts at 30° to the horizontal. Find horizontal and vertical components:

\[ F_x = 100 \cos(30°) ≈ 86.60 \text{ N} \] \[ F_y = 100 \sin(30°) = 50 \text{ N} \]

Wind Velocity

Wind is blowing 25 km/h from the southwest (bearing 225°). Find components:

\[ x = 25 \cos(225°) ≈ -17.68 \text{ km/h} \] \[ y = 25 \sin(225°) ≈ -17.68 \text{ km/h} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Convert \(\begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ 12 \end{pmatrix}\) to magnitude-bearing form

\[ \text{Magnitude} = \sqrt{5^2 + 12^2} = 13 \] \[ \text{Bearing} = \tan^{-1}(5/12) ≈ 22.62^\circ \] \[ \text{Answer: } 13 \text{ at } 022.62^\circ \]

Question 2

Convert 10 at 150° to component form

\[ x = 10 \sin(150°) = 5 \] \[ y = 10 \cos(150°) ≈ -8.66 \] \[ \text{Answer: } \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ -8.66 \end{pmatrix} \]

Question 3

Find the magnitude of \(\begin{pmatrix} -8 \\ -6 \end{pmatrix}\)

\[ \text{Magnitude} = \sqrt{(-8)^2 + (-6)^2} = \sqrt{64 + 36} = 10 \]

Question 4

Convert \(\begin{pmatrix} -4 \\ 7 \end{pmatrix}\) to magnitude-bearing form

\[ \text{Magnitude} = \sqrt{(-4)^2 + 7^2} ≈ 8.06 \] \[ \text{Reference angle} = \tan^{-1}(4/7) ≈ 29.74^\circ \] \[ \text{Bearing} = 360° - 29.74° ≈ 330.26° \] \[ \text{Answer: } 8.06 \text{ at } 330.26^\circ \]

Question 5

Convert 15 at 240° to component form

\[ x = 15 \sin(240°) ≈ -12.99 \] \[ y = 15 \cos(240°) = -7.5 \] \[ \text{Answer: } \begin{pmatrix} -12.99 \\ -7.5 \end{pmatrix} \]

Question 6

A plane flies 200 km at bearing 320°. Find its west and north components.

\[ x = 200 \sin(320°) ≈ -128.56 \text{ km (west)} \] \[ y = 200 \cos(320°) ≈ 153.21 \text{ km (north)} \]
