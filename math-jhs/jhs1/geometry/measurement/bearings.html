Introduction to Bearings

A bearing is a way to describe the direction of one point from another. In mathematics, we use three-digit bearings measured in degrees clockwise from North.

Point B is at a bearing of 045° from point A

Key Concepts

True bearings are always:

  • Measured from North (0°)
  • Measured in a clockwise direction
  • Expressed as three digits (e.g., 045° not 45°)

Example bearings:

  • North: 000°
  • East: 090°
  • South: 180°
  • West: 270°

Measuring Bearings

Using a Protractor

To find the bearing of point B from point A:

  1. Draw a North line at point A
  2. Draw a straight line connecting A to B
  3. Measure the angle clockwise from North to line AB
  4. Write the angle as a three-digit number (e.g., 045°)

Example 1: Simple bearing

The bearing of B from A is 060°

Example 2: Larger angle

The bearing of C from A is 210°

Back Bearings

The bearing from point B back to point A is called the back bearing. To find it:

\[ \text{Back bearing} = \text{Original bearing} \pm 180° \]

Example: If bearing of B from A is 060°, then bearing of A from B is 240° (060° + 180°)

Drawing Vectors

To draw a vector with a given bearing:

  1. Draw the starting point
  2. Draw a North line
  3. Measure the bearing angle clockwise from North
  4. Draw the vector at this angle

Example Vector

A vector of length 5cm at bearing 120°

Measuring Vector Angles

To measure the bearing of a drawn vector:

  1. Identify the starting point of the vector
  2. Draw a North line at this point
  3. Use a protractor to measure the clockwise angle from North to the vector
  4. Record as a three-digit bearing

Applications of Bearings

Real-World Uses

Bearings are used in many practical situations:

Navigation

Ships and aircraft use bearings to plot courses:

"Fly at bearing 075° for 200km"

Surveying

Surveyors use bearings to map land boundaries:

"The property line runs at bearing 315° for 50 meters"

Search and Rescue

Rescuers use bearings to locate people:

"The distress signal is at bearing 210° from our position"

Question 5

A ship sails on bearing 150° for 30km. What direction is this compass direction?

150° is South-East (30° east of south)

Question 6

From a point X, Y is at bearing 300° and Z is at bearing 040°. Which point is more northerly?

Z is more northerly (040° is closer to North than 300°)
