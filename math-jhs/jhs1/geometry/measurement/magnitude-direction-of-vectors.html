Introduction to Vectors

A vector is a quantity that has both magnitude (size) and direction. Unlike scalars which only have magnitude, vectors tell us both how much and which way.

Examples of vector quantities:

  • Displacement (5 km northeast)
  • Velocity (60 km/h due west)
  • Force (10 N at 30Â° above horizontal)

Representing Vectors

Vectors can be represented in several ways:

  1. Graphically: As an arrow where length represents magnitude and direction is shown by the arrow
  2. In words: "5 units at 30 degrees from the x-axis"
  3. Component form: \(\begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix}\) where x and y are horizontal and vertical components

Example: A vector with horizontal component 3 and vertical component 4:

\[ \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} \]

Component Form of Vectors

Column Vector Representation

The component form represents a vector as a column of numbers showing its horizontal (x) and vertical (y) components:

\[ \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} \]

Example 1: \(\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}\)

This vector moves 2 units right (positive x) and 3 units up (positive y)

Example 2: \(\begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}\)

This vector moves 1 unit left (negative x) and 4 units up (positive y)

Drawing Vectors from Components

To draw a vector given in component form:

  1. Start at the origin (0,0)
  2. Move horizontally by the x-component
  3. From there, move vertically by the y-component
  4. Draw an arrow from origin to the final point

Example: Drawing \(\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}\):

  1. Start at (0,0)
  2. Move 3 units right
  3. From there, move 2 units down
  4. Draw arrow from (0,0) to (3,-2)

Magnitude of Vectors

Calculating Magnitude

The magnitude (length) of a vector \(\begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix}\) is calculated using the Pythagorean theorem:

\[ \text{Magnitude} = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2} \]

Example 1: Find magnitude of \(\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}\)

\[ \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{9 + 16} = \sqrt{25} = 5 \]

Example 2: Find magnitude of \(\begin{pmatrix} -6 \\ 8 \end{pmatrix}\)

\[ \sqrt{(-6)^2 + 8^2} = \sqrt{36 + 64} = \sqrt{100} = 10 \]

Properties of Magnitude

  • Magnitude is always positive or zero
  • The zero vector \(\begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\) has magnitude 0
  • Multiplying a vector by a scalar k multiplies its magnitude by |k|
