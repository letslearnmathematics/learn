Introduction to Bearings

A bearing is a measurement of direction between two points. It is an angle measured in degrees from the north direction, always in a clockwise direction. Bearings are always written as three-digit numbers (e.g., 045° instead of 45°).

Example: A bearing of 060° means 60° clockwise from North.

What is a Back Bearing?

A back bearing (or reverse bearing) is the opposite direction of a given bearing. It's what you would use to return to your original starting point.

  • Forward bearing: The original bearing from point A to point B
  • Back bearing: The bearing from point B back to point A

If you walk from point A to point B on a bearing of 120°, your back bearing would be the direction to return from B to A.

Finding Back Bearing (When Forward Bearing < 180°)

The Simple Rule

When the forward bearing is less than 180°, to find the back bearing:

  1. Add 180° to the forward bearing

Example 1: Forward Bearing = 060°

\[ \text{Back Bearing} = 060° + 180° = 240° \]

Example 2: Forward Bearing = 120°

\[ \text{Back Bearing} = 120° + 180° = 300° \]

Finding Back Bearing (When Forward Bearing > 180°)

The Simple Rule

When the forward bearing is greater than 180°, to find the back bearing:

  1. Subtract 180° from the forward bearing

Example 1: Forward Bearing = 240°

\[ \text{Back Bearing} = 240° - 180° = 060° \]

Example 2: Forward Bearing = 300°

\[ \text{Back Bearing} = 300° - 180° = 120° \]

Applications

Practical Examples

Understanding back bearings is useful in many real-world situations:

Navigation

If a ship sails on a bearing of 080°, its return path would be:

\[ 080° + 180° = 260° \]

Hiking

A hiker walks on a bearing of 310°. To return, they would use:

\[ 310° - 180° = 130° \]

Aviation

An airplane flies on a bearing of 150°. Its return bearing would be:

\[ 150° + 180° = 330° \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Question 2

Question 3

Question 4

\[ 140° + 180° = 320° \]

Question 5

An airplane flies on a bearing of 290°. What is its back bearing?

\[ 290° - 180° = 110° \]

Question 6

Which is the correct back bearing for 170°: 350° or 010°?

170° + 180° = 350°

The correct back bearing is 350°
