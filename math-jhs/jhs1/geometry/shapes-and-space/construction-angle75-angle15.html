Introduction

In geometry, constructing angles accurately is an essential skill. Today we'll learn how to construct angles of 15° and 75° using just a pair of compasses and a ruler. These constructions build upon your knowledge of constructing basic angles like 60° and 90°.

\[ \text{We'll use: } 30° \rightarrow 15° \text{ and } 60° + 15° = 75° \]

Tools Needed

  • Pair of compasses: For drawing arcs and circles
  • Ruler: For drawing straight lines and measuring lengths
  • Pencil: For making temporary marks
  • Protractor: For verification (not for construction)

Constructing a 15° Angle

Method: Bisecting a 30° Angle

We'll construct a 15° angle by first constructing a 30° angle and then bisecting it:

  1. Draw a base line AB using your ruler
  2. With A as center, draw an arc that crosses AB at point C
  3. With C as center and same radius, draw another arc that intersects the first arc at D
  4. Draw line AD to form a 60° angle
  5. Bisect the 60° angle to get 30° (angle EAB)
  6. Bisect the 30° angle to get 15° (angle FAB)

Key Points:

  • Keep your compass at the same radius for all arcs in the construction
  • Make light construction lines that can be erased later
  • Verify your angle with a protractor after construction

Constructing a 75° Angle

Method: Combining 60° and 15° Angles

We'll construct a 75° angle by combining a 60° angle and a 15° angle:

  1. Construct a 60° angle (angle CAB) as before
  2. On the same base line AB, construct a 15° angle (angle DAB) adjacent to the 60° angle
  3. The combination of these two angles forms a 75° angle (angle CAD)
  4. Alternatively, you can construct a 90° angle and subtract a 15° angle from it

Step-by-Step Diagram:

Alternative Method:

  1. Construct a 90° angle (right angle)
  2. Construct a 15° angle within it
  3. The remaining angle will be 75° (90° - 15° = 75°)

Verification

After construction, always verify your angles:

  • Use a protractor to measure the constructed angles
  • Check that 15° × 5 = 75° (consistency check)
  • Ensure all construction lines are light and clean

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Construct a 15° angle using the bisection method described

  1. Construct a 60° angle first
  2. Bisect it to get 30°
  3. Bisect the 30° angle to get 15°

Question 2

Construct a 75° angle by combining two smaller angles

  1. Construct a 60° angle
  2. Construct an adjacent 15° angle
  3. The combination is 75°

Question 3

Explain how you would construct a 105° angle using similar methods

Combine a 90° angle with a 15° angle (90° + 15° = 105°)

Question 4

What angle would you get if you bisected a 75° angle?

75° ÷ 2 = 37.5°

Question 5

Construct a 45° angle and explain how it relates to 15° and 75° angles

  1. Construct a 90° angle
  2. Bisect it to get 45°
  3. Note: 45° - 30° = 15° and 45° + 30° = 75°
