Introduction

A perpendicular bisector is a line that cuts another line segment into two equal parts at a right angle (90 degrees). It's an important construction in geometry that helps us find the midpoint of a line segment and create right angles.

AB is the original line segment, CD is the perpendicular bisector

Key Terms

  • Line Segment: A straight path connecting two points with a definite length
  • Perpendicular: Two lines that meet at a right angle (90째)
  • Bisector: A line that divides something into two equal parts
  • Midpoint: The exact middle point of a line segment

Construction Steps

Using Compass and Ruler

Follow these steps to construct a perpendicular bisector of line segment AB:

  1. Draw the given line segment AB using a ruler
  2. Place the compass at point A and set its width to more than half of AB
  3. Draw arcs above and below the line segment
  4. Without changing the compass width, place it at point B and draw arcs intersecting the first arcs
  5. Use a ruler to draw a straight line through the intersection points
  6. This new line is the perpendicular bisector of AB

Detailed Construction Guide

Step 1: Draw the Line Segment

Use your ruler to draw line segment AB of any length (e.g., 6 cm). Mark points A and B clearly.

Step 2: First Arc from Point A

Open your compass to more than half the length of AB (about 4 cm for a 6 cm segment). Place the compass point on A and draw arcs above and below the line.

Step 3: Second Arc from Point B

Without changing the compass width, place the compass point on B and draw arcs that intersect the first arcs. You should have two intersection points (C and D).

Step 4: Draw the Perpendicular Bisector

Use your ruler to draw a straight line through points C and D. This line CD is the perpendicular bisector of AB.

Verification

To verify your construction:

  1. Measure the two parts of AB - they should be equal
  2. Use a protractor to check that the angles formed are 90째
  3. The bisector should pass through the midpoint of AB

Properties

Characteristics

The perpendicular bisector has several important properties:

Equal Division

It divides the original line segment into two equal parts.

If AB = 6 cm, then AC = CB = 3 cm

Right Angles

It forms four right angles (90째) where it intersects the original line segment.

Locus Property

Every point on the perpendicular bisector is equidistant from both endpoints of the original segment.

For any point P on CD, PA = PB

Relationship with Other Geometric Concepts

Perpendicular bisectors are connected to several other geometric ideas:

  • Triangles: The perpendicular bisectors of a triangle's sides meet at the circumcenter
  • Circles: The perpendicular bisector of a chord passes through the circle's center
  • Symmetry: The perpendicular bisector is a line of symmetry for the line segment

Applications

Practical Uses

Perpendicular bisectors have many real-world applications:

Construction and Architecture

Used to find center points and create right angles in buildings.

Surveying

Helps divide land parcels equally and establish boundaries.

Navigation

Used in finding equidistant points between locations.

Art and Design

Creates symmetrical patterns and balanced compositions.

Problem Solving

Perpendicular bisectors help solve various geometric problems:

  • Finding the midpoint of a line segment
  • Constructing right angles without a protractor
  • Locating the center of a circle given three points
  • Dividing shapes into equal parts

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Construct the perpendicular bisector of a 5 cm line segment using compass and ruler.

  1. Draw AB = 5 cm
  2. From A, draw arcs with radius > 2.5 cm above and below AB
  3. From B, draw intersecting arcs with same radius
  4. Connect intersection points to form perpendicular bisector

Question 2

If the perpendicular bisector of a 8 cm line segment divides it into two parts, what is the length of each part?

Each part will be half of 8 cm = 4 cm

Question 3

True or False: Every point on the perpendicular bisector is closer to one endpoint than the other.

False. Every point on the perpendicular bisector is equidistant to both endpoints.

Question 4

Construct a perpendicular bisector for a 7 cm line segment and measure the angles it forms with the original segment.

All four angles formed should measure 90째.

Question 5

What is the minimum number of arcs needed to construct a perpendicular bisector?

Four arcs (two from each endpoint).

Question 6

If point P lies on the perpendicular bisector of AB and PA = 5 cm, what is PB?

PB = 5 cm (since all points on perpendicular bisector are equidistant from A and B)
