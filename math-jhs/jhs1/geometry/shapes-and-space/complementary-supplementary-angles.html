Introduction

In geometry, angles can have special relationships when their measures add up to certain values. Two important relationships are complementary angles and supplementary angles.

Key Facts:

  • The sum of complementary angles is 90°
  • The sum of supplementary angles is 180°

Understanding Angle Relationships

Angles are measured in degrees (°). When two angles are combined, their measures add up:

\[ \text{Angle A} + \text{Angle B} = \text{Total Degrees} \]
  • Complementary: Two angles that add up to 90°
  • Supplementary: Two angles that add up to 180°

Examples:

  • 30° and 60° are complementary (30° + 60° = 90°)
  • 70° and 110° are supplementary (70° + 110° = 180°)

Complementary Angles

Finding Missing Complementary Angles

To find a missing complementary angle:

  1. Identify the known angle
  2. Subtract the known angle from 90°
  3. The result is the measure of the missing angle

Example 1: Find the complement of 35°

\[ 90° - 35° = 55° \]

The complementary angle is 55°

Example 2: Find angle x if x and 48° are complementary

\[ x + 48° = 90° \] \[ x = 90° - 48° \] \[ x = 42° \]

Supplementary Angles

Finding Missing Supplementary Angles

To find a missing supplementary angle:

  1. Identify the known angle
  2. Subtract the known angle from 180°
  3. The result is the measure of the missing angle

Example 1: Find the supplement of 75°

\[ 180° - 75° = 105° \]

The supplementary angle is 105°

Example 2: Find angle y if y and 112° are supplementary

\[ y + 112° = 180° \] \[ y = 180° - 112° \] \[ y = 68° \]

Problem Solving

Solving for Missing Angles

Apply your knowledge of complementary and supplementary angles to solve for unknown angles:

Problem Type 1: Complementary Angles

Find the angle that is complementary to:

a) 27°

\[ 90° - 27° = 63° \]

b) 15.5°

\[ 90° - 15.5° = 74.5° \]

Problem Type 2: Supplementary Angles

Find the angle that is supplementary to:

a) 83°

\[ 180° - 83° = 97° \]

b) 145.2°

\[ 180° - 145.2° = 34.8° \]

Problem Type 3: Word Problems

Two angles are complementary. One angle is twice the other. Find both angles.

Let x = smaller angle

\[ x + 2x = 90° \] \[ 3x = 90° \] \[ x = 30° \]

The angles are 30° and 60°
