Introduction

Constructing angles accurately is an important skill in geometry. In this lesson, we'll learn how to construct angles of 90° and 45° using just a pair of compasses and a ruler.

Materials Needed:

  • Pair of compasses
  • Ruler
  • Pencil
  • Clean sheet of paper

Understanding Angle Construction

Angle construction involves creating precise angles using geometric tools without measuring. We'll use:

  • Compass: For drawing arcs and circles
  • Ruler: For drawing straight lines and measuring lengths

Key terms:

  • Vertex: The point where two lines meet to form an angle
  • Bisect: To divide into two equal parts

Constructing a 90° Angle

Step-by-Step Construction

Follow these steps to construct a perfect right angle (90°):

  1. Draw a straight line AB using your ruler
  2. Mark a point P where you want the 90° angle to be
  3. Place the compass point on P and draw an arc that crosses line AB at two points (call them C and D)
  4. With the compass set wider than before, place the point on C and draw an arc above P
  5. Without changing the compass width, place the point on D and draw another arc that intersects the first arc (call this intersection E)
  6. Use your ruler to draw a straight line from P through E
  7. Angle APE is exactly 90°

Visual Representation

Key Points to Remember

  • Keep your compass at the same width when drawing the intersecting arcs
  • Make sure your pencil is sharp for precise lines
  • The arcs must intersect to form the perpendicular line

Verifying Your Construction

To check if your angle is exactly 90°:

  1. Use a protractor to measure the angle
  2. Alternatively, fold the paper so that both arms of the angle align perfectly
  3. The angle should form a perfect "L" shape

Common mistakes to avoid:

  • Changing compass width accidentally
  • Not making arcs large enough to intersect
  • Drawing lines freehand instead of using a ruler

Constructing a 45° Angle

Bisecting a 90° Angle

A 45° angle is half of a 90° angle. We'll construct it by bisecting our 90° angle:

Step-by-Step Construction

  1. First construct a 90° angle following the previous steps
  2. Place the compass point at the vertex P
  3. Draw an arc that crosses both arms of the 90° angle (call these points F and G)
  4. Place the compass point on F and draw an arc between the two arms
  5. Without changing the compass width, place the point on G and draw another arc that intersects the first arc (call this intersection H)
  6. Use your ruler to draw a straight line from P through H
  7. This line bisects the 90° angle, creating two 45° angles

Visual Representation

Why This Works

The bisection method works because:

  • We're dividing the angle into two equal parts
  • The intersecting arcs create points equidistant from both arms
  • The line through these points must divide the angle equally

Mathematically:

\[ \frac{90°}{2} = 45° \]

Common Errors

Watch out for these mistakes when constructing 45° angles:

  • Not constructing a perfect 90° angle first
  • Making the bisecting arcs too small to intersect
  • Changing compass width during construction
  • Not aligning the ruler properly when drawing the bisector

Practice Exercise

Question 1

List the steps to construct a 90° angle without using a protractor

  1. Draw a straight line AB
  2. Mark point P where the angle should be
  3. Draw an arc from P crossing AB at C and D
  4. Draw intersecting arcs from C and D above P
  5. Draw line from P through intersection point E

Question 2

How would you construct a 45° angle using only a compass and ruler?

  1. First construct a 90° angle
  2. Bisect the 90° angle by drawing arcs from its arms
  3. Connect the vertex to the intersection of these arcs

Question 3

What is the purpose of the intersecting arcs in angle construction?

The intersecting arcs create points that are equidistant from key positions, allowing us to construct precise angles and perpendicular lines without measurement.

Question 4

If you accidentally change your compass width during construction, what might happen?

Changing the compass width will make your arcs inconsistent, likely resulting in an inaccurate angle that isn't exactly 90° or 45°.

Question 5

Why is it important to keep your pencil sharp during construction?

A sharp pencil makes precise marks, ensuring your lines and arcs are accurate. A dull pencil makes thick, imprecise lines that can lead to construction errors.

Question 6

What practical application does constructing 90° angles have in daily life?

Constructing 90° angles is essential in building furniture (ensuring square corners), hanging pictures straight, laying tiles, and any construction work requiring perpendicular lines.
