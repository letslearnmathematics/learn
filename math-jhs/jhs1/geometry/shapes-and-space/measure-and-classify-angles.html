Introduction

Angles are formed when two or more lines or line segments meet or intersect at a common point called vertex. The measure of the angle is the amount of rotation or turn around the fixed point.

Key facts about angles:

  • Formed by two lines/rays meeting at a vertex
  • Measured in degrees (°)
  • Can be classified by their size
  • Important in geometry and real-world applications

Angle Notation

Angles are named using three points, with the vertex point in the middle:

This angle is written as: \(\angle ABC\)

Angle Types

Classification by Size

Angles can be classified into five main types based on their measurement:

1. Acute Angles

Less than 90°

Examples: 30°, 45°, 60°, 75°

2. Right Angles

Exactly 90°

Forms perfect "L" shape

3. Obtuse Angles

Between 90° and 180°

Examples: 120°, 135°, 150°

4. Straight Angles

Exactly 180°

Forms a straight line

5. Reflex Angles

Between 180° and 360°

Examples: 210°, 270°, 300°

Angle Relationships

Understanding angle types helps in solving geometry problems:

Angle Type Measurement Range
Acute 0° < angle < 90°
Right Exactly 90°
Obtuse 90° < angle < 180°
Straight Exactly 180°
Reflex 180° < angle < 360°

Measuring Angles

Using a Protractor

To measure angles accurately, we use a protractor:

  1. Place the protractor - Align the center with the vertex
  2. Align the baseline - Match one ray with the 0° line
  3. Read the measurement - Where the other ray crosses the scale
  4. Classify the angle - Determine if it's acute, right, obtuse or reflex

Example 1: Measuring an Acute Angle

This angle measures 45° → acute angle

Example 2: Measuring an Obtuse Angle

This angle measures 120° → obtuse angle

Common Mistakes

Watch out for these common errors when measuring angles:

  • Misaligning the protractor center with the vertex
  • Reading from the wrong scale (inner vs outer)
  • Confusing acute and obtuse angles
  • Forgetting to check if the angle is reflex (>180°)

Drawing Angles

Using a Protractor

Follow these steps to draw angles accurately:

Step 1: Draw a baseline

Use a ruler to draw a straight line (this will be one side of your angle)

Step 2: Mark the vertex

Place a point at one end of your line - this will be the angle's vertex

Step 3: Position the protractor

Place the protractor's center on the vertex, aligning the baseline with your drawn line

Step 4: Mark the angle

Make a small dot at the desired angle measurement (e.g., 60°)

Step 5: Complete the angle

Remove the protractor and draw a line from the vertex through your marked point

Example: Drawing a 120° Angle
