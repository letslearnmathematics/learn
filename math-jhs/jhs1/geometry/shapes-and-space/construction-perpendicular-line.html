Introduction

Perpendicular lines are lines that intersect at right angles (90Â°). In construction and geometry, being able to construct perpendicular lines accurately is essential for creating precise diagrams and solving problems.

Tools Needed

To construct perpendicular lines, you will need:

  • Ruler: For drawing straight lines and measuring lengths
  • Pair of compasses: For drawing arcs and circles
  • Pencil: For making marks and constructions
  • Protractor (optional): For verifying right angles

Copying a Line Segment

Constructing a Copy of a Given Line Segment

Before constructing perpendicular lines, it's important to know how to accurately copy a line segment:

  1. Draw a straight line with your ruler (this will be your reference line)
  2. Place the compass point on one end of the original line segment
  3. Adjust the compass width to exactly match the length of the original segment
  4. Without changing the compass width, place the point on your reference line and draw an arc
  5. The intersection of the arc with your reference line marks the endpoint of your copied segment

Verification

To verify your copied segment is accurate:

  1. Measure both segments with your ruler
  2. They should have exactly the same length
  3. You can also compare them by placing one over the other

Perpendicular Construction

Constructing a Perpendicular at a Point on a Line

Follow these steps to construct a perpendicular line at a specific point on a line segment:

  1. Draw your original line segment AB and mark point P where you want the perpendicular
  2. With P as center, draw an arc that intersects AB at two points (C and D)
  3. Using C as center, draw an arc above P (radius greater than CP)
  4. Using D as center, draw another arc intersecting the first arc (point E)
  5. Draw a line from P through E - this is your perpendicular line

Dropping a Perpendicular from an External Point

To construct a perpendicular from a point outside the line to the line:

  1. Draw your original line segment AB and mark external point P
  2. With P as center, draw an arc intersecting AB at two points (C and D)
  3. Using C as center, draw an arc below AB
  4. Using D as center, draw another arc intersecting the first arc (point E)
  5. Draw a line from P through E - this perpendicular meets AB at right angles

Verifying Right Angles

After construction, verify your perpendicular lines:

  • Use a protractor to confirm the angle is exactly 90Â°
  • Check that the distance from the intersection point to any point on one line is equal when measured along the perpendicular
  • Fold the paper along the constructed perpendicular - the lines should align perfectly when folded

Applications

Practical Uses

Perpendicular constructions are used in many real-world situations:

Architecture and Construction

Ensuring walls meet at right angles in building plans

Engineering Drawings

Creating precise technical diagrams and blueprints

Coordinate Geometry

Plotting graphs with perpendicular x and y axes

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Construct a perpendicular to line AB at point C, where C is exactly at the midpoint of AB (AB = 8cm)

  1. Draw AB = 8cm and mark midpoint C at 4cm
  2. With C as center, draw arcs intersecting AB at P and Q
  3. With P and Q as centers, draw intersecting arcs above C
  4. Draw line through C and the intersection point

Question 2

From point D located 3cm above line EF, drop a perpendicular to EF

  1. Draw EF and mark D 3cm above it
  2. With D as center, draw arc intersecting EF at G and H
  3. With G and H as centers, draw arcs below EF
  4. Draw line from D through intersection point to EF

Question 3

Construct a rectangle with sides 5cm and 3cm using perpendicular constructions

  1. Draw base line = 5cm
  2. Construct perpendiculars at both ends
  3. Mark 3cm on both perpendiculars
  4. Connect the endpoints to complete rectangle

Question 4

Construct a right-angled triangle with perpendicular sides of 4cm and 6cm

  1. Draw base line = 6cm
  2. Construct perpendicular at one end
  3. Mark 4cm on perpendicular
  4. Connect endpoints to complete triangle

Question 5

Construct two perpendicular lines that intersect at a point 2cm from each end of a 6cm line segment

  1. Draw line AB = 6cm
  2. Mark point P 2cm from A
  3. Construct perpendicular at P
  4. Extend perpendicular in both directions
