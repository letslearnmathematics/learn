Introduction

A coordinate plane is a two-dimensional surface formed by two perpendicular number lines: the x-axis (horizontal) and y-axis (vertical). We use coordinates (x, y) to locate points on this plane.

Key Components:

  • Origin (0,0): Where x-axis and y-axis intersect
  • Quadrants: Four regions created by the axes
  • Coordinates (x, y): First number is x-coordinate (horizontal), second is y-coordinate (vertical)

Important Lines

Some special lines on the coordinate plane:

  • Line 1 (y-axis): x = 0
  • Line 2 (x-axis): y = 0
  • Line 3 (y = x): The line where x and y values are equal
  • Line 4 (y = -x): The line where y is the negative of x
  • Line 5 (y = -1): A horizontal line one unit below the x-axis

Plotting Points and Shapes

Plotting Points

To plot a point with coordinates (a, b):

  1. Start at the origin (0,0)
  2. Move 'a' units horizontally (right if positive, left if negative)
  3. Move 'b' units vertically (up if positive, down if negative)
  4. Mark the point at this location

Example 1: Plotting (3, 2)

Move 3 units right, then 2 units up

Example 2: Plotting (-1, -4)

Move 1 unit left, then 4 units down

Plotting Shapes

To plot a shape (like a triangle):

  1. Plot all the vertices (corners) using their coordinates
  2. Connect the points with straight lines in order
  3. Close the shape by connecting back to the first point

Example: Plot triangle ABC with vertices A(1,1), B(3,1), C(2,3)

  1. Plot point A at (1,1)
  2. Plot point B at (3,1)
  3. Plot point C at (2,3)
  4. Connect A-B, B-C, and C-A

Reflections on the Coordinate Plane

Reflection Over Axes

A reflection flips a point or shape over a line (called the line of reflection) to create a mirror image.

Reflection Over y-axis (x=0)

Point A(1,1) reflected over y-axis becomes A'(-1,1)

The x-coordinate changes sign, y-coordinate stays same

Reflection Over x-axis (y=0)

Point A(1,1) reflected over x-axis becomes A'(1,-1)

The y-coordinate changes sign, x-coordinate stays same

Reflection Over y=x

Point P(1,-1) reflected over y=x becomes P'(-1,1)

The x and y coordinates swap places

Reflecting Shapes

To reflect a shape:

  1. Reflect each vertex (corner point) individually
  2. Plot the reflected points
  3. Connect them in the same order as the original shape

Example: Reflect triangle ABC (A(1,1), B(3,1), C(2,3)) over y-axis:

  1. A(1,1) → A'(-1,1)
  2. B(3,1) → B'(-3,1)
  3. C(2,3) → C'(-2,3)
  4. Connect A'-B'-C' to form the reflected triangle

Coordinate Rules for Reflections

General Reflection Rules

These rules tell us how coordinates change under reflection:

Reflection Over x-axis

\[ (a, b) \rightarrow (a, -b) \]

x stays same, y changes sign

Reflection Over y-axis

\[ (a, b) \rightarrow (-a, b) \]

y stays same, x changes sign

Reflection Over y = x

\[ (a, b) \rightarrow (b, a) \]

x and y coordinates swap

Reflection Over y = -x

\[ (a, b) \rightarrow (-b, -a) \]

swap and change both signs

How to Remember

Remember these patterns:

  • x-axis reflection: Think "x stays, y flips"
  • y-axis reflection: Think "y stays, x flips"
  • y=x reflection: Think "swap the numbers"
  • y=-x reflection: Think "swap and flip both"

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Plot the point (2, -3) and its reflection over the x-axis.

Original: (2, -3)

Reflected over x-axis: (2, 3)

Question 2

What is the image of (-4, 5) under reflection in the y-axis?

Reflected over y-axis: (4, 5)

Question 3

Plot triangle PQR with vertices P(1,2), Q(3,2), R(2,4) and draw its reflection over y=x.

Reflected vertices:

  • P'(2,1)
  • Q'(2,3)
  • R'(4,2)

Question 4

If point (5, -2) is reflected over y=-x, what are the coordinates of its image?

Reflected over y=-x: (2, -5)

Question 5

Draw rectangle ABCD with A(1,1), B(4,1), C(4,3), D(1,3) and its reflection over y=-1.

Reflected vertices:

  • A'(1,-3)
  • B'(4,-3)
  • C'(4,-5)
  • D'(1,-5)

Question 6

What is the reflection of (0,6) over the x-axis?

Reflected over x-axis: (0, -6)
