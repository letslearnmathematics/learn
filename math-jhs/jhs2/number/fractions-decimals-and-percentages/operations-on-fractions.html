Introduction to Fractions

A fraction represents a part of a whole or, more generally, any number of equal parts. It consists of two numbers separated by a line:

\[ \frac{\text{Numerator}}{\text{Denominator}} \]

Numerator:

The top number that represents how many parts we have.

Denominator:

The bottom number that represents how many equal parts the whole is divided into.

Example: In the fraction \(\frac{3}{4}\):

  • 3 is the numerator (we have 3 parts)
  • 4 is the denominator (the whole is divided into 4 equal parts)
  • It represents 3 out of 4 equal parts of a whole

Types of Fractions

Proper Fractions

A fraction where the numerator is less than the denominator.

Examples:

\[ \frac{1}{2}, \frac{3}{4}, \frac{7}{8} \]

Value is always less than 1.

Improper Fractions

A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator.

Examples:

\[ \frac{5}{2}, \frac{7}{3}, \frac{4}{4} \]

Value is always greater than or equal to 1.

Mixed Numbers

A combination of a whole number and a proper fraction.

Examples:

\[ 2\frac{1}{3}, 5\frac{3}{4}, 1\frac{1}{2} \]

Can be converted to improper fractions and vice versa.

Conversion Methods

Mixed to Improper:

Multiply whole number by denominator, add numerator, keep denominator:

\[ 2\frac{1}{3} = \frac{(2 \times 3) + 1}{3} = \frac{7}{3} \]

Improper to Mixed:

Divide numerator by denominator, quotient is whole number, remainder is numerator:

\[ \frac{7}{3} = 2\frac{1}{3} \]

Equivalent Fractions

Fractions that represent the same value, even though they look different.

Examples:

\[ \frac{1}{2} = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{4}{8} \]

Created by multiplying or dividing numerator and denominator by the same number.

Basic Operations on Fractions

Addition

Same Denominators

Add numerators, keep denominator:

\[ \frac{a}{c} + \frac{b}{c} = \frac{a + b}{c} \]

Example:

\[ \frac{2}{5} + \frac{1}{5} = \frac{3}{5} \]

Different Denominators

Find LCD, convert fractions, then add:

  1. Find Least Common Denominator (LCD)
  2. Convert fractions to equivalent fractions with LCD
  3. Add numerators, keep denominator

Example:

\[ \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{3} = \frac{3}{6} + \frac{2}{6} = \frac{5}{6} \]

Subtraction

Same Denominators

Subtract numerators, keep denominator:

\[ \frac{a}{c} - \frac{b}{c} = \frac{a - b}{c} \]

Example:

\[ \frac{5}{7} - \frac{2}{7} = \frac{3}{7} \]

Different Denominators

Find LCD, convert fractions, then subtract:

  1. Find Least Common Denominator (LCD)
  2. Convert fractions to equivalent fractions with LCD
  3. Subtract numerators, keep denominator

Example:

\[ \frac{3}{4} - \frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{4} - \frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{4} \]

Multiplication

Multiply numerators together and denominators together:

\[ \frac{a}{b} \times \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a \times c}{b \times d} \]

Example:

\[ \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{4}{5} = \frac{8}{15} \]

Simplification:

Can simplify before multiplying by canceling common factors:

\[ \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{8}{9} = \frac{1}{1} \times \frac{2}{3} = \frac{2}{3} \]

Division

Multiply by the reciprocal of the second fraction:

\[ \frac{a}{b} \div \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a}{b} \times \frac{d}{c} = \frac{a \times d}{b \times c} \]

Example:

\[ \frac{3}{4} \div \frac{2}{5} = \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{5}{2} = \frac{15}{8} \]

Reciprocal:

The reciprocal of a fraction is obtained by flipping numerator and denominator:

\[ \text{Reciprocal of } \frac{a}{b} \text{ is } \frac{b}{a} \]

Word Problems with Fractions

Problem-Solving Approach

  1. Read the problem carefully
  2. Identify what is being asked
  3. Determine which operation(s) to use
  4. Perform the calculations
  5. Check if the answer makes sense

Practice Problems

Problem 1

John ate \(\frac{1}{4}\) of a pizza and Mary ate \(\frac{1}{3}\) of the same pizza. What fraction of the pizza did they eat together?

Solution:

Total eaten = \(\frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{3}\)

LCD of 4 and 3 is 12

\(\frac{3}{12} + \frac{4}{12} = \frac{7}{12}\)

They ate \(\frac{7}{12}\) of the pizza together.

Problem 2

A recipe requires \(\frac{3}{4}\) cup of flour. If you want to make \(\frac{1}{2}\) of the recipe, how much flour should you use?

Solution:

\(\frac{1}{2}\) of \(\frac{3}{4}\) cup = \(\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{3}{8}\) cup

You should use \(\frac{3}{8}\) cup of flour.

Problem 3

A tank is \(\frac{5}{6}\) full. After using \(\frac{1}{3}\) of the water, what fraction of the tank remains?

Solution:

Water used = \(\frac{1}{3}\) of \(\frac{5}{6}\) = \(\frac{5}{18}\)

Water remaining = \(\frac{5}{6} - \frac{5}{18} = \frac{15}{18} - \frac{5}{18} = \frac{10}{18} = \frac{5}{9}\)

\(\frac{5}{9}\) of the tank remains.

Problem 4

If \(\frac{2}{5}\) of a number is 12, what is \(\frac{3}{4}\) of that number?

Solution:

Let the number be \(x\)

\(\frac{2}{5}x = 12\)

\(x = 12 \times \frac{5}{2} = 30\)

\(\frac{3}{4}\) of 30 = \(\frac{3}{4} \times 30 = \frac{90}{4} = 22.5\)

The answer is 22.5.
