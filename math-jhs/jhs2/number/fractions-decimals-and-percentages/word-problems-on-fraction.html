Introduction

Fraction word problems involve applying your understanding of fractions to solve real-world situations. These problems often require you to perform basic operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division) with fractions and interpret the results in context.

In this lesson, we'll review different types of word problems involving fractions, including:

  • Calculating perimeter and area with fractional sides
  • Converting fractions to percentages
  • Solving problems with mixed fractions
  • Comparing fractions in real-world contexts

Perimeter & Area of Rectangles

Rectangle with Fractional Sides

When a rectangle has sides expressed as fractions, we calculate its perimeter and area using the same formulas as with whole numbers, but performing fraction operations.

Perimeter

Sum of all sides: \( P = 2 \times (length + width) \)

Area

Product of length and width: \( A = length \times width \)

Example Problem:

A rectangle has length \( \frac{3}{4} \)m and width \( \frac{1}{2} \)m. Find its:

(i) Perimeter

(ii) Area

Solution:

(i) Perimeter:

\[ P = 2 \times \left(\frac{3}{4} + \frac{1}{2}\right) = 2 \times \left(\frac{3}{4} + \frac{2}{4}\right) = 2 \times \frac{5}{4} = \frac{10}{4} = \frac{5}{2} \text{m} \]

(ii) Area:

\[ A = \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{3 \times 1}{4 \times 2} = \frac{3}{8} \text{m}^2 \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Find the (i) perimeter and (ii) area of a rectangle with length \( \frac{5}{6} \)cm and width \( \frac{2}{3} \)cm.

(i) Perimeter:

\[ P = 2 \times \left(\frac{5}{6} + \frac{2}{3}\right) = 2 \times \left(\frac{5}{6} + \frac{4}{6}\right) = 2 \times \frac{9}{6} = \frac{18}{6} = 3 \text{cm} \]

(ii) Area:

\[ A = \frac{5}{6} \times \frac{2}{3} = \frac{10}{18} = \frac{5}{9} \text{cm}^2 \]

Fractions to Percentage

Converting Fractions to Percentages

To convert a fraction to a percentage, multiply by 100%:

\[ \text{Percentage} = \left(\frac{\text{Part}}{\text{Whole}}\right) \times 100\% \]

Example Problem:

Faako answers 42 out of 60 questions correctly. What percentage of her answers are correct?

Solution:

Fraction correct: \( \frac{42}{60} \)

Convert to percentage:

\[ \frac{42}{60} \times 100\% = 0.7 \times 100\% = 70\% \]

Practice Exercise

Question 2

A student scored 35 out of 50 on a test. What percentage is this?

Fraction correct: \( \frac{35}{50} \)

Convert to percentage:

\[ \frac{35}{50} \times 100\% = 0.7 \times 100\% = 70\% \]

Mixed Fractions Problems

Combining Fractions

Some problems involve combining different fractions of a whole. To find the remaining fraction:

\[ \text{Remaining} = 1 - \left(\text{Sum of known fractions}\right) \]

Example Problem:

In a school \( \frac{2}{3} \) of the students eat from the school feeding programme, \( \frac{1}{4} \) bring their packed lunch, and the rest go home to eat. What fraction of the students go home for lunch?

Solution:

Total accounted for:

\[ \frac{2}{3} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{8}{12} + \frac{3}{12} = \frac{11}{12} \]

Fraction that go home:

\[ 1 - \frac{11}{12} = \frac{12}{12} - \frac{11}{12} = \frac{1}{12} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 3

A farmer plants \( \frac{3}{8} \) of his field with maize, \( \frac{1}{4} \) with beans, and the rest with yams. What fraction is planted with yams?

Total planted with maize and beans:

\[ \frac{3}{8} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{8} + \frac{2}{8} = \frac{5}{8} \]

Fraction planted with yams:

\[ 1 - \frac{5}{8} = \frac{8}{8} - \frac{5}{8} = \frac{3}{8} \]

Comparing Fractions

Strength of Mixtures

When comparing mixtures with different fractions of ingredients, the mixture with the higher fraction of the key ingredient is stronger.

Example Problem:

Esi and Fusena prepared an orange drink by mixing orange squash and water. Esi's drink was made of \( \frac{2}{7} \) orange squash and Fusena's was made up of \( \frac{1}{4} \) orange squash. Whose drink tastes stronger of orange?

Solution:

Compare \( \frac{2}{7} \) and \( \frac{1}{4} \):

Convert to common denominator (28):

\[ \frac{2}{7} = \frac{8}{28} \] \[ \frac{1}{4} = \frac{7}{28} \]

Since \( \frac{8}{28} > \frac{7}{28} \), Esi's drink has more orange squash and tastes stronger.

Practice Exercise

Question 4

Two friends make lemonade. Kwame uses \( \frac{3}{10} \) lemon juice while Ama uses \( \frac{2}{7} \) lemon juice. Whose lemonade is more lemony?

Compare \( \frac{3}{10} \) and \( \frac{2}{7} \):

Convert to common denominator (70):

\[ \frac{3}{10} = \frac{21}{70} \] \[ \frac{2}{7} = \frac{20}{70} \]

Since \( \frac{21}{70} > \frac{20}{70} \), Kwame's lemonade is more lemony.
