Introduction

Adding and subtracting large numbers (more than four digits) requires a solid understanding of place value and systematic methods. In this lesson, we'll explore two powerful techniques: partitioning (expanded form) and the place value system.

These methods work for both whole numbers and decimal numbers, helping you break down complex calculations into simpler, more manageable steps.

Partitioning Method

What is Partitioning?

Partitioning means breaking down a number into its constituent parts based on place value. This is also known as writing a number in expanded form.

For example, the number 45,678 can be partitioned as:

\[ 45,678 = 40,000 + 5,000 + 600 + 70 + 8 \]

Adding Using Partitioning

To add large numbers using partitioning:

  1. Write each number in expanded form
  2. Add the corresponding place values
  3. Combine the results

Example: Add 23,456 and 12,345

\[ \begin{align*} 23,456 &= 20,000 + 3,000 + 400 + 50 + 6 \\ 12,345 &= 10,000 + 2,000 + 300 + 40 + 5 \\ \hline \text{Sum} &= 30,000 + 5,000 + 700 + 90 + 11 \\ &= 30,000 + 5,000 + 700 + 100 + 1 \quad (\text{regrouping}) \\ &= 30,000 + 5,000 + 800 + 1 \\ &= 35,801 \end{align*} \]

Subtracting Using Partitioning

To subtract large numbers using partitioning:

  1. Write each number in expanded form
  2. Subtract the corresponding place values
  3. Combine the results (adjusting if needed)

Example: Subtract 12,345 from 23,456

\[ \begin{align*} 23,456 &= 20,000 + 3,000 + 400 + 50 + 6 \\ 12,345 &= 10,000 + 2,000 + 300 + 40 + 5 \\ \hline \text{Difference} &= (20,000-10,000) + (3,000-2,000) + (400-300) + (50-40) + (6-5) \\ &= 10,000 + 1,000 + 100 + 10 + 1 \\ &= 11,111 \end{align*} \]

Place Value System

Understanding Place Value

The place value system organizes numbers based on the position of each digit. Each position represents a power of 10, making it easier to perform calculations.

For the number 45,678:

Ten Thousands Thousands Hundreds Tens Units
4 5 6 7 8

Adding Using Place Value

To add large numbers using place value:

  1. Write numbers vertically aligned by place value
  2. Add from right to left (units first)
  3. Carry over when sums exceed 9

Example: Add 23,456 and 12,345

\[ \begin{array}{r} \phantom{1}23,456 \\ +\phantom{2}12,345 \\ \hline \phantom{1}35,801 \\ \end{array} \]

Step-by-step:

  1. Units: 6 + 5 = 11 (write 1, carry 1)
  2. Tens: 5 + 4 + 1 (carry) = 10 (write 0, carry 1)
  3. Hundreds: 4 + 3 + 1 (carry) = 8
  4. Thousands: 3 + 2 = 5
  5. Ten Thousands: 2 + 1 = 3

Subtracting Using Place Value

To subtract large numbers using place value:

  1. Write numbers vertically aligned by place value
  2. Subtract from right to left (units first)
  3. Borrow when needed

Example: Subtract 12,345 from 23,456

\[ \begin{array}{r} 23,456 \\ -\phantom{2}12,345 \\ \hline \phantom{1}11,111 \\ \end{array} \]

Step-by-step:

  1. Units: 6 - 5 = 1
  2. Tens: 5 - 4 = 1
  3. Hundreds: 4 - 3 = 1
  4. Thousands: 3 - 2 = 1
  5. Ten Thousands: 2 - 1 = 1

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Use place value to add: 56,789 + 34,567

\[ \begin{array}{r} \phantom{1}56,789 \\ +\phantom{5}34,567 \\ \hline \phantom{1}91,356 \\ \end{array} \]

Step-by-step:

  1. Units: 9 + 7 = 16 (write 6, carry 1)
  2. Tens: 8 + 6 + 1 = 15 (write 5, carry 1)
  3. Hundreds: 7 + 5 + 1 = 13 (write 3, carry 1)
  4. Thousands: 6 + 4 + 1 = 11 (write 1, carry 1)
  5. Ten Thousands: 5 + 3 + 1 = 9

Question 2

Use place value to subtract: 78,901 - 56,789

\[ \begin{array}{r} 78,901 \\ -\phantom{7}56,789 \\ \hline \phantom{1}22,112 \\ \end{array} \]

Step-by-step:

  1. Units: 1 - 9 (need to borrow)
  2. Tens: 0 - 1 (after borrow) = 9 - 8 = 1
  3. Hundreds: 8 - 1 (after borrow) = 7 - 7 = 0
  4. Thousands: 7 - 1 (after borrow) = 6 - 6 = 0
  5. Ten Thousands: 7 - 5 = 2

Question 3

Use place value to add: 123,456 + 456,789

\[ \begin{array}{r} 123,456 \\ +456,789 \\ \hline 580,245 \\ \end{array} \]

Step-by-step:

  1. Units: 6 + 9 = 15 (write 5, carry 1)
  2. Tens: 5 + 8 + 1 = 14 (write 4, carry 1)
  3. Hundreds: 4 + 7 + 1 = 12 (write 2, carry 1)
  4. Thousands: 3 + 6 + 1 = 10 (write 0, carry 1)
  5. Ten Thousands: 2 + 5 + 1 = 8
  6. Hundred Thousands: 1 + 4 = 5

Decimal Numbers

Decimal Place Value

Decimal numbers extend the place value system to the right of the decimal point. Each position represents a negative power of 10.

For the number 45.678:

Tens Units Decimal Point Tenths Hundredths Thousandths
4 5 . 6 7 8

Adding Decimals

To add decimal numbers:

  1. Align the decimal points vertically
  2. Add zeros to make numbers the same length
  3. Add as with whole numbers
  4. Place the decimal point in the answer directly below the others

Example: Add 23.456 and 12.345

\[ \begin{array}{r} \phantom{1}23.456 \\ +\phantom{2}12.345 \\ \hline \phantom{1}35.801 \\ \end{array} \]

Subtracting Decimals

To subtract decimal numbers:

  1. Align the decimal points vertically
  2. Add zeros to make numbers the same length
  3. Subtract as with whole numbers
  4. Place the decimal point in the answer directly below the others

Example: Subtract 12.345 from 23.456

\[ \begin{array}{r} 23.456 \\ -\phantom{2}12.345 \\ \hline \phantom{1}11.111 \\ \end{array} \]
