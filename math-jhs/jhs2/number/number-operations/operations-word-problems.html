Introduction

Decimal word problems involve real-life situations where we need to perform calculations with decimal numbers. These problems often require us to use addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division to find solutions.

In this lesson, we will learn how to solve various types of word problems involving decimals. We'll start with basic operations and then move to more complex problems involving multiple steps and data presented in tables.

Basic Operations with Decimals

Addition and Subtraction

When adding or subtracting decimals:

  1. Align the decimal points vertically
  2. Add zeros if needed to make all numbers have the same number of decimal places
  3. Perform the operation as with whole numbers
  4. Place the decimal point in the answer directly below the other decimal points

Example: 12.45 + 3.6

Solution:

\[ \begin{array}{r} \phantom{0}12.45 \\ +\phantom{12}3.60 \\ \hline \phantom{+}16.05 \\ \hline \end{array} \]

Multiplication and Division

Multiplication:

  1. Multiply as with whole numbers
  2. Count the total number of decimal places in the factors
  3. Place the decimal point in the product so it has that many decimal places

Example: 2.5 × 1.3

Solution:

\[ 25 \times 13 = 325 \quad \text{(2 decimal places total)} \\ 2.5 \times 1.3 = 3.25 \]

Division:

  1. If the divisor is a decimal, multiply both numbers by 10, 100, etc. to make it a whole number
  2. Divide as with whole numbers
  3. Place the decimal point in the quotient directly above its position in the dividend

Example: 6.4 ÷ 0.4

Solution:

\[ 6.4 ÷ 0.4 = (6.4 \times 10) ÷ (0.4 \times 10) = 64 ÷ 4 = 16 \]

Solving Word Problems

Problem-Solving Steps

  1. Read the problem carefully
  2. Identify what is being asked
  3. Determine the operation(s) needed
  4. Solve the problem
  5. Check your answer for reasonableness

Practice Problems

Problem (i)

Kofi bought 8 notebooks at GHȻ 12.00 each. Ama bought 12 pens at GHȻ 5.00 each. How much altogether did they spend on the items?

Solution:

Kofi's notebooks: 8 × GHȻ 12.00 = GHȻ 96.00

Ama's pens: 12 × GHȻ 5.00 = GHȻ 60.00

Total spent: GHȻ 96.00 + GHȻ 60.00 = GHȻ 156.00

Answer: They spent GHȻ 156.00 altogether.

Problem (ii)

A man gave an amount of GHȻ 2477.25 to be shared equally among his three children. How much did each receive?

Solution:

Total amount: GHȻ 2477.25

Number of children: 3

Each child's share: GHȻ 2477.25 ÷ 3 = GHȻ 825.75

Answer: Each child received GHȻ 825.75.

Problem (iii)

On Adwoa's birthday, her father bought her a pack of chocolate containing 250 bars. If Adwoa took 90 bars of the chocolates and gave the rest to her four friends to share equally, how many bars of chocolates did each receive?

Solution:

Total chocolates: 250 bars

Adwoa took: 90 bars

Remaining chocolates: 250 - 90 = 160 bars

Number of friends: 4

Each friend's share: 160 ÷ 4 = 40 bars

Answer: Each friend received 40 bars.

Problem (iv)

Mrs Yaboi bought 25.25 metres of cloth for her five children. If they share the material equally, how many metres of cloth did each receive?

Solution:

Total cloth: 25.25 metres

Number of children: 5

Each child's share: 25.25 ÷ 5 = 5.05 metres

Answer: Each child received 5.05 metres of cloth.

Solving Word Problems with Tables

Many real-world problems present data in tables. To solve these:

  1. Carefully read the table and understand what each column represents
  2. Identify the data needed to solve the problem
  3. Perform the necessary calculations
  4. Check if your answer makes sense in the context of the problem

Example Table:

Item Price per unit (GHȻ) Quantity
Notebook 12.50 5
Pen 3.75 8
Pencil 1.25 12

Question: What is the total cost of all items in the table?

Solution:

Notebooks: 5 × GHȻ 12.50 = GHȻ 62.50

Pens: 8 × GHȻ 3.75 = GHȻ 30.00

Pencils: 12 × GHȻ 1.25 = GHȻ 15.00

Total: GHȻ 62.50 + GHȻ 30.00 + GHȻ 15.00 = GHȻ 107.50

Practice Problem with Table

Problem

The table below shows items bought by a student:

Item Unit Price (GHȻ) Quantity
Exercise Book 8.50 4
Textbook 35.75 2
Calculator 120.00 1

Questions:

  1. What is the total cost of all items?
  2. If the student pays with GHȻ 300.00, how much change will he receive?

Solution 1:

Exercise Books: 4 × GHȻ 8.50 = GHȻ 34.00

Textbooks: 2 × GHȻ 35.75 = GHȻ 71.50

Calculator: 1 × GHȻ 120.00 = GHȻ 120.00

Total: GHȻ 34.00 + GHȻ 71.50 + GHȻ 120.00 = GHȻ 225.50

Solution 2:

Amount paid: GHȻ 300.00

Total cost: GHȻ 225.50

Change: GHȻ 300.00 - GHȻ 225.50 = GHȻ 74.50

Answers:

  1. Total cost is GHȻ 225.50
  2. Change received is GHȻ 74.50
