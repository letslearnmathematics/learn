Introduction

Mental mathematics refers to solving math problems in your head without using paper, calculators, or other tools. Developing strong mental math skills helps you solve problems faster and more efficiently, especially word problems that require quick calculations.

In this lesson, we'll explore various strategies to solve word problems mentally, play mental math games to build fluency, and learn shortcut methods to make calculations easier.

Mental Math Strategies

Key Strategies

Here are some powerful mental math strategies:

  1. Break numbers apart: Split numbers into easier-to-manage parts (e.g., 37 + 48 = (30 + 40) + (7 + 8))
  2. Use complements: Find numbers that add up to 10, 100, etc. (e.g., 76 + 24 = 100)
  3. Round and adjust: Round numbers to make calculations easier, then adjust (e.g., 298 × 5 = 300 × 5 - 10)
  4. Look for patterns: Recognize multiplication patterns (e.g., 5 × any even number ends with 0)
  5. Use known facts: Memorize basic math facts to build on (times tables, squares, etc.)

Breakdown Method

Break numbers into tens and ones for easier addition:

Example: 47 + 56

Step 1: Break into tens and ones: (40 + 50) + (7 + 6)

Step 2: Add tens: 40 + 50 = 90

Step 3: Add ones: 7 + 6 = 13

Step 4: Combine: 90 + 13 = 103

Compensation Method

Adjust numbers to make calculations easier, then compensate:

Example: 203 - 98

Step 1: Subtract 100 instead of 98: 203 - 100 = 103

Step 2: Add back the extra 2 you subtracted: 103 + 2 = 105

Word Problem Games

Mental Math Word Games

Playing word problem games helps develop fluency in mental calculations. Here are some engaging games:

1. Number Chain

Start with a number. Each student applies an operation (add, subtract, multiply, divide) based on a word problem prompt. The chain continues until someone makes a mistake.

Example: Start with 50. "Add the number of days in a week." (50 + 7 = 57) Next: "Subtract the number of sides on a triangle." (57 - 3 = 54), etc.

2. Story Problem Race

Present a word problem. Students race to solve it mentally and shout the answer. First correct answer wins a point.

Example: "If a box contains 24 oranges and you remove 7, how many remain?"

3. Mental Math Bingo

Create bingo cards with answers to word problems. Read problems aloud, students solve mentally and mark answers.

Shortcut Methods

Quick Calculation Techniques

Learn these shortcut methods to solve problems faster:

Multiplying by 5

Multiply by 10 and halve the result: 24 × 5 = (24 × 10) ÷ 2 = 240 ÷ 2 = 120

Multiplying by 11 (for 2-digit numbers)

Add the digits and put the sum in the middle: 35 × 11 = 3 (3+5) 5 = 385

Squaring numbers ending with 5

Multiply the first digit by (itself + 1), then add 25: 65² = (6 × 7) = 42, then add 25 → 4225

Percentage shortcuts

10% = divide by 10; 20% = divide by 5; 25% = divide by 4; 50% = divide by 2

Speed Drill

Calculate 45 × 5 using the shortcut method

Question 3

Word Problem Practice

Problem 1

A school has 12 classrooms with 28 students each. How many students are there in total?

Mental strategy: Break 28 into 30 - 2

12 × 30 = 360

12 × 2 = 24

360 - 24 = 336 students

Problem 2

If a book costs Gh₵75 and you buy 8 books, how much do you spend?

Mental strategy: Use the 8-times table shortcut (8 × 70 + 8 × 5)

8 × 70 = 560

8 × 5 = 40

560 + 40 = Gh₵600

Problem 3

A farmer harvested 365 oranges on Monday and 238 on Tuesday. How many oranges were harvested in total?

Mental strategy: Round and adjust

365 + 240 = 605

605 - 2 = 603 oranges
