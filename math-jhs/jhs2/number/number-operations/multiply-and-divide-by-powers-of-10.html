Introduction

Multiplying and dividing by powers of 10 is a fundamental skill in mathematics that helps simplify calculations with large and small numbers. This lesson will also cover benchmark fractions and their decimal equivalents, which are commonly used in everyday life.

By the end of this lesson, you should be able to:

  • Recall multiplication and division facts up to 144
  • Convert between benchmark fractions, decimals, and percentages
  • Multiply and divide decimals by powers of 10 (10, 100, 1000, etc.)
  • Apply these skills to solve real-world problems

Multiplication Facts

Multiplication Table (Up to 12×12)

Mastering multiplication facts up to 12×12 is essential for quick calculations:

Related Division Facts

Every multiplication fact has four related division facts. For example:

Multiplication Fact:

\[ 7 \times 8 = 56 \]

Related Division Facts:

\[ \begin{eqnarray*} 56 \div 7 &=& 8 \\ 56 \div 8 &=& 7 \\ 7 &=& 56 \div 8 \\ 8 &=& 56 \div 7 \end{eqnarray*} \]

Benchmark Fractions

Common Benchmark Fractions

Benchmark fractions are common fractions that we should memorize along with their decimal and percentage equivalents:

\[ \begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline \text{Fraction} & \text{Decimal} & \text{Percentage} \\ \hline \hline \frac{1}{2} & 0.5 & 50\% \\ \hline \frac{1}{4} & 0.25 & 25\% \\ \hline \frac{3}{4} & 0.75 & 75\% \\ \hline \frac{1}{5} & 0.2 & 20\% \\ \hline \frac{2}{5} & 0.4 & 40\% \\ \hline \frac{3}{5} & 0.6 & 60\% \\ \hline \frac{4}{5} & 0.8 & 80\% \\ \hline \frac{1}{8} & 0.125 & 12.5\% \\ \hline \frac{3}{8} & 0.375 & 37.5\% \\ \hline \frac{5}{8} & 0.625 & 62.5\% \\ \hline \frac{7}{8} & 0.875 & 87.5\% \\ \hline \frac{1}{10} & 0.1 & 10\% \\ \hline \frac{1}{100} & 0.01 & 1\% \\ \hline \end{array} \]

Conversion Methods

To convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages:

Fraction to Decimal: Divide numerator by denominator
Decimal to Percentage: Multiply by 100 and add %
Percentage to Decimal: Divide by 100
Decimal to Fraction: Write as fraction with denominator 10, 100, etc. and simplify

Powers of 10

Multiplying by Powers of 10

When multiplying by powers of 10 (10, 100, 1000, etc.), the decimal point moves to the right:

\[ \begin{eqnarray*} 3.45 \times 10 &=& 34.5 \\ 3.45 \times 100 &=& 345 \\ 3.45 \times 1000 &=& 3,450 \\ 3.45 \times 10,000 &=& 34,500 \end{eqnarray*} \]

For fractions like 1/10, 1/100, etc., multiplying is equivalent to dividing by the denominator:

\[ \begin{eqnarray*} 3.45 \times \frac{1}{10} &=& 0.345 \\ 3.45 \times \frac{1}{100} &=& 0.0345 \\ 3.45 \times \frac{1}{1000} &=& 0.00345 \end{eqnarray*} \]

Dividing by Powers of 10

When dividing by powers of 10, the decimal point moves to the left:

\[ \begin{eqnarray*} 3.45 \div 10 &=& 0.345 \\ 3.45 \div 100 &=& 0.0345 \\ 3.45 \div 1000 &=& 0.00345 \\ 3.45 \div 10,000 &=& 0.000345 \end{eqnarray*} \]

Notice that dividing by 10 is the same as multiplying by 1/10.

Real-World Applications

Why Powers of 10 Matter

Powers of 10 are used in many real-life situations, especially when dealing with large or small numbers. For example:

Money Example:

If a bag of rice costs GH₵12.50, then the cost of 100 bags is:

\[ 12.50 \times 100 = 1,\!250 \]

Discount Example:

If a shirt costs GH₵80 and is on sale for \(25\%\) off, the discount amount is:

\[ 80 \times \frac{25}{100} = 20 \]

Measurement Example:

If a map scale is \(1\,\text{cm} = 10\,\text{km}\), then \(3.5\,\text{cm}\) on the map represents:

\[ 3.5 \times 10 = 35\,\text{km} \]

Science and Measurements

Powers of 10 are used extensively in metric conversions and scientific notation:

Example 1: Convert 3.5 kilometers to meters (1 km = 1000 m)

Solution: 3.5 × 1000 = 3,500 meters

Example 2: Convert 450 milliliters to liters (1000 ml = 1 L)

Solution: 450 ÷ 1000 = 0.45 liters
