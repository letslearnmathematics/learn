Introduction

In this lesson, we will use what we have learnt so far on place values and numbers in general to solve word problems. So far, we have learnt how to;
  1. read and write numbers in words and in figures
  2. skip count forwards and backwards in given intervals,
  3. use the comparison operators (>, < and =) to compare numbers,
  4. express numbers in:
    standard form
    significant figures and
    decimal places.

We will now combine all that we have learnt to solve story, or word, problems.

Understanding Word Problems

Key Steps to Understand Word Problems

Always, when solving word problems try to get or gather some data from the problem given. For instance, if the question says, "Kofi is 20 years old now. What will be his age in 5 years time?" Then your data from the story problem will look like the statement below: \[\text{Kofi's age now} = 20 \ years\]

  1. Read carefully: Identify what the problem is asking.
  2. Identify given information: Note all numbers and relationships mentioned.
  3. Determine what's being asked: Find out exactly what you need to solve.
  4. Visualize if needed: Draw diagrams or write down relationships.

Solving Word Problems

Problem-Solving Steps

  1. Break down the problem: Separate into smaller, manageable parts.
  2. Find relationships: Determine how quantities relate to each other.
  3. Perform calculations: Solve step by step, paying attention to place values.
  4. Check your answer: Verify if the result makes sense in the context.

Creating Word Problems

How to Create Your Own Problems

  1. Choose a real-life scenario: Pick situations involving money, measurements, populations, etc.
  2. Define quantities: Assign values with different place values.
  3. Establish relationships: Create multiplicative or additive relationships between quantities.
  4. Formulate a question: Ask something that requires place value understanding to solve.

Example of Created Problem:

A small business has monthly expenses of Gh₵3,200 and earns four times that amount in revenue. If they save half of their monthly profit, how much will they save in 3 years?

Solution Guide:

  1. Calculate monthly revenue (4 × expenses)
  2. Find monthly profit (revenue - expenses)
  3. Calculate monthly savings (½ of profit)
  4. Multiply by 36 months (3 years)

Examples

Question 1

Adom earns Gh₵2500 a month after tax and his elder brother Arko earns three times as much. How much is their total income after five years if there are no increases in their earnings?

Understanding the Problem:

  • Given:
    • Adom's monthly income = Gh₵2,500
    • Arko earns 3 times Adom's income
    • Time period = 5 years
  • Asked: Total combined income after 5 years


Solution:

Adom's monthly income $=$ Gh₵2,500

Arko's monthly income $=$ 3 $\times$ Gh₵2,500 $=$ Gh₵7,500

Their total income in a month:

$\Rightarrow$ Gh₵2,500 $+$ Gh₵7,500

$\Rightarrow$ Gh₵10,000

Their total income in a year:

$\Rightarrow$ 12 $\times$ Gh₵10,000

$\Rightarrow$ Gh₵120,000

Their total income in 5 year:

$\Rightarrow$ 5 $\times$ Gh₵120,000

$\Rightarrow$ Gh₵600,000

Question 2

[B.E.C.E 2005]

In sharing 95 oranges with Dede, Fofo kept 45 of them and shared the rest equally with Dede. How many oranges did Dede get?

Solution:

Total oranges to be shared $=$ 95 oranges

Number kept by Fofo $=$ 45 oranges

Oranges left to be shared:

\(\Rightarrow\) $95 - 45$

\(\Rightarrow\) $50$ oranges

\(\therefore\) Fofo shared 50 oranges with Dede.

Dede's share:

\(\Rightarrow\) $50 \ \text{oranges} \div 2$

\(\Rightarrow\) 25 oranges.

\(\therefore\) Dede's share was 25 oranges.

Question 3

Maame Araba's monthly salary is twice his brother's earning each month. Calculate her salary for March, if her brother earns Gh₵ 3,500.00 per month.

Solution:

Her brother's monthly salary = Gh₵ 3,500.00

Maame Araba's salary:

\(\Rightarrow 2 \times\) her brother's salary:

\(\Rightarrow 2 \times 3,500\)

\(\Rightarrow\) Gh₵ 7,000.00

\(\therefore\) Maame Araba's salary for March is Gh₵ 7,000.00.

Question 4

Queenster paid Gh₵ 49,000.00 for a brand new car. If she was given a discount of Gh₵ 7,000.00. What was the actual value of the car?

Solution:

Amount paid for the car $=$ Gh₵ 49,000.00

Discount allowed $=$ Gh₵ 7,000.00

Actual value of Queenster's car:

\(\Rightarrow\) Amount paid $+$ discount given

\(\Rightarrow 49,000 + 7,000\)

\(\Rightarrow\) Gh₵ 56,000.00

\(\therefore\) the actual value of Queester's car was Gh₵ 56,000.00

Question 5

Kofi is 20 years old now. What will be his age in 5 years time?

Solution:

Kofi's age now = 20 years

In five years time:

\(\Rightarrow\) add 5 to his age now

\(\Rightarrow\) \(20 + 5\)

\(\Rightarrow 25\)

\(\therefore\) In 5 years time, Kofi will be 25 years of age.

Question 4

Create your own word problem involving place values with the following requirements:

  • Include at least two quantities with different place values
  • Establish a multiplicative relationship between them
  • Ask a question that requires calculations over time (months/years)
  • Provide the solution to your problem

Sample Problem:

A bookstore sells 450 books each month at an average price of Gh₵35 per book. The store's monthly expenses are Gh₵8,500. How much profit does the store make in 2 years?

Sample Solution:

1. Monthly revenue: 450 × Gh₵35 = Gh₵15,750

2. Monthly profit: Gh₵ 15,750 - Gh₵8,500 = Gh₵7,250

3. 2-year profit: Gh₵7,250 × 24 months = Gh₵174,000

Question 5

A pencil sells at Gh₵ 3.40 and an eraser costs Gh₵ 1.00. How much will Kofi pay if he bought three pencils and five erasers?

Solution:

Answer: Kofi will pay Gh₵ 15.20 for 3 pencils and 5 erasers.
