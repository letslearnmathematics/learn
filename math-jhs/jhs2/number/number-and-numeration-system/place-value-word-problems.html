Introduction

Place value word problems are mathematical questions that use real-life situations to test our understanding of how digits represent different values based on their position in a number. These problems help us apply our knowledge of place values to practical scenarios.

In this lesson, we will learn how to:

  • Understand and interpret word problems involving place values
  • Solve these problems using our knowledge of place values
  • Create our own word problems based on place value concepts

Understanding Word Problems

Key Steps to Understand Word Problems

  1. Read carefully: Identify what the problem is asking.
  2. Identify given information: Note all numbers and relationships mentioned.
  3. Determine what's being asked: Find out exactly what you need to solve.
  4. Visualize if needed: Draw diagrams or write down relationships.

Example Problem:

Adom earns Gh₵2500 a month after tax and his elder brother Arko earns three times as much. How much is their total income after five years if there are no increases in their earnings?

Understanding the Problem:

  • Given:
    • Adom's monthly income = Gh₵2,500
    • Arko earns 3 times Adom's income
    • Time period = 5 years
  • Asked: Total combined income after 5 years

Solving Word Problems

Problem-Solving Steps

  1. Break down the problem: Separate into smaller, manageable parts.
  2. Find relationships: Determine how quantities relate to each other.
  3. Perform calculations: Solve step by step, paying attention to place values.
  4. Check your answer: Verify if the result makes sense in the context.

Solving the Example Problem:

Problem: Adom earns Gh₵2500 a month after tax and his elder brother Arko earns three times as much. How much is their total income after five years if there are no increases in their earnings?

Solution:

Step 1: Find Arko's monthly income

Arko earns 3 times Adom's income:

\[ \text{Arko's income} = 3 \times \text{Adom's income} = 3 \times \text{Gh₵}2,500 = \text{Gh₵}7,500 \]

Step 2: Calculate their combined monthly income

\[ \text{Total monthly income} = \text{Adom's income} + \text{Arko's income} = \text{Gh₵}2,500 + \text{Gh₵}7,500 = \text{Gh₵}10,000 \]

Step 3: Calculate yearly income (12 months)

\[ \text{Yearly income} = \text{Monthly income} \times 12 = \text{Gh₵}10,000 \times 12 = \text{Gh₵}120,000 \]

Step 4: Calculate income over 5 years

\[ \text{5-year income} = \text{Yearly income} \times 5 = \text{Gh₵}120,000 \times 5 = \text{Gh₵}600,000 \]

Final Answer: Their total income after five years is Gh₵600,000.

Creating Word Problems

How to Create Your Own Problems

  1. Choose a real-life scenario: Pick situations involving money, measurements, populations, etc.
  2. Define quantities: Assign values with different place values.
  3. Establish relationships: Create multiplicative or additive relationships between quantities.
  4. Formulate a question: Ask something that requires place value understanding to solve.

Example of Created Problem:

A small business has monthly expenses of Gh₵3,200 and earns four times that amount in revenue. If they save half of their monthly profit, how much will they save in 3 years?

Solution Guide:

  1. Calculate monthly revenue (4 × expenses)
  2. Find monthly profit (revenue - expenses)
  3. Calculate monthly savings (½ of profit)
  4. Multiply by 36 months (3 years)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A school has 1,250 students. If each student pays Gh₵120 in fees per term, and there are 3 terms in a year, how much does the school collect in fees over 4 years?

Solution:

1. Calculate termly collection: 1,250 students × Gh₵120 = Gh₵150,000

2. Calculate yearly collection: Gh₵150,000 × 3 terms = Gh₵450,000

3. Calculate 4-year collection: Gh₵450,000 × 4 = Gh₵1,800,000

Answer: The school collects Gh₵1,800,000 over 4 years.

Question 2

A factory produces 2,500 bottles of juice daily. Each bottle contains 350ml of juice. How many liters of juice does the factory produce in a 30-day month?

Solution:

1. Calculate daily production in ml: 2,500 × 350ml = 875,000ml

2. Calculate monthly production in ml: 875,000ml × 30 = 26,250,000ml

3. Convert to liters (1,000ml = 1L): 26,250,000 ÷ 1,000 = 26,250L

Answer: The factory produces 26,250 liters in a month.

Question 3

A rectangular farm is 1,200m long and 750m wide. What is the area of the farm in hectares? (1 hectare = 10,000m²)

Solution:

1. Calculate area in m²: 1,200m × 750m = 900,000m²

2. Convert to hectares: 900,000 ÷ 10,000 = 90 hectares

Answer: The farm area is 90 hectares.

Question 4

Create your own word problem involving place values with the following requirements:

  • Include at least two quantities with different place values
  • Establish a multiplicative relationship between them
  • Ask a question that requires calculations over time (months/years)
  • Provide the solution to your problem

Sample Problem:

A bookstore sells 450 books each month at an average price of Gh₵35 per book. The store's monthly expenses are Gh₵8,500. How much profit does the store make in 2 years?

Sample Solution:

1. Monthly revenue: 450 × Gh₵35 = Gh₵15,750

2. Monthly profit: Gh₵15,750 - Gh₵8,500 = Gh₵7,250

3. 2-year profit: Gh₵7,250 × 24 months = Gh₵174,000
