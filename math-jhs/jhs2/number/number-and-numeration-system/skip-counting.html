Introduction

Skip counting is a method of counting numbers by skipping forward or backward by a fixed number other than 1. This technique helps in developing number sense and understanding patterns in numbers.

Skip counting can also be thought of as a way to perform addition and/or subtraction on the real number line. In this lesson, we will learn how to skip count forward and backward in intervals of 10,000s, 100,000s, 500,000s, etc.

RECALL:

In skip counting;
Movement to the right \(\Rightarrow\) addition
Movement to the left \(\Rightarrow\) subtraction

Counting Forward

Counting Forward in 10,000s, 100,000s, 500,000s

When skip counting to the \(n^{th}\) term:

Starting point \(\Rightarrow \) first term \(\left( a \right)\)

Number of jumps or skips \(\Rightarrow n - 1\)

Difference between two subsequent terms \(\Rightarrow\) Interval \((d)\)

The last term (\(l\)) can be calculated using the formular: \begin{equation*} l = a + (n - 1)d \end{equation*}

Remember that the number of skips is the number of times you will be adding the interval onto each previous term.

To skip count forward in a particular interval simply means to add the interval value onto each term to get the next term. Hence, to skip count forward in an interval of 10,000, means adding 10,000 to each previous term to get the next term.

200,000, $\quad$ 210,000, $\quad$ $\dots$

Counting Backward

Counting Backward in 10,000s, 100,000s, 500,000s

To skip count backward in a particular interval simply means to subtract the interval value from each term to get the next term

Since we are subtracting, it means our interval, \(\left(d\right)\), will now have a negative sign beside it, \(\left(-d\right)\) , hence the formula for the last term \((l)\) becomes: \begin{equation*} l = a - \left(n - 1\right)d \end{equation*}

Examples

Question 1

Skip count backward up to the 5th term in intervals of 10,000:
200,000, $\quad$ 190,000, $\quad$ $\dots$

Question 2

Skip count backwards in 500000s up to the 5th number:
2,200,000, $\quad$ 1,700,000, $\quad$ $\dots$

Question 3

Find the value of the 5th term when skip counting backward in intervals of 100,000 from 700,000.

Question 4

Skip count backward up to the 4th term in intervals of 10,500::
231,500, $\quad$ 221,000, $\quad$ ...

Question 5

Skip count backwards up to the 5th term in intervals of 100,000:
600,000, $\quad$ 500,500, $\quad$ ...
