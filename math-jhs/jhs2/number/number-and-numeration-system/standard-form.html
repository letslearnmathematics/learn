Introduction

Standard form (also called scientific notation) is a way of writing very large or very small numbers in a compact form. It expresses numbers as a product of a number between 1 and 10 and a power of 10.

A number in standard form is written as: \[a \times 10^n\]

In this lesson, we will learn how to:

  1. Write integers as powers of 10
  2. Write multiples of 10 in standard form
  3. Write any integer in standard form

Writing Integers as Powers of 10

Understanding Powers of 10

A power of 10 is any number that can be expressed as 10 raised to an integer exponent. For example:

Positive Exponents:

  • \(10^1 = 10\)
  • \(10^2 = 100\)
  • \(10^3 = 1,000\)
  • \(10^6 = 1,000,000\) (one million)

Negative Exponents:

  • \(10^{-1} = 0.1\)
  • \(10^{-2} = 0.01\)
  • \(10^{-3} = 0.001\)

Examples

Question 1

Write the number 10,000,000,000 (ten billion) as a power of 10

Solution:

Number given $\Rightarrow$ 10,000,000,000

Number of zeros in the number $\Rightarrow$ 10

$\therefore$ 10,000,000,000 $= 10^{10}$

Question 2

Write the number 1,000,000,000 (1 billion) as a power of 10

Solution:

Number given $\Rightarrow$ 1,000,000,000

Number of zeros in the number $\Rightarrow$ 9

$\therefore$ 10,000,000,000 $= 10^{9}$

Question 3

Write the number 0.0001 as a power of 10

Solution:

Number given $\Rightarrow$ 0.0001

Number of decimal places $\Rightarrow$ 4

$\therefore$ 0.0001 $= 10^{-4}$

Question 4

Write the number 0.000001 as a power of 10

Solution:

Number given $\Rightarrow$ 0.000001

Number of zeros in the number $\Rightarrow$ 6

$\therefore$ 10,000,000,000 $= 10^{-6}$

Question 5

Write the number 100,000,000 (100 million) as a power of 10

Solution:

Number given $\Rightarrow$ 100,000,000

Number of zeros in the number $\Rightarrow$ 8

$\therefore$ 10,000,000,000 $= 10^{8}$

Writing Multiples of 10 in Standard Form

Standard Form Structure

Standard form has two parts:

  1. A number between 1 and 10 (can be 1 but must be less than 10)
  2. A power of 10

The general form is:

\[ a \times 10^n \]

where \(1 \leq a < 10\) and \(n\) is an integer.

Key Steps

  1. Identify the number between 1 and 10 by placing a decimal after the first non-zero digit
  2. Count how many places you moved the decimal from its original position to its new position
  3. This count becomes the exponent on the 10
  4. If the original number was greater than 10, the exponent is positive
  5. If the original number was less than 1, the exponent is negative

Examples

Question 1

Write 300 in standard form

Solution:

Number given $\Rightarrow$ 300

$\Rightarrow 3 \times 100$

$\Rightarrow 3 \times 10^2 $

Question 2

Write 45,000 in standard form

Solution:

Number given $\Rightarrow$ 45,000

$\Rightarrow 4.5 \times 10000$

$\Rightarrow 4.5 \times 10^4 $

Question 3

Express \(4000\) in standard form.

Solution:

Number given $\Rightarrow$ 4000

$\Rightarrow 4 \times 1000$

$\Rightarrow 4 \times 10^3 $

Question 4

Express \(80000\) in standard form.

Solution:

Number given $\Rightarrow$ 80000

$\Rightarrow 8 \times 10000$

$\Rightarrow 8 \times 10^4 $

Question 5

Express \(70,000,000\) in standard form.

Solution:

Number given $\Rightarrow$ 70,000,000

$\Rightarrow 7 \times 10,000,000$

$\Rightarrow 7 \times 10^7 $

Writing Integers in Standard Form

General Method

The process is similar for any integer, not just multiples of 10:

  1. Place a decimal point after the first non-zero digit
  2. Count how many places you moved the decimal from its original position
  3. Write as a product of this new number and 10 raised to the count

Examples

Question 1

Write 45,678 in standard form

Solution:

Number given $\Rightarrow$ 45,678

$\Rightarrow 4.5678 \times 10,000$

$\Rightarrow 4.5678 \times 10^4 $

Question 2

Write 203 in standard form

Solution:

Number given $\Rightarrow$ 203

$\Rightarrow 2.03 \times 100$

$\Rightarrow 2.03 \times 10^2 $

Question 3

Write \(375\) in standard form..

Solution:

Number given $\Rightarrow$ 375

$\Rightarrow 3.75 \times 100$

$\Rightarrow 3.75 \times 10^2 $

Question 4

(BECE 2002 A)

Write \(2474.5\) in standard form.

Solution:

Number given $\Rightarrow$ 2474.5

$\Rightarrow 2.4745 \times 1000$

$\Rightarrow 2.4745 \times 10^3 $

Question 5

Express \(8,765,049\) in standard form.

Solution:

Number given $\Rightarrow$ 8,765,049

$\Rightarrow 8.765049 \times 1,000,000$

$\Rightarrow 8.765049 \times 10^6 $

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Forgetting to make the first number between 1 and 10
  • Counting the wrong number of decimal places moved
  • Using the wrong sign for the exponent (positive for large numbers, negative for small numbers)
  • Including trailing zeros that aren't significant
