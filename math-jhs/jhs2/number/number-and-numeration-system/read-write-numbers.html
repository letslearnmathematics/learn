Introduction

In this lesson, we'll learn how to read and write numbers greater than 1,000,000,000 (one billion) in both words and figures. This skill is necessary for understanding large quantities in real-world contexts like population statistics, financial transactions, and scientific measurements.

We'll start by reviewing place values, then practice converting between numerals and words, including working with serial numbers on currency notes as practical examples.

Place Value Review

Place Value Chart

Each digit in a number has a different place value depending on its position from right to left.

Here's the complete place value chart for numbers up to billions:

Billions Millions Thousands Units
Hundreds Hundreds Hundreds Hundreds
Tens Tens Tens Tens
Ones Ones Ones Ones

For example, in the number 5,842,136,709:

  • 5 is in the billions place
  • 8 is in the hundred millions place
  • 4 is in the ten millions place
  • 2 is in the millions place
  • 1 is in the hundred thousands place
  • 3 is in the ten thousands place
  • 6 is in the thousands place
  • 7 is in the hundreds place
  • 0 is in the tens place
  • 9 is in the ones place

Numbers in Words

Writing Numbers in Words

To write numbers in words:

  1. Start from the leftmost digit (highest place value)
  2. Read each group of three digits (hundreds, tens, ones) separately
  3. Add the appropriate period name (billion, million, thousand)
  4. Use commas to separate the periods in numbers
  5. Use "and" before the last two digits (optional in some styles)

Example 1:

Write 2,408,321 in words:

Solution: Two million, four hundred and eight thousand, three hundred and twenty-one

Example 2:

Write 3,750,000,000 in words:

Solution: Three billion, seven hundred and fifty million

Example 3:

Write 12,006,450,318 in words:

Solution: Twelve billion, six million, four hundred and fifty thousand, three hundred and eighteen

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Write 5,203,417,896 in words.

Solution: Five billion, two hundred and three million, four hundred and seventeen thousand, eight hundred and ninety-six

Question 2

Write 1,000,500,300 in words.

Solution: One billion, five hundred thousand, three hundred

Question 3

Write 30,040,000,107 in words.

Solution: Thirty billion, forty million, one hundred and seven

Numbers in Figures

Writing Words in Figures

To convert number words to numerals:

  1. Identify the period names (billion, million, thousand)
  2. Write each period's number separately
  3. Combine them with commas separating the periods
  4. Ensure each period has three digits (add zeros if needed)

Example 1:

Write "Seven billion, two hundred one million, thirty-four thousand, five hundred six" in figures:

Solution: 7,201,034,506

Example 2:

Write "Fifteen billion, eighty million, two hundred" in figures:

Solution: 15,080,000,200

Example 3:

Write "Nine hundred billion, five million, sixty" in figures:

Solution: 900,005,000,060

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Write "Twenty-three billion, four hundred five million, six thousand, seven hundred eight" in figures.

Solution: 23,405,006,708

Question 2

Write "One hundred billion, seventy million, three thousand" in figures.

Solution: 100,070,003,000

Question 3

Write "Six hundred five billion, eight million, ninety" in figures.

Solution: 605,008,000,090

Currency Serial Numbers

Reading Serial Numbers

Currency notes often have serial numbers with both letters and numbers. We focus on the numeric part:

Example 1:

Read the numeric part of the serial number TD1567451:

Solution: 1,567,451 → One million, five hundred and sixty-seven thousand, four hundred and fifty-one

Example 2:

Read the numeric part of the serial number GH20348765:

Solution: 20,348,765 → Twenty million, three hundred and forty-eight thousand, seven hundred and sixty-five

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Write the numeric part of serial number AB34056789 in words.

Solution: 34,056,789 → Thirty-four million, fifty-six thousand, seven hundred and eighty-nine

Question 2

Write the numeric part of serial number CD10020030 in words.

Solution: 10,020,030 → Ten million, twenty thousand, thirty

Question 3

Write "Five billion, two hundred one million, three thousand, four hundred five" as it would appear in a currency serial number (add any two letters as prefix).

Solution: XY5,201,003,405 (Note: Serial numbers typically don't go up to billions, this is for practice)
