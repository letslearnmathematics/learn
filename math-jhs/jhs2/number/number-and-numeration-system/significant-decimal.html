Introduction

Significant figures and decimal places are important concepts in mathematics that help us express numbers with appropriate precision. They are used in various fields including science, engineering, and finance where exact values are needed.

In this lesson, we will learn how to:

  1. Express any given integer to a given number of significant figures
  2. Express a given decimal to two or three decimal places

Significant Figures

What are Significant Figures?

Significant figures are the digits in a number that carry meaning contributing to its precision. This includes all digits except:

  • Leading zeros (zeros before the first non-zero digit)
  • Trailing zeros when they are merely placeholders (unless specified by a decimal point)

Rules for Significant Figures

  1. All non-zero digits are significant (1-9)
  2. Zeros between non-zero digits are significant
  3. Leading zeros are not significant
  4. Trailing zeros in a decimal number are significant
  5. Trailing zeros in a whole number without a decimal point may or may not be significant

Example: Identify the significant figures in 0.00456

Solution: The significant figures are 4, 5, and 6 (the leading zeros are not significant)

Expressing Numbers to Significant Figures

To express a number to a given number of significant figures:

  1. Count the number of significant figures needed
  2. Look at the next digit after the last significant figure
  3. If it's 5 or more, round up the last significant figure
  4. If it's less than 5, keep the last significant figure the same

Examples

Question 1

Express 45,678 to 3 significant figures

Solution:

\[ 45,678 \rightarrow 45,700 \] (The 6 is the third significant figure, and we look at the next digit 7 which is ≥5, so we round up, that is, add 1 to 6)
(6 + 1 = 7)

Question 2

Express 0.0342567 to 5 significant figures.

Solution:

Number given: 0.0342567
Number of significant figures required $\Rightarrow$ 5 significant figures
Required significant figures $\Rightarrow$ 3, 4, 2, 5 and 6
Immediate right digit $\Rightarrow$ 7
Rounding off last required digit $\Rightarrow$ (add 1 to 6)
$\Rightarrow$ 0.0342567 $\approx$ 0.034257 to 5 s.f
\(\therefore\) 0.0342567 rounded to 5 siginificant figures is 0.034257.

Question 3

Express 0.0023456 to 2 significant figures

Solution:

\[ 0.0023456 \rightarrow 0.0023 \] (The 3 is the second significant figure, and we look at the next digit 4 which is $<$5, so we keep the 3)

Question 4

Express 987.654 to 4 significant figures.

Solution:

\[ 987.654 \rightarrow 987.7 \] (The 6 is the fourth significant figure, and we look at the next digit to its right, 5 which is $≥5$, so we add 1 to 6)

Question 5

(B.E.C.E 1993)

Write 39.9748 km correct to three significant figures.

Solution:

Number given: 39.9748 km
Number of significant figures required: 3
The first three significant figures are 3, 9, and 9.
The next digit is 7 (immediately after the third significant figure).
Since 7 is greater than or equal to 5, we round up the last significant figure (9).
Rounding up 9 gives 10, so we carry 1 to the previous digit:
39.9748 $\rightarrow$ 40.0 (to 3 significant figures)
$\therefore$ 39.9748 km correct to three significant figures is 40.0 km.

Decimal Places

What are Decimal Places?

Decimal places refer to the number of digits after the decimal point in a number. When we express a number to a certain number of decimal places, we're specifying how precise we want the number to be.

Example The number 3.14159 has 5 decimal places

Rules for Decimal Places

  1. Count the number of digits after the decimal point
  2. To round to a certain number of decimal places, look at the next digit
  3. If it's 5 or more, round up the last decimal digit
  4. If it's less than 5, keep the last decimal digit the same

Expressing Numbers to Decimal Places

To express a number to a given number of decimal places:

  1. Identify the digit at the required decimal place
  2. Look at the next digit (the one immediately to its right)
  3. If it's 5 or more, round up the identified digit
  4. If it's less than 5, keep the identified digit the same

Examples

Question 1

Express 2.71828 to 2 decimal places

Solution:

\[ 2.71828 \rightarrow 2.72 \] (The 1 is at the second decimal place, and we look at the next digit 8 which is $≥5$, so we round up)

Question 2

Express 15.9876 to 3 decimal places

Solution:

\[ 15.9876 \rightarrow 15.988 \] (The 7 is at the third decimal place, and we look at the next digit 6 which is ≥5, so we round up)

Question 3

Express 0.12345 to 4 decimal places

Solution:

\[ 0.12345 \rightarrow 0.1235 \] (The 4 is at the fourth decimal place, and we look at the next digit 5 which is ≥5, so we round up)

Question 4

Express 98.7654 to 1 decimal place

Solution:

\[ 98.7654 \rightarrow 98.8 \] (The 7 is at the first decimal place, and we look at the next digit 6 which is ≥5, so we round up)

Question 5

Express 7.65432 to 4 decimal places

Solution:

\[ 7.65432 \rightarrow 7.6543 \] (The 3 is at the fourth decimal place, and we look at the next digit 2 which is <5, so we keep the 3)
