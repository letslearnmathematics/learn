Introduction

Unit rate is a ratio that compares two different quantities where one of the quantities is one unit. It helps us understand how much of one thing corresponds to one unit of another thing.

In this lesson, we will learn how to solve problems involving unit rates, including those related to unit pricing (like cost per item) and constant speed (distance per time). Understanding unit rates is essential for making comparisons and decisions in everyday life.

Understanding Unit Rate

What is Unit Rate?

A unit rate tells us how many units of the first quantity correspond to one unit of the second quantity. For example, if a car travels 120 miles in 2 hours, the unit rate would be 60 miles per hour (120 ÷ 2).

The general formula for unit rate is:

\[ \text{Unit Rate} = \frac{\text{Total Quantity}}{\text{Number of Units}} \]

Example Problem 1

Problem: If it took 7 hours to mow 4 lawns, then at that rate, how many lawns could be mowed in 35 hours? At what rate were lawns being mowed?

Solution:

First, find the rate of lawn mowing per hour:

\[ \text{Rate} = \frac{\text{Number of Lawns}}{\text{Time}} = \frac{4 \text{ lawns}}{7 \text{ hours}} \approx 0.571 \text{ lawns per hour} \]

Now, find how many lawns can be mowed in 35 hours:

\[ \text{Number of Lawns} = \text{Rate} \times \text{Time} = 0.571 \times 35 \approx 20 \text{ lawns} \]

Alternatively, you can use proportion:

\[ \frac{4 \text{ lawns}}{7 \text{ hours}} = \frac{x \text{ lawns}}{35 \text{ hours}} \] \[ x = \frac{4 \times 35}{7} = 20 \text{ lawns} \]

Answer: At that rate, 20 lawns could be mowed in 35 hours. The mowing rate was approximately 0.571 lawns per hour (or exactly 4/7 lawns per hour).

Example Problem 2

Problem: Salamatu is a drummer for a band. She burns 756 calories while drumming for 3 hours. She burns the same number of calories each hour. How many calories does Salamatu burn per hour?

Solution:

To find the calories burned per hour (unit rate):

\[ \text{Calories per hour} = \frac{\text{Total Calories}}{\text{Total Time}} = \frac{756 \text{ calories}}{3 \text{ hours}} = 252 \text{ calories/hour} \]

Answer: Salamatu burns 252 calories per hour while drumming.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A factory produces 1,200 bottles in 8 hours. How many bottles does it produce per hour?

Solution:

\[ \text{Bottles per hour} = \frac{1200}{8} = 150 \text{ bottles/hour} \]

Answer: The factory produces 150 bottles per hour.

Question 2

A car travels 360 km using 30 liters of petrol. What is the car's rate of petrol consumption in km per liter?

Solution:

\[ \text{km per liter} = \frac{360}{30} = 12 \text{ km/liter} \]

Answer: The car travels 12 km per liter of petrol.

Question 3

A printing press can print 5,400 pages in 6 hours. How many pages can it print in 10 hours at the same rate?

Solution:

First find rate per hour:

\[ \frac{5400}{6} = 900 \text{ pages/hour} \]

Then calculate for 10 hours:

\[ 900 \times 10 = 9000 \text{ pages} \]

Answer: The press can print 9,000 pages in 10 hours.

Unit Pricing

Understanding Unit Pricing

Unit pricing helps us compare the cost of items when they come in different quantities or sizes. It tells us the cost per single unit (like per item, per liter, per kilogram, etc.), making it easier to determine which option gives better value for money.

The formula for unit price is:

\[ \text{Unit Price} = \frac{\text{Total Cost}}{\text{Number of Units}} \]

Example Problem

Problem: A 500g packet of sugar costs Gh₵5.50, while a 1kg packet costs Gh₵10.00. Which option gives better value for money?

Solution:

Calculate cost per gram for both options:

500g packet:

\[ \text{Price per gram} = \frac{5.50}{500} = 0.011 \text{ Gh₵/g} \]

1kg (1000g) packet:

\[ \text{Price per gram} = \frac{10.00}{1000} = 0.010 \text{ Gh₵/g} \]

Answer: The 1kg packet is better value at 0.010 Gh₵ per gram compared to 0.011 Gh₵ per gram for the 500g packet.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A 250ml bottle of juice costs Gh₵3.00, while a 1-liter bottle costs Gh₵10.00. Which option has a lower cost per milliliter?

Solution:

250ml bottle:

\[ \frac{3.00}{250} = 0.012 \text{ Gh₵/ml} \]

1-liter (1000ml) bottle:

\[ \frac{10.00}{1000} = 0.010 \text{ Gh₵/ml} \]

Answer: The 1-liter bottle has a lower cost per milliliter (0.010 Gh₵/ml vs 0.012 Gh₵/ml).

Question 2

A pack of 5 pencils costs Gh₵2.50, while a pack of 12 pencils costs Gh₵5.40. Which pack offers a better price per pencil?

Solution:

5-pencil pack:

\[ \frac{2.50}{5} = 0.50 \text{ Gh₵/pencil} \]

12-pencil pack:

\[ \frac{5.40}{12} = 0.45 \text{ Gh₵/pencil} \]

Answer: The 12-pencil pack offers a better price per pencil (0.45 Gh₵ vs 0.50 Gh₵).

Constant Speed

Understanding Speed

Speed is a measure of how fast an object is moving. When speed is constant, it means the object covers equal distances in equal time intervals.

The formula for speed is:

\[ \text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} \]

This can be rearranged to find distance or time:

\[ \text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time} \] \[ \text{Time} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} \]

Example Problem

Problem: A car travels at a constant speed of 80 km/h. How far will it travel in 3.5 hours?

Solution:

\[ \text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time} = 80 \times 3.5 = 280 \text{ km} \]

Answer: The car will travel 280 km in 3.5 hours.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A cyclist maintains a constant speed of 15 km/h. How long will it take to cycle 45 km?

Solution:

\[ \text{Time} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} = \frac{45}{15} = 3 \text{ hours} \]

Answer: It will take 3 hours to cycle 45 km.

Question 2

A train travels 300 km in 2.5 hours. What is its average speed in km/h?

Solution:

\[ \text{Speed} = \frac{300}{2.5} = 120 \text{ km/h} \]

Answer: The train's average speed is 120 km/h.

Question 3

A plane flies at a constant speed of 600 km/h. How far will it travel in 45 minutes?

Solution:

First convert 45 minutes to hours: 45/60 = 0.75 hours

\[ \text{Distance} = 600 \times 0.75 = 450 \text{ km} \]

Answer: The plane will travel 450 km in 45 minutes.
