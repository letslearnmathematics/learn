Introduction

Measurement units are standard quantities used to express and compare physical quantities like length, mass, time, etc. Different situations require different units of measurement, and we often need to convert between them.

In this lesson, we'll learn how to convert between different measurement units using ratio reasoning, which helps us understand the proportional relationships between units. This skill is essential for solving real-world problems involving measurements.

Basic Conversions

Common Conversion Factors

Here are some common conversion factors you need to know:

Length:

  • 1 kilometer (km) = 1,000 meters (m)
  • 1 meter (m) = 100 centimeters (cm)
  • 1 centimeter (cm) = 10 millimeters (mm)

Mass:

  • 1 kilogram (kg) = 1,000 grams (g)
  • 1 gram (g) = 1,000 milligrams (mg)

Volume:

  • 1 liter (L) = 1,000 milliliters (mL)
  • 1 cubic meter (m³) = 1,000 liters (L)

Conversion Methods

There are two main methods for converting units:

Multiplication Method

Multiply by the conversion factor when converting to a smaller unit:

Convert 3 km to meters:

\[ 3 \text{ km} \times \frac{1000 \text{ m}}{1 \text{ km}} = 3000 \text{ m} \]

Division Method

Divide by the conversion factor when converting to a larger unit:

Convert 500 cm to meters:

\[ 500 \text{ cm} \div \frac{100 \text{ cm}}{1 \text{ m}} = 5 \text{ m} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Convert 2.5 kilometers to meters.

Since 1 km = 1000 m:

\[ 2.5 \text{ km} \times \frac{1000 \text{ m}}{1 \text{ km}} = 2500 \text{ m} \]

Question 2

Convert 450 centimeters to meters.

Since 100 cm = 1 m:

\[ 450 \text{ cm} \div \frac{100 \text{ cm}}{1 \text{ m}} = 4.5 \text{ m} \]

Question 3

Convert 3.2 liters to milliliters.

Since 1 L = 1000 mL:

\[ 3.2 \text{ L} \times \frac{1000 \text{ mL}}{1 \text{ L}} = 3200 \text{ mL} \]

Ratio Reasoning

Understanding Ratios

Ratio reasoning involves understanding the proportional relationship between different units. This helps us convert between units more intuitively and solve complex problems.

The key idea is to recognize that conversion factors are essentially ratios that remain constant regardless of the quantity being converted.

Using Ratio Tables

Ratio tables help organize conversion information clearly:

Example: Convert 5 km to cm

Kilometers (km) Meters (m) Centimeters (cm)
1 1000 100,000
5 5000 500,000

We can see that 5 km = 500,000 cm by following the ratios:

\[ 1 \text{ km} : 1000 \text{ m} : 100,000 \text{ cm} \]

Multi-step Conversions

Sometimes we need to convert through intermediate units:

Convert 2.7 km to cm:

  1. First convert km to m: \( 2.7 \text{ km} \times 1000 = 2700 \text{ m} \)
  2. Then convert m to cm: \( 2700 \text{ m} \times 100 = 270,000 \text{ cm} \)

Or using direct ratio:

\[ 1 \text{ km} = 100,000 \text{ cm} \] \[ 2.7 \text{ km} = 2.7 \times 100,000 = 270,000 \text{ cm} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Convert 0.8 km to cm using ratio reasoning.

We know: 1 km = 1000 m and 1 m = 100 cm, so:

\[ 1 \text{ km} = 1000 \times 100 = 100,000 \text{ cm} \] \[ 0.8 \text{ km} = 0.8 \times 100,000 = 80,000 \text{ cm} \]

Question 2

Convert 3500 mL to liters using ratio reasoning.

We know: 1000 mL = 1 L, so:

\[ 3500 \text{ mL} \div \frac{1000 \text{ mL}}{1 \text{ L}} = 3.5 \text{ L} \]

Question 3

Convert 2.4 kg to mg using ratio reasoning.

We know: 1 kg = 1000 g and 1 g = 1000 mg, so:

\[ 1 \text{ kg} = 1000 \times 1000 = 1,000,000 \text{ mg} \] \[ 2.4 \text{ kg} = 2.4 \times 1,000,000 = 2,400,000 \text{ mg} \]

Problem Solving

Speed Comparison Problem

Agbo walks 4km to school every day. He uses 60 minutes. Rukiya uses 45 minutes to cover 4200m. Which of the two learners is faster?

Solution Steps

To compare their speeds, we need to calculate their speeds in the same units (m/min):

For Agbo:

  1. Convert distance to meters: \( 4 \text{ km} = 4000 \text{ m} \)
  2. Time = 60 minutes
  3. Speed = Distance ÷ Time = \( 4000 \text{ m} ÷ 60 \text{ min} ≈ 66.67 \text{ m/min} \)

For Rukiya:

  1. Distance = 4200 m (already in meters)
  2. Time = 45 minutes
  3. Speed = Distance ÷ Time = \( 4200 \text{ m} ÷ 45 \text{ min} ≈ 93.33 \text{ m/min} \)

Comparison:

Rukiya's speed (93.33 m/min) is greater than Agbo's speed (66.67 m/min), so Rukiya is faster.

Alternative Approach

We could also convert both speeds to km/h for comparison:

For Agbo:

  1. Distance = 4 km
  2. Time = 60 minutes = 1 hour
  3. Speed = 4 km/h

For Rukiya:

  1. Convert distance to km: \( 4200 \text{ m} = 4.2 \text{ km} \)
  2. Convert time to hours: \( 45 \text{ min} = 0.75 \text{ hours} \)
  3. Speed = Distance ÷ Time = \( 4.2 \text{ km} ÷ 0.75 \text{ h} = 5.6 \text{ km/h} \)

Comparison:

Rukiya's speed (5.6 km/h) is greater than Agbo's speed (4 km/h), confirming Rukiya is faster.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A car travels 120 km in 2 hours. A bicycle travels 3000 m in 10 minutes. Which is faster in km/h?

Car: 120 km ÷ 2 h = 60 km/h

Bicycle: 3000 m = 3 km, 10 min = 1/6 h → 3 km ÷ (1/6 h) = 18 km/h

The car is faster (60 km/h > 18 km/h).

Question 2

A water tank holds 1500 liters. How many 250 mL bottles can be filled from the tank?

Convert liters to mL: 1500 L × 1000 = 1,500,000 mL

Number of bottles: 1,500,000 mL ÷ 250 mL = 6,000 bottles

Question 3

A recipe calls for 500 g of flour. If you only have a 1 kg bag, what fraction of the bag should you use?

Convert to same units: 1 kg = 1000 g

Fraction needed: 500 g ÷ 1000 g = 1/2 of the bag
