Introduction

Constant of proportionality (also called the unit rate) is the ratio between two directly proportional quantities. When two quantities are directly proportional, their ratio is constant.

The constant of proportionality can be found in tables, graphs, equations, and word problems. It represents how much one quantity changes in relation to another.

If \( y \) is directly proportional to \( x \), then:

\[ y = kx \]

Where \( k \) is the constant of proportionality.

Key Characteristics:

  • The graph passes through the origin (0,0)
  • The ratio \( \frac{y}{x} \) is always the same
  • The equation is always of the form \( y = kx \)

Finding from Tables

Method

To find the constant of proportionality from a table:

  1. Check that all ratios \( \frac{y}{x} \) are equal (confirm it's proportional)
  2. Calculate \( k = \frac{y}{x} \) for any pair of values

Example Table:

\( x \) \( y \) \( \frac{y}{x} \)
2 5 2.5
4 10 2.5
6 15 2.5

Since all ratios are equal to 2.5, the constant of proportionality \( k = 2.5 \).

The equation would be \( y = 2.5x \).

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Find the constant of proportionality from this table:

\( x \) \( y \)
3 12
5 20
7 28

Calculate \( \frac{y}{x} \) for each pair:

\( \frac{12}{3} = 4 \), \( \frac{20}{5} = 4 \), \( \frac{28}{7} = 4 \)

All ratios equal 4, so \( k = 4 \).

The equation is \( y = 4x \).

Question 2

Determine if this table shows a proportional relationship and find the constant if it does:

\( x \) \( y \)
1 1.5
2 3
4 6
5 7.5

Calculate \( \frac{y}{x} \) for each pair:

\( \frac{1.5}{1} = 1.5 \), \( \frac{3}{2} = 1.5 \), \( \frac{6}{4} = 1.5 \), \( \frac{7.5}{5} = 1.5 \)

All ratios equal 1.5, so it's proportional with \( k = 1.5 \).

The equation is \( y = 1.5x \).

Finding from Equations

Method

For equations in the form \( y = kx \), the constant of proportionality is simply the coefficient of \( x \).

Examples:

  1. \( y = 7x \) → \( k = 7 \)
  2. \( y = \frac{2}{3}x \) → \( k = \frac{2}{3} \)
  3. \( y = 0.25x \) → \( k = 0.25 \)

Note:

If the equation isn't in \( y = kx \) form, you may need to solve for \( y \):

Example: \( 2y = 8x \) → Divide both sides by 2 → \( y = 4x \) → \( k = 4 \)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Find the constant of proportionality for these equations:

  1. \( y = 12x \)
  2. \( y = \frac{3}{5}x \)
  3. \( 4y = 20x \)

Solutions:

  1. Directly from equation: \( k = 12 \)
  2. Directly from equation: \( k = \frac{3}{5} \)
  3. First solve for \( y \): \( y = 5x \) → \( k = 5 \)

Question 2

Find the constant of proportionality:

  1. \( 3y = 15x \)
  2. \( \frac{1}{2}y = 4x \)
  3. \( y = 0.8x \)

Solutions:

  1. Divide both sides by 3: \( y = 5x \) → \( k = 5 \)
  2. Multiply both sides by 2: \( y = 8x \) → \( k = 8 \)
  3. Directly from equation: \( k = 0.8 \)

Finding from Graphs

Method

To find the constant of proportionality from a graph:

  1. Identify a point where both coordinates are clear (not (0,0))
  2. Calculate \( k = \frac{y}{x} \) using the coordinates of that point

Example Graph:

Imagine a straight line passing through the origin and the point (3,6).

The constant of proportionality would be:

\[ k = \frac{y}{x} = \frac{6}{3} = 2 \]

So the equation is \( y = 2x \).

Important:

The graph must be a straight line passing through the origin (0,0) to represent a proportional relationship.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A proportional relationship graph passes through the point (5,15). What is the constant of proportionality?

Calculate \( k = \frac{y}{x} = \frac{15}{5} = 3 \).

The constant of proportionality is 3.

Question 2

A graph shows a proportional relationship passing through (2,7). Find the constant and write the equation.

Calculate \( k = \frac{y}{x} = \frac{7}{2} = 3.5 \).

The equation is \( y = 3.5x \).

Word Problems

Method

For word problems:

  1. Identify the two quantities that are proportional
  2. Find two corresponding values
  3. Calculate \( k = \frac{\text{dependent quantity}}{\text{independent quantity}} \)

Example Problem:

An ant travels \(\frac{9}{8}\) inches in 45 seconds and \(\frac{27}{8}\) inches in 2 minutes and 15 seconds. What is the constant of proportionality (speed)?

Solution:

First convert all times to seconds:

2 minutes 15 seconds = 135 seconds

Now calculate speed (distance/time) for both cases:

First case: \( \frac{\frac{9}{8}}{45} = \frac{9}{8} \times \frac{1}{45} = \frac{1}{40} \) inches per second

Second case: \( \frac{\frac{27}{8}}{135} = \frac{27}{8} \times \frac{1}{135} = \frac{1}{40} \) inches per second

Both give the same constant \( k = \frac{1}{40} \) inches per second.

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A car travels 240 miles in 4 hours. What is the constant of proportionality (speed)?

Calculate speed = distance/time = 240 miles / 4 hours = 60 mph.

The constant is 60 miles per hour.

Question 2

A printer can print 120 pages in 3 minutes. What is the constant of proportionality (print rate)?

Calculate rate = pages/time = 120 pages / 3 minutes = 40 pages per minute.

The constant is 40 pages per minute.

Question 3

An ant travels \(\frac{9}{8}\) inches in 45 seconds and \(\frac{27}{8}\) inches in 2 minutes and 15 seconds. What is the constant of proportionality?

Convert all times to seconds:

2 minutes 15 seconds = 135 seconds

Calculate for both cases:

First case: \( \frac{\frac{9}{8}}{45} = \frac{1}{40} \) in/sec

Second case: \( \frac{\frac{27}{8}}{135} = \frac{1}{40} \) in/sec

Constant is \( \frac{1}{40} \) inches per second.
