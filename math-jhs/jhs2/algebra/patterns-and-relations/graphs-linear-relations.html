Introduction

A linear relation is a relationship between two variables that forms a straight line when graphed on a coordinate plane. These relations can be represented by equations of the form \( y = mx + b \), where:

  • \( m \) is the slope (steepness) of the line
  • \( b \) is the y-intercept (where the line crosses the y-axis)

Ordered pairs \((x, y)\) represent points on the line. Given a graph of a linear relation, we can find missing elements in ordered pairs by using the information from the graph.

Key Concepts

To find missing elements in ordered pairs from a graph:

  1. Identify the pattern or rule of the linear relation from the graph
  2. Use the graph to find corresponding x or y values
  3. Verify your answers by checking if the points lie on the line

Find Missing Elements

Finding y when x is given

When you know the x-value and need to find the corresponding y-value:

  1. Locate the x-value on the x-axis
  2. Move vertically until you reach the line
  3. Move horizontally to read the y-value on the y-axis

Solution: From the graph, when x = 3, y = 4.

Finding x when y is given

When you know the y-value and need to find the corresponding x-value:

  1. Locate the y-value on the y-axis
  2. Move horizontally until you reach the line
  3. Move vertically to read the x-value on the x-axis

Example: Find x when y = 2 from the graph below:

Solution: From the graph, when y = 2, x = 1.

Important Notes

  • Always check that your point lies exactly on the line
  • For values between grid lines, estimate carefully
  • Some linear relations may require calculating the equation first

Real-World Applications

Linear relations are used in many real-world situations. Here are some examples where you might need to find missing elements:

Cost Analysis

A phone company charges a base fee plus per-minute rate. The graph shows total cost vs minutes used.

Question: If a customer used 15 minutes, what was their total cost?

Distance-Time Graphs

A car's distance from home over time is shown on the graph.

Question: How far was the car from home after 3 hours?

Practice Exercises

Question 1

Using the graph below, find the missing y-values for the following x-values: (a) x = 2, (b) x = 5

Solution:

(a) When x = 2, y = 3

(b) When x = 5, y = 6

Question 2

Using the graph below, find the missing x-values for the following y-values: (a) y = 4, (b) y = 1

Solution:

(a) When y = 4, x = 4

(b) When y = 1, x = 1

Question 3

The graph shows the relationship between temperature in Celsius and Fahrenheit. Find the Fahrenheit equivalent of 20°C.

Solution: 20°C is equivalent to 68°F

Question 4

A taxi company charges a base fare plus per-kilometer rate. From the graph, determine the total fare for a 8km trip.

Solution: The fare for 8km is Gh₵12.50
