Introduction

Evaluating algebraic expressions means substituting given values for variables and calculating the result. This is a fundamental skill in algebra that helps us solve real-world problems.

In this lesson, we'll learn how to:

  • Substitute values into expressions with whole numbers
  • Evaluate expressions containing fractions
  • Apply these skills to solve word problems

For example, evaluate \( 3x + 5 \) when \( x = 2 \):

\[ 3(2) + 5 = 6 + 5 = 11 \]

Step-by-Step Process

To evaluate an algebraic expression:

  1. Replace each variable with its given value
  2. Follow the order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS)
  3. Simplify the expression step by step

Example 1

Evaluate \( 4x - 7 \) when \( x = 5 \):

\[ \begin{align*} 4x - 7 &= 4(5) - 7 \quad \text{(Substitute 5 for } x) \\ &= 20 - 7 \quad \text{(Multiply first)} \\ &= 13 \quad \text{(Then subtract)} \end{align*} \]

Example 2

Evaluate \( 2a + 3b \) when \( a = 4 \) and \( b = -1 \):

\[ \begin{align*} 2a + 3b &= 2(4) + 3(-1) \\ &= 8 + (-3) \\ &= 5 \end{align*} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Evaluate \( 5y + 2 \) when \( y = 3 \)

\[ \begin{align*} 5y + 2 &= 5(3) + 2 \\ &= 15 + 2 \\ &= 17 \end{align*} \]

Question 2

Evaluate \( 7 - 2x \) when \( x = 4 \)

\[ \begin{align*} 7 - 2x &= 7 - 2(4) \\ &= 7 - 8 \\ &= -1 \end{align*} \]

Question 3

Evaluate \( 3m + 4n \) when \( m = 2 \) and \( n = 5 \)

\[ \begin{align*} 3m + 4n &= 3(2) + 4(5) \\ &= 6 + 20 \\ &= 26 \end{align*} \]

Question 4

Evaluate \( p^2 - 3p \) when \( p = -2 \)

\[ \begin{align*} p^2 - 3p &= (-2)^2 - 3(-2) \\ &= 4 - (-6) \\ &= 4 + 6 \\ &= 10 \end{align*} \]

Working with Fractions

When evaluating expressions with fractions:

  1. Substitute the given values carefully
  2. Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions if needed
  3. Follow order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS)
  4. Simplify fractions in your final answer

Example 1

Evaluate \( \frac{x}{3} + \frac{1}{2} \) when \( x = 5 \):

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{x}{3} + \frac{1}{2} &= \frac{5}{3} + \frac{1}{2} \\ &= \frac{10}{6} + \frac{3}{6} \quad \text{(Find common denominator)} \\ &= \frac{13}{6} \quad \text{or} \quad 2\frac{1}{6} \end{align*} \]

Example 2

Evaluate \( \frac{2a + b}{4} \) when \( a = \frac{1}{2} \) and \( b = 3 \):

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{2a + b}{4} &= \frac{2(\frac{1}{2}) + 3}{4} \\ &= \frac{1 + 3}{4} \\ &= \frac{4}{4} \\ &= 1 \end{align*} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Evaluate \( \frac{y}{4} - \frac{1}{3} \) when \( y = 2 \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{y}{4} - \frac{1}{3} &= \frac{2}{4} - \frac{1}{3} \\ &= \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{3} \\ &= \frac{3}{6} - \frac{2}{6} \\ &= \frac{1}{6} \end{align*} \]

Question 2

Evaluate \( \frac{3x + 1}{5} \) when \( x = \frac{2}{3} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{3x + 1}{5} &= \frac{3(\frac{2}{3}) + 1}{5} \\ &= \frac{2 + 1}{5} \\ &= \frac{3}{5} \end{align*} \]

Question 3

Evaluate \( \frac{a}{2} + \frac{b}{3} \) when \( a = 1 \) and \( b = \frac{3}{2} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{a}{2} + \frac{b}{3} &= \frac{1}{2} + \frac{\frac{3}{2}}{3} \\ &= \frac{1}{2} + \frac{3}{6} \\ &= \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2} \\ &= 1 \end{align*} \]

Word Problems

Solving Real-World Problems

To solve word problems involving algebraic expressions:

  1. Identify what is being asked
  2. Determine the given values
  3. Write the correct expression
  4. Substitute the values and evaluate
  5. Interpret your answer in context

Example 1

The area of a rectangle is given by \( A = lw \). Find the area when \( l = 5 \) cm and \( w = 3\frac{1}{2} \) cm.

\[ \begin{align*} A &= lw \\ &= 5 \times 3\frac{1}{2} \\ &= 5 \times \frac{7}{2} \\ &= \frac{35}{2} \\ &= 17\frac{1}{2} \text{ cm}^2 \end{align*} \]

Example 2

The cost of \( n \) books at \$12 each plus a \$5 delivery fee is given by \( C = 12n + 5 \). Find the total cost for 7 books.

\[ \begin{align*} C &= 12n + 5 \\ &= 12(7) + 5 \\ &= 84 + 5 \\ &= \$89 \end{align*} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

The perimeter of a square is \( P = 4s \). Find the perimeter when \( s = 5\frac{1}{4} \) cm.

\[ \begin{align*} P &= 4s \\ &= 4 \times 5\frac{1}{4} \\ &= 4 \times \frac{21}{4} \\ &= 21 \text{ cm} \end{align*} \]

Question 2

A taxi charges \$3 as base fare plus \$2 per km. The cost is \( C = 3 + 2d \) where \( d \) is distance in km. Find the cost for a 12.5 km ride.

\[ \begin{align*} C &= 3 + 2d \\ &= 3 + 2(12.5) \\ &= 3 + 25 \\ &= \$28 \end{align*} \]

Question 3

The average of three numbers is \( A = \frac{a + b + c}{3} \). Find the average when \( a = 8 \), \( b = 12 \), and \( c = 7 \).

\[ \begin{align*} A &= \frac{a + b + c}{3} \\ &= \frac{8 + 12 + 7}{3} \\ &= \frac{27}{3} \\ &= 9 \end{align*} \]
