Introduction to Factoring

Factoring is the process of breaking down an expression into simpler parts (factors) that when multiplied together give the original expression. It's like "un-multiplying" an expression.

Factoring is very useful in algebra for simplifying expressions, solving equations, and finding roots of polynomials. In this lesson, we'll learn two main factoring methods:

  1. Factoring using Common Factors
  2. Factoring by Grouping

Example of a factored expression:

\[ 3x + 6 = 3(x + 2) \]

Here, 3 is the common factor of both terms.

Factoring Using Common Factors

What is a Common Factor?

A common factor is a term that divides exactly into each term of an expression. To factor using common factors:

  1. Identify the greatest common factor (GCF) of all terms
  2. Divide each term by the GCF
  3. Write the expression as GCF × (result from division)

Step-by-Step Examples

Example 1: Factor \( 4x + 8 \)

Step 1: GCF of 4x and 8 is 4

Step 2: Divide each term by 4 → \( \frac{4x}{4} = x \) and \( \frac{8}{4} = 2 \)

Step 3: Write as \( 4(x + 2) \)

\[ \boxed{4x + 8 = 4(x + 2)} \]

Example 2: Factor \( 6a^2b - 9ab^2 \)

Step 1: GCF of coefficients is 3, GCF of variables is ab

Step 2: Divide each term by 3ab → \( \frac{6a^2b}{3ab} = 2a \) and \( \frac{-9ab^2}{3ab} = -3b \)

Step 3: Write as \( 3ab(2a - 3b) \)

\[ \boxed{6a^2b - 9ab^2 = 3ab(2a - 3b)} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Factor completely: \( 5x + 15 \)

GCF is 5

\( 5x ÷ 5 = x \)

\( 15 ÷ 5 = 3 \)

\(\boxed{5(x + 3)}\)

Question 2

Factor completely: \( 12y^2 - 18y \)

GCF is 6y

\( 12y^2 ÷ 6y = 2y \)

\( -18y ÷ 6y = -3 \)

\(\boxed{6y(2y - 3)}\)

Question 3

Factor completely: \( 7a^3b^2 + 14a^2b^3 \)

GCF is 7a²b²

\( 7a^3b^2 ÷ 7a^2b^2 = a \)

\( 14a^2b^3 ÷ 7a^2b^2 = 2b \)

\(\boxed{7a^2b^2(a + 2b)}\)

Factoring by Grouping

When to Use Grouping

The grouping method is used when an expression has four or more terms with no common factor in all terms. The steps are:

  1. Group terms that have common factors
  2. Factor out the GCF from each group
  3. If the remaining binomials match, factor them out

Step-by-Step Examples

Example 1: Factor \( ax + ay + bx + by \)

Step 1: Group terms: \( (ax + ay) + (bx + by) \)

Step 2: Factor each group: \( a(x + y) + b(x + y) \)

Step 3: Factor out common binomial: \( (x + y)(a + b) \)

\[ \boxed{ax + ay + bx + by = (x + y)(a + b)} \]

Example 2: Factor \( 2x^2 + 4x + 3x + 6 \)

Step 1: Group terms: \( (2x^2 + 4x) + (3x + 6) \)

Step 2: Factor each group: \( 2x(x + 2) + 3(x + 2) \)

Step 3: Factor out common binomial: \( (x + 2)(2x + 3) \)

\[ \boxed{2x^2 + 4x + 3x + 6 = (x + 2)(2x + 3)} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Factor completely: \( mn + mp + n^2 + np \)

Group: \( (mn + mp) + (n^2 + np) \)

Factor groups: \( m(n + p) + n(n + p) \)

Factor binomial: \( \boxed{(n + p)(m + n)} \)

Question 2

Factor completely: \( 3x^2 + 6x + 4x + 8 \)

Group: \( (3x^2 + 6x) + (4x + 8) \)

Factor groups: \( 3x(x + 2) + 4(x + 2) \)

Factor binomial: \( \boxed{(x + 2)(3x + 4)} \)

Question 3

Factor completely: \( ab + ac - b - c \)

Group: \( (ab + ac) + (-b - c) \)

Factor groups: \( a(b + c) -1(b + c) \)

Factor binomial: \( \boxed{(b + c)(a - 1)} \)

Applying Factoring to Word Problems

Real-World Applications

Factoring helps solve problems involving area, perimeter, and other real-world scenarios. Here's how to approach them:

  1. Read the problem carefully and identify what's given and what's needed
  2. Write an algebraic expression representing the situation
  3. Factor the expression to simplify it
  4. Solve for the unknown if needed

Problem Examples

Example 1: The area of a rectangle is \( 3x^2 + 6x \). Find expressions for its length and width.

Solution: Factor the area expression

\( 3x^2 + 6x = 3x(x + 2) \)

Possible dimensions: \( \boxed{3x} \) and \( \boxed{x + 2} \)

Example 2: A garden has length \( x + 5 \) and area \( 2x^2 + 10x \). Find its width.

Solution: Area = length × width, so width = Area ÷ length

Factor numerator: \( 2x^2 + 10x = 2x(x + 5) \)

Width = \( \frac{2x(x + 5)}{x + 5} = \boxed{2x} \)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

The area of a rectangle is \( 5x^2 + 15x \). If its width is 5x, find its length.

Factor area: \( 5x^2 + 15x = 5x(x + 3) \)

Length = Area ÷ Width = \( \frac{5x(x + 3)}{5x} = \boxed{x + 3} \)

Question 2

The perimeter of a square is \( 12x + 20 \). Find an expression for its side length.

Perimeter = 4 × side, so side = Perimeter ÷ 4

Factor numerator: \( 12x + 20 = 4(3x + 5) \)

Side length = \( \frac{4(3x + 5)}{4} = \boxed{3x + 5} \)

Question 3

A box has volume \( x^3 + 2x^2 \). If its height is x and width is x, find its length.

Volume = length × width × height

Factor volume: \( x^3 + 2x^2 = x^2(x + 2) \)

Length = \( \frac{x^2(x + 2)}{x \times x} = \boxed{x + 2} \)
