Introduction

The distributive property is a fundamental property in algebra that allows us to multiply a single term by each term inside a bracket. This property is essential for simplifying expressions and solving equations.

The distributive property states that for any numbers a, b, and c:

\[ a(b + c) = ab + ac \]

Key Concept:

The term outside the brackets is multiplied by each term inside the brackets.

Visual Representation:

If you have 3 groups of (x + 2), it's the same as 3x + 6:

\[ 3(x + 2) = 3 \times x + 3 \times 2 = 3x + 6 \]

Why It's Important

The distributive property helps us:

  • Simplify algebraic expressions
  • Solve equations more easily
  • Multiply binomial expressions
  • Factorize expressions

Basic Expansion

To expand an expression means to remove the brackets by multiplying each term inside by the term outside.

Example 1: Expand \( 2(x + 3) \)

\[ 2(x + 3) = 2 \times x + 2 \times 3 = 2x + 6 \]

Example 2: Expand \( -3(2y - 5) \)

\[ -3(2y - 5) = -3 \times 2y + (-3) \times (-5) = -6y + 15 \]

Remember:

Pay attention to the signs when multiplying:

  • Positive × Positive = Positive
  • Positive × Negative = Negative
  • Negative × Negative = Positive

Common Mistakes

Students often make these errors when expanding:

  1. Forgetting to multiply the second term: \( 3(x + 2) \neq 3x + 2 \)
  2. Mishandling negative signs: \( -2(x - 3) \neq -2x - 6 \) (should be \(-2x + 6\))
  3. Not distributing to all terms: \( x(2y + 3z - 4) \neq 2xy + 3z - 4 \)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Expand \( 4(x + 5) \)

\[ 4(x + 5) = 4 \times x + 4 \times 5 = 4x + 20 \]

Question 2

Expand \( -2(3y - 7) \)

\[ -2(3y - 7) = -2 \times 3y + (-2) \times (-7) = -6y + 14 \]

Question 3

Expand \( x(2x + 3y - 4) \)

\[ x(2x + 3y - 4) = x \times 2x + x \times 3y + x \times (-4) = 2x^2 + 3xy - 4x \]

Question 4

Expand \( \frac{1}{2}(4a + 6b - 8) \)

\[ \frac{1}{2}(4a + 6b - 8) = \frac{1}{2} \times 4a + \frac{1}{2} \times 6b + \frac{1}{2} \times (-8) = 2a + 3b - 4 \]

Multiply Binomial Expressions

FOIL Method

To multiply two binomials, we use the FOIL method, which stands for:

  • First terms
  • Outer terms
  • Inner terms
  • Last terms

Example: Multiply \( (x + 2)(x + 3) \)

\[ \begin{align*} \text{F} & = x \times x = x^2 \\ \text{O} & = x \times 3 = 3x \\ \text{I} & = 2 \times x = 2x \\ \text{L} & = 2 \times 3 = 6 \\ \text{Total} & = x^2 + 3x + 2x + 6 = x^2 + 5x + 6 \end{align*} \]

Alternative Approach: Distributive Property

You can also think of binomial multiplication as applying the distributive property twice:

\[ (a + b)(c + d) = a(c + d) + b(c + d) = ac + ad + bc + bd \]

Example: Multiply \( (2x - 3)(x + 4) \)

\[ \begin{align*} (2x - 3)(x + 4) & = 2x(x + 4) - 3(x + 4) \\ & = 2x^2 + 8x - 3x - 12 \\ & = 2x^2 + 5x - 12 \end{align*} \]

Special Cases

There are special patterns to recognize:

Difference of Squares:

\[ (a + b)(a - b) = a^2 - b^2 \]

Example: \( (x + 5)(x - 5) = x^2 - 25 \)

Perfect Square Trinomial:

\[ (a + b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2 \]

Example: \( (x + 3)^2 = x^2 + 6x + 9 \)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Multiply \( (x + 4)(x + 2) \)

\[ \begin{align*} (x + 4)(x + 2) & = x \times x + x \times 2 + 4 \times x + 4 \times 2 \\ & = x^2 + 2x + 4x + 8 \\ & = x^2 + 6x + 8 \end{align*} \]

Question 2

Multiply \( (2y - 3)(y + 5) \)

\[ \begin{align*} (2y - 3)(y + 5) & = 2y \times y + 2y \times 5 - 3 \times y - 3 \times 5 \\ & = 2y^2 + 10y - 3y - 15 \\ & = 2y^2 + 7y - 15 \end{align*} \]

Question 3

Multiply \( (3a - 4)(2a - 1) \)

\[ \begin{align*} (3a - 4)(2a - 1) & = 3a \times 2a + 3a \times (-1) - 4 \times 2a - 4 \times (-1) \\ & = 6a^2 - 3a - 8a + 4 \\ & = 6a^2 - 11a + 4 \end{align*} \]

Question 4

Multiply \( (x + 7)(x - 7) \)

\[ (x + 7)(x - 7) = x^2 - 7^2 = x^2 - 49 \]

Question 5

Multiply \( (2x + 5)^2 \)

\[ (2x + 5)^2 = (2x)^2 + 2 \times 2x \times 5 + 5^2 = 4x^2 + 20x + 25 \]
