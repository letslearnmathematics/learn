Introduction

Algebraic fractions are fractions where the numerator, denominator, or both contain algebraic expressions. They follow the same rules as numerical fractions but require additional steps for simplification.

In this lesson, we will learn how to perform all four basic operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division) with algebraic fractions. The key to working with algebraic fractions is to:

  1. Factorize expressions where possible
  2. Find common denominators for addition/subtraction
  3. Multiply numerators and denominators separately for multiplication
  4. Invert and multiply for division

Examples of algebraic fractions:

\[ \frac{x}{2}, \quad \frac{3}{x+1}, \quad \frac{x^2-4}{x-2}, \quad \frac{a+b}{a-b} \]

Multiplication and Division

Multiplication Rule

To multiply algebraic fractions:

  1. Factorize numerators and denominators completely
  2. Multiply numerators together and denominators together
  3. Simplify by canceling common factors
\[ \frac{a}{b} \times \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a \times c}{b \times d} \]

Division Rule

To divide algebraic fractions:

  1. Keep the first fraction as is
  2. Change the division sign to multiplication
  3. Flip (take reciprocal of) the second fraction
  4. Follow multiplication rules
\[ \frac{a}{b} \div \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a}{b} \times \frac{d}{c} = \frac{a \times d}{b \times c} \]

Example 1: Multiplication

Simplify: \( \frac{x}{y} \times \frac{y^2}{x^2} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{x}{y} \times \frac{y^2}{x^2} &= \frac{x \times y^2}{y \times x^2} \\ &= \frac{xy^2}{x^2y} \\ &= \frac{y}{x} \quad \text{(after canceling common factors)} \end{align*} \]

Example 2: Division

Simplify: \( \frac{a^2 - b^2}{a + b} \div \frac{a - b}{a} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{a^2 - b^2}{a + b} \div \frac{a - b}{a} &= \frac{a^2 - b^2}{a + b} \times \frac{a}{a - b} \\ &= \frac{(a - b)(a + b)}{a + b} \times \frac{a}{a - b} \\ &= \frac{a}{1} = a \end{align*} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Simplify: \( \frac{3x}{4y} \times \frac{8y^2}{9x^2} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{3x}{4y} \times \frac{8y^2}{9x^2} &= \frac{24xy^2}{36x^2y} \\ &= \frac{2y}{3x} \end{align*} \]

Question 2

Simplify: \( \frac{x^2 - 9}{x + 2} \div \frac{x - 3}{x^2 - 4} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{x^2 - 9}{x + 2} \div \frac{x - 3}{x^2 - 4} &= \frac{x^2 - 9}{x + 2} \times \frac{x^2 - 4}{x - 3} \\ &= \frac{(x-3)(x+3)}{x+2} \times \frac{(x-2)(x+2)}{x-3} \\ &= (x+3)(x-2) \end{align*} \]

Question 3

Simplify: \( \frac{a^2 - b^2}{a^2 + ab} \times \frac{a}{a - b} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{a^2 - b^2}{a^2 + ab} \times \frac{a}{a - b} &= \frac{(a-b)(a+b)}{a(a+b)} \times \frac{a}{a-b} \\ &= 1 \end{align*} \]

Addition and Subtraction

Key Steps

To add or subtract algebraic fractions:

  1. Find the Least Common Denominator (LCD)
  2. Rewrite each fraction with the LCD
  3. Add or subtract the numerators
  4. Simplify the resulting fraction
\[ \frac{a}{b} \pm \frac{c}{d} = \frac{ad \pm bc}{bd} \]

Example 1: Same Denominator

Simplify: \( \frac{3}{x} + \frac{5}{x} - \frac{2}{x} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{3}{x} + \frac{5}{x} - \frac{2}{x} &= \frac{3 + 5 - 2}{x} \\ &= \frac{6}{x} \end{align*} \]

Example 2: Different Denominators

Simplify: \( \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} &= \frac{y}{xy} + \frac{x}{xy} \\ &= \frac{x + y}{xy} \end{align*} \]

Example 3: Complex Denominators

Simplify: \( \frac{2}{x-1} - \frac{3}{x+2} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{2}{x-1} - \frac{3}{x+2} &= \frac{2(x+2)}{(x-1)(x+2)} - \frac{3(x-1)}{(x-1)(x+2)} \\ &= \frac{2x + 4 - 3x + 3}{(x-1)(x+2)} \\ &= \frac{-x + 7}{(x-1)(x+2)} \end{align*} \]

Question 1

Simplify: \( \frac{2}{a} + \frac{3}{b} \)

\[ \frac{2b + 3a}{ab} \]

Question 2

Simplify: \( \frac{x}{x+1} - \frac{1}{x-1} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{x}{x+1} - \frac{1}{x-1} &= \frac{x(x-1) - (x+1)}{(x+1)(x-1)} \\ &= \frac{x^2 - x - x - 1}{(x+1)(x-1)} \\ &= \frac{x^2 - 2x - 1}{x^2 - 1} \end{align*} \]

Question 3

Simplify: \( \frac{3}{x-2} + \frac{2}{x+3} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{3}{x-2} + \frac{2}{x+3} &= \frac{3(x+3) + 2(x-2)}{(x-2)(x+3)} \\ &= \frac{3x + 9 + 2x - 4}{(x-2)(x+3)} \\ &= \frac{5x + 5}{(x-2)(x+3)} \\ &= \frac{5(x+1)}{(x-2)(x+3)} \end{align*} \]

Mixed Operations

Order of Operations

When dealing with mixed operations in algebraic fractions:

  1. Follow the standard order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS)
  2. Perform operations inside parentheses first
  3. Do multiplication and division from left to right
  4. Do addition and subtraction last

Example 1: Combined Operations

Simplify: \( \frac{x}{y} \times \frac{y^2}{x^2} + \frac{1}{x} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{x}{y} \times \frac{y^2}{x^2} + \frac{1}{x} &= \frac{xy^2}{x^2y} + \frac{1}{x} \\ &= \frac{y}{x} + \frac{1}{x} \\ &= \frac{y + 1}{x} \end{align*} \]

Example 2: Complex Expression

Simplify: \( \frac{a}{a-b} - \frac{b}{a+b} \div \frac{a^2 - b^2}{a^2 + 2ab + b^2} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{a}{a-b} - \frac{b}{a+b} \div \frac{a^2 - b^2}{a^2 + 2ab + b^2} &= \frac{a}{a-b} - \frac{b}{a+b} \times \frac{(a+b)^2}{(a-b)(a+b)} \\ &= \frac{a}{a-b} - \frac{b(a+b)}{(a-b)(a+b)} \\ &= \frac{a - b}{a - b} = 1 \end{align*} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Simplify: \( \frac{x}{x+1} \times \frac{x^2-1}{x} + \frac{2}{x+1} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{x}{x+1} \times \frac{x^2-1}{x} + \frac{2}{x+1} &= \frac{x(x-1)(x+1)}{x(x+1)} + \frac{2}{x+1} \\ &= (x-1) + \frac{2}{x+1} \\ &= \frac{(x-1)(x+1) + 2}{x+1} \\ &= \frac{x^2 - 1 + 2}{x+1} \\ &= \frac{x^2 + 1}{x+1} \end{align*} \]

Question 2

Simplify: \( \frac{a+b}{a-b} \div \frac{a^2-b^2}{a^2-2ab+b^2} - \frac{a}{a+b} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{a+b}{a-b} \div \frac{a^2-b^2}{a^2-2ab+b^2} - \frac{a}{a+b} &= \frac{a+b}{a-b} \times \frac{(a-b)^2}{(a-b)(a+b)} - \frac{a}{a+b} \\ &= 1 - \frac{a}{a+b} \\ &= \frac{a+b - a}{a+b} = \frac{b}{a+b} \end{align*} \]
