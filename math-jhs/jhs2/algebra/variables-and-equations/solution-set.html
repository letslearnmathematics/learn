Introduction

Linear inequalities are mathematical expressions that show the relationship between two quantities where one is not necessarily equal to the other. They use inequality symbols:

Inequality Symbols:

  • \( < \) (less than)
  • \( > \) (greater than)
  • \( \leq \) (less than or equal to)
  • \( \geq \) (greater than or equal to)

Examples:

  • \( x + 3 > 7 \)
  • \( 2y - 5 \leq 10 \)
  • \( \frac{z}{4} < 3 \)

Key Concept

Unlike equations which have specific solutions, inequalities typically have a range of solutions that satisfy the condition. We represent these solutions as solution sets.

Solving Linear Inequalities

Solving Process

Solving linear inequalities follows similar steps to solving equations, with one important exception:

Basic Steps:

  1. Isolate the variable term on one side
  2. Simplify both sides as needed
  3. Divide or multiply to solve for the variable

Important Rule:

When you multiply or divide both sides by a negative number, you must reverse the inequality sign.

\[ \text{Example: } -2x > 6 \implies x < -3 \]

Example 1

Solve: \( 3x + 5 < 17 \)

\[ \begin{align*} 3x + 5 &< 17 \\ 3x &< 17 - 5 \\ 3x &< 12 \\ x &< \frac{12}{3} \\ x &< 4 \end{align*} \]

Solution: All real numbers less than 4.

Example 2

Solve: \( -4y + 7 \geq 19 \)

\[ \begin{align*} -4y + 7 &\geq 19 \\ -4y &\geq 19 - 7 \\ -4y &\geq 12 \\ y &\leq \frac{12}{-4} \quad \text{(Sign reversed)} \\ y &\leq -3 \end{align*} \]

Solution: All real numbers less than or equal to -3.

Solution Sets

Understanding Solution Sets

The solution set of an inequality is the collection of all values that satisfy the inequality. We can represent solution sets in different ways:

1. Inequality Notation

Express the range of solutions using inequality symbols:

For \( x > 2 \):

\[ \{x \mid x > 2\} \]

Read as "the set of all x such that x is greater than 2"

2. Number Line

Visual representation on a number line:

For \( x \leq 5 \):

(Picture of number line with closed circle at 5 and shading to the left)

Closed circle includes the endpoint, open circle excludes it.

3. Set Notation

List all possible solutions when domain is limited:

For \( x < 4 \) where \( x \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5\} \):

\[ \{1, 2, 3\} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Solve the inequality: \( 2x - 3 < 7 \)

\[ \begin{align*} 2x - 3 &< 7 \\ 2x &< 7 + 3 \\ 2x &< 10 \\ x &< 5 \end{align*} \]

Solution: \( \{x \mid x < 5\} \)

Question 2

Solve the inequality: \( 5 - 3y \geq 11 \)

\[ \begin{align*} 5 - 3y &\geq 11 \\ -3y &\geq 11 - 5 \\ -3y &\geq 6 \\ y &\leq -2 \quad \text{(Sign reversed)} \end{align*} \]

Solution: \( \{y \mid y \leq -2\} \)

Question 3

Find the solution set of \( \frac{x}{4} + 2 > 3 \) for \( x \in \{0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8\} \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{x}{4} + 2 &> 3 \\ \frac{x}{4} &> 1 \\ x &> 4 \end{align*} \]

Solution: \( \{5, 6, 7, 8\} \)

Solution Sets in Given Domains

Working with Domains

Often, we need to find solutions to inequalities within specific domains (given sets of numbers). This involves:

  1. Solving the inequality generally
  2. Identifying which numbers in the domain satisfy the inequality
  3. Listing these numbers as the solution set

Example 1

Find all integer solutions to \( 2x + 1 \leq 7 \) where \( x \in \{-2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, 4\} \)

\[ \begin{align*} 2x + 1 &\leq 7 \\ 2x &\leq 6 \\ x &\leq 3 \end{align*} \]

From the domain \(\{-2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, 4\}\), the numbers satisfying \( x \leq 3 \) are:

\[ \{-2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3\} \]

Example 2

Find all whole number solutions to \( 3 < x \leq 6 \) where \( x \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7\} \)

The inequality \( 3 < x \leq 6 \) means x is greater than 3 and less than or equal to 6.

From the domain \(\{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7\}\), the numbers satisfying this are:

\[ \{4, 5, 6\} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Find the solution set of \( 5 - 2x > 1 \) for \( x \in \{0, 1, 2, 3, 4\} \)

\[ \begin{align*} 5 - 2x &> 1 \\ -2x &> -4 \\ x &< 2 \quad \text{(Sign reversed)} \end{align*} \]

From the domain, numbers less than 2: \( \{0, 1\} \)

Question 2

Find all integer solutions to \( 3 \leq x < 7 \) where \( x \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8\} \)

Numbers greater than or equal to 3 and less than 7:

\[ \{3, 4, 5, 6\} \]

Question 3

Solve \( \frac{x+1}{2} \geq 2 \) for \( x \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5\} \) and list the solution set

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{x+1}{2} &\geq 2 \\ x + 1 &\geq 4 \\ x &\geq 3 \end{align*} \]

From the domain: \( \{3, 4, 5\} \)
