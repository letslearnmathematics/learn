Introduction

Linear inequalities are mathematical expressions that show the relationship between two quantities where one is not strictly equal to the other. Unlike equations which use the equals sign (=), inequalities use symbols like:

Greater than:

\[ > \]

Less than:

\[ < \]

Greater than or equal to:

\[ \geq \]

Less than or equal to:

\[ \leq \]

Solving linear inequalities is similar to solving linear equations, but with some important differences in the rules, especially when multiplying or dividing by negative numbers.

Balancing Method

Key Idea

The balancing method for solving inequalities works just like solving equations - what you do to one side, you must do to the other side to maintain the inequality relationship.

The goal is to isolate the variable on one side of the inequality sign while keeping the inequality balanced.

Adding/Subtracting

You can add or subtract the same number from both sides without changing the inequality.

Example:

\[ \begin{align*} x + 3 &> 7 \\ x + 3 - 3 &> 7 - 3 \\ x &> 4 \end{align*} \]

Multiplying/Dividing by Positive Numbers

You can multiply or divide both sides by the same positive number without changing the inequality.

Example:

\[ \begin{align*} 2x &\leq 10 \\ \frac{2x}{2} &\leq \frac{10}{2} \\ x &\leq 5 \end{align*} \]

Inequality Rules

Special Rule for Negative Numbers

When you multiply or divide both sides of an inequality by a negative number, you must reverse the inequality sign.

Example:

\[ \begin{align*} -3x &< 6 \\ \frac{-3x}{-3} &> \frac{6}{-3} \quad \text{(Sign reversed from < to >)} \\ x &> -2 \end{align*} \]

Why Reverse the Sign?

Consider the true statement: 4 < 5

Multiply both sides by -1: -4 > -5

The inequality reverses because -4 is actually greater than -5 on the number line.

Solving Examples

Worked Examples

Example 1: Solve \( 2x + 5 \leq 17 \)

\[ \begin{align*} 2x + 5 &\leq 17 \\ 2x &\leq 17 - 5 \quad \text{(Subtract 5 from both sides)} \\ 2x &\leq 12 \\ x &\leq \frac{12}{2} \quad \text{(Divide both sides by 2)} \\ x &\leq 6 \end{align*} \]

Example 2: Solve \( -4x + 3 > 11 \)

\[ \begin{align*} -4x + 3 &> 11 \\ -4x &> 11 - 3 \quad \text{(Subtract 3 from both sides)} \\ -4x &> 8 \\ x &< \frac{8}{-4} \quad \text{(Divide by -4 and reverse the sign)} \\ x &< -2 \end{align*} \]

Example 3: Solve \( 5 - 3x \geq 20 \)

\[ \begin{align*} 5 - 3x &\geq 20 \\ -3x &\geq 20 - 5 \quad \text{(Subtract 5 from both sides)} \\ -3x &\geq 15 \\ x &\leq \frac{15}{-3} \quad \text{(Divide by -3 and reverse the sign)} \\ x &\leq -5 \end{align*} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Solve the inequality: \( x + 7 > 12 \)

\[ \begin{align*} x + 7 &> 12 \\ x &> 12 - 7 \\ x &> 5 \end{align*} \]

Question 2

Solve the inequality: \( 3x - 4 \leq 11 \)

\[ \begin{align*} 3x - 4 &\leq 11 \\ 3x &\leq 11 + 4 \\ 3x &\leq 15 \\ x &\leq 5 \end{align*} \]

Question 3

Solve the inequality: \( -2x + 5 < 13 \)

\[ \begin{align*} -2x + 5 &< 13 \\ -2x &< 13 - 5 \\ -2x &< 8 \\ x &> -4 \quad \text{(Sign reversed)} \end{align*} \]

Question 4

Solve the inequality: \( 4(x - 3) \geq 2x + 6 \)

\[ \begin{align*} 4(x - 3) &\geq 2x + 6 \\ 4x - 12 &\geq 2x + 6 \\ 4x - 2x &\geq 6 + 12 \\ 2x &\geq 18 \\ x &\geq 9 \end{align*} \]

Question 5

Solve the inequality: \( \frac{1}{2}x + 3 \leq \frac{1}{4}x + 5 \)

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{1}{2}x + 3 &\leq \frac{1}{4}x + 5 \\ \frac{1}{2}x - \frac{1}{4}x &\leq 5 - 3 \\ \frac{1}{4}x &\leq 2 \\ x &\leq 8 \end{align*} \]

Word Problems

Inequalities are often used to solve real-world problems where quantities have minimum or maximum limits rather than exact values.

Example 1: Budget Problem

Kwame wants to buy some notebooks that cost ₵5 each. He has ₵50 to spend but wants to have at least ₵20 left. How many notebooks can he buy?

Solution:

Let \( n \) = number of notebooks

\[ \begin{align*} 50 - 5n &\geq 20 \\ -5n &\geq 20 - 50 \\ -5n &\geq -30 \\ n &\leq 6 \quad \text{(Divided by -5, so sign reverses)} \end{align*} \]

Kwame can buy up to 6 notebooks.

Example 2: Age Problem

Esi is 5 years older than Kofi. The sum of their ages is less than 31. How old can Kofi be?

Solution:

Let \( k \) = Kofi's age

Then Esi's age = \( k + 5 \)

\[ \begin{align*} k + (k + 5) &< 31 \\ 2k + 5 &< 31 \\ 2k &< 26 \\ k &< 13 \end{align*} \]

Kofi must be less than 13 years old.

Word Problem Practice

Question 1

A school is planning a field trip. Each bus can hold 35 students. There are at least 280 students going on the trip. How many buses are needed?

Let \( b \) = number of buses needed

\[ \begin{align*} 35b &\geq 280 \\ b &\geq \frac{280}{35} \\ b &\geq 8 \end{align*} \]

At least 8 buses are needed.

Question 2

A phone plan charges ₵0.50 per minute plus a ₵5 monthly fee. If Ama wants to spend no more than ₵30 this month, how many minutes can she use?

Let \( m \) = number of minutes

\[ \begin{align*} 5 + 0.5m &\leq 30 \\ 0.5m &\leq 25 \\ m &\leq 50 \end{align*} \]

Ama can use up to 50 minutes.

Question 3

The length of a rectangle is 10 cm more than its width. The perimeter must be at least 60 cm. What are possible widths?

Let \( w \) = width, then length = \( w + 10 \)

Perimeter \( P = 2(w + l) = 2(w + w + 10) = 2(2w + 10) = 4w + 20 \)

\[ \begin{align*} 4w + 20 &\geq 60 \\ 4w &\geq 40 \\ w &\geq 10 \end{align*} \]

The width must be at least 10 cm.
