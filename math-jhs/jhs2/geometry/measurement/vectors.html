Introduction to Vectors

A vector is a quantity that has both magnitude (size) and direction. Unlike scalars which only have magnitude, vectors are represented by arrows where:

  • The length represents the magnitude
  • The arrowhead shows the direction

Vectors can be written in component form:

\[ \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} \]

Where x is the horizontal component and y is the vertical component.

Notation:

Vectors can be written as:

  • \(\vec{v}\) (with an arrow)
  • \(\mathbf{v}\) (boldface)
  • \(v\) (with an underline)

Examples:

Some vector quantities include:

  • Displacement
  • Velocity
  • Force
  • Acceleration

Adding Vectors Graphically

The Triangle Method

To add two vectors \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) graphically:

  1. Draw vector \(\vec{A}\) to scale
  2. From the head of \(\vec{A}\), draw vector \(\vec{B}\) to scale
  3. The resultant vector \(\vec{R}\) is drawn from the tail of \(\vec{A}\) to the head of \(\vec{B}\)

Example: Adding \(\vec{A} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\vec{B} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}\):

The resultant \(\vec{R} = \vec{A} + \vec{B}\)

The Parallelogram Method

An alternative graphical method:

  1. Draw both vectors \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) from the same origin point
  2. Complete the parallelogram using these vectors as adjacent sides
  3. The resultant \(\vec{R}\) is the diagonal from the origin

Question 1

Add the vectors \(\vec{A} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\vec{B} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}\) using the graphical method.

The resultant vector goes from the tail of \(\vec{A}\) to the head of \(\vec{B}\).

Question 2

Using the parallelogram method, add \(\vec{C} = \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\vec{D} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -3 \end{pmatrix}\).

The resultant is the diagonal of the parallelogram formed by \(\vec{C}\) and \(\vec{D}\).

Adding and Subtracting Vectors in Component Form

Vector Addition

To add vectors in component form, simply add their corresponding components:

For vectors \(\vec{A} = \begin{pmatrix} a_x \\ a_y \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\vec{B} = \begin{pmatrix} b_x \\ b_y \end{pmatrix}\):

\[ \vec{A} + \vec{B} = \begin{pmatrix} a_x + b_x \\ a_y + b_y \end{pmatrix} \]

Example:

\[ \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 + (-1) \\ -2 + 5 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} \]

Vector Subtraction

To subtract vectors, subtract their corresponding components:

For vectors \(\vec{A} = \begin{pmatrix} a_x \\ a_y \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\vec{B} = \begin{pmatrix} b_x \\ b_y \end{pmatrix}\):

\[ \vec{A} - \vec{B} = \begin{pmatrix} a_x - b_x \\ a_y - b_y \end{pmatrix} \]

Example:

\[ \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} - \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 - 2 \\ 1 - 4 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -3 \end{pmatrix} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Find \(\vec{A} + \vec{B}\) where \(\vec{A} = \begin{pmatrix} -2 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\vec{B} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix}\).

Add corresponding components:

\[ \begin{pmatrix} -2 + 3 \\ 5 + (-1) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} \]

Question 2

Calculate \(\vec{C} - \vec{D}\) for \(\vec{C} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\vec{D} = \begin{pmatrix} -3 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}\).

Subtract corresponding components:

\[ \begin{pmatrix} 0 - (-3) \\ -4 - 2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -6 \end{pmatrix} \]

Question 3

Given \(\vec{P} = \begin{pmatrix} 1.5 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\vec{Q} = \begin{pmatrix} -0.5 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}\), find \(2\vec{P} - \vec{Q}\).

First multiply, then subtract:

\[ 2\vec{P} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix} \] \[ 2\vec{P} - \vec{Q} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 - (-0.5) \\ -4 - 3 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3.5 \\ -7 \end{pmatrix} \]

Scalar Multiplication of Vectors

Multiplying a Vector by a Scalar

A scalar is a real number. When we multiply a vector by a scalar, we multiply each component by that number:

For vector \(\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix}\) and scalar \(k\):

\[ k\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} kx \\ ky \end{pmatrix} \]

Example:

\[ 3 \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 6 \\ -3 \end{pmatrix} \]

Effect on Magnitude:

The magnitude of the vector is scaled by the absolute value of \(k\):

\[ |k\vec{v}| = |k| \cdot |\vec{v}| \]

Effect on Direction:

If \(k > 0\), direction remains the same

If \(k < 0\), direction is reversed

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Find \(-2\vec{A}\) where \(\vec{A} = \begin{pmatrix} 1.5 \\ -3 \end{pmatrix}\).

Multiply each component by -2:

\[ -2 \begin{pmatrix} 1.5 \\ -3 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -3 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix} \]

Question 2

Given \(\vec{B} = \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\), find \(\frac{1}{2}\vec{B}\).

Multiply each component by 0.5:

\[ \frac{1}{2} \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \]

Question 3

Calculate \(3\vec{C} - 2\vec{D}\) where \(\vec{C} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\vec{D} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix}\).

First multiply, then subtract:

\[ 3\vec{C} = \begin{pmatrix} -3 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix}, \quad 2\vec{D} = \begin{pmatrix} 6 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} \] \[ 3\vec{C} - 2\vec{D} = \begin{pmatrix} -3 - 6 \\ 6 - (-2) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -9 \\ 8 \end{pmatrix} \]

Combined Vector Operations

Performing Multiple Operations

We can combine addition, subtraction, and scalar multiplication in various ways:

Example: Find \(2\vec{A} + 3\vec{B} - \vec{C}\) where:

\[ \vec{A} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \vec{B} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \vec{C} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} \]

Solution:

\[ 2\vec{A} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix}, \quad 3\vec{B} = \begin{pmatrix} 9 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \] \[ 2\vec{A} + 3\vec{B} = \begin{pmatrix} 11 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix} \] \[ 2\vec{A} + 3\vec{B} - \vec{C} = \begin{pmatrix} 11 - (-1) \\ -4 - 4 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 12 \\ -8 \end{pmatrix} \]

Properties of Vector Operations

Commutative Property:

\[ \vec{A} + \vec{B} = \vec{B} + \vec{A} \]

Associative Property:

\[ (\vec{A} + \vec{B}) + \vec{C} = \vec{A} + (\vec{B} + \vec{C}) \]

Distributive Property:

\[ k(\vec{A} + \vec{B}) = k\vec{A} + k\vec{B} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Find \(3\vec{P} - 2\vec{Q}\) where \(\vec{P} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\vec{Q} = \begin{pmatrix} -3 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}\).

Perform the operations step by step:

\[ 3\vec{P} = \begin{pmatrix} 6 \\ -3 \end{pmatrix}, \quad 2\vec{Q} = \begin{pmatrix} -6 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} \] \[ 3\vec{P} - 2\vec{Q} = \begin{pmatrix} 6 - (-6) \\ -3 - 4 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 12 \\ -7 \end{pmatrix} \]

Question 2

Calculate \(\frac{1}{2}(\vec{A} + \vec{B})\) for \(\vec{A} = \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\vec{B} = \begin{pmatrix} -2 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix}\).

First add, then multiply by 0.5:

\[ \vec{A} + \vec{B} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} \] \[ \frac{1}{2}(\vec{A} + \vec{B}) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} \]

Question 3

Given \(\vec{X} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\), \(\vec{Y} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}\), and \(\vec{Z} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix}\), find \(2\vec{X} + \vec{Y} - 3\vec{Z}\).

Perform each operation:

\[ 2\vec{X} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad 3\vec{Z} = \begin{pmatrix} 9 \\ -3 \end{pmatrix} \] \[ 2\vec{X} + \vec{Y} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} \] \[ 2\vec{X} + \vec{Y} - 3\vec{Z} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 - 9 \\ 2 - (-3) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -8 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix} \]
