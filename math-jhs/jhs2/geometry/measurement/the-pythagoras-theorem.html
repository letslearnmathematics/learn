Introduction

The Pythagorean Theorem is a fundamental principle in geometry that relates the sides of a right-angled triangle. It states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides.

This theorem is named after the ancient Greek mathematician Pythagoras, although it was known to Babylonian and Egyptian mathematicians long before Pythagoras lived.

A right-angled triangle has:

  • Two shorter sides called legs
  • One longest side called the hypotenuse (opposite the right angle)

Theorem Statement

The Pythagorean Formula

For any right-angled triangle with legs a and b, and hypotenuse c:

\[ a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \]

Key Points

The Pythagorean Theorem only applies to right-angled triangles. It does not work for other types of triangles.

Identifying the Hypotenuse

The hypotenuse is always:

  • The longest side of the right triangle
  • Opposite the right angle
  • The side that stands alone in the formula (c)

Verifying Right Angles

You can use the theorem to check if a triangle is right-angled:

If \( a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \), then it's a right triangle.

Finding the Hypotenuse

Step-by-Step Method

When you know both legs of a right triangle and need to find the hypotenuse:

Step 1: Identify the legs and hypotenuse

Label the sides: a and b are legs, c is hypotenuse

Step 2: Apply the formula

\[ c = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2} \]

Step 3: Substitute the known values

Replace a and b with the given lengths

Step 4: Calculate

Square the numbers, add them, then take the square root

Example Problem

Find the hypotenuse of a right triangle with legs 3 cm and 4 cm.

Solution:

\[ c = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} \]

\[ c = \sqrt{9 + 16} \]

\[ c = \sqrt{25} \]

\[ c = 5 \text{ cm} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A right triangle has legs of 6 cm and 8 cm. Find the length of the hypotenuse.

\[ c = \sqrt{6^2 + 8^2} = \sqrt{36 + 64} = \sqrt{100} = 10 \text{ cm} \]

Question 2

Find the hypotenuse of a right triangle with legs 5 m and 12 m.

\[ c = \sqrt{5^2 + 12^2} = \sqrt{25 + 144} = \sqrt{169} = 13 \text{ m} \]

Question 3

The legs of a right triangle are 9 cm and 12 cm. What is the length of the hypotenuse?

\[ c = \sqrt{9^2 + 12^2} = \sqrt{81 + 144} = \sqrt{225} = 15 \text{ cm} \]

Finding a Leg

Step-by-Step Method

When you know one leg and the hypotenuse, and need to find the other leg:

Step 1: Identify the known and unknown sides

Label them: a is known leg, c is hypotenuse, find b

Step 2: Rearrange the formula

\[ b = \sqrt{c^2 - a^2} \]

Step 3: Substitute the known values

Replace c and a with the given lengths

Step 4: Calculate

Square the numbers, subtract, then take the square root

Example Problem

Find the missing leg of a right triangle with one leg 5 cm and hypotenuse 13 cm.

Solution:

\[ b = \sqrt{13^2 - 5^2} \]

\[ b = \sqrt{169 - 25} \]

\[ b = \sqrt{144} \]

\[ b = 12 \text{ cm} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A right triangle has one leg of 9 cm and hypotenuse of 15 cm. Find the other leg.

\[ b = \sqrt{15^2 - 9^2} = \sqrt{225 - 81} = \sqrt{144} = 12 \text{ cm} \]

Question 2

Find the missing leg of a right triangle with one leg 7 m and hypotenuse 25 m.

\[ b = \sqrt{25^2 - 7^2} = \sqrt{625 - 49} = \sqrt{576} = 24 \text{ m} \]

Question 3

The hypotenuse of a right triangle is 17 cm and one leg is 8 cm. Find the other leg.

\[ b = \sqrt{17^2 - 8^2} = \sqrt{289 - 64} = \sqrt{225} = 15 \text{ cm} \]

Word Problems

Problem-Solving Strategy

To solve real-world problems using the Pythagorean Theorem:

Step 1: Draw a diagram

Visualize the problem with a right triangle

Step 2: Identify the sides

Label the legs and hypotenuse

Step 3: Apply the formula

Use \( a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \) or its variations

Step 4: Solve and interpret

Calculate and answer the original question

Example Problem

A ladder 5 m long leans against a wall. If the foot of the ladder is 3 m from the wall, how high up the wall does the ladder reach?

Solution:

Let the height be h:

\[ 3^2 + h^2 = 5^2 \]

\[ 9 + h^2 = 25 \]

\[ h^2 = 16 \]

\[ h = 4 \text{ m} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A rectangular field is 30 m long and 16 m wide. What is the length of its diagonal?

\[ d = \sqrt{30^2 + 16^2} = \sqrt{900 + 256} = \sqrt{1156} = 34 \text{ m} \]

Question 2

A television screen measures 24 inches by 18 inches. What is the length of its diagonal?

\[ d = \sqrt{24^2 + 18^2} = \sqrt{576 + 324} = \sqrt{900} = 30 \text{ inches} \]

Question 3

A flagpole is 12 m tall. A rope tied to the top of the pole reaches a point on the ground 5 m from the base. How long is the rope?

\[ r = \sqrt{12^2 + 5^2} = \sqrt{144 + 25} = \sqrt{169} = 13 \text{ m} \]
