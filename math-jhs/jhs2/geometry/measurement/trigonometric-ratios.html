Introduction

Trigonometric ratios are relationships between the sides of a right-angled triangle and its angles. These ratios help us solve problems involving right-angled triangles in mathematics and real-world applications.

In this lesson, we will learn about the three primary trigonometric ratios (sine, cosine, and tangent), understand angles of elevation and depression, and apply these concepts to solve practical problems.

Primary Trigonometric Ratios

The Three Primary Ratios

For any right-angled triangle with angle θ, we define:

1. Sine (sin) - opposite/hypotenuse

2. Cosine (cos) - adjacent/hypotenuse

3. Tangent (tan) - opposite/adjacent

Definitions

Consider a right-angled triangle ABC with right angle at C. For angle θ at A:

Sine (sinθ)

\[ \sinθ = \frac{\text{Opposite side}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} = \frac{BC}{AB} \]

Cosine (cosθ)

\[ \cosθ = \frac{\text{Adjacent side}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} = \frac{AC}{AB} \]

Tangent (tanθ)

\[ \tanθ = \frac{\text{Opposite side}}{\text{Adjacent side}} = \frac{BC}{AC} \]

Example: For a triangle with sides 3, 4, 5 (right-angled at the side between 3 and 4):

\[ \begin{align*} \sinθ &= \frac{4}{5} \\ \cosθ &= \frac{3}{5} \\ \tanθ &= \frac{4}{3} \end{align*} \]

Remember with SOH-CAH-TOA

A helpful way to remember the trigonometric ratios:

SOH: Sine = Opposite / Hypotenuse

CAH: Cosine = Adjacent / Hypotenuse

TOA: Tangent = Opposite / Adjacent

Practice Exercise

Question 1

In a right-angled triangle, the opposite side to angle θ is 5cm and the hypotenuse is 13cm. Find sinθ and cosθ.

First find the adjacent side using Pythagoras theorem:

\[ \text{Adjacent} = \sqrt{13^2 - 5^2} = \sqrt{169 - 25} = \sqrt{144} = 12\text{cm} \] \[ \sinθ = \frac{5}{13}, \quad \cosθ = \frac{12}{13} \]

Question 2

If tanθ = 3/4 and the adjacent side is 8cm, find the length of the opposite side and hypotenuse.

Given tanθ = opposite/adjacent = 3/4:

\[ \frac{\text{Opposite}}{8} = \frac{3}{4} \Rightarrow \text{Opposite} = 6\text{cm} \] \[ \text{Hypotenuse} = \sqrt{6^2 + 8^2} = \sqrt{36 + 64} = \sqrt{100} = 10\text{cm} \]

Question 3

Find all trigonometric ratios for angle θ in a right-angled triangle where the adjacent side is 5cm and hypotenuse is 13cm.

First find the opposite side:

\[ \text{Opposite} = \sqrt{13^2 - 5^2} = \sqrt{169 - 25} = \sqrt{144} = 12\text{cm} \] \[ \sinθ = \frac{12}{13}, \quad \cosθ = \frac{5}{13}, \quad \tanθ = \frac{12}{5} \]

Angles of Elevation & Depression

Definitions

Angle of Elevation: The angle between the horizontal and the line of sight when looking upward at an object.

Angle of Depression: The angle between the horizontal and the line of sight when looking downward at an object.

Real-life Applications

These concepts are used in various real-world situations:

Architecture

Determining heights of buildings or structures

Aviation

Calculating aircraft approach angles

Navigation

Determining positions relative to landmarks

Surveying

Measuring heights and distances

Example: If a person standing 20m from a building looks up at 30° to see the top, the building height (h) is:

\[ \tan30° = \frac{h}{20} \Rightarrow h = 20 \times \tan30° \approx 11.55\text{m} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A ladder leans against a wall at 60° to the ground. If the ladder is 10m long, how high up the wall does it reach?

Using sine ratio:

\[ \sin60° = \frac{\text{Height}}{10} \] \[ \text{Height} = 10 \times \sin60° = 10 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 8.66\text{m} \]

Question 2

From the top of a 50m tower, the angle of depression to a car on the ground is 25°. How far is the car from the base of the tower?

Using tangent ratio:

\[ \tan25° = \frac{50}{\text{Distance}} \] \[ \text{Distance} = \frac{50}{\tan25°} \approx 107.2\text{m} \]

Question 3

A plane flying at 5000m spots a ship at an angle of depression of 15°. What is the horizontal distance from the plane to the ship?

Using tangent ratio:

\[ \tan15° = \frac{5000}{\text{Distance}} \] \[ \text{Distance} = \frac{5000}{\tan15°} \approx 18,660\text{m} \]

Solving Problems

Problem-Solving Approach

When solving trigonometric problems:

1. Draw a clear diagram of the situation

2. Identify the right-angled triangle(s)

3. Label known sides and angles

4. Choose the appropriate trigonometric ratio

5. Set up and solve the equation

Combined Problems

Some problems require using both trigonometric ratios and Pythagoras' theorem.

Example: A flagpole is anchored by a 10m wire making 50° with the ground. How tall is the flagpole, and how far is the anchor from the base?

Solution:

\[ \text{Height} = 10 \times \sin50° \approx 7.66\text{m} \] \[ \text{Distance} = 10 \times \cos50° \approx 6.43\text{m} \]

Verify with Pythagoras:

\[ \sqrt{7.66^2 + 6.43^2} \approx \sqrt{58.68 + 41.34} \approx \sqrt{100.02} \approx 10\text{m} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1

A 5m ladder reaches 4.5m up a wall. What angle does it make with the ground, and how far is the base from the wall?

First find the angle using sine:

\[ \sinθ = \frac{4.5}{5} = 0.9 \] \[ θ \approx \sin^{-1}(0.9) \approx 64.16° \]

Then find base distance using Pythagoras:

\[ \text{Distance} = \sqrt{5^2 - 4.5^2} = \sqrt{25 - 20.25} = \sqrt{4.75} \approx 2.18\text{m} \]

Question 2

From a point 100m from a tower, the angle of elevation to the top is 30°. Find the height of the tower. If a bird flies from this point directly to the top of the tower, how far does it fly?

Height calculation:

\[ \tan30° = \frac{h}{100} \Rightarrow h = 100 \times \tan30° \approx 57.74\text{m} \]

Direct distance (hypotenuse):

\[ \text{Distance} = \sqrt{100^2 + 57.74^2} \approx \sqrt{10,000 + 3,333.9} \approx \sqrt{13,333.9} \approx 115.47\text{m} \]

Question 3

A ship observes a lighthouse at an angle of elevation of 15°. After sailing 500m towards the lighthouse, the angle is now 30°. Find the height of the lighthouse and the original distance from the ship.

Let h be height, x be original distance:

\[ \tan15° = \frac{h}{x} \] \[ \tan30° = \frac{h}{x - 500} \]

From first equation: h = x tan15°

Substitute into second equation:

\[ \tan30° = \frac{x \tan15°}{x - 500} \]

Solving gives x ≈ 1299m, h ≈ 348.2m
