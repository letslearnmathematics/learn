  2. Solve \(\dfrac{x}{4} + \dfrac{3}{5} = \dfrac{3x}{2} - 2\)

    Watch this comprehensive explanation of the solution.

    This video lesson is locked.

    Pay Gh₵ 2.00 to watch the full solution.

    This helps us to bring you more lessons.



    Solution

    \(\dfrac{x}{4} + \dfrac{3}{5} = \dfrac{3x}{2} - 2\)

    Multiplying through by LCM of 20:

    \(\Rightarrow \dfrac{x}{4}(20) +\) \(\dfrac{3}{5}(20) =\) \(\dfrac{3x}{2}(20) - 2(20)\)

    \(\Rightarrow 5x + 3(4) = 3x(10) - 40\)

    \(\Rightarrow 5x + 12 = 30x - 40\)

    Grouping like terms:

    \(\Rightarrow 12 + 40 = 30x - 5x\)

    \(\Rightarrow 52 = 25x\)

    \(\Rightarrow \dfrac{52}{25} = \dfrac{25x}{25}\)

    \(\Rightarrow x = \dfrac{52}{25}\)

    \(\Rightarrow x = 2\dfrac{2}{25}\)

    \(\therefore x\) is \(2\dfrac{2}{25}\)






  3. Find the solution set of \(x + 3 > 19 - 3x\), where \(x\) is a real number.
    Illustrate your answer on the number line.

    Watch this comprehensive explanation of the solution.

    This video lesson is locked.

    Pay Gh₵ 2.00 to watch the full solution.

    This helps us to bring you more lessons.



    Solution

    \(x + 3 > 19 - 3x\)

    Grouping like-terms:

    \(\Rightarrow x + 3x > 19 - 3\)

    \(\Rightarrow 4x > 16\)

    \(\Rightarrow \dfrac{4x}{4} > \dfrac{16}{4}\)

    \(\Rightarrow x > 4\)

    \(\therefore\) the solution set is {\(x:x > 4, x \in\) real numbers}

Question 4

The following is the result of a survey conducted in a class of a Junior Secondary School to find the favourite soft drink of each pupil in the class.

  1. Draw a bar chart showing this information, using a scale of 2cm to 1 unit on the vertical axis.
  2. How many pupils are in the class?
  3. What percentage of pupils in the class prefer Club Cola?

Solution

Question 4\((a)\)

Question 4\((b)\)

Total students in the class

\(\Rightarrow \) 6 + 5 + 8 + 3 + 5 + 4 + 6 + 3

\(\Rightarrow \) 40

\(\therefore \) there are 40 students in the class.


Question 4\((c)\)

Total students in the class \( = 40\)

Number of students who prefer Club Cola \( = 6\)

Percentage of students who prefer Club Cola

\(\Rightarrow \dfrac{Number \ prefering \ Club \ Cola}{Total \ Students} \times 100\% \)

\(\Rightarrow \dfrac{6}{40} \times 100\% \)

\(\Rightarrow \dfrac{2 \times 3}{2 \times 20} \times 100\% \)

\(\Rightarrow \dfrac{3}{20} \times 100\% \)

\(\Rightarrow 3 \times 5\% \)

\(\Rightarrow 15\% \)

\(\therefore \underline{15\% \ of \ students \ prefer \ Club \ Cola.}\)
