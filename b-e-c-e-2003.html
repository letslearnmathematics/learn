Question 1


\(ξ\) = {1, 2, 3, 4, ..., 18};

\(A\) = {Prime numbers} and

\(B\) = {Odd numbers greater than 3}


\(a)\) If \(A\) and \(B\) are subsets of the universal set, \(ξ\), list the members of \(A\) and \(B\).


\(b)\) Find the set

\(\hspace{0.5cm} i)\) \(A \cap B\)
\(\hspace{0.5cm} ii)\) \(A \cup B\)


\(c) \hspace{0.2cm} i)\) Illustrate \(ξ\), \(A\) and \(B\) on a Venn diagram

\(\hspace{0.5cm} ii)\) Shade the region for prime factors of 18 on the Venn diagram.





Question 2


\(a)\) If \(2n - 5m + 10 = 0\), find

\(\hspace{0.5cm} i)\) \(m\), when \(n = 2\)

\(\hspace{0.5cm} ii)\) \(n\), when \(m = 5\)


\(b)\) Simplify \(\frac{6.4 \times 0.25 \times 16}{0.8 \times 0.5}\) leaving your answer in standard form.


\(c)\) A number of sweets were shared among 8 children and each child received 30. If 12 children shared the same number of sweets, how many will each receive?






Question 3


The table shows the distribution of the ages (in years) of children in a nursery school.

\(a)\) Find

\(\hspace{0.5cm} i)\) the modal age

\(\hspace{0.5cm} ii)\) the mean age.


\(b)\) Draw a bar chart for the distribution.


\(c)\) What is the probability that a child chosen from the school is 4 years old?






Question 4


\(a)\) If \(\mathbf{p} = \begin{pmatrix}4 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix}\), \(\mathbf{q} = \begin{pmatrix}0 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\mathbf{r} = \begin{pmatrix}-3 \\ 7 \end{pmatrix}\),

\(\hspace{0.5cm}\) Find \(\mathbf{p} + 2\mathbf{q} + \mathbf{r}\)


\(b)\) Find the solution set of the inequality \(x - \frac{4}{5} \leq \frac{1}{5}\), if the domain is the set
\(\hspace{0.5cm}\){\(-2, -1, 0, 1, 2\)}


\(c)\) A rectangular sheet of metal has length 44 cm and breadth 10 cm. It is folded to form a cylinder with the breadth becoming the height. Calculate

\(\hspace{0.5cm} i)\) the radius of the cylinder formed;

\(\hspace{0.5cm} ii)\) the volume of the cylinder.

\(\hspace{0.5cm}\) [Take \(\pi = \frac{22}{7}\)]





Question 5


\(a)\) Using a pair of compasses and ruler only,

\(\hspace{0.5cm} i)\) Construct the triangle \(ABC\) with \(|AB| = 8\) cm, \(|BC| = 8\) cm and \(|AC| = 7\) cm.

\(\hspace{0.5cm} ii)\) Bisect \(ABC\) and let the bisector meet \(AC\) at \(D\). Produce \(|BD|\) to \(P\) such that \(|BD| = |DP|\). Join \(AP\) and \(CP\).
