Introduction to Limits

A limit describes the value that a function approaches as the input approaches some value. Limits are fundamental to calculus and mathematical analysis.

The limit of f(x) as x approaches a is L:

\[ \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L \]

Intuitive Understanding:

Imagine walking towards a wall. As you get closer, you can predict exactly where you'll touch the wall - this is similar to how limits work in mathematics.

Historical Context:

The concept of limits was developed independently by Newton and Leibniz in the 17th century as part of their work on calculus.

One-Sided Limits

Limits can approach from either the left (negative) or right (positive) side:

Left-hand limit:

\[ \lim_{x \to a^-} f(x) \]

Right-hand limit:

\[ \lim_{x \to a^+} f(x) \]

Existence of Limit:

A limit exists if and only if both one-sided limits exist and are equal:

\[ \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L \iff \lim_{x \to a^-} f(x) = \lim_{x \to a^+} f(x) = L \]

When Limits Don't Exist

Limits may fail to exist in several cases:

  1. Function approaches different values from left and right
  2. Function grows without bound (approaches ±∞)
  3. Function oscillates infinitely as x approaches a

Limits of Functions

Evaluating Limits

Several techniques exist for evaluating limits:

1. Direct Substitution

If f(a) exists, then:

\[ \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a) \]

Works for polynomials and many continuous functions

2. Factoring

For indeterminate forms (0/0):

\[ \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2-4}{x-2} = \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{(x-2)(x+2)}{x-2} = 4 \]

3. Rationalizing

For roots in numerator/denominator:

\[ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sqrt{x+1}-1}{x} = \frac{1}{2} \]

Limits at Infinity

Behavior of functions as x approaches ±∞:

For rational functions:

\[ \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{3x^2+2x-1}{5x^2-4x+7} = \frac{3}{5} \]

Rule: Compare highest powers in numerator and denominator

Horizontal Asymptotes:

If \(\lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = L\), then y = L is a horizontal asymptote

Limit Laws

Basic properties that make limit calculations easier:

Sum Law \(\lim (f+g) = \lim f + \lim g\)
Product Law \(\lim (f \cdot g) = \lim f \cdot \lim g\)
Quotient Law \(\lim \frac{f}{g} = \frac{\lim f}{\lim g}\) (if \(\lim g \neq 0\))
Power Law \(\lim [f(x)]^n = [\lim f(x)]^n\)

Trigonometric Limits

Fundamental Trigonometric Limit

The most important trigonometric limit:

\[ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1 \]

This limit is crucial for deriving derivatives of trigonometric functions.

Related Limits:

\[ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x} = 0 \]

\[ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\tan x}{x} = 1 \]

Evaluating Trigonometric Limits

Techniques for solving trigonometric limits:

1. Direct Substitution

Works for continuous trig functions:

\[ \lim_{x \to \pi/2} \sin x = 1 \]

2. Fundamental Limit

Using \(\frac{\sin x}{x}\) or variations:

\[ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin 3x}{x} = 3 \]

3. Trigonometric Identities

Simplify using identities:

\[ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x^2} = \frac{1}{2} \]

Squeeze Theorem

Also called the Sandwich Theorem, useful for complex trigonometric limits:

If \( g(x) \leq f(x) \leq h(x) \) near a, and

\[ \lim_{x \to a} g(x) = \lim_{x \to a} h(x) = L \]

Then:

\[ \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L \]

Application:

Used to prove \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1\)

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Evaluate \(\lim_{x \to 3} \frac{x^2 - 9}{x - 3}\)

Factor the numerator:

\[ \frac{x^2 - 9}{x - 3} = \frac{(x-3)(x+3)}{x-3} = x+3 \] \[ \lim_{x \to 3} (x+3) = 6 \]

Question 2

Find \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin 5x}{3x}\)

Use the fundamental trigonometric limit:

\[ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin 5x}{3x} = \frac{5}{3} \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin 5x}{5x} = \frac{5}{3} \times 1 = \frac{5}{3} \]

Question 3

Determine \(\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{4x^3 - 2x + 1}{2x^3 + x^2 - 5}\)

Divide numerator and denominator by \(x^3\):

\[ \frac{4 - \frac{2}{x^2} + \frac{1}{x^3}}{2 + \frac{1}{x} - \frac{5}{x^3}} \]

As \(x \to \infty\), terms with \(x\) in denominator approach 0:

\[ \frac{4 - 0 + 0}{2 + 0 - 0} = 2 \]
