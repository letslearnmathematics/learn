Introduction to Sequences

A sequence is an ordered list of numbers following a specific pattern, while a series is the sum of the terms of a sequence.

Example sequence:

\[ 2, 5, 8, 11, 14, \ldots \]

Corresponding series:

\[ 2 + 5 + 8 + 11 + 14 + \cdots \]

Terminology:

  • Term: Each number in the sequence (a₁, a₂, a₃, ...)
  • General term: Formula for the nth term (aₙ)
  • Finite sequence: Has a last term
  • Infinite sequence: Continues indefinitely

Arithmetic Progression (AP)

A sequence where each term after the first is obtained by adding a constant common difference (d).

Example AP:

\[ 5, 9, 13, 17, 21, \ldots \quad (d = 4) \]

AP Formulas

nth term:

\[ a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d \]

Sum of first n terms:

\[ S_n = \frac{n}{2}[2a_1 + (n-1)d] \]

or

\[ S_n = \frac{n}{2}(a_1 + a_n) \]

Example Problem:

Find the 15th term and sum of first 15 terms of the AP: 3, 7, 11, 15, ...

Given: a₁ = 3, d = 4

15th term:

\[ a_{15} = 3 + (15-1) \times 4 = 3 + 56 = 59 \]

Sum of first 15 terms:

\[ S_{15} = \frac{15}{2}(3 + 59) = \frac{15}{2} \times 62 = 465 \]

Geometric Progression (GP)

A sequence where each term after the first is obtained by multiplying by a constant common ratio (r).

Example GP:

\[ 2, 6, 18, 54, 162, \ldots \quad (r = 3) \]

GP Formulas

nth term:

\[ a_n = a_1 \times r^{n-1} \]

Sum of first n terms:

\[ S_n = \frac{a_1(1 - r^n)}{1 - r} \quad (r \neq 1) \]

Sum to infinity:

\[ S_\infty = \frac{a_1}{1 - r} \quad (|r| < 1) \]

Example Problem:

Find the 6th term and sum of first 6 terms of the GP: 5, 10, 20, 40, ...

Given: a₁ = 5, r = 2

6th term:

\[ a_6 = 5 \times 2^{5} = 5 \times 32 = 160 \]

Sum of first 6 terms:

\[ S_6 = \frac{5(1 - 2^6)}{1 - 2} = \frac{5(1 - 64)}{-1} = \frac{-315}{-1} = 315 \]

Recurrent Sequences

Sequences where each term is defined as a function of preceding terms.

Fibonacci sequence:

\[ F_n = F_{n-1} + F_{n-2} \] \[ \text{with } F_1 = 1, F_2 = 1 \] \[ 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, \ldots \]

Characteristics:

  • Requires initial terms
  • Recurrence relation defines subsequent terms
  • Can be linear or nonlinear

Example Problem:

A sequence is defined by \( a_n = 2a_{n-1} + 3 \) with \( a_1 = 1 \). Find the first 5 terms.

Given: a₁ = 1

Subsequent terms:

\[ a_2 = 2(1) + 3 = 5 \] \[ a_3 = 2(5) + 3 = 13 \] \[ a_4 = 2(13) + 3 = 29 \] \[ a_5 = 2(29) + 3 = 61 \]

Sequence: 1, 5, 13, 29, 61, ...

Ghanaian Example:

A small business in Accra starts with 10 customers. Each month, the number of customers grows by 20%. How many customers will they have after 1 year?

This is a GP with a₁ = 10, r = 1.2, n = 12

\[ a_{12} = 10 \times (1.2)^{11} \approx 10 \times 7.43 \approx 74 \text{ customers} \]

Practice Exercise

Question 1 (AP)

The 4th term of an AP is 10 and the 10th term is 34. Find the first term and common difference.

Using aₙ = a₁ + (n-1)d:

\[ a_4 = a_1 + 3d = 10 \] \[ a_{10} = a_1 + 9d = 34 \]

Subtract equations:

\[ 6d = 24 \Rightarrow d = 4 \] \[ a_1 = 10 - 3(4) = -2 \]

Question 2 (GP)

The 3rd term of a GP is 36 and the 6th term is 972. Find the common ratio and first term.

Using aₙ = a₁rⁿ⁻¹:

\[ a_3 = a_1r^2 = 36 \] \[ a_6 = a_1r^5 = 972 \]

Divide equations:

\[ r^3 = 27 \Rightarrow r = 3 \] \[ a_1 = 36 / 3^2 = 4 \]

Question 3 (Recurrent)

A sequence is defined by \( x_n = x_{n-1} + 2x_{n-2} \) with \( x_1 = 1 \) and \( x_2 = 3 \). Find \( x_5 \).

Calculate sequentially:

\[ x_3 = x_2 + 2x_1 = 3 + 2(1) = 5 \] \[ x_4 = x_3 + 2x_2 = 5 + 2(3) = 11 \] \[ x_5 = x_4 + 2x_3 = 11 + 2(5) = 21 \]
