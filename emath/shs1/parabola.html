Introduction to Parabola

A parabola is a U-shaped curve that is the graph of a quadratic function. It is a conic section formed by the intersection of a cone with a plane parallel to its side.

Standard form of a parabola:

\[ y = ax^2 + bx + c \]

or

\[ y = a(x - h)^2 + k \]

Key Features:

  • Vertex: The turning point (minimum/maximum)
  • Axis of Symmetry: Vertical line through vertex
  • Focus: Fixed point inside the parabola
  • Directrix: Fixed line outside the parabola

Real-World Examples:

  • Path of projectiles
  • Satellite dishes
  • Car headlights
  • Suspension bridges

Basic Properties

Concavity

Determined by coefficient a:

  • If \( a > 0 \): Opens upwards
  • If \( a < 0 \): Opens downwards

Width

Determined by |a|:

  • Large |a|: Narrow parabola
  • Small |a|: Wide parabola

Vertex Form

Reveals vertex directly:

\[ y = a(x - h)^2 + k \]

Vertex at \( (h, k) \)

Equations of a Parabola

Standard Forms

1. Vertical Parabola

\[ y = ax^2 + bx + c \]

Vertex at \( x = -\frac{b}{2a} \)

2. Vertex Form

\[ y = a(x - h)^2 + k \]

Vertex at \( (h, k) \)

3. Horizontal Parabola

\[ x = ay^2 + by + c \]

Opens left or right

Converting Between Forms

General to Vertex Form (Completing the Square)

  1. Start with \( y = ax^2 + bx + c \)
  2. Factor out a: \( y = a(x^2 + \frac{b}{a}x) + c \)
  3. Complete the square: \( y = a\left[(x^2 + \frac{b}{a}x + (\frac{b}{2a})^2) - (\frac{b}{2a})^2\right] + c \)
  4. Simplify: \( y = a(x + \frac{b}{2a})^2 + (c - \frac{b^2}{4a}) \)

Example: Convert \( y = 2x^2 - 4x + 5 \) to vertex form

Solution:

\[ y = 2(x^2 - 2x) + 5 \] \[ y = 2[(x^2 - 2x + 1) - 1] + 5 \] \[ y = 2(x - 1)^2 - 2 + 5 \] \[ y = 2(x - 1)^2 + 3 \]

Vertex at \( (1, 3) \)

Focus and Directrix

For a parabola \( y = ax^2 + bx + c \):

Focus

For vertex form \( y = a(x - h)^2 + k \):

\[ \text{Focus} = \left(h, k + \frac{1}{4a}\right) \]

Directrix

Horizontal line:

\[ y = k - \frac{1}{4a} \]

Example: Find focus and directrix of \( y = \frac{1}{8}x^2 \)

Solution:

Vertex at (0,0), \( a = \frac{1}{8} \)

\[ \text{Focus} = \left(0, 0 + \frac{1}{4 \times \frac{1}{8}}\right) = (0, 2) \] \[ \text{Directrix}: y = 0 - 2 = -2 \]

Sketching a Parabola

Step-by-Step Sketching

  1. Identify the form (standard or vertex)
  2. Find the vertex (h,k) or calculate \( (-\frac{b}{2a}, f(-\frac{b}{2a})) \)
  3. Determine direction (up if a>0, down if a<0)
  4. Find y-intercept (set x=0, solve for y)
  5. Find x-intercepts (set y=0, solve quadratic)
  6. Plot points and draw smooth curve

Example: Sketch \( y = x^2 - 4x + 3 \)

  1. Vertex: \( x = -\frac{-4}{2} = 2 \), \( y = (2)^2 -4(2)+3 = -1 \) → (2,-1)
  2. Opens upward (a=1>0)
  3. Y-intercept: (0,3)
  4. X-intercepts: \( x^2-4x+3=0 \) → (x-1)(x-3)=0 → (1,0) and (3,0)
Vertex (2,-1) Y-int (0,3) X-ints (1,0), (3,0)

Transformations

Vertical Shift

\[ y = x^2 + k \]

Shifts up/down by k units

Horizontal Shift

\[ y = (x - h)^2 \]

Shifts left/right by h units

Vertical Stretch/Compress

\[ y = ax^2 \]

|a|>1: Narrower, 0<|a|<1: Wider

Reflection

\[ y = -ax^2 \]

Flips parabola upside down

Describe transformations from \( y = x^2 \) to \( y = -2(x-3)^2 + 4 \):

  1. Reflected over x-axis (negative sign)
  2. Vertically stretched by factor of 2
  3. Shifted right 3 units
  4. Shifted up 4 units

Tangent and Normal to a Parabola

Tangent Line

A line that touches the parabola at exactly one point (point of tangency).

1. Using Derivatives

  1. Find derivative \( \frac{dy}{dx} \) (gives slope)
  2. Evaluate at point of tangency for slope m
  3. Use point-slope form: \( y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \)

2. Algebraic Method

For parabola \( y = ax^2 + bx + c \) and line \( y = mx + d \):

  1. Set equations equal: \( ax^2 + bx + c = mx + d \)
  2. Rearrange: \( ax^2 + (b-m)x + (c-d) = 0 \)
  3. Set discriminant to zero (one solution): \( (b-m)^2 - 4a(c-d) = 0 \)

Example: Find tangent to \( y = x^2 - 3x + 4 \) at x=2

Solution:

  1. Point: \( y = (2)^2 -3(2) + 4 = 2 \) → (2,2)
  2. Derivative: \( \frac{dy}{dx} = 2x - 3 \)
  3. Slope at x=2: \( m = 2(2) - 3 = 1 \)
  4. Equation: \( y - 2 = 1(x - 2) \) → \( y = x \)

Normal Line

A line perpendicular to the tangent at the point of contact.

  1. Find slope \( m_t \) of tangent (as above)
  2. Slope of normal \( m_n = -\frac{1}{m_t} \)
  3. Use point-slope form with \( m_n \)

Continuing previous example:

  1. Tangent slope \( m_t = 1 \)
  2. Normal slope \( m_n = -1 \)
  3. Equation: \( y - 2 = -1(x - 2) \) → \( y = -x + 4 \)
Tangent: y = x Normal: y = -x + 4 Point (2,2)

Applications of Parabola

Real-World Applications

1. Projectile Motion

The path of a thrown object follows a parabolic trajectory under gravity:

\[ y = x\tanθ - \frac{gx^2}{2v^2\cos^2θ} \]

Where θ = launch angle, v = initial velocity

2. Satellite Dishes

Parabolic shape focuses incoming signals to the receiver at the focus.

Equation: \( y = \frac{1}{4f}x^2 \) where f = focal length

3. Architecture

Parabolic arches distribute weight efficiently in bridges and buildings.

Example: The Gateway Arch in St. Louis

Optimization Problems

Example Problem:

A farmer has 200m of fencing to enclose a rectangular area along a river (needs no fencing on river side). Find maximum possible area.

  1. Let x = length perpendicular to river, y = parallel
  2. Fencing: \( 2x + y = 200 \) → \( y = 200 - 2x \)
  3. Area: \( A = xy = x(200-2x) = 200x - 2x^2 \)
  4. This is a parabola opening downward
  5. Vertex at \( x = -\frac{b}{2a} = -\frac{200}{-4} = 50 \)
  6. Maximum area: \( A = 200(50) - 2(50)^2 = 5000 \) m²

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Convert \( y = 3x^2 - 12x + 5 \) to vertex form and identify the vertex.

\[ y = 3(x^2 - 4x) + 5 \] \[ y = 3[(x^2 - 4x + 4) - 4] + 5 \] \[ y = 3(x - 2)^2 - 12 + 5 \] \[ y = 3(x - 2)^2 - 7 \]

Vertex at (2, -7)

Question 2

Find the equation of the tangent to \( y = 2x^2 + 3x - 1 \) at x = -1.

Point: \( y = 2(-1)^2 + 3(-1) - 1 = -2 \) → (-1,-2)

Derivative: \( \frac{dy}{dx} = 4x + 3 \)

Slope at x=-1: \( m = 4(-1) + 3 = -1 \)

Equation: \( y + 2 = -1(x + 1) \) → \( y = -x - 3 \)

Question 3

A ball is thrown upward from ground level with initial velocity 20 m/s. Its height follows \( h(t) = -5t^2 + 20t \). Find maximum height and time to hit ground.

Maximum height at vertex:

\[ t = -\frac{b}{2a} = -\frac{20}{-10} = 2 \text{ sec} \] \[ h(2) = -5(4) + 20(2) = 20 \text{ m} \]

Time to hit ground (h=0):

\[ -5t^2 + 20t = 0 \] \[ -5t(t - 4) = 0 \]

t=0 (start) or t=4 sec
