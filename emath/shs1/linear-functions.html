Linear Functions

A linear function is a polynomial function of degree 1 that can be written in the form:

\[ f(x) = mx + b \]

Where:
• \( m \) = slope (gradient)
• \( b \) = y-intercept

Characteristics:

  • Forms a straight line when graphed
  • Constant rate of change (slope)
  • Domain and range are all real numbers

Standard Form:

\[ Ax + By = C \]

Where A, B, and C are integers

Finding Slope

The slope (\( m \)) measures the steepness of a line:

\[ m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} \]

Given two points \((x_1, y_1)\) and \((x_2, y_2)\)

Positive Slope

Line rises from left to right
(e.g., \( y = 2x + 1 \))

Negative Slope

Line falls from left to right
(e.g., \( y = -x + 3 \))

Zero Slope

Horizontal line
(e.g., \( y = 4 \))

Undefined Slope

Vertical line
(e.g., \( x = -2 \))

Equation Forms

Slope-Intercept Form

\[ y = mx + b \]

Most common for graphing

Point-Slope Form

\[ y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \]

When you know a point and slope

Two-Point Form

\[ \frac{y - y_1}{x - x_1} = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} \]

When you know two points

Graphs of Linear Functions

Plotting Linear Equations

To graph a linear equation:

  1. Identify the y-intercept (\( b \)) and plot it
  2. Use the slope (\( m \)) to find another point
  3. Draw a straight line through the points

Example: Graph \( y = 2x + 1 \)

  • Y-intercept at (0, 1)
  • Slope = 2 (rise 2, run 1)

Finding Equations from Graphs

To determine the equation of a line from its graph:

From Two Points

  1. Find two clear points
  2. Calculate slope \( m \)
  3. Use point-slope form

From Slope and Intercept

  1. Identify y-intercept \( b \)
  2. Determine slope \( m \)
  3. Write as \( y = mx + b \)

Example: Find the equation of this line:

Points: (0, -2) and (2, 2)

\[ m = \frac{2 - (-2)}{2 - 0} = 2 \] \[ y = 2x - 2 \]

Parallel and Perpendicular Lines

Parallel Lines

Have identical slopes

\[ m_1 = m_2 \]

Perpendicular Lines

Slopes are negative reciprocals

\[ m_1 \times m_2 = -1 \]

Areas Enclosed by Graphs

Calculating Areas

To find areas enclosed by linear graphs and axes:

Triangle Area

For right triangles formed by axes:

\[ \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height} \]

Polygon Area

For irregular shapes:

  1. Find all vertices
  2. Plot points
  3. Use shoelace formula

Shoelace Formula

\[ A = \frac{1}{2} \left| \sum_{i=1}^n (x_i y_{i+1}) - \sum_{i=1}^n (y_i x_{i+1}) \right| \]

Where \( (x_{n+1}, y_{n+1}) = (x_1, y_1) \)

Example Problem:

Find the area enclosed by the lines \( y = 2x \), \( y = -x + 6 \), and the x-axis.

  1. Find intersection points:
    • \( y = 2x \) meets x-axis at (0,0)
    • \( y = -x + 6 \) meets x-axis at (6,0)
    • Lines intersect when \( 2x = -x + 6 \) → \( x = 2 \), \( y = 4 \)
  2. Vertices: (0,0), (2,4), (6,0)
  3. Using shoelace formula: \[ A = \frac{1}{2} |(0×4 + 2×0 + 6×0) - (0×2 + 4×6 + 0×0)| = 12 \text{ units}^2 \]

Between Two Lines and Axes

For area between two lines and coordinate axes:

  1. Find x- and y-intercepts for both lines
  2. Determine intersection point
  3. Calculate area as sum/difference of triangles

Modelling with Linear Equations

Real-World Applications

Linear functions model many real-life situations:

Economics

Cost functions: \( C(x) = mx + b \)

Where \( b \) is fixed cost, \( m \) is variable cost per unit

Physics

Motion: \( d(t) = vt + d_0 \)

Where \( v \) is velocity, \( d_0 \) is initial position

Business

Revenue: \( R(x) = px \)

Where \( p \) is price per unit, \( x \) is quantity sold

Problem-Solving Approach

Steps to model real-world situations:

  1. Identify variables (independent and dependent)
  2. Determine known quantities (slope, intercepts)
  3. Write the equation based on given information
  4. Solve for required unknowns
  5. Interpret results in context

Example Problem:

A taxi company charges GH₵5.00 base fare plus GH₵2.50 per km. Write the fare function and find the cost for a 12km trip.

  1. Variables:
    • Independent: distance (\( x \)) in km
    • Dependent: fare (\( F \)) in GH₵
  2. Known quantities:
    • Fixed cost (y-intercept): GH₵5.00
    • Variable cost (slope): GH₵2.50/km
  3. Equation: \( F(x) = 2.50x + 5.00 \)
  4. For 12km: \( F(12) = 2.50(12) + 5.00 = \) GH₵35.00

Break-Even Analysis

Finding when cost equals revenue:

Set \( C(x) = R(x) \) and solve for \( x \):

\[ \text{Fixed Cost} + \text{Variable Cost} = \text{Price} \times \text{Quantity} \]

The intersection point is the break-even quantity

Practice Exercise

Question 1

Find the equation of the line passing through (2,5) with slope -3.

Using point-slope form:

\[ y - 5 = -3(x - 2) \] \[ y = -3x + 6 + 5 \] \[ y = -3x + 11 \]

Question 2

Calculate the area enclosed by \( y = x + 2 \), \( y = -2x + 8 \), and the y-axis.

  1. Find intersection points:
    • Both lines meet y-axis at (0,2) and (0,8)
    • Lines intersect when \( x + 2 = -2x + 8 \) → \( x = 2 \), \( y = 4 \)
  2. Vertices: (0,2), (2,4), (0,8)
  3. Area is a triangle with base 6 (8-2) and height 2: \[ A = \frac{1}{2} \times 6 \times 2 = 6 \text{ units}^2 \]

Question 3

A phone plan charges GH₵10 monthly fee plus GH₵0.50 per minute. Write the cost function and find cost for 45 minutes.

\[ C(x) = 0.50x + 10 \] \[ C(45) = 0.50(45) + 10 = 22.50 + 10 = \text{GH₵32.50} \]
