\(c)\) A fair die is thrown once.

\(\hspace{0.5cm} i)\) Write down the set of all the possible outcomes.

\(\hspace{0.5cm} ii)\) Find the probability of obtaining a multiple of 2.

Question 2


\(a)\) A water tank in the form of a cuboid of height 22cm and a rectangular base of length 7cm and width 5cm is filled with water. The water is then poured into a cylindrical container of diameter 14cm. Calculate the height of the water in the cylindrical container. (Take \(\pi = \frac{22}{7}\))


\(b)\) A trader is given 15 percent discount on goods bought from a factory. If the original price of an item in the factory is ₵45,000,000.00, calculate

\(\hspace{0.5cm} i)\) the discount on the item

\(\hspace{0.5cm} ii)\) the amount the trader paid for the item.


\(c)\) Find the truth set of \(\frac{x - 3}{3} \leq \frac{1}{2} + x\) and illustrate your answer on the number line.






Question 3


Using a ruler and a pair of compasses only,

\(a)\) Construct triangle \(PQR\) such that the length of \(PQ = 10\) cm, angle \(QPR = 90^\circ\) and angle \(PQR = 30^\circ\).

\(\hspace{0.5cm}\)Measure the length of \(PR\).

\(b)\) Bisect the angle \(QRP\) to meet \(PQ\) at \(M\).

\(c)\) With \(M\) as center, and radius \(MP\) draw a circle.

\(d)\) Measure the radius of the circle.






Question 4


The bar chart above is for the distribution of marks in a class test.

\(a)\) \(i)\) Write down the frequency table for the distribution.

\(\hspace{0.5cm} ii)\) Use the table to find the mean mark.

\(b)\) If the pass mark is 4, how many pupils failed the test?






Question 5


\(a)\) Mr Jones used 173 units of electricity last month. If the charge for the first 110 units was ₵150 per unit and ₵200 per unit for the rest, calculate the total bill for Mr. Jones.


\(b)\) Express 4 hours in seconds leaving your answer in standard form.


\(c)\) Given the vectors \(\mathbf{r} = \begin{pmatrix}3 \\ -5\end{pmatrix}\) and \(\mathbf{p} = \begin{pmatrix}-7 \\ -9\end{pmatrix}\), and \(\mathbf{q} = 2\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{r}\), find \(\mathbf{q}\)





Question 6


\(a)\) Using a scale of 2 cm to 2 units on both axes, draw two perpendicular axes \(Ox\) and \(Oy\) on a graph sheet. On the same graph sheet, mark the \(x\)-axis from \(-10\) to \(10\) and the \(y\)-axis from \(-10\) to \(10\).

\(\hspace{0.5cm} i)\) Plot \(A(2, 2)\), \(B(6, 2)\) and \(C(4, 6)\). Join \(AB\), \(BC\) and \(AC\).

\(\hspace{0.5cm} ii)\) Draw the image triangle \(A_1B_1C_1\) of triangle \(ABC\) under a clockwise rotation of \(90^\circ\) about the origin, where \(A \rightarrow A_1\), \(B \rightarrow B_1\) and \(C \rightarrow C_1\).

\(\hspace{0.5cm} iii)\) Draw the image triangle \(A_2B_2C_2\) of triangle \(ABC\) under a reflection in the \(y-\)axis, where \(A \rightarrow A_2\), \(B \rightarrow B_2\) and \(C \rightarrow C_2\).


\(b)\) When 12 is added to a certain number and the sum is multiplied by 4, the result is 60. Find the number.
