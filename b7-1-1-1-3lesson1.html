Rounding (off, up, down)


Rounding off a number simply means to round the number up or down given a particular place on the place value chart.

So when you round up or down a number, you say, you have rounded off the given number.

When rounding off a number, we are mostly given a particular place value as our reference point.

We then look at the digit to the immediate right of our reference point and check whether it is part of these two sets below:


\(*\) {\(0, 1, 2, 3, 4\)} \(\Rightarrow\) We shall call this set "Small digits"

OR

\(*\) {\(5, 6, 7, 8, 9\)} \(\Rightarrow\) We shall also call this set "Big digits"





Rounding down a Number


If the digit to the immediate right of our reference point is part of the small digits set above:


1. Change all the digits to the right of our reference point to zero, including the digit to the immediate right of our reference point.

2. Then rewrite the new number as your rounded value.


Note:
Don't forget to include the zeros when rewriting the new number formed.


This is what we mean by rounding down a number, given a particular place value.





Rounding up a Number


If the digit to the immediate right of our reference point is part of the big digits set above:


1. Add one to the digit at our reference point.

2. Then change all the digits to the right of our reference point to zero (0), including the digit to the immediate right of our reference point.

3. Rewrite the new number formed as your rounded value.


Note:
Don't forget to include the zeros when rewriting the new number formed.

This is what we mean by rounding up a number, given a particular place value.
