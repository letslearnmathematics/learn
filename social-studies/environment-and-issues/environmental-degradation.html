Definition of Environmental Degradation

This can be defined as the gradual distraction of the quality of the environment which in most cases is caused by human activities that makes it impure for living organisms. There are three main types of environmental degradation:

  • Land Degradation
  • Water Pollution
  • Air Pollution

In the video below you will learn more about the meaning of environmental degradation, its causes, and effects on the environment. You will also learn about some examples of environmental degradation or some activities that lead to environmental degradation and the types of environmental degradation.

Land Degradation

This is the gradual destruction of the quality of lands (farm lands) either by human activities such as illegal mining, poor quarreling, indiscriminate sand winning and other natural phenomena such as volcanic eruption, tsunami, earthquake, earth tremor etc. that makes the land impure for productive use.

Causes

Below are some of the causes of land degradation:

Overgrazing by farm animals

Indiscriminate sand winning

Illegal mining activities (galamsey)

Deforestation

Poor/bad farming practices or activities

Drought

Open defecation

Urbanization

Effects

Below are some of the effects of land degradation:

Loss of soil fertility

Desertification

Global warming

Deforestation

Distinction of rare wildlife animals

Climate change

Prevention

Below are some of the ways we can prevent land degradation:

Avoid over-grazing by farm animals

Proper mining activity

Public education

Legislation

Afforestation

Re-afforestation

Environmentally friendly farming practice eg. Organic farming etc.

Water Pollution

This is the release of a harmful substances such as DDT, oil etc. into water bodies. These substances, since they are poisonous in nature, kills the aquatic life in that particular water source as well as any living organism that may come into contact with such water body.

Causes

Below are some of the causes of Water Pollution:

Oil spillage

Farming near water bodies

Application of dangerous chemicals such as DDT for fishing

Swimming, bathing and washing in water bodies

Defecation in water bodies

Indiscriminate disposal of solid waste

Effects of Water Pollution

Below are some of the effects of water pollution:

Destruction of aquatic life

Outbreak of water-borne diseases

Unpleasant odour

Low agricultural produce

Drying of water bodies

High cost of water treatment

Adverse effect on fishing and transportation

Prevention

Below are some of the ways we can prevent water pollution:

Proper disposal of solid and industrial wastes

The use of appropriate fishing method either than DDT.

Public education

Proper sewage treatment and management

Practice organic farming

Recycling

Planting of more trees and other cover crops near water banks



Air Pollution

This refers to the release of poisonous gases and other harmful substances into the atmosphere that mix up with the air we breathe, making it impure/unhealthy for human and other living organisms.

This is mostly caused by some human activities such as farming, fumes from vehicle exhaust pipes and other heavy industrial machines

Causes

Below are some of the causes of Air Pollution:

Burning of fossil fuel

Agricultural activities

Fumes from vehicle exhaust pipes and other heavy industrial

Illegal mining activities

Domestic fuel wood fires

Bush fire

Unpleasant odour

Effects of Air Pollution

Below are some of the ways we can prevent water pollution:

Health problems

Acid rain

Global warming

Destruction of ozone layer

Ways to Prevent Air Pollution

Below are some of the ways we can prevent air pollution:

The use of public mode transport

Energy conservation

Ban on the importation of old and averaged vehicles

The use of LPG instead of firewood

Control the establishment of factories

Public education
