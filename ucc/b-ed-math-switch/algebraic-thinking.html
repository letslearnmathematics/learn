Course Outline

The Developing Algebraic Thinking (EMA 111D) course is designed to expose students to the various elements of algebraic thinking necessary for prospective teachers to promote meaningful teaching and learning of algebra in schools. The course focuses on three main components of algebraic thinking: generalization, equality, and unknown quantities.

The major topics covered in this course include:

Components of Algebraic Thinking

Algebra of Sets

Relations, Functions and Mapping

Properties of Integers

Linear and Exponential Series

Convergence and Divergence of Series

Partial Fractions

Mathematical Induction

Linear Inequalities

By studying this course, you will learn to understand generalization in algebraic functions, the purpose and use of unknowns in algebraic expressions and equations, and how to use algebraic thinking to analyze the conceptual structures of selected topics in algebra. You will also learn effective teaching strategies for each algebraic topic covered in the course.

The course uses a variety of teaching methods including discussions, small group work, role play, and effective questioning to accommodate different learning styles. Assessment includes quizzes, assignments, presentations, and an end-of-semester examination.
