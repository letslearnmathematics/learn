Course Outline

The Psychological Basis of Teaching and Learning Mathematics focuses on understanding how learners acquire mathematical concepts, develop cognitive structures, and apply psychological principles in mathematical instruction. This course equips students with the knowledge needed to enhance effective teaching and meaningful learning of mathematics.

The major topics covered in this course include:

Introduction to the Psychology of Mathematics

Theories of Learning Mathematics and Their Implications

Concept and Concept Formation in Mathematics

Schema and Understanding in Mathematics

Intuitive Thinking, Reflective Thinking, and Discovery Teaching

Abstraction, Generalization, Symbols, and Models in Mathematics

By studying this course, you will learn about cognitive development, theories of learning such as behaviorism, cognitivism, and constructivism, and how these apply to teaching mathematics effectively. You will also explore the processes of concept formation, the role of schemas in understanding, and the importance of reflective and intuitive thinking in mathematical learning.

In the video below, you will gain further insight into these psychological principles and how they influence effective mathematics instruction, learner understanding, and classroom practice.
