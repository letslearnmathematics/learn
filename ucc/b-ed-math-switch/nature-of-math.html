Course Outline

The Nature of Mathematics (EMA 201) is designed to expose prospective mathematics teachers to the historical development of mathematics, the major philosophical schools of thought, and the basis of classical proofs in mathematics. This course helps students understand what mathematics is, how it has evolved, and how it is justified philosophically.

The major topics covered in this course include:

Definition of Mathematics

Terminologies in Mathematics Education (e.g., axioms, theorems, conjectures)

Historical Development of Numbers, Algebra, Geometry, and Logic

Philosophy of Mathematics (e.g., Platonism, Formalism, Intuitionism)

Proofs in Mathematics (including mathematical induction)

By studying this course, you will learn to define mathematics, explain key mathematical terms, discuss the historical evolution of major branches of mathematics, compare philosophical views on mathematics, and understand the nature and methods of mathematical proof.

The course uses a blend of lectures, group presentations, discussions, and research activities, supported by ICT tools. Assessment includes quizzes, assignments, and an end-of-semester examination.
