What are Parts of Speech?

Parts of speech are the basic types of words that make up the English language. Each word in a sentence belongs to a particular part of speech, depending on how it is used.

There are eight main parts of speech in English:

  • Nouns
  • Pronouns
  • Adjectives
  • Verbs
  • Adverbs
  • Prepositions
  • Conjunctions
  • Interjections

Understanding the parts of speech helps you to construct correct sentences and improve your grammar.

Nouns

A noun is a word used to name a person, animal, place, thing, action, event or occasion and abstract idea or quality.

Proper Nouns

A proper noun is a specific word identified with a person, place or a thing. There are two things to look out for when writing a proper noun. There are two things to look out for when writing a proper noun.

The word being used as a proper noun must be unique - one-of-a-kind.

It should begin with a capital letter no matter where it occurs in a sentence.


Examples of proper nouns.

Unique words identified with persons:

Jacob Brako, Sampson Ahenkora, Kwadjo Kumi, Mubarak, Musa, Jesus etc.

Unique words identified with places:

Accra Cultural Centre, Accra Sports Stadium, Kotoka International Airport, Christ Apostolic Church, Odorkor Police Station, Ernest Publications Ltd., Nkawkaw, Accra, Mankessim, Ghana, Nigeria, United States of America, etc.

Unique words identified with days and months:

Sunday, Monday, Tuesday ... Saturday, January, February, March ... December.

Unique words identified with event or occasion:

Aboakyiir Festival, Winneba Masquerade Festival, Independence Anniversary, Trade Fair, Christmas, Easter, Eid -ul- Adha, Farmers Day, etc.


Usage

Proper nouns are always written with their first letter capitalized no matter where they are found in the sentence.

Example:

Kofi is reading the book.

Give the book to Kofi for me.

The book is being read by Kofi.


NOTE:

The first letter of proper noun "Kofi" in the above three sentences is capitalized, no matter where it is found in the sentence.

Common Nouns

Common nouns are nouns that do not name any particular persons, places, things, etc. Common noun words do not point to specific persons, places, animals or things.

Examples.

Common nouns of people:

mother, father, baby, child, toddler, teenager, grandmother, student, teacher, minister, businessperson, salesclerk, woman, man, etc.

Common nouns of animals:

lion, tiger, bear, dog, cat, alligator, cricket, bird, wolf, etc.

Common nouns of things:

table, truck, book, pencil, iPad, computer, coat, boots, etc.

Common nouns of places:

city, state, country, continent, coffee shop, restaurant, park, zoo, etc.

Common nouns of ideas:

envy, love, hate, respect, patriotism, pride, etc.

NOTE:

Don't capitalize a common noun unless the common noun is the first word in a sentence or part of a title.
